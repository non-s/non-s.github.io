Privacy Policy

Wild Brief · Last updated: May 20, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Wild Brief ("the Service") collects, uses and protects information when it operates against third-party platforms such as TikTok and YouTube. Wild Brief is operated by the owner of the repository non-s/non-s.github.io.

1. Scope

The Service is an automated publishing pipeline that posts to a single operator-owned account on each integrated platform. It does not collect personal data from end users, does not display advertising, does not build profiles of viewers, and does not have any user-facing sign-up.

2. Information We Process

The Service handles only the following categories of data:

  • API credentials — OAuth access tokens, refresh tokens and client secrets for the platforms the operator has connected. Stored exclusively as encrypted GitHub Actions secrets and shredded from process memory at the end of every workflow run.
  • Operator account metadata — the operator's own TikTok / YouTube channel ID, video IDs of content the Service has published, and aggregate analytics (view counts, retention) returned by the platform APIs for the operator's own channel.
  • Source footage and AI-generated metadata — Pexels clip identifiers, narration text and titles produced by the pipeline.

3. Information We Do Not Process

  • No data about third-party TikTok or YouTube users.
  • No comments, messages, follower lists or any other audience data beyond the operator's own channel-level aggregates.
  • No biometric, facial recognition or location data.
  • No cookies, tracking pixels or third-party analytics on this site.

4. How We Use Information

API credentials are used solely to call the integrated platforms on the operator's behalf — specifically to upload videos and to read back analytics for the operator's own channel. Credentials are never shared with any third party.

5. Sharing

Wild Brief does not sell or share data with third parties. The only external services contacted by the pipeline are the platform APIs the operator has authorized (TikTok, YouTube), the footage source (Pexels) and AI inference providers (Mistral, Cerebras, Gemini, Groq) under their respective privacy policies.

6. Retention

Credentials are stored only for as long as the operator keeps them in GitHub Secrets. The operator can revoke access at any time from the TikTok Developer Portal or the YouTube account settings, at which point the Service will no longer be able to publish.

7. Your Choices

End viewers on TikTok and YouTube interact with the Service only by watching the videos it publishes. Their relationship is governed by each platform's own privacy policy. The Service does not store any data about them.

8. Security

All credentials live in encrypted GitHub Actions secrets. Tokens are not written to disk, are scrubbed from process memory at the end of each run, and are never logged. A panic kill switch (PANIC_HALT=1) halts every entry point in case of incident.

9. Children

The Service is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any data from children.

10. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated. Material changes will be reflected in the "Last updated" date above.

11. Contact

For privacy inquiries, open an issue at github.com/non-s/non-s.github.io/issues.
