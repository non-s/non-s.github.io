# ADR 0003: Rainy-Night Anime Lofi as Sub-Niche

## Status
Accepted

## Context
Lofi and chillhop are saturated head terms. Channels like Lofi Girl dominate broad searches. A small new channel cannot compete on generic "lofi" alone.

## Decision
Focus on a specific sub-niche: **rainy-night, cozy anime lofi**. All titles, tags, thumbnails, and playlists reinforce this identity.

Examples:
- Title: "Rainy Night Lofi 🌧️ Cozy Beats for Late Night Study"
- Playlist: "Rainy Night Lofi Shorts", "Midnight Vibes Mix"
- Tags: ["rainy night lofi", "anime lofi", "cozy anime lofi", "midnight lofi", ...]
- Thumbnail: Purple/blue rainy night aesthetic, anime girl studying

## Rationale
1. **Long-tail SEO:** "rainy night lofi" has less competition than "lofi"
2. **Recognizable brand:** Viewers remember the consistent aesthetic and mood
3. **Mood buckets:** Each video gets a consistent title/tag/hook tied to its mood (e.g., "Cozy Cat Lofi", "Midnight Library Lofi")
4. **Playlist strategy:** Separate series per mood, not one generic "Lofi Beats" folder

## Consequences
- ✅ Findability: niche SEO is more attainable for new channels
- ✅ Retention: consistent visual branding helps viewers find you again
- ✅ Growth potential: niche community is smaller but more engaged
- ⚠️ Narrower appeal: some viewers looking for generic lofi will skip
- ⚠️ High maintenance: branding consistency requires careful QA on every upload

## Alternatives Considered
1. **Broad lofi approach:** Impossible to compete with Lofi Girl on generic terms
2. **Daily vlog or stream:** Different audience, requires narration (breaks lofi aesthetic)
3. **Gaming lofi:** Requires gameplay footage, changes licensing requirements
