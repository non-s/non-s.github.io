# Wild Brief Million-View Scale System

## Strategic Read

Wild Brief has found discovery. The current constraint is not awareness; it is
turning Shorts Feed spikes into a repeat audience.

Current Studio snapshot:

- 30,314 views in the last 28 days.
- 10,500 engaged views.
- 31.8% stayed to watch and 68.2% swiped away.
- 96.2% of traffic comes from Shorts Feed.
- 9,800 monthly viewers.
- 99.8% new viewers, 0.2% casual viewers, less than 0.1% recurring viewers.
- 37 subscribers gained in the period, 46 total subscribers at the live read.
- 81.3% of watch time is mobile.
- 51.6% of views are from the United States.

The channel is being tested by YouTube. The next phase is to teach viewers that
Wild Brief is a recognizable series worth returning to.

## Operating Laws

1. Frame zero must win the swipe.

   Every Short opens with the animal, a visible cue, and a payoff promise. If the
   first frame or first spoken line could belong to any animal channel, it does
   not publish.

2. One video gets one payoff.

   Remove second facts. The viewer should know exactly what they are waiting to
   see and why the ending satisfies the opening.

3. No isolated uploads.

   Every strong Short belongs to a lane: Farmyard Minds, Body Clues, Ocean
   Signals, Predator Tells, or Pet Signals. Random animal facts do not build
   memory.

4. Winners produce sequels before broad exploration.

   High-retention winners become immediate sequel prompts. High-view,
   low-retention videos become opening remakes, not copied topics.

5. The CTA comes after the payoff.

   The channel promise is simple: follow for one animal signal a day. It should
   feel like the next useful episode, not a sales line.

6. Shorts Feed is the engine, search is the tail.

   Winners get query-shaped metadata around animal + behavior + why/how so the
   channel can gain durable discovery outside the initial Shorts Feed burst.

## The New System

`scripts/scale_blueprint.py` writes `_data/scale_blueprint.json`.

It reads:

- `_data/channel_objective.json`
- `_data/analytics/latest.json`
- `_data/channel_success.json`
- `_data/queue_audit.json`
- `_data/next_shorts.json`
- `_data/early_performance.json`

It outputs:

- channel phase
- north-star strategy
- normalized Studio baseline
- bottlenecks
- operating rules
- series lanes
- winner/remake actions
- production commands
- milestone path toward 100, 1,000, 1,000,000 views/28d, and creator-award scale

The dashboard now renders this as the first executive growth layer.

## Current Priority

The highest leverage move is not more volume. It is making every upload feel
like a memorable episode from a specific channel:

- first second: stronger cue
- middle: cleaner mechanism
- end: payoff plus next episode question
- next day: sequel or remake based on retention, not vibes
