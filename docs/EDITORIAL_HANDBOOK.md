# Wild Brief Editorial Handbook

## Series

| Series | Promise | Use when | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Animal Minds | intelligence, memory, social signals | behavior shows learning or decision-making | vague "smart animal" claims |
| Survival Cheats | escape, defense, camouflage, hunting | visible action changes survival odds | gore-first openings |
| Baby Nature | young animals and care systems | the hook has a newborn, nest, parent or first skill | cute-only stories without payoff |
| Impossible Biology | anatomy, physiology, extreme adaptation | the body mechanism is the surprise | unsupported superlatives |
| Hidden Behaviors | quiet, rare or missed actions | the first frame hides the reveal | generic "secret" phrasing |

## Opening Contract

- First frame text target: 2-4 words.
- First spoken word target: before 0.7s; acceptable before 1.5s.
- First 1.5s must carry one visible action or unmistakable subject.
- `opening_gate_v2` may warn by default and can block with `OPENING_GATE_MODE=block`.

## Story Contract

- Hook names a visible subject and a specific change.
- Payoff should land before the viewer feels the promise expired.
- Final line should call back to the opening keyword or image.
- Every risky factual claim needs a source URL, GBIF/Wikimedia provenance or scientific name.
- Every render needs an originality pack and source provenance row before upload.

## Experiment Contract

- One creative axis at a time while engaged views/day remain low.
- Promotions require an explicit hypothesis in `_data/experiment_registry.json`.
- Guardrail metric is swipe rate; do not promote a variant that wins retention while raising swipe risk.
