The UK’s [public inquiry into the Covid-19 pandemic](https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/covid-19-inquiry) released its fourth report Tuesday, highlighting the vaccine rollout as a rare success amid widespread criticism of the government’s handling of the crisis. The probe, led by retired judge [Lady Heather Hallett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heather_Hallett,_Baroness_Hallett), is examining decisions made during the pandemic to determine what went wrong and how future responses can improve.

The inquiry’s latest findings commended the rapid deployment of vaccines as an 'extraordinary feat,' noting that over 150 million doses were administered in the UK by early 2022. The rollout began in December 2020, just months after the virus was declared a global pandemic. Despite early supply delays and logistical challenges, health officials prioritized vulnerable groups, achieving one of the highest vaccination rates in Europe.

## How the UK Covid inquiry works

Established in 2022, the inquiry operates independently of the government, with the power to summon witnesses and request documents. Its seven modules cover topics including lockdowns, testing, and the impact on care homes. The fourth module, which focuses on vaccines, is the first to assess a major success of the pandemic response. Public hearings began in June 2023, with testimonies from scientists, ministers, and health officials.

Lady Hallett’s team has faced criticism for its slow pace, with the final report not expected until 2026. Critics argue delays risk losing critical insights as memories fade and key figures move on. However, supporters say thoroughness is necessary to avoid rushed conclusions that could obscure lessons for future pandemics.

The inquiry’s broader scope includes examining the UK’s preparedness for Covid-19, which was widely criticized after years of warnings about pandemic risks. Reports have already highlighted failures in protective equipment supply chains and delayed lockdown decisions, which contributed to higher death tolls compared to some European neighbors.

## What’s next for the inquiry?

The fourth module’s report is just one of several planned, with the fifth and sixth modules set to examine the impact on health services and care homes. The final report, due in 2026, will include recommendations for government and public bodies. Meanwhile, political parties are already debating how the inquiry’s findings might shape future health policies and spending.

Public submissions remain open, allowing individuals and organizations to contribute evidence. The inquiry’s website provides updates on hearings and published reports, ensuring transparency as the process unfolds.

As the inquiry progresses, its findings could influence how the UK—and other nations—prepare for future global health threats. The vaccine rollout’s success, while praised, also raises questions about why other aspects of the response lagged behind.

O inquérito, uma das maiores investigações públicas já realizadas no país, analisa desde decisões políticas até a logística de distribuição de vacinas, passando pelo impacto social e econômico da pandemia.
