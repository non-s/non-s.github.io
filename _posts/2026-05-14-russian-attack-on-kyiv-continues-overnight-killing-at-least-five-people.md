Russian forces launched multiple drone and missile strikes on Kyiv overnight, killing at least five people, Ukraine’s emergency services reported Friday. The overnight assaults followed a rare daytime attack earlier in the day that killed six people, according to Ukrainian officials. Civilian infrastructure and residential neighborhoods bore the brunt of the latest strikes, which began around midnight local time and continued for several hours.

Ukrainian President [Volodymyr Zelenskyy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volodymyr_Zelenskyy) condemned the attacks in a late-night statement, calling them an attempt to terrorize the capital. "The occupiers are trying to break our will with constant strikes," Zelenskyy said in a video address. Ukraine’s air force reported intercepting 18 cruise missiles and 15 drones during the overnight raids, though some projectiles struck their targets.

## Kyiv targets civilian areas in latest wave
The strikes hit several districts, including [Obolon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obolon_District), a residential area in northern Kyiv, and [Sviatoshyn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sviatoshyn), where a multi-story apartment building sustained heavy damage. Emergency crews worked through the night to extinguish fires and recover victims from the rubble. At least two children were among the dead, Ukraine’s State Emergency Service confirmed.

Ukraine’s military said the overnight attacks included [Kh-101](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kh-101) cruise missiles launched from Russian bombers and Iranian-made Shahed drones. The daytime attack earlier featured a mix of ballistic missiles and drones, marking a shift in Russia’s tactics. Military analysts noted the increased use of drones in recent weeks, which are harder to intercept than traditional missiles.

## International response to escalating strikes
The United Nations [Secretary-General](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Secretary-General) Antonio Guterres condemned the attacks in a statement Thursday, calling them "unacceptable violations of international law." The [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union) reiterated its support for Ukraine, with EU foreign policy chief [Josep Borrell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Josep_Borrell) urging immediate de-escalation. Russia has not commented publicly on the strikes, which come as its forces continue to make slow gains in eastern Ukraine.

Ukrainian air defenses remain on high alert as Russian forces show no signs of reducing the intensity of their strikes. The overnight attacks follow a pattern of increasing Russian offensives targeting energy infrastructure and civilian areas ahead of winter. Kyiv’s mayor [Vitali Klitschko](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitali_Klitschko) urged residents to remain in bomb shelters until authorities confirm the all-clear.

The escalation underscores the growing strain on Ukraine’s air defense systems, which have struggled to intercept all incoming threats. Analysts warn the attacks could intensify as winter approaches, with Russia aiming to weaken civilian morale and disrupt critical infrastructure. Ukraine has called for faster delivery of advanced air defense systems from Western allies to counter the sustained assaults.
