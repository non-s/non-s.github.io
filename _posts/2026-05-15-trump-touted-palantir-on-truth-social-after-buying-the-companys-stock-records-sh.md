Former President Donald Trump [Donald Trump](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Trump) disclosed stock purchases in Palantir Technologies [Palantir Technologies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palantir_Technologies) totaling between $630,000 and $2.47 million during the first three months of 2024, according to federal filings. The purchases were made as the company’s shares surged following increased defense sector interest. Trump also sold at least $1.1 million in Palantir stock within the same quarter, highlighting a mixed trading strategy.

Trump’s financial disclosures, filed with the U.S. Office of Government Ethics, reveal the timing of his Palantir investments. On April 8, 2024, he posted on Truth Social: *“Great to see Palantir doing so well – and with our military, they are doing even better! They are highly respected and getting major contracts.”* The post came just days after his reported stock purchases were finalized.

Palantir’s stock price rose sharply in early 2024, driven by contracts with the U.S. Department of Defense and other government agencies. The company, known for its data analytics platforms, has become a key player in defense and intelligence contracting. Trump’s public endorsement coincided with a period of notable growth for Palantir.

Financial experts note that Trump’s trades are publicly disclosed due to his status as a federal officeholder, requiring transparency in investments. The purchases and sales were documented in periodic transaction reports, notifying regulators of his financial activity. His social media promotion of Palantir followed shortly after these filings were made public.

Legal and ethics experts say Trump’s trades do not violate federal conflict-of-interest laws, as they involve publicly traded companies and do not directly relate to government policy. However, the timing of his purchases and subsequent public praise raises questions about potential market influence. Palantir has not commented on the trades or Trump’s statements.

Moving forward, Trump’s financial disclosures will continue to be monitored for any additional investments in defense or technology firms. Palantir’s performance remains tied to government contracts, with analysts watching for further stock movements tied to national security developments.
