United States and Nigerian military forces eliminated Abu-Bilal al-Minuki, the alleged second-in-command of ISIL (ISIS), in a joint operation on Friday, President [Donald Trump](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Trump) announced. Trump described al-Minuki as the 'most active terrorist in the world' and credited American and Nigerian troops for executing a 'flawless' mission.

**ISIL leader’s deputy killed in Africa**
Abu-Bilal al-Minuki, a [Sahel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahel)-based senior leader of ISIL, was targeted in an operation that Trump said involved 'sources' keeping U.S. forces informed of his movements. The president did not disclose specific locations or methods used in the raid but thanked the Nigerian government for its partnership. Al-Minuki had been under U.S. sanctions since 2023 for his ties to ISIL, when the State Department designated him a 'global terrorist.'

**US sanctions and ISIL’s administrative structure**
The U.S. State Department first labeled al-Minuki in 2023, identifying him as part of ISIL’s General Directorate of Provinces—the group’s administrative arm that oversees operations across multiple regions. At the time, the department described him as a key figure in ISIL’s Sahel operations, which have expanded in recent years amid instability in countries like [Nigeria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria), [Niger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niger), and [Mali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mali). His death comes as ISIL affiliates continue to conduct attacks across Africa’s Sahel region.

**Trump praises cooperation, details remain sparse**
Trump’s announcement on [Truth Social](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Truth_Social) emphasized the precision of the operation but offered no further operational details. The White House has not yet issued a formal statement or held a press briefing on the matter. Nigerian officials have not independently confirmed al-Minuki’s death, though Trump stated the mission was conducted with full coordination between U.S. and Nigerian forces.

Experts note that al-Minuki’s elimination could disrupt ISIL’s administrative and operational capabilities in Africa, where the group has increasingly relied on local leaders to sustain its influence. However, the death of a high-ranking figure rarely leads to the collapse of such networks, and ISIL has repeatedly replaced fallen commanders with little long-term impact on its operations.

The operation follows recent U.S. counterterrorism efforts in Africa, where the military has increased drone strikes and special forces missions targeting ISIL and [al-Qaeda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Qaeda) affiliates. The Sahel remains a hotspot for violent extremism, with ongoing conflicts in Burkina Faso, Niger, and Mali fueling instability and providing fertile ground for militant groups.

The next 48 hours will likely bring additional confirmation from intelligence sources or local reports. Analysts will be watching for any statements from ISIL or its supporters regarding al-Minuki’s death and whether it triggers retaliatory attacks in the region.
