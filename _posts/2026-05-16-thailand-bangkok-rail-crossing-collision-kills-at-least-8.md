A freight train collided with a public bus at a busy Bangkok rail crossing Saturday afternoon, killing at least eight people and injuring 32 others, Thai officials confirmed. The accident occurred at the Asoke-Din Daeng intersection near the [Airport Rail Link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Airport_Rail_Link_(Bangkok))’s Makkasan Station in the Ratchathewi district at about 3 p.m., authorities said. Video footage shared on social media shows the train plowing into the orange bus, which burst into flames within minutes and dragged nearby cars and motorcycles along the tracks.

Deputy Transport Minister [Siripong Angkasakulkiat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Transport_(Thailand)) told reporters the bus had been stopped at a red light, preventing the crossing barriers from closing. The freight train, unable to stop in time, collided with the bus. “All eight dead were on the bus,” Siripong said. He added that the number of passengers on the bus at the time remains unclear. Emergency crews worked for hours to extinguish the fire and clear debris from the tracks.

## Witnesses describe scene of chaos
Witnesses described panic as the train barreled into the bus. Wanthong Kokpho, a motorcycle taxi driver who saw the crash, said the bus was stationary at the red light, blocking traffic. “Cars were also blocked and unable to move forward,” he told local media. Social media videos show the bus engulfed in flames moments after impact, with flames shooting high into the air as firefighters arrived. Authorities have not released the names of the victims pending notification of next of kin.

The crash raised immediate questions about rail crossing safety in Bangkok, where similar accidents have occurred. Thailand’s road safety record is among the worst in Southeast Asia, with thousands of fatalities annually from traffic collisions. The [Ministry of Transport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Transport_(Thailand)) has not indicated whether the crossing was malfunctioning or if human error contributed to the incident. Investigators are reviewing traffic camera footage and interviewing witnesses.

## Emergency response and aftermath
Firefighters and paramedics arrived within minutes of the crash, but the intensity of the fire and structural damage delayed rescue efforts. Three nearby cars and two motorcycles were crushed or severely damaged in the collision. The train’s engineer was unharmed and cooperating with investigators, officials said. The Airport Rail Link service was suspended between Makkasan and Ramkhamhaeng stations for nearly four hours as crews cleared the scene.

Thailand’s prime minister ordered an urgent review of rail crossing safety nationwide following the incident. “We must ensure no such tragedy happens again,” he said in a statement Sunday. The Transport Ministry has pledged to install additional warning lights and barriers at high-risk crossings. Families of the victims are demanding answers about why the bus was allowed to stop on the tracks.

Investigators are expected to release a preliminary report within a week. The crash underscores broader concerns about infrastructure safety in Bangkok, where rapid urbanization has outpaced upgrades to rail and road systems. The government faces pressure to improve traffic enforcement and public transit reliability amid rising accident rates.
