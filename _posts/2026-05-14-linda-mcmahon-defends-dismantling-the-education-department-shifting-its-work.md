## Introduction to the Issue
The [U.S. Department of Education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Education) has been at the center of controversy lately, with [Linda McMahon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linda_McMahon), the Education Secretary, defending the Trump administration's new budget proposal. The proposal has sparked heated debates among lawmakers, with Democrats expressing concerns over the potential dismantling of the Education Department and its impact on students. 
## Key Concerns and Issues
The [House education committee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_House_Committee_on_Education_and_the_Workforce) questioned McMahon on various issues, including the proposed limits on federal student loan borrowing and the oversight of education for students with disabilities. The lawmakers also discussed the role of the government in improving literacy rates among [U.S. students](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Education_in_the_United_States). The Trump administration's budget proposal has been criticized for its potential to exacerbate existing education disparities. 
## Implications and Future Directions
As the debate continues, it remains to be seen how the proposed changes will affect the lives of millions of Americans. The [Education Department](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Education) plays a crucial role in shaping the country's education system, and any changes to its structure or funding could have far-reaching implications. The [Trump administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidency_of_Donald_Trump) must balance its budget priorities with the need to ensure that all students have access to quality education.
