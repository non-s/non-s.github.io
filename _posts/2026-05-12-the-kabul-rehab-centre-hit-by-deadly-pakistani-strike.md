A Pakistani military strike struck a rehabilitation center in Kabul on Monday, killing at least 43 people and injuring 24, according to Afghan officials. The attack targeted the [Kabul Orthopedic and Physiotherapy Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kabul_Orthopedic_and_Physiotherapy_Center), a facility supporting disabled and injured Afghans, many of whom are war victims. Pakistani authorities asserted the strike hit 'military and terrorist infrastructure,' but the United Nations Assistance Mission in Afghanistan [UNAMA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Assistance_Mission_in_Afghanistan) and family members of the victims dismissed the claim as false, stating the building housed only civilians.## Kabul rehab center strike sparks international condemnation
The attack drew immediate condemnation from Afghan officials and international bodies. Taliban spokesperson Zabihullah Mujahid [Zabihullah Mujahid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zabihullah_Mujahid) labeled the strike a 'barbaric act' and demanded an explanation from Pakistan. The UNAMA issued a statement expressing 'deep concern' over the civilian casualties and called for an independent investigation. Survivors and relatives described chaotic scenes as the strike destroyed parts of the center, leaving bodies and injured trapped under rubble.## Pakistan defends strike amid conflicting accounts
Pakistan’s Foreign Ministry reiterated its stance, asserting the strike was a legitimate counterterrorism operation targeting 'high-value terrorist targets' near the Afghanistan-Pakistan border. However, no evidence has been provided to substantiate the claim. The Afghan Taliban, which controls Kabul, denied harboring militants capable of launching attacks on Pakistan. Local residents near the rehab center reported hearing multiple explosions before the strike, suggesting a precision attack.## Victims include disabled Afghans and aid workers
Among the dead were at least 15 disabled patients and five staff members, according to hospital officials. The center, funded by international donors, had been operational since 1992, providing prosthetics and physical therapy to thousands of Afghans. A survivor, who asked not to be named, described the scene as 'hell on earth,' with children and elderly victims among the casualties.## Broader implications of the strike on regional tensions
The attack risks escalating tensions between Afghanistan’s Taliban-led government and Pakistan, which accuses the Taliban of sheltering militants responsible for attacks on its soil. Pakistan has conducted several cross-border strikes in recent months, citing self-defense under international law. The strike on the rehab center follows a pattern of civilian casualties in such operations, raising questions about the precision of Pakistan’s targeting. Diplomats from the United Nations and neighboring countries have urged restraint to prevent further destabilization in the region.

El ataque, perpetrado en la madrugada del pasado martes, sacudió el barrio de Karte Char, donde el centro de rehabilitación de drogas atendía a pacientes de bajos recursos, en su mayoría jóvenes afganos. La respuesta pakistaní, que justificó la operación como un "golpe preventivo" contra células de Tehreek-e-Taliban Pakistan (TTP), ha sido tachada de desproporcionada por analistas, que ven en ella un nuevo capítulo de las tensiones transfronterizas y un riesgo para la ya frágil estabilidad regional. Para los hispanohablantes, el suceso evoca ecos de otros conflictos donde la población civil paga el precio de la geopolítica, recordando que, más allá de las fronteras asiáticas, la violencia asimétrica sigue siendo un fantasma global con consecuencias humanas devastadoras.
