Los Angeles Dodgers left-hander Blake Snell did not take the mound Friday at Angel Stadium after team doctors discovered loose bodies in his left elbow. The two-time Cy Young Award winner was scratched from his scheduled start just before first pitch, a move that sent ripples through the MLB landscape. Snell, [31](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_in_Major_League_Baseball), had allowed four earned runs over 4.2 innings in his previous outing, a 6-5 loss to the Seattle Mariners on April 5. The Dodgers placed Snell on the 15-day injured list immediately following the late scratch, but did not provide a timetable for his return.

## Dodgers face immediate pitching crisis
The sudden absence of Snell, one of the Dodgers' most reliable starters, comes at a critical time. The team is set to face the Los Angeles Angels in a three-game series starting Friday night at Angel Stadium, just miles from where Snell was scratched. The Dodgers will now turn to an untested rotation, relying heavily on [Tony Gonsolin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tony_Gonsolin) and [Emil Ramos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emil_Ramos_(baseball)) to fill the void. Manager Dave Roberts acknowledged the challenge, stating the team would "adapt and overcome" with their current roster.

Medical imaging revealed the loose bodies, which can cause discomfort and affect performance. Loose bodies in a pitcher’s elbow often stem from repetitive stress or prior injury, though the Dodgers have not detailed Snell’s medical history regarding this issue. The team will conduct further evaluations to determine the severity and next steps. Snell, who signed a five-year, $120 million contract with the Dodgers in December 2023, has been a cornerstone of their rotation since joining the team.

## Dodgers' rotation depth tested early
The injury to Snell adds to the Dodgers’ growing list of pitching concerns. The team has already dealt with injuries to relievers [Bryan Hudson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bryan_Hudson_(baseball)) and [Michael Grove](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Grove), who are also on the injured list. The Dodgers’ depth in the starting rotation, once considered one of their strengths, is now being tested just weeks into the season. Rookie [Emil Ramos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emil_Ramos_(baseball)) is expected to step into the rotation, but his experience pales in comparison to Snell’s established track record.

The Dodgers will rely on their bullpen to bridge gaps in games where their starters may not go deep. Key relievers like [Evan Phillips](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evan_Phillips_(baseball)) and [Alex Vesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alex_Vesia) will likely see increased workloads. The team’s early-season performance hinges on how quickly they can adjust to Snell’s absence and whether their pitching staff can maintain consistency. Fans and analysts alike are watching closely to see how the Dodgers manage this unexpected hurdle.

Snell’s injury also raises questions about the Dodgers’ long-term pitching strategy. With [Clayton Kershaw](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clayton_Kershaw) nearing the end of his Hall of Fame career and other veterans aging, the team’s reliance on young arms is becoming more pronounced. The front office may need to explore trade options or call-ups from the minor leagues to bolster the rotation. For now, the Dodgers must focus on getting through the next few weeks without their ace left-hander.
