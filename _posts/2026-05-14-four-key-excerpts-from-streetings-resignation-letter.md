Wes Streeting has resigned as the UK’s health secretary, becoming the most high-profile Labour figure to step down amid growing speculation he was preparing a leadership challenge against Sir Keir Starmer. Streeting confirmed his departure in a resignation letter released on Monday, ending days of intense media scrutiny over his political future.

The resignation comes after repeated reports that Streeting, a prominent centrist within Labour, was considering challenging Starmer for the party leadership. Sources close to Streeting indicated he had grown frustrated with the direction of Labour policy and what he viewed as a lack of bold action on key issues. His decision to quit marks a significant moment in Labour’s internal dynamics, raising questions about the party’s cohesion ahead of the next general election.

## What did Wes Streeting’s resignation letter say?

In his resignation letter, Streeting cited personal reasons for his departure but did not explicitly address the leadership speculation. He wrote that his time in government had been an ‘honour’ but that he needed to take a step back to focus on his family. The letter, excerpted by multiple news outlets, did not contain direct criticism of Starmer or the government. However, political analysts noted that the timing of the resignation—just days after Labour’s poor performance in local elections—suggested deeper discontent within the party.

Streeting’s resignation letter also included a commitment to continue supporting Labour’s policies and campaigning for the party in future elections. He thanked colleagues for their support during his tenure as health secretary, which began after the 2024 general election. His departure leaves a vacancy in a key cabinet position just months before the party is expected to unveil its next major policy agenda.

## How did Labour react to the resignation?

Labour Party officials have not yet publicly commented on Streeting’s resignation, but insiders described the move as a ‘setback’ for Starmer’s leadership. Streeting, a former Treasury economist, has been seen as a potential successor to Starmer, though his public statements have largely aligned with the party’s official line. His resignation adds to a list of departures of high-profile MPs who have left government roles in recent months, fueling speculation about broader dissatisfaction within the party.

The resignation also raises questions about Labour’s strategy on healthcare, a key policy area where Streeting had been expected to play a leading role. As health secretary, he oversaw the implementation of several NHS reforms, including plans to reduce waiting lists and improve workforce retention. His departure leaves a leadership void in a department that has been under intense public scrutiny over performance and funding.

## What happens next for Wes Streeting and Labour?

Streeting’s resignation does not immediately trigger a leadership contest, as he has not formally challenged Starmer. However, his departure could embolden other MPs who are dissatisfied with the current leadership to voice their concerns more openly. Political observers suggest that Streeting may now focus on rebuilding his public profile, possibly by taking on a high-profile role outside government or within Labour’s policy development team.

For Labour, the resignation underscores the challenges Starmer faces in maintaining party unity ahead of the next election. Streeting’s departure follows the resignations of other senior figures, including former shadow chancellor Rachel Reeves, who stepped down earlier this year. The party is expected to address internal divisions in the coming weeks, with Starmer likely to use his upcoming conference speech to reaffirm his vision for Labour’s future.
