Michael, the Michael Jackson biopic, just cleared a major box office milestone. The film has now topped $700 million globally, becoming one of the year's biggest commercial successes. Over the weekend, it reclaimed the top spot at the international box office, pulling in $57.7 million from 83 foreign markets. That keeps it firmly ahead of most other releases this year, proving the enduring appeal of the King of Pop nearly 15 years after his death in 2009. The film's distributor, Universal Pictures International, hasn't released weekend numbers for North America yet, but domestic earnings are expected to push the total past $750 million soon. If it does, it'll join the rarified air of only a handful of biopics to hit that mark, including Bohemian Rhapsody and Rocketman. The movie stars [Jackson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Jackson) impersonator [Jaafar Jackson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jaafar_Jackson) as the late icon and includes a soundtrack packed with his biggest hits. Fans have snapped up tickets in waves, especially in markets like Japan and Brazil, where Jackson's music remains hugely popular. The film's success comes despite mixed reviews from critics, who called it uneven but praised its visuals and music. Moviegoers, however, seem to care more about the nostalgia factor than the acting. It’s no surprise the Jackson estate is thrilled—they’ll likely see a hefty payout from the box office haul, plus future streaming and merchandise deals. Universal has already greenlit a sequel, though nothing’s confirmed yet about who’ll play Jackson next or what era it’ll cover. For now, fans are just happy to see the legacy live on in theaters.

## Devil Wears Prada 2 closes in on half a billion
Meanwhile, Devil Wears Prada 2 is charging toward its own milestone. The sequel has now pulled in nearly $550 million worldwide, with most of that coming from international markets. The film stars [Meryl Streep](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meryl_Streep) and [Anne Hathaway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anne_Hathaway) reprising their roles as Miranda Priestly and Andy Sachs, and it’s riding a wave of nostalgia for the original 2006 hit. Over the weekend, it added $29.3 million overseas, keeping it in the top five globally. The domestic take is smaller at around $135 million, but that’s still strong for a mid-budget sequel in today’s market. Critics have been kinder to this one, calling it a worthy follow-up that balances humor and heart. Fans who grew up with the first film are flocking to theaters, especially younger audiences discovering it for the first time. The fashion industry has also embraced it, with designers like [Anna Wintour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anna_Wintour) making cameos and praising the sequel’s respect for its roots. [20th Century Studios](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/20th_Century_Studios) has been tight-lipped about plans for a third movie, but the numbers suggest the franchise isn’t done yet. If it crosses $600 million, it’ll silence any doubts about sequels being risky business in Hollywood these days.

Both films are proving that star power and nostalgia can still move the needle at the box office, even in a year crowded with sequels and superhero movies. Michael’s success is especially notable because it’s a music biopic, a genre that’s struggled to find its footing lately. The original Devil Wears Prada was a cultural phenomenon, and its sequel is capitalizing on that legacy without feeling like a cash grab. Universal and 20th Century Studios are likely popping champagne over these numbers, but the real winners are the fans who keep these stories alive. For the Jackson estate, it’s a financial win. For the fashion world, it’s a reminder of how one iconic film can shape an industry. And for moviegoers, it’s proof that sometimes the best sequels and biopics are the ones you least expect. The question now is how long both films can keep drawing crowds—and whether Hollywood will take the hint and bet bigger on music biopics and mid-budget sequels moving forward.
