WASHINGTON — The U.S. House of Representatives [House of Representatives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives) votes Friday on a $60 billion aid package for Ukraine, with final approval hinging on bipartisan support amid ongoing negotiations. The package includes military, humanitarian and budgetary assistance as Kyiv faces intensified Russian attacks in the Donbas region. Ukraine’s President [Volodymyr Zelenskyy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volodymyr_Zelenskyy) urged Congress to pass the bill quickly, warning delays could cost lives on the front lines. A previous $95 billion package stalled for months before clearing the Senate in April but remains stalled in the House due to Republican opposition over border security measures. Speaker [Mike Johnson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mike_Johnson_(American_politician)) has not yet scheduled a vote but indicated urgency given the conflict’s escalation. ## European markets fall as economic data weakens European stock indices tumbled Friday after Germany reported weaker-than-expected industrial output and France posted stagnant GDP growth for the first quarter. The Euro Stoxx 50 [Euro Stoxx 50](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euro_Stoxx_50) dropped 2.1%, extending losses from Thursday’s U.S. market decline. Analysts cited rising energy costs and sluggish demand across the Eurozone as key drivers. The European Central Bank [European Central Bank](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Central_Bank) is widely expected to hold interest rates steady next month but faces pressure to ease policy amid recession fears. Germany’s DAX [DAX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DAX) fell 1.8%, France’s CAC 40 [CAC 40](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAC_40) dropped 1.5%, and Italy’s FTSE MIB [FTSE MIB](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FTSE_MIB) slid 2.3%. ## Royal family news: Princess Anne marks public engagement milestone Britain’s Princess Anne [Anne, Princess Royal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anne,_Princess_Royal) attended a public event in Edinburgh Friday, marking her 75th consecutive year of official engagements. The Princess Royal, known for her decades of military and charitable work, met with local leaders and schoolchildren during a visit to the Scottish capital. Her milestone reflects the longest active public service career of any British royal in history. Buckingham Palace confirmed the engagement was part of her ongoing duties, with no new announcements made. ## Ukraine war enters 30th month with intensified fighting The war in Ukraine enters its 30th month Friday, with heavy fighting reported near Chasiv Yar in the Donetsk region. Russian forces have advanced cautiously in recent weeks, capturing small villages as Ukrainian defenders face shortages of artillery and air defense systems. Moscow claims to have destroyed 12 Ukrainian military facilities in overnight strikes, while Kyiv reports repelling several drone and missile attacks. Western intelligence suggests Russia is preparing for a new offensive in the coming weeks, though Moscow has not confirmed plans. ## EU leaders meet Sunday on energy and migration The European Union’s 27 leaders gather in Brussels Sunday to address energy security and migration amid growing political tensions. The meeting follows a report warning of potential blackouts this winter if gas storage levels fall below critical thresholds. Leaders are also expected to debate a new pact on migration and asylum, with Poland and Hungary resisting mandatory quotas. European Commission President [Ursula von der Leyen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ursula_von_der_Leyen) called the summit urgent, citing risks to economic stability and social cohesion. The discussions come as protests over housing shortages spread in Berlin, Paris and other major cities.
