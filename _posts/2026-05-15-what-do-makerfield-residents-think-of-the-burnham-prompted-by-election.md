Andy Burnham’s resignation as MP for Makerfield has set the stage for a by-election, drawing attention to local concerns ahead of the vote. BBC Radio Manchester canvassed residents to understand their priorities, with many highlighting healthcare access and economic pressures as top issues. The by-election, triggered by Burnham’s move to Greater Manchester’s mayoral role, will test Labour’s dominance in a traditionally safe seat.

## Burnham’s resignation reshapes Makerfield’s political map
Burnham’s decision to step down as MP follows his election as Greater Manchester mayor, a role he won with 67% of the vote in May 2024. His resignation, effective immediately, creates an opening for a new representative in Makerfield, a constituency Labour has held since 1997. Political observers note the by-election could serve as a barometer for Labour’s support ahead of the next general election.

Residents interviewed by BBC Radio Manchester expressed a range of opinions. Some praised Burnham’s leadership during the pandemic, while others questioned the timing of his resignation. The by-election will now focus attention on local issues, with many voters citing NHS funding and rising living costs as their biggest concerns.

## Labour faces test in historically safe seat
Makerfield, covering Wigan and parts of Leigh in Greater Manchester, has been a Labour stronghold for decades. The party’s candidate will likely face scrutiny over its ability to address pressing local issues, including hospital waiting times and employment opportunities. Analysts suggest the by-election could reveal shifts in voter sentiment, even in traditionally loyal Labour areas.

The announcement comes amid broader political shifts in the North West. With Burnham’s mayoral tenure expanding, his influence over regional policy—including transport and housing—will grow, but the by-election forces Labour to defend its local base. Campaigning is expected to intensify as parties vie for voter trust in a tight political climate.

## Voters prioritize NHS and cost of living
During BBC Radio Manchester’s survey, residents emphasized healthcare and economic stability as their top priorities. One Wigan resident, a mother of two, cited long waits for GP appointments as a major frustration. Another voter, a small business owner, highlighted the impact of inflation on household budgets. These concerns reflect wider national trends, with polls showing economic issues dominating voter priorities.

The by-election also raises questions about Burnham’s political future. His mayoral role gives him significant platform, but his resignation from Westminster could prompt speculation about his next steps. For now, the focus remains on Makerfield, where candidates will campaign on promises to tackle local challenges.

With the by-election date yet to be confirmed, parties are already mobilizing resources. Labour, Conservatives, and smaller parties are expected to field candidates, setting up a competitive race. The outcome could signal broader trends ahead of the next general election, particularly in the North West.
