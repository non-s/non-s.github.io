IDEO, the Silicon Valley design powerhouse that pioneered human-centered design, is confronting an existential challenge. For decades, the firm’s signature approach—putting user needs first—reshaped industries, creating iconic products like Procter & Gamble’s [standing toothpaste tubes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Procter_%26_Gamble), the [Palm V PDA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palm_OS), and Bank of America’s [Keep the Change program](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bank_of_America). But today, the company’s future hangs in the balance as clients slash budgets and increasingly turn to artificial intelligence for faster, cheaper alternatives.

The crisis stems from a shift in corporate priorities. As companies like [Ford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company) hired IDEO to redesign its electric vehicle factories, others began questioning the value of external design consultants. Many now bring design work in-house, mimicking IDEO’s methods without the hefty fees. Others demand immediate returns on investment, pressuring internal teams to deliver measurable results quickly.

## AI tools challenge IDEO’s traditional model

The rise of AI-driven design tools has intensified the pressure. Startups now offer software that claims to generate user-friendly products in hours, not weeks or months. These tools analyze customer data and churn out dozens of design iterations based on trends, often at a fraction of IDEO’s cost. For businesses racing to launch new products, AI presents a compelling alternative to human-centered design’s slower, research-heavy process.

IDEO’s leadership acknowledges the threat. In recent interviews, executives admit the firm must adapt or risk irrelevance. Some clients still seek IDEO’s expertise for complex problems where human insight and creativity outweigh algorithmic speed. But the company’s once-unassailable position as the go-to design authority is eroding.

## In-house design teams mimic IDEO’s methods

The trend of in-sourcing design work began years ago but has accelerated. Companies like [Apple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc.) and [Google](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google) now employ large internal design teams trained in human-centered principles. These teams apply IDEO’s methodologies—user research, prototyping, iterative testing—without the premium price tag. For IDEO, this means losing high-profile contracts to cheaper, internal alternatives.

Executives at IDEO argue that human-centered design remains superior for nuanced challenges. The firm’s approach involves deep empathy with users, a process difficult for AI to replicate. But as AI tools improve and companies prioritize speed and cost savings, IDEO must prove its method still justifies its expense.

## The road ahead for IDEO

IDEO is not surrendering without a fight. The company has invested in its own AI tools to streamline parts of the design process, hoping to combine efficiency with its signature human touch. It has also expanded into new areas, such as redesigning healthcare systems and corporate workplaces, where AI’s limitations become more apparent.

Yet the core question remains: Can human-centered design survive in an era dominated by speed and automation? IDEO’s answer may determine whether the firm thrives for another 30 years—or fades into the background like so many design trends before it. For now, the company’s legacy as an innovator is being tested like never before.

A gigante do design IDEO, conhecida por revolucionar a forma como empresas criam produtos e serviços com foco no ser humano, enfrenta agora um de seus maiores desafios: a redução de custos das corporações e a ascensão da inteligência artificial estão pondo em xeque seu modelo de negócios. Fundada há mais de quatro décadas, a consultoria californiana viu sua relevância diminuir à medida que clientes, pressionados pela inflação e pela busca por eficiência, optam por internalizar equipes de design ou apostar em soluções automatizadas.

No Brasil, onde a inovação ainda depende fortemente de metodologias ágeis e centradas no usuário, a crise da IDEO serve como um alerta sobre os riscos de se subestimar a concorrência das ferramentas digitais. Setores como o de tecnologia e varejo, que tradicionalmente buscavam a consultoria para repensar experiências de consumo, agora migram para soluções low-cost, como softwares de IA que prometem resultados rápidos e baratos. Ainda assim, especialistas brasileiros destacam que a abordagem humanizada da IDEO — baseada em empatia e pesquisa qualitativa — dificilmente será substituída por completo, embora precise se adaptar a um novo cenário.

O futuro da IDEO pode depender de sua capacidade de integrar IA ao seu processo criativo sem perder a essência que a consagrou, enquanto empresas brasileiras aprendem a equilibrar custo e inovação em um mercado cada vez mais competitivo.
