Israel targeted and killed Izz al-Din al-Haddad, the head of [Hamas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamas)’s armed wing in Gaza, in an airstrike Friday evening that also killed six others, including his wife and daughter. Hamas confirmed the death in a statement Saturday, calling the attack a “treacherous and cowardly assassination.” The strike hit the Remal neighborhood west of [Gaza City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_City), according to medical sources and local reports.

Israeli Prime Minister [Benjamin Netanyahu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_Netanyahu) and Defense Minister [Israel Katz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel_Katz) announced Friday that Israeli forces had killed al-Haddad, describing him as “one of the architects” of the October 7, 2023, attacks on southern Israel. Hamas rejected the claim, stating al-Haddad was killed alongside civilians, including three women and a baby, in the residential area.

## Civilian casualties amid ongoing conflict

Medical sources in Gaza reported that three Palestinians were killed in a strike on a civilian vehicle, while four others died when a nearby building was hit. Dozens more were wounded in the attack, according to Al Jazeera’s local correspondent. The Israeli military has not immediately commented on the civilian casualties cited by Palestinian sources.

The strike occurred as ceasefire negotiations between Israel and Hamas remain stalled, with sporadic violence continuing in Gaza. Hamas vowed to respond to the killing, raising concerns about further escalation in the already fragile security situation. The group has accused Israel of deliberately targeting civilians, a claim Israel has not addressed in its public statements.

## Broader implications of al-Haddad’s death

Al-Haddad, a senior military figure in Hamas, played a key role in the group’s operations in Gaza and was a prominent target for Israeli forces since October 7. His death marks the latest high-profile killing in a campaign that has targeted multiple Hamas commanders and leaders over the past six months. The attack follows a pattern of Israeli strikes in densely populated areas, drawing international criticism over civilian casualties.

The Israeli government has framed al-Haddad’s killing as a strategic blow to Hamas’s military capabilities, particularly his alleged involvement in planning attacks against Israel. However, the deaths of civilians, including women and children, have intensified calls for accountability and raised questions about the proportionality of the strikes. The incident is likely to further strain already tense relations between Israel, Hamas, and international mediators seeking a lasting ceasefire.
