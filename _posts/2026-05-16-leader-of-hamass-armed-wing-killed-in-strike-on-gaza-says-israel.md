The Israeli military announced Saturday it had killed Ezzedine Al-Haddad, the commander of Hamas’s [Qassam Brigades](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qassam_Brigades), the group’s armed wing, in an airstrike conducted Friday in Gaza. The strike targeted a building in the southern Gaza Strip, according to military statements. Israeli officials said Al-Haddad was directly responsible for planning and executing attacks against Israel, including recent rocket fire from Gaza into Israeli territory.

## Hamas reports casualties
Hamas officials, speaking to French news outlet France 24, confirmed Al-Haddad’s death but said his wife and daughter were also killed in the strike. The militant group called the attack a "heinous crime" and vowed retaliation. The Israeli military has not yet commented on the reported civilian casualties. The strike comes amid a surge in violence between Israel and Hamas in recent weeks, with frequent exchanges of fire across the Gaza-Israel border.

The death of Al-Haddad, a senior figure in Hamas’s military hierarchy, is expected to escalate tensions in the already volatile region. Al-Haddad, also known as Abu Khaled, had been a key target for Israeli forces for years due to his role in orchestrating attacks. His elimination follows the killing of other high-ranking Hamas commanders in past airstrikes, including [Ahmed Jabari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmed_Jabari) in 2012.

## Regional reactions and context
Palestinian factions in Gaza condemned the strike, calling it an "assassination" and a violation of ceasefire agreements. The [Palestinian Islamic Jihad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestinian_Islamic_Jihad), another militant group active in Gaza, issued a statement warning of "severe consequences" for Israel’s actions. The [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations) and other international bodies have repeatedly called for restraint to avoid further civilian casualties.

Israel has justified its military operations in Gaza as necessary to counter rocket attacks and other threats posed by Hamas and allied groups. The Israeli military stated that its strikes target only militant leaders and infrastructure, denying accusations of indiscriminate attacks. However, Gaza health officials frequently report civilian deaths in such operations, complicating efforts to de-escalate the conflict.

## Broader implications and next steps
The killing of Al-Haddad could lead to a significant shift in Hamas’s military leadership, potentially sparking a power struggle within the group. Analysts suggest the move might provoke a sharp response from Hamas, including increased rocket fire or other retaliatory measures. The international community continues to urge both sides to avoid actions that could worsen the humanitarian crisis in Gaza, where living conditions have deteriorated due to years of blockade and conflict.

The latest strike underscores the ongoing cycle of violence that has defined the Israel-Hamas conflict for decades. With no immediate signs of de-escalation, the region remains on edge as both sides brace for further retaliation or counterstrikes.
