Israeli airstrikes struck a densely populated area of Gaza City late Friday, killing at least seven Palestinians and destroying a residential apartment building, local officials said. A second strike hit a civilian vehicle nearby, adding to the toll. Israel’s military stated the operation targeted [Yahya Sinwar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yahya_Sinwar), the head of Hamas’s armed wing in Gaza, though Al Jazeera has not confirmed this independently.

The strikes occurred amid ongoing tensions between Israel and Hamas, which has controlled Gaza since 2007. Palestinian health officials reported the dead included civilians, though exact numbers and identities remained unconfirmed Saturday. Israel’s military said it conducted the strikes in response to recent rocket fire from Gaza into southern Israel. No Israeli casualties were reported in the strikes or subsequent clashes.

## Escalation in Gaza amid unconfirmed Hamas leadership target

Gaza City’s Al-Remal neighborhood, where the strikes hit, is a frequent flashpoint during escalations between Israel and Palestinian factions. Residents described scenes of chaos as emergency crews searched for survivors under rubble. The Israeli military warned nearby residents via phone messages and leaflets ahead of the strikes, a common practice in Gaza operations to reduce civilian harm.

Hamas did not immediately comment on Israel’s claim regarding Sinwar’s targeting. The group has previously denied Israeli reports of leadership casualties during similar operations. The Gaza Health Ministry, run by Hamas, reported at least 12 injuries in addition to the fatalities, with rescue efforts continuing as of early Saturday.

## International reactions to the Gaza airstrikes

The strikes drew immediate condemnation from Palestinian factions and regional observers. The [Palestinian Authority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestinian_Authority) in the West Bank called the operation a violation of international law, while Qatar and Egypt, key mediators in past ceasefire talks, urged restraint. The United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA) expressed concern over the rising civilian toll in Gaza, which has seen repeated violence since October 2023.

Israel’s military spokesperson stated the operation was precise and aimed to prevent future attacks. However, human rights groups have criticized the high civilian casualties in similar past operations. The Gaza Strip remains under a blockade imposed by Israel and Egypt since 2007, restricting the movement of goods and people.

What happens next depends on Hamas’s response and whether further escalation occurs. Past cycles of violence in Gaza have often lasted days or weeks before temporary ceasefires are brokered through Egyptian or Qatari mediation. The current situation remains fluid as both sides assess the aftermath of the strikes.
