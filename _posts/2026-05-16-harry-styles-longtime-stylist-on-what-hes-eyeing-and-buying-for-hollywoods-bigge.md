Harry Lambert isn’t just the man behind Harry Styles’ [iconic looks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Styles) for the past decade. He’s the stylist Hollywood’s biggest names trust to curate their wardrobes—whether it’s for red carpets or everyday life. Lambert’s approach blends vintage charm with modern edge, and he’s happy to share how he does it. His secret? A sharp eye, a clear plan, and a willingness to dig for hidden gems on eBay. Lambert spoke to *The Hollywood Reporter* about his favorite fashion moments, the non-negotiables in his own closet, and the tricks he uses to shop smarter—not harder.

## How Lambert Shops for the Stars (and Himself)

Lambert’s process starts with listening. Before buying anything for a client or himself, he asks one question: *Does this feel right?* For Styles, that’s meant everything from a 1970s Gucci suit to a sheer black blouse from a small designer. Lambert doesn’t chase trends; he chases authenticity. “Harry’s always been about expressing himself, not following rules,” Lambert says. That philosophy extends to his other clients, who range from musicians to actors. Lambert’s goal isn’t to make everyone look the same—it’s to make them look like the best version of themselves.

## The eBay Tricks Lambert Swears By

eBay isn’t just a last-resort for Lambert—it’s a treasure trove. He’s bought vintage Levi’s jackets, rare designer sneakers, and even entire wardrobes for clients on the platform. His tip? Search for specific lot numbers or seller ratings above 98%. “People underestimate how much quality you can find on eBay if you know where to look,” he says. Lambert also avoids mass-produced items, focusing instead on one-of-a-kind pieces that tell a story. For him, fashion isn’t just about what’s new—it’s about what’s *meaningful*.

## Closet Non-Negotiables: What Lambert Never Buys Secondhand

Not everything in Lambert’s closet is vintage. He draws a hard line at underwear, socks, and anything that touches the skin. “There’s a reason those items are marked ‘new only’ in my book,” he laughs. But everything else? Fair game. He loves the hunt for a perfect vintage shirt or a pair of boots that tell a story. Lambert’s own closet is a mix of thrifted finds and modern staples, all chosen for their fit and function. He rarely spends more than $200 on a single item, proving you don’t need deep pockets to dress well.

## The Fashion Moments That Stuck With Him

Some outfits leave a mark. For Lambert, it’s Styles’ 2020 *Vogue* cover—a sheer blouse paired with wide-leg trousers, styled with a pearl necklace. It wasn’t just about the clothes; it was about breaking barriers. Lambert also points to Styles’ 2019 Grammy Awards look, where he wore a Gucci suit with a feather boa. “Those moments weren’t just about fashion,” Lambert says. “They were about freedom.” He applies that same mindset to his other clients, pushing them to take risks without sacrificing their personal style.

## What’s Next for Lambert—and His Clients

Lambert isn’t slowing down. He’s currently working on a capsule collection with a small British brand, blending his signature vintage aesthetic with modern cuts. He’s also helping a major pop star revamp their image ahead of a world tour. But no matter the project, Lambert’s focus stays the same: authenticity over everything. “Fashion should make you feel powerful,” he says. “Not like you’re trying to be someone else.”
