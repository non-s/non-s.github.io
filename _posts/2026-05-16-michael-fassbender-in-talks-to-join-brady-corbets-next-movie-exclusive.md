Michael Fassbender is in advanced negotiations to join Brady Corbet’s next feature film, 'The Origin of the World,' Variety has learned exclusively. The project, shrouded in secrecy, marks Corbet’s follow-up to his 2024 historical drama 'The Brutalist,' which garnered 10 Academy Award nominations, including Best Actor for Adrien Brody, Best Cinematography, and Best Original Score. Sources close to the production confirmed Fassbender’s involvement is pending final contract details, though talks are nearing completion.

The untitled film—working title 'The Origin of the World'—draws its name from Gustave Courbet’s 1866 painting of the same title, a provocative work that sparked controversy for its explicit depiction of female genitalia. The painting, now housed in the Musée d’Orsay in Paris, has long been a subject of artistic and cultural debate, making it a fitting title for a mystery-driven narrative. Corbet, known for his meticulous and often provocative filmmaking style, has not publicly confirmed the project’s plot or cast, but industry insiders suggest Fassbender’s character may play a central role in unraveling a layered, enigmatic story.

'The Brutalist,' Corbet’s previous film, received critical acclaim for its sweeping narrative set against the backdrop of post-WWII architecture and the rise of brutalist design. The movie starred Brody as a Hungarian Jewish immigrant navigating the art world in 1950s New York, a role that earned him widespread praise. The film’s success at the Oscars—despite not winning any awards—cemented Corbet’s reputation as a director with a distinct, ambitious vision. His next project, 'The Origin of the World,' is expected to continue this trend, blending mystery with historical or artistic themes, though details remain scarce.

Fassbender, known for his versatility across genres, could bring depth to Corbet’s cryptic narrative. The British-German actor has previously collaborated with directors like Danny Boyle and Ridley Scott, earning nominations for his roles in '12 Years a Slave,' 'Steve Jobs,' and 'The Mauritanian.' His involvement in 'The Origin of the World' would add star power to a project that is still in its early stages, with filming expected to begin later this year. Insiders indicate that the script is in its final stages, with a tight-knit cast likely to round out Fassbender’s role.

The film’s title has already sparked curiosity due to its direct reference to Courbet’s painting, which was originally commissioned by Ottoman diplomat Khalil-Bey and later became a symbol of artistic rebellion. The painting’s history—marked by censorship and scandal—parallels Corbet’s own tendency to explore taboo subjects in his work. Whether the film will directly reference the painting or simply adopt its provocative tone remains unclear, but the title alone suggests a narrative steeped in controversy or hidden truths.

As negotiations with Fassbender wrap up, the project is expected to enter pre-production soon. Corbet, who also serves as a writer and editor, is known for his hands-on approach to filmmaking, often immersing himself in every aspect of a film’s development. Given his track record, 'The Origin of the World' could emerge as a visually striking and thematically rich addition to his filmography. Industry observers will closely watch how Fassbender’s casting shapes the project’s trajectory, especially as it follows the high-profile success of 'The Brutalist'. With Corbet’s reputation for crafting layered narratives, the film is poised to attract both critics and audiences eager for his next move.
