OpenAI has named co-founder [Greg Brockman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greg_Brockman) as its new head of product strategy, the company announced in an internal memo obtained by TechCrunch. The move comes as OpenAI prepares to combine its popular ChatGPT conversational AI with its Codex programming tool. The reorganization follows a series of leadership changes, including the ouster of CEO [Sam Altman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Altman) and subsequent reinstatement in November 2023.

Brockman, who previously served as OpenAI’s president, will now oversee the integration of ChatGPT and Codex, two of the company’s most prominent products. The merger aims to streamline OpenAI’s offerings, creating a more unified platform for both general and technical use cases. Industry analysts view the move as part of a broader effort to stabilize the company after months of turmoil.

The internal memo, sent to employees Tuesday, outlined Brockman’s expanded role and the product consolidation plan. It also emphasized a renewed focus on safety and reliability across OpenAI’s platforms. Brockman will report directly to the company’s board, which has undergone significant turnover in recent months.

## OpenAI’s product shift follows leadership shakeups

The reorganization follows a tumultuous period for [OpenAI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenAI), including the abrupt firing of Sam Altman in November 2023. Altman was reinstated just five days later after employee backlash and investor pressure, but the episode left the company in disarray. Brockman, who previously stepped down from his role as president, has now taken on a more central position in shaping the company’s future.

ChatGPT, launched in November 2022, became a global sensation for its advanced conversational AI capabilities. Codex, meanwhile, was designed primarily for code generation and programming assistance. Combining the two could create a more versatile tool for developers and general users alike. The merger reflects a growing trend in AI development, where companies seek to integrate multiple functionalities into single platforms.

## Industry reacts to OpenAI’s strategic changes

Tech analysts say the merger could help OpenAI compete more effectively with rivals like Google and Microsoft, both of which have heavily invested in AI tools. However, the move also raises questions about execution and user adoption. Some developers who rely on Codex for coding assistance may resist the transition, preferring to keep the tools separate.

The company’s board, now led by [Emmett Shear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmett_Shear) as interim CEO, has signaled its support for Brockman’s strategy. The board has also pledged to prioritize transparency and community engagement as OpenAI rebuilds trust. Brockman’s appointment is seen as a step toward restoring stability and clarity in the company’s direction.

OpenAI plans to roll out the merged product in phases, with a public beta expected later this year. The company will also host community events to gather feedback and refine the integration. Brockman will hold a town hall for employees Friday to address questions about the changes.

What happens next will depend on OpenAI’s ability to execute the merger smoothly while maintaining user trust. The company’s next moves could reshape the AI industry, particularly if competitors follow suit with similar integrations.
