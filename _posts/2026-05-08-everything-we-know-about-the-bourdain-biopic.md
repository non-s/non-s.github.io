The Bourdain biopic is advancing with [Scarlett Johansson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scarlett_Johansson) in early talks to play Ottavia Busia-Bourdain, the former spouse of the late chef [Anthony Bourdain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthony_Bourdain). Sources confirm Johansson’s involvement would mark her first major role in a food-focused film. The project, still in early development, aims to adapt Bourdain’s life and career, including his groundbreaking work in global cuisine and television.

Director [Morgan Neville](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morgan_Neville) has been attached to the project for months, known for his documentary work including *20 Feet from Stardom* and *The Neville Brothers*. Neville’s approach to the biopic is expected to blend archival footage with dramatized scenes, staying true to Bourdain’s raw and unfiltered public persona. Production has not yet announced a release date, but filming is anticipated to begin in late 2024.

## Rodney Scott Closes Two South Carolina Restaurants

Celebrity chef [Rodney Scott](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rodney_Scott_(chef)) has announced the closure of two of his Charleston-area restaurants, Rodney Scott’s Whole Hog BBQ and Rodney Scott’s BBQ in Mount Pleasant. Scott cited rising operational costs and the need to focus on his expanding business ventures as reasons for the closures. Both locations will shut their doors on June 15, 2024.

Scott, a James Beard Award winner, remains committed to his food truck and catering operations. He recently opened a new location in Birmingham, Alabama, and continues to expand his brand through merchandise and pop-up events. The closures mark a shift in his business strategy amid economic pressures facing the restaurant industry.

## Delta Cuts In-Flight Snack Options

[Delta Air Lines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines) has quietly removed several snack options from its in-flight menu, including some pre-packaged items and premium selections. The changes, which took effect this month, are part of the airline’s ongoing cost-cutting measures to improve profitability. A Delta spokesperson confirmed the move but declined to specify which snacks were discontinued.

Passengers flying in certain cabins may notice fewer choices, particularly on domestic routes. The airline has emphasized its commitment to maintaining quality while adjusting to inflation and supply chain challenges. Frequent flyers have expressed mixed reactions, with some praising the streamlined selection and others missing favorite options.

## Chef Cooks with Hinge Dates in Viral Video

A chef in [Los Angeles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles) has gone viral for a social media video documenting a series of cooking dates with people they met on [Hinge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hinge_(app)). The video, posted on TikTok and Instagram, shows the chef preparing simple meals while assessing compatibility with potential partners.

The clip has amassed over 2 million views, sparking discussions about modern dating and culinary skills. Commentators have praised the chef’s creativity and humor, while others question the premise of evaluating relationships through cooking. The trend highlights the intersection of food and social media, a recurring theme in viral content.

The broader implications of this biopic extend beyond entertainment, as Bourdain’s legacy continues to influence food media and travel storytelling. For Scott’s restaurant closures, the move underscores the financial strain on independent eateries. Delta’s snack cuts reflect a wider trend in airline cost management, while the viral cooking date video illustrates the evolving role of food in digital culture.
