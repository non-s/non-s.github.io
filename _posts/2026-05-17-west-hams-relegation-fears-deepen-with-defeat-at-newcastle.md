West Ham’s stay in the Premier League is hanging by a thread after a damaging 3-1 defeat at [Newcastle United](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newcastle_United_Football_Club) on Sunday. The loss means [Tottenham Hotspur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_Football_Club) only need a draw from their final two games to mathematically seal West Ham’s relegation. The Hammers now sit 12th but are just two points above the drop zone with a 13-goal difference in Tottenham’s favor. A win for Spurs at [Chelsea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chelsea_Football_Club) on Tuesday—live on Sky Sports—would all but confirm West Ham’s fate unless they can pull off an unlikely turnaround themselves. The London side had a golden chance to pile pressure on Spurs at St James’ Park but instead found themselves staring down the barrel of an early exit. Newcastle struck twice inside the first 18 minutes, both goals capitalizing on West Ham’s sloppy defending. The first came from an unforced error, with [Mads Hermansen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mads_Hermansen)’s poor back-pass allowing [Harvey Barnes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvey_Barnes) to set up an unmarked [Nick Woltemade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_Woltemade) for the opener. The second was a swift counter, with [Jacob Ramsey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacob_Ramsey) threading a pass to [Will Osula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Will_Osula), who finished coolly. West Ham’s issues didn’t end there. From their own throw-in, they lost possession again, and a quick exchange between Osula and [Joe Willock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joe_Willock) led to the third—again, no challenge from the Hammers’ backline. ## West Ham’s late fightback falls short The game looked over until [Taty Castellanos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taty_Castellanos) was thrown on at halftime and almost made an immediate impact. His first touch forced [Nick Pope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_Pope) into a sharp save, and he nearly equalized when [Callum Wilson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Callum_Wilson) couldn’t capitalize on a chance at the back post. But the real moment of hope came in the 68th minute when Castellanos latched onto a misplaced pass from [Sven Botman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sven_Botman) and curled a stunning 25-yard strike past Pope. It was a beauty, but it wasn’t nearly enough. ## What happens next? West Ham’s path to survival now depends on two results going their way. First, they need to beat [Leeds United](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leeds_United_Football_Club) at the London Stadium next weekend. Then, they’ll need [Everton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Everton_Football_Club) to upset Spurs when they play at [Tottenham Hotspur Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_Stadium) the following Sunday. Even if Spurs lose at Chelsea on Tuesday, West Ham can’t afford to drop another point. The pressure is now squarely on manager [Nuno Espírito Santo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuno_Esp%C3%ADrito_Santo), who changed his system at halftime but couldn’t prevent another collapse. With just two games left, every mistake is magnified, and the margin for error is razor-thin. The Premier League’s bottom three will likely be decided on goal difference, meaning West Ham’s goal difference of -17 puts them in a precarious spot. If they fail to secure at least a point against Leeds, and Spurs claim even a draw at Stamford Bridge, the Hammers are almost certainly going down. The final day of the season promises fireworks, but West Ham’s hopes are fading fast.
