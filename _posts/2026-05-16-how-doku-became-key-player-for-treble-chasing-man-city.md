Manchester City winger Jeremy Doku will likely feature prominently when the club face Chelsea in the FA Cup final at Wembley on Saturday. The 21-year-old Belgian international has quickly become a key part of Pep Guardiola’s squad since joining from Rennes for £55m last summer.

Doku’s emergence comes as City aim for an unprecedented quadruple, having already secured the Premier League and Champions League this season. The FA Cup final against Chelsea represents another opportunity to complete a historic treble. His electric pace and creativity down the left flank have added a new dimension to City’s attack, complementing established stars like Erling Haaland and Kevin De Bruyne.

The winger’s rise under Guardiola has been swift. Initially used as a squad option, Doku has forced his way into the starting lineup with consistent performances, including crucial goals in big games. His direct dribbling and ability to stretch defenses have made him a constant threat. Guardiola has repeatedly highlighted Doku’s work ethic and tactical awareness as reasons for his rapid integration.

## Doku’s path to Manchester City
Doku joined Manchester City in August 2023 after impressing in Ligue 1 with Rennes. His £55m transfer fee made him one of the most expensive Belgian players in history. The move followed a standout Euro 2020 campaign where he announced himself on the international stage.

At Rennes, Doku scored 15 goals and provided 11 assists in just over two seasons, showcasing his potential as a dynamic forward. His performances caught the attention of Europe’s elite clubs, with Manchester City ultimately winning the race for his signature. Guardiola described him as a “complete” winger who could thrive in multiple attacking roles.

## Impact on Manchester City’s season
Since arriving at the Etihad Stadium, Doku has made 38 appearances, scoring 10 goals and providing 8 assists. His contributions have been particularly notable in high-pressure matches, including the Champions League knockout stages. The winger has also formed a strong partnership with Phil Foden on the left side of City’s attack, offering a different profile to traditional wingers.

City’s treble ambitions hinge on their ability to rotate effectively, and Doku has become a trusted option in Guardiola’s plans. His inclusion in the FA Cup final squad underscores his importance to the team’s success. The final against Chelsea will be another test of his composure under pressure.

## What’s next for Doku and Man City
With the FA Cup final looming, Doku faces his biggest challenge yet in England. A win would cement his legacy as a key player in one of the most successful seasons in City’s history. Beyond the final, his development under Guardiola will be closely watched, with speculation linking him to potential summer transfers for top European clubs.

For now, Doku’s focus remains on delivering in the remaining fixtures. His rise from a promising talent to a treble-chasing star highlights both his individual growth and Manchester City’s ability to integrate new players seamlessly.
