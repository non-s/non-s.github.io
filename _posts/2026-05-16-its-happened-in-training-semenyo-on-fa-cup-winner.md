Antoine Semenyo [capitalized on a second-half rebound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_FA_Cup_Final) to fire Manchester City ahead of Chelsea in the FA Cup final at Wembley on Saturday. The 24-year-old striker, who has struggled for minutes under manager Pep Guardiola, converted a loose ball after Kai Havertz’s initial shot was parried by Chelsea goalkeeper [Robert Sánchez](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_S%C3%A1nchez) to seal a 2-1 victory. The win completed a domestic double for City, who had already secured the Premier League title earlier this month.

Semenyo, who joined City from [Bournemouth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A.F.C._Bournemouth) in January, called the moment a chance to 'shine' and compete for top trophies. 'I’ve always wanted to win things, and today was my moment,' he told BBC Sport. 'I knew I could do it in training, so I just had to take my chance.' His goal marked his first major silverware with the club, ending a frustrating season of limited appearances. The striker, who has made just 12 Premier League starts this term, admitted his confidence grew from consistent training performances.

## Manchester City’s dominance continues

City’s victory at Wembley extended their dominance in English football under Guardiola, securing their fourth FA Cup in six years. The manager, who has now won 13 major trophies since 2022, praised his squad’s resilience despite a disjointed season. 'We controlled the game and deserved the win,' Guardiola said. 'The players showed character when it mattered most.' The victory also marked City’s second consecutive domestic double, following their Premier League triumph earlier this month.

Chelsea, who had beaten City in the Carabao Cup semifinal, struggled to contain City’s attacking threats. [Erling Haaland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erling_Haaland) and [Kevin De Bruyne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kevin_De_Bruyne) dominated midfield, while [Rodri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rodri) controlled the tempo. Chelsea’s lone goal came from [Cole Palmer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cole_Palmer), whose deflected strike gave Sánchez a momentary scare. However, City’s depth and quality proved decisive in the final stages.

## Semenyo’s redemption arc

Semenyo’s goal capped a season of redemption after a slow start to his City career. The Ghanaian international had struggled to break into Guardiola’s starting lineup, often finding himself an unused substitute. His only league goal prior to the final came in December against [Crystal Palace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C.), and his confidence appeared fragile. Yet, his FA Cup final strike underscored his belief in his ability, even amid limited game time.

The striker’s celebration with his teammates highlighted his emotional investment in the victory. 'This is for the fans, for the club, and for myself,' he said. 'I’ve worked hard, and today was my reward.' His performance may now force Guardiola to reconsider his squad depth, particularly with Champions League football returning next season.

## What’s next for Manchester City?

City’s focus now shifts to the Champions League, where they will aim to end their 44-year wait for the trophy. They face a daunting knockout-stage draw following their third-place finish in the group stage. Meanwhile, Chelsea will regroup after their second major final defeat in two months, with manager [Mauricio Pochettino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauricio_Pochettino) under pressure to deliver silverware next season.

For Semenyo, the FA Cup winner could be the catalyst for a breakthrough season. If he continues to deliver in key moments, Guardiola may hand him more opportunities in 2024-25.
