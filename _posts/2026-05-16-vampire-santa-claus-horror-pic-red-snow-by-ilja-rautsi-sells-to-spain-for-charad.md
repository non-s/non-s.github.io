Spain’s [Selecta Vision](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selecta_Vision) has snapped up the Spanish rights to “Red Snow,” the groundbreaking horror comedy that reimagines Christmas with a fanged Santa Claus. The deal, announced after the film’s debut at Cannes’ Next from the Nordics showcase, marks a bold entry into the holiday horror genre.

Filmmaker [Ilja Rautsi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilja_Rautsi), known for his gritty Finnish horror films, directs “Red Snow.” The movie blends dark humor with holiday themes, introducing audiences to a Santa Claus who survives by drinking blood rather than milk and cookies. The film’s premise has sparked curiosity and debate about the future of Christmas-themed horror.

“Red Snow” is the first film to feature a vampire Santa Claus, a concept that merges festive iconography with classic horror tropes. The story follows Santa as he battles modern holiday pressures while fending off vampire hunters during a deadly Christmas Eve. Rautsi described the idea as a dark twist on holiday nostalgia, aiming to challenge traditional Christmas narratives.

The acquisition by Selecta Vision comes as Spain’s appetite for international horror grows. The distributor, known for licensing edgy genre films, plans to release “Red Snow” in Spanish theaters ahead of Christmas. Industry insiders suggest the film could appeal to horror fans tired of conventional holiday movies.

At Cannes, “Red Snow” drew attention for its unconventional concept and Rautsi’s direction. The Next from the Nordics showcase highlighted Nordic films with global potential, and “Red Snow” stood out for its unique blend of horror and holiday themes. Critics at the festival praised the film’s originality but noted its potential divisiveness among traditional holiday audiences.

For Rautsi, “Red Snow” represents another step in his career, following earlier works like “The Girl and the Pictures.” The director has carved a niche in Finnish horror, earning a reputation for blending social commentary with genre storytelling. With the Spanish deal secured, Rautsi’s vampire Santa may soon terrorize European screens.

What’s next for “Red Snow” remains unclear, but the film’s international appeal could lead to wider distribution deals. For horror fans and holiday enthusiasts alike, the movie offers a fresh take on two beloved yet contrasting themes. Whether it becomes a cult classic or sparks controversy, one thing is certain: Christmas will never look the same.

A obra, que estreou em Cannes, já desperta curiosidade no mercado europeu e promete ser um dos lançamentos mais inusitados do ano.

A trama, que mistura terror e humor negro durante o Natal, chega em um momento em que o cinema europeu busca por produções originais e de baixo custo, capazes de conquistar audiências internacionais sem depender de grandes orçamentos. Para o público brasileiro, acostumado a comédias natalinas mais leves, a perspectiva de um Santa Claus sugador de sangue pode soar inovadora, mas reflete uma tendência crescente de subverter tradições em busca de novas narrativas. Além disso, o sucesso da venda para a Selecta Vision pode abrir portas para mais coproduções entre a Europa e a América Latina, especialmente em gêneros que fogem do óbvio.
