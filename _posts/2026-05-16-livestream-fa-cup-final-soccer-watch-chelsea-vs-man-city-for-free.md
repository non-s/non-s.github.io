England’s top two clubs meet in Saturday’s FA Cup Final, the oldest competition in world football. [Chelsea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chelsea_F.C.) and [Manchester City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C.) last met in this match in 2011, when Chelsea won 1–0. This year’s final is the 143rd edition and could deliver a classic under London’s late-spring skies at [Wembley Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wembley_Stadium), the usual home for the showpiece game since 2007. Kickoff is set for 5:00 p.m. ET across the U.S., giving American fans the perfect Saturday afternoon slot to tune in without heavy traffic between work and play. Fans in the UK get a 10:00 p.m. BST start, just before bedtime for a drama-filled nightcap.

For viewers cutting the cord, the good news is you don’t need Sky Sports or BT Sport to watch live. The FA Cup’s broadcasting deal with [BBC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC) and [ITV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)) in the UK means the full match will air on those channels. In the U.S., [Paramount+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paramount%2B) streams every FA Cup game live, including the final, for free with just an email signup. That’s a deal even City and Chelsea fans can cheer for—no monthly fees, no hidden costs. Mobile viewers can also catch the action on the official FA Cup app and website, though expect some buffering hiccups if you’re on a weak signal.

## How to watch Chelsea vs Man City FA Cup Final in the U.S.

Sign up for Paramount+ (free tier) at paramountplus.com or download the app, then search for the FA Cup Final. The stream starts 30 minutes before kickoff with pre-match analysis. No cable login is needed, and the service doesn’t ask for a credit card upfront. If you already pay for Paramount+, you’ll get the same feed in 1080p HD. For cord-cutters who prefer a bigger screen, the game also streams on [Amazon Prime Video](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon_Prime_Video) via a free trial if you activate one by Saturday afternoon. Just cancel before the trial ends if you don’t want to pay.

## How to watch Chelsea vs Man City FA Cup Final in the UK

Grab a TV with an aerial for free-to-air channels BBC One and ITV1. The BBC’s stream on iPlayer and ITV’s on ITVX both work on smart TVs, mobiles, and tablets without signing up. If you’re out and about, the BBC Sport app offers a mobile-friendly stream with live stats and multi-camera angles. Radio fans can follow the match on [BBC Radio 5 Live](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Radio_5_Live) or [Talksport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Talksport), both carrying expert commentary from Wembley.

## Who’s watching and why it matters

This final has extra spice because City manager [Pep Guardiola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pep_Guardiola) is chasing his first FA Cup win since arriving in England in 2016. Chelsea, managed by [Mauricio Pochettino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauricio_Pochettino), needs a trophy to silence recent criticism after a rocky season. The winner earns a spot in next year’s [UEFA Champions League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League) and roughly £2 million in prize money. For neutrals, it’s a chance to see Erling Haaland, Kevin De Bruyne, and Cole Palmer trade passes against Chelsea’s youngsters like Cole Palmer’s former teammate Conor Gallagher. Both squads are packed with players who’ve worn the famous Wembley shirts before, so the nerves should be real.

## What to expect from the broadcast

Paramount+ in the U.S. is using multiple cameras, including one above the halfway line and another behind the dugouts. The in-game graphics show xG (expected goals) in real time, which helps casual fans follow the ebb and flow. Commentary comes from [Ian Darke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ian_Darke) and [Tyler Terrett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tyler_Terrett) on Paramount+, while the BBC’s team includes [Guy Mowbray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guy_Mowbray) and [Dion Dublin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dion_Dublin). If the match goes to extra time, the stream won’t cut to ads, so you won’t miss a second of the drama.

## After the final: trophy presentation and reactions

The trophy handover happens minutes after the final whistle on the pitch, with the captain lifting the famous silver FA Cup. The BBC and ITV will carry it live, including close-ups of the winning manager’s speech. Expect a flood of player reactions on social media within minutes—City’s Haaland and Chelsea’s Christopher Nkunku are both active on X (formerly Twitter), so their feeds will light up fast. The losing side heads straight to the mixed zone for quick interviews before the post-match press conferences begin around 7:45 p.m. ET.

Streaming hiccups often happen when thousands log on at once, so give the stream a test run 10 minutes early. If you’re using a mobile hotspot, switch to Wi-Fi to avoid buffering. For those watching in bars or pubs, many U.S. locations show the FA Cup Final on big screens via Paramount+’s bar partnership program—check local listings.
