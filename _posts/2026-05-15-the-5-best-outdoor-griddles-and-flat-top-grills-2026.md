Making breakfast, lunch, or dinner outside used to mean a small cast-iron skillet or a half-hearted grill. Now, flat top griddles and outdoor grills give you the space to cook like a short-order cook—without stepping inside. We cooked hundreds of tacos, burgers, and pancakes on 20 models to find the five griddles that actually work in 2026. They’re not just big metal plates; they’re grilling machines with burners hot enough to sear steaks, keep eggs from sticking, and flip pancakes without flipping the whole griddle.

## What to look for in a flat top grill

Burner power tops the list. The best griddles pack at least 40,000 BTUs if you want even heat across the whole surface. Cast iron sounds classic, but it’s heavy and slow to heat. Stainless steel tops with cold-rolled steel plates heat faster and last longer. Grease management matters too—look for deep grease trays that slide out for quick cleanups. Portability counts if you move it from patio to tailgate. Wheels help, but they need to be solid. And don’t ignore the side shelves. You’ll need a place to set your tongs before the burgers stick.

## 1. Blackstone 17-Inch Adventure Ready Griddle

Blackstone’s 17-Inch Adventure Ready tops the list for one reason: pancakes. The cold-rolled steel plate stays flat, so batter doesn’t pool in the corners. It hits 40,000 BTUs on the single burner, enough to melt cheese on a burger in under 90 seconds. The griddle weighs 33 pounds, light enough to toss in an SUV but heavy enough not to tip on windy days. The grease tray slides out in one pull, and the side shelf folds down for tight storage. It’s not the biggest, but it’s the most versatile for a small yard or apartment balcony.

## 2. Camp Chef Flat Top Grill 24-Inch

Camp Chef dropped the price this year to $399, making it the best value in the 24-Inch class. It runs on two 12,000-BTU burners that combine for 24,000 BTUs—not as hot as Blackstone’s single burner, but enough to cook 16 burgers at once. The stainless steel top resists rust, and the grease tray locks in place so it doesn’t spill during a flip. The fold-down side tables save space, and the legs fold for easy transport. If you want a mid-size griddle that won’t break the bank, this is it.

## 3. Royal Gourmet 36-Inch Industrial Flat Top Grill

Need to feed a crowd? Royal Gourmet’s 36-Inch model cooks 30 burgers at once. It runs on four 10,000-BTU burners, totaling 40,000 BTUs—enough to keep the whole surface at 500°F even with the door open. The stainless steel top is 3/16-inch thick, so it won’t warp like thinner plates. The grease tray holds a quart of oil, and the wheels lock for stability. It’s heavy at 120 pounds, but it rolls like a shopping cart. If your patio can handle the weight, it’s the best party griddle money can buy.

## 4. Traeger Flat Top Grill with Wi-Fi

Traeger added a flat top to its lineup this year, and it’s the only one with Wi-Fi. You set the temperature from your phone, and the grill adjusts the burners automatically. The 22-Inch surface runs on two 10,000-BTU burners, totaling 20,000 BTUs—enough for a family dinner, not a block party. The griddle comes with a grease tray and side shelf, and the legs fold for storage. It’s not the hottest, but the tech makes it stand out. If you love smart grills, this is your flat top.

## 5. Weber Original Kettle Premium Charcoal Grill with Griddle

Weber’s charcoal grill isn’t a flat top, but it’s the best way to get smoky flavor on a griddle. The hinged griddle accessory drops right into the kettle, so you can sear burgers and then smoke ribs without moving the food. The griddle is cast iron, pre-seasoned from the factory, so it won’t rust. The kettle itself holds 18 burgers, and the ash catcher simplifies cleanup. It’s not electric or gas, but if you want real smoke on your griddle, this is the move.

## Which one should you buy?

If you cook pancakes or need a small setup, grab the Blackstone 17-Inch. For a mid-size crowd on a budget, Camp Chef’s 24-Inch is the best deal. Royal Gourmet’s 36-Inch is the king of parties, Traeger’s flat top is for tech lovers, and Weber’s charcoal grill is for purists who want smoke. None of these griddles are cheap, but they’re built to last. Cook on them for a decade, and you’ll still be flipping burgers like a pro.
