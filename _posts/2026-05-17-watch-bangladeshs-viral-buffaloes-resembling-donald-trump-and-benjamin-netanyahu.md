A pair of albino buffaloes with eerily familiar faces are pulling in huge crowds at cattle farms just outside Bangladesh’s capital. The animals—nicknamed Trump and Netanyahu—have become the unlikely stars of the season ahead of Eid al-Adha, the country’s biggest Muslim festival, which starts on June 16 this year. Locals and tourists are making special trips to see the buffaloes, which look almost identical to the former U.S. president and Israel’s longest-serving prime minister. One farm owner said he never expected his animals to go viral, but their resemblance is too striking to ignore.## The Trump buffalo stands out with its golden-tinted hair and steady demeanor. Visitors describe it as unusually calm, almost regal, as it lounges in its enclosure. Staff at the farm near Savar, about 25 miles northwest of Dhaka, say the animal barely reacts to noise or movement, making it a favorite for photos and videos. Its calm nature contrasts sharply with its counterpart, the buffalo named Netanyahu, who’s known for charging at fences and startling onlookers.## Netanyahu the buffalo is anything but subtle. The animal charges fences, tosses its head, and seems to enjoy riling up the crowd. Farm workers say it’s the most energetic buffalo they’ve ever handled, and its antics have made it a social media darling. Videos of the buffalo snorting and butting its horns against metal gates have racked up thousands of shares online. Locals joke that it’s acting out the real-life drama of international politics.## The farms hosting the buffaloes are cashing in on the sudden fame. Ticket sales are up 40% compared to last year, with weekend crowds sometimes topping 500 people. Vendors sell snacks and drinks, and some are even offering “Trump-Netanyahu” combo photos with the animals. The farm owners say they’re thrilled by the attention but admit they’re baffled by how two water buffaloes became overnight celebrities.## Animal behavior experts say the buffaloes’ albinism plays a role in their striking looks. Albino buffaloes have pale skin and light-colored eyes, which can make their features appear more human-like under certain lighting. While not rare, albinism in large animals is uncommon enough to draw attention. Experts also note that buffaloes, like many animals, can develop distinct personalities that shape how they interact with visitors.## For now, the Trump and Netanyahu buffaloes are living their best lives—at least until the Eid rush ends. The farms plan to keep them on display as long as the crowds hold up, though they’re already talking about expanding enclosures to handle the extra foot traffic. One thing’s certain: these two aren’t going anywhere anytime soon. Their fame has cemented their place in local lore, and they’ve earned their spot as this year’s most unusual Eid attraction.
