Wales and Italy meet at [Cardiff Arms Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardiff_Arms_Park) on Sunday in the Six Nations' first Super Sunday double-header. The match kicks off at 15:00 GMT, with the second game featuring England and Scotland following two hours later. For Wales, it's do-or-die after two heavy defeats to Ireland and France left them bottom of the table with just one point from two games.

Italy come to Wales in slightly better shape. The Azzurri lost their opener to England but edged Scotland in their second match to move off the bottom. A win for Italy would lift them to third in the table, while Wales need points to avoid their worst Six Nations start in over a decade. The hosts haven’t lost their opening three games since 2011, but head coach Warren Gatland faces intense scrutiny after a string of poor results.

Gatland’s side have struggled to break down defences in recent matches. Against France, Wales managed just one try in a 28-0 defeat, while Ireland scored four tries in their 31-point win. Italy, meanwhile, have shown flashes of promise under new coach [Massimiliano Allegri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massimiliano_Allegri), particularly in their scrum and set-piece work.

## Gatland’s job on the line
Wales’ poor form has reignited debates about Gatland’s future. The 51-year-old has been in charge since 2020 but has yet to win a Six Nations title with Wales. His contract expires after the 2024 Six Nations, and another poor run could force the WRU to make a change. Gatland has reshuffled his squad multiple times this year, but injuries to key players like [Taulupe Faletau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taulupe_Faletau) and [George North](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_North) have disrupted his plans.

Italy, meanwhile, have quietly rebuilt under Allegri. The former Juventus manager took over in July and has brought a more structured approach to the national team. His side’s 20-17 win over Scotland was their first in 14 Six Nations matches, ending a 36-game losing streak away from home. Allegri’s focus on discipline and set-pieces has started to pay off.

## What’s at stake for both teams
For Wales, Sunday is about pride. The team’s last home win was against Italy in 2023, and a loss would leave them staring at a potential wooden spoon. Italy, meanwhile, are playing with confidence after their shock victory over Scotland. A second win in a row would be their first back-to-back successes in the Six Nations since 2013.

The match also serves as a barometer for both teams ahead of the World Cup later this year. Wales need to rediscover their attacking flair to avoid another humiliating tournament exit, while Italy are determined to prove they can compete with Europe’s top sides. The WRU’s decision on Gatland’s future could hinge on the result, adding extra pressure to an already fraught situation.

Sunday’s double-header will be broadcast live on BBC One in the UK and internationally on various platforms. Kick-off for the Wales-Italy game is at 15:00 GMT, with England vs Scotland following at 17:30 GMT. Both matches promise high stakes, with implications for the Six Nations title race and teams’ preparations for the World Cup.
