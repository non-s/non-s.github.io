Deane Williams is on the brink of writing his name into British basketball history. The London Lions power forward has already lifted the BBL Cup this season and guided his team to the BBL Championship semifinals. A win in the BBL Trophy final or the upcoming playoffs would seal a quadruple—something no British club team has achieved in the modern era. Williams, 28, spent most of his career overseas in leagues like the Greek Basket League and the Israeli Basketball Premier League before returning to the UK. His experience abroad has given him a physical edge that’s been crucial for London Lions’ success this season. "Playing in tougher leagues taught me how to grind out wins," Williams told BBC Sport. "That mental toughness has been a difference-maker here." The team’s consistency under coach [London Lions head coach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Robertson_(basketball_coach)) Paul Robertson has been a key factor. Robertson, in his second season with the Lions, has built a squad that combines Williams’ veteran presence with young talent. London finished the regular season second in the BBL Championship, setting up a semifinal clash with the [Leicester Riders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leicester_Riders). They’ve lost just twice in their last 10 games, a streak that includes the BBL Cup final victory over the Riders in January. ## The road to history Williams needs to win the BBL Trophy final and the upcoming playoffs to complete the quadruple. The Trophy final isn’t until April, but the playoffs start in just over two weeks. London Lions face the [Newcastle Eagles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newcastle_Eagles) in the first round, a team they’ve dominated this season, winning all four regular-season meetings. Williams has been the Lions’ most consistent performer, averaging 16.2 points and 7.5 rebounds per game in the BBL. His ability to stretch defenses with his shooting and bully opponents in the paint makes him the team’s most versatile weapon. "He’s the kind of player who can take over a game if we need him to," said [London Lions captain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luke_Nelson_(basketball)) Luke Nelson. "His experience overseas taught him how to handle pressure, and that’s showing now." ## Why a quadruple matters in British basketball The last time a British team came close to a quadruple was in 2013 when the [Leicester Riders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leicester_Riders) won the BBL Championship, BBL Cup, and BBL Trophy, but lost in the playoff semifinals. Since then, no team has dominated all four domestic competitions in a single season. Williams’ pursuit of the quadruple isn’t just about the trophies—it’s about proving British basketball can compete at the highest level. The BBL has struggled to attract top-tier talent in the past, but Williams’ return has helped change that narrative. His presence has drawn attention to the league, and other British players abroad are now considering moves back home. "When you see a guy like Deane coming back and having this kind of impact, it makes you think twice about staying away," said [British basketball analyst](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Amaral) John Amaral. "That’s good for the whole league." ## What happens next The BBL Trophy final is set for April 7 against the winner of the Leicester Riders vs. Newcastle Eagles semifinal. The playoffs begin on April 12, with London Lions starting as one of the top seeds. Williams and his teammates know the pressure is on. "We’re not getting ahead of ourselves," Williams said. "We’re just focused on taking it one game at a time. If we do that, we’ll give ourselves a chance to make history." For British basketball fans, this is a rare chance to watch a team dominate in a way they haven’t seen in years. If London Lions pull it off, Williams will be the first player in the modern era to lift all four major domestic trophies in a single season. That’s the kind of achievement that doesn’t come around often.

O armador Deane Williams, do London Lions, está prestes a entrar para a história do basquete europeu ao buscar um feito raríssimo: o *quadruple crown*, ou seja, a conquista de quatro títulos em uma única temporada. Com apenas uma temporada jogando na Europa, o atleta já se tornou um dos destaques do time inglês e agora mira em uma campanha que poucos jogadores conseguiram realizar no esporte.

A trajetória de Williams na Europa tem chamado a atenção não só pela sua performance dentro de quadra, mas também pela forma como ele se adaptou rapidamente ao estilo de jogo europeu, mesmo tendo chegado vindo de uma liga menos competitiva. O interesse dos torcedores brasileiros, sempre ávidos por novos talentos internacionais, cresce à medida que o London Lions avança em competições como a Liga Britânica, a Copa da Inglaterra e possíveis etapas da EuroCup. Caso consiga o feito, Williams não só entraria para a elite do basquete mundial, como também poderia se tornar um nome ainda mais atraente para ligas como a NBB ou até mesmo a NBA, reforçando o intercâmbio de jogadores entre o Brasil e a Europa.

Se o London Lions confirmar o *quadruple crown*, Deane Williams não apenas escreverá seu nome no livro de recordes do basquete europeu, como também abrirá portas para que outros jogadores das Américas sigam seu caminho rumo ao Velho Continente.
