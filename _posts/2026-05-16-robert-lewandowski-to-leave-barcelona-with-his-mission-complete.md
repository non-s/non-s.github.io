Barcelona striker [Robert Lewandowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Lewandowski) announced Saturday he will leave the club when his contract expires this summer after four seasons. The 37-year-old Polish forward, who joined from [Bayern Munich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich) in 2022, scored 119 goals in 191 appearances across all competitions.

Lewandowski helped restore Barcelona to the top of Spanish football after a period of financial struggles. He contributed to three La Liga titles, including this season’s championship, and the Copa del Rey in 2025. “After four years full of challenges and hard work, it’s time to move on,” Lewandowski wrote in an [Instagram post](https://www.instagram.com/p/). “I leave with the feeling that the mission is complete. Four seasons, three championships.”

## Lewandowski’s Barcelona legacy and final match

The veteran striker arrived at a low point for Barca, who were battling financial crises and on-field struggles. His leadership and goal-scoring ability helped revive the club’s fortunes. Lewandowski also played a key role in Barca’s push for Champions League contention during his tenure.

He will play his final match for Barcelona on Sunday at [Camp Nou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Camp_Nou) against [Real Betis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Betis). The club has scheduled a farewell tribute for the retiring star, who has won widespread praise from fans and teammates alike.

## Potential destinations after Barcelona

Reports suggest Lewandowski has received offers from clubs in [Saudi Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia), [Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy), and the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States). At 37, he remains a sought-after striker with a proven track record in top European leagues.

Lewandowski’s departure marks the end of an era for Barcelona, who will now look to rebuild in the summer transfer window. His contributions on and off the pitch have left a lasting legacy at the club, where he was a fan favorite from his first days.

The Polish striker’s announcement comes as Barca prepare for life after [Lionel Messi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lionel_Messi), another club legend who left in 2023. Lewandowski’s exit signals another transition period for the Catalan giants, who are balancing sporting ambitions with financial constraints.
