England have dropped Zak Crawley after another disappointing start to the season, with Test captain Ben Stokes delivering the final blow both with bat and ball. The 28-year-old opener was left out of England’s three-Test series against New Zealand, which begins on June 4 and will be shown live on Sky Sports. His axing follows a string of unproductive performances that have raised serious questions about his future in the red-ball side.

Crawley’s struggles have mounted since the Ashes, where he averaged just 20.33 across five Tests, and his form in the County Championship has done little to silence critics. In Kent’s first innings against Durham, Crawley made 30 before miscuing a pull shot straight to Matthew Potts, handing Stokes his first scalp of the match. The dismissal came after Stokes had already claimed one wicket and would go on to finish with figures of one for 70 from 18 overs.

## Stokes responds with bat and ball

While Stokes was making early inroads with the ball, his Durham teammate Ben Dawkins was compiling a monumental innings at the other end. The 19-year-old marked his maiden first-class century with an unbeaten 181, steering Kent to a dominant position at close of play on day one. Veteran batter Sam Northeast, 36, contributed 141 before falling just short of his own milestone, but it was Dawkins who stole the show with a composed performance under pressure.

Stokes, who had opened the bowling for Durham last week, switched to first change in this match and made an immediate impact. His wicket of Crawley was followed by the removal of Northeast, leaving Kent at 237 for three after a mammoth 303-run partnership between Dawkins and Northeast. The pair’s stand revived Kent’s innings after Fisher had earlier removed England white-ball opener Jonny Bairstow, who was playing his first game as Yorkshire captain.

Michael Atherton, former England captain and now a pundit for Sky Sports, argued that Crawley’s position at the top of the order had become unsustainable. In 64 Test matches, Crawley averages 31.00 with the bat, a return that Atherton described as insufficient for retention at international level. “His appearances are high on the all-time list for England batters, but his returns don’t put him high up,” Atherton said. “His position had become untenable.”

## What’s next for Crawley and England?

With Crawley now out of the England squad, the door appears closed for his immediate return. Atherton acknowledged that while doors aren’t closed forever in sport, the opportunities now lie with younger players pushing for selection. “People need to feel they have a chance when they’re playing domestic cricket,” he added. “Otherwise, what’s the point?”

England’s three-Test series against New Zealand presents a fresh start, with Stokes leading the side and a new-look top order likely to emerge. For Crawley, the path back will require strong domestic performances, but with Dawkins and Northeast showing form, the competition for batting spots has intensified. The question now is whether Crawley can regain his place—or if England will move forward without him.
