Northern Ireland’s Rory McIlroy [Rory McIlroy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rory_McIlroy) carded a four-under-par round of 66 Friday to move to 9-under par for the tournament and take a one-shot lead entering the final round of the PGA Championship at [Oak Hill Country Club](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oak_Hill_Country_Club) (East Course) in Rochester, New York.

McIlroy, 34, made seven birdies against just one bogey during his round, including a 15-foot putt on the 11th to extend his lead. His score moved him past American Xander Schauffele [Xander Schauffele](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xander_Schauffele), who shot a two-under 69 to sit at 8 under, and South African Branden Grace, who also carded 69 to sit at 7 under.

## McIlroy's round lifts him into contention

McIlroy’s round followed a Saturday-Wednesday schedule disrupted by rain delays. Despite the weather, Oak Hill’s firm and fast greens rewarded aggressive play, with McIlroy capitalizing on every opportunity. His round included birdies at Nos. 2, 5, 7, 11, 12, 13 and 16, while his lone bogey came at the par-3 14th after a missed putt.

American [Tiger Woods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiger_Woods) [Tiger Woods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiger_Woods), chasing his fifth PGA title, added a one-under 70 to sit at 7 under—two shots adrift of McIlroy. Woods, playing ahead of McIlroy, posted bogeys at Nos. 6 and 17 but recovered with birdies at Nos. 11 and 15 to remain in contention.

## Remaining field faces final-round challenge

Defending champion [Brooks Koepka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brooks_Koepka) shot a two-over 73 to fall to 4 under, while American [Jon Rahm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jon_Rahm) [Jon Rahm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jon_Rahm) added a one-under 69 to sit at 5 under. Masters champion [Scottie Scheffler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scottie_Scheffler) [Scottie Scheffler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scottie_Scheffler) carded a one-over 72, leaving him two shots behind Woods.

With the weather forecast dry for Saturday’s final round, players will face Oak Hill’s challenging layout, which has historically favored accurate iron play and precise putting. McIlroy, seeking his second PGA Championship title after his 2012 victory, now holds the clubhouse lead but knows Woods and Schauffele will be pushing hard to overturn it.

If McIlroy holds his nerve, he could become the first player since [Tiger Woods in 2007](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2007_PGA_Championship) to win the PGA Championship wire-to-wire. His final-round pairing with Woods could add extra drama to an already tense finish.
