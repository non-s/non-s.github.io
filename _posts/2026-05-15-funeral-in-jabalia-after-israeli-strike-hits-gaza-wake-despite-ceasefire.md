Residents of Jabalia in northern Gaza buried two Palestinians on Sunday after an Israeli airstrike targeted a funeral wake, defying a declared ceasefire. The attack occurred in the densely populated area, killing two mourners and injuring several others, according to local health officials. The Israeli military has not issued an immediate response regarding the strike, which violated the temporary truce in place.

The Gaza Health Ministry reported that the Palestinian death toll has now reached nearly 73,000 since the conflict escalated in October 2023. The ministry, operated by Hamas, has faced scrutiny over casualty figures but remains the primary source for death tolls in Gaza. The strike in Jabalia underscores the fragile nature of the ceasefire, which has seen repeated violations despite international efforts to broker lasting peace.

Israeli officials have not confirmed the strike, and the circumstances surrounding the attack remain unclear. Witnesses described chaotic scenes as rescue teams scrambled to recover bodies from the rubble of the destroyed wake tent. The Israeli military typically cites military necessity for such strikes but has not provided details in this case.

The Jabalia area has been a frequent target of Israeli operations due to its proximity to [Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel) and its role as a Hamas stronghold. The strike occurred just days after a brief humanitarian pause in fighting, which had allowed limited aid deliveries into Gaza. The resumption of violence has raised concerns about the sustainability of ceasefire agreements.

Gaza City and other northern regions remain under heavy Israeli bombardment, with hospitals overwhelmed by the influx of casualties. The international community has repeatedly called for restraint, but both sides continue to exchange fire, prolonging the humanitarian crisis. Aid organizations warn of worsening conditions as food, water, and medical supplies dwindle.

The latest violence comes as mediators attempt to revive negotiations for a permanent ceasefire. Egypt and Qatar have been key brokers in past talks, but progress has stalled amid mutual distrust. The Biden administration has urged both sides to return to negotiations, emphasizing the need to protect civilians.

As funerals continue in Jabalia and across Gaza, the toll on civilians grows. The strike on the funeral wake highlights the precarious nature of ceasefire agreements and the ongoing risks faced by Palestinian civilians in the conflict.
