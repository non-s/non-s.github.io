Lawyers for Mahmoud Khalil [Columbia University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Columbia_University) student, have petitioned an immigration appeals court to reopen and terminate his deportation case, citing new evidence they say proves the Trump administration manipulated the outcome to make an example of him. Khalil, a US permanent resident who is married to a US citizen, was detained by immigration enforcement agents in March 2025, one of several students targeted for their involvement in pro-Palestine campus protests across the US the previous year.

The legal appeal, filed Friday, argues that recently uncovered evidence—including media reports—indicates officials in the Trump administration secretly orchestrated the case against Khalil to suppress pro-Palestine activism. Khalil has consistently denied any wrongdoing and maintains he was targeted for his political views. His lawyers contend the new material contradicts the initial ruling by the Board of Immigration Appeals, which issued a final order of removal just over a month ago.

## Trump administration’s alleged role in Khalil’s deportation case

The claim of administrative interference adds a new dimension to Khalil’s legal battle, which began after he was arrested during protests at Columbia University in 2024. His detention in March 2025 marked the start of a protracted immigration process that culminated in the removal order. The latest appeal challenges the legitimacy of that process, citing procedural irregularities and what the legal team calls “coordinated efforts” to influence the outcome.

Khalil’s case has drawn attention from civil rights groups, who argue that his treatment reflects a broader pattern of targeting students and activists for expressing support for Palestine. His legal team has not publicly released the full body of evidence but says it includes documents and witness statements that suggest government officials interfered in the judicial process.

## What happens next in Khalil’s deportation appeal

The appeals court now faces a decision on whether to reopen Khalil’s case for further review. If granted, the case could be remanded for additional hearings or dismissed entirely. Khalil’s legal team has requested termination of the removal order, arguing that the new evidence undermines the original case against him. Immigration officials have not commented on the claims.

Khalil’s situation also raises questions about due process in immigration enforcement, particularly in cases involving political speech. His lawyers have emphasized that as a permanent resident, Khalil has the right to live in the US and that his deportation would violate constitutional protections. Advocacy groups are closely monitoring the case, calling it a test of whether immigration proceedings can be weaponized for political purposes.

The outcome could influence how future immigration cases involving free speech are handled, especially in politically charged environments. Khalil’s legal team has vowed to pursue all available avenues if the appeal is denied, including potential appeals to federal courts. For now, Khalil remains in the US while the court considers the motion to reopen his case.
