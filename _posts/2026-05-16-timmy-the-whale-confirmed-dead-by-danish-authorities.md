Danish authorities confirmed Saturday that Timmy the humpback whale, whose controversial rescue attempt sparked outrage, has died. Environmental Protection Agency officials said the whale’s carcass was found Friday near [Anholt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anholt), a small island in the Kattegat strait between Denmark and Sweden. Genetic testing confirmed the identity of the 27-foot humpback on Saturday.

## Controversial rescue attempt drew immediate backlash

Critics slammed the July 19 rescue operation as inhumane after rescuers attempted to tow Timmy from the shallow waters of the [North Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Sea) back to deeper waters near Denmark’s west coast. Video of the attempt showed the whale struggling as ropes were attached to his tail, drawing accusations of unnecessary suffering. Animal rights groups called the method outdated and cruel, while marine experts questioned its scientific basis.

The rescue team, led by Denmark’s [Environmental Protection Agency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_Protection_Agency_(Denmark)), argued the whale’s health was deteriorating and relocation was a last resort. They cited previous cases where stranded whales recovered after being moved to open waters. But the public reaction was swift and overwhelmingly negative, with social media flooded with criticism and calls for accountability.

## Necropsy planned to determine cause of death

Danish officials have not yet released details about Timmy’s condition when he was found or the likely cause of death. A necropsy is scheduled to determine whether his injuries were directly related to the rescue attempt or pre-existing conditions. The [Danish Veterinary and Food Administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danish_Veterinary_and_Food_Administration) will lead the examination, with results expected within weeks.

The [Horns Rev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horns_Rev) wind farm area, where Timmy was first spotted, remains under scrutiny. Environmentalists argue offshore construction may have disrupted marine habitats, contributing to the stranding. The Danish Energy Agency has not commented on potential links to human activity.

## Whale strandings rise amid climate and human activity concerns

Timmy’s death follows a 30% increase in humpback whale strandings along Denmark’s coast this year, according to marine biologists. Experts cite rising sea temperatures, shipping noise, and offshore wind farm expansion as possible factors. The Danish Nature Agency has called for stricter monitoring of marine mammal health in the region.

The case has reignited debates over wildlife rescue protocols and the ethics of intervention. While some conservationists support relocation efforts, others advocate for letting nature take its course in cases of extreme distress. The Danish government has not indicated plans to change current guidelines, but the public outcry may force a review.

No further updates are expected until necropsy results are released. Timmy’s death marks the latest high-profile casualty in a string of marine strandings across Northern Europe this summer.
