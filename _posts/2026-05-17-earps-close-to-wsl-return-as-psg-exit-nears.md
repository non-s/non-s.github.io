Former England goalkeeper [Mary Earps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mary_Earps) is on the brink of a Women’s Super League comeback after her time at Paris St-Germain winds down. The 31-year-old shot-stopper, who spent last season with PSG following a spell at Manchester United, is now just days away from finalizing a return to England’s top flight. Sources close to the situation confirm talks are at an advanced stage, with clubs in the WSL circling as her PSG exit nears completion.

Earps left Manchester United in 2023 after five years at the club, where she became a fan favorite and England’s first-choice goalkeeper. Her move to PSG last summer was seen as a step up, but injuries and competition for the starting spot limited her game time. Now, with PSG set to let her leave, Earps is poised to revive her domestic career. The WSL is her likely destination, with clubs like Arsenal and Chelsea reportedly interested in signing her.

## PSG’s changing goalkeeping plans

PSG’s decision to release Earps reflects their own goalkeeping overhaul. The French club recently signed Norway’s [Guro Pettersen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guro_Pettersen) from Tottenham Hotspur, signaling they’re moving in a new direction. Earps’ contract with PSG expires this summer, and the club has opted not to renew it, freeing her to negotiate elsewhere. Her departure leaves a gap in PSG’s squad, but the club’s focus appears to be on building a younger, homegrown team.

For Earps, the timing couldn’t be better. At 31, she’s still in her prime and remains England’s number one goalkeeper. Her experience in top European competitions, including the Women’s Champions League, makes her a valuable asset for any WSL club. Clubs in the league are always looking for reliable goalkeepers, and Earps fits the bill with her shot-stopping ability and leadership qualities.

## What’s next for Earps and the WSL?

Earps’ return to the WSL would be a major boost for the league’s profile. The WSL has struggled to retain top English talent in recent years, with many players opting for moves abroad. Earps’ presence would add star power and experience, which could help the league compete more effectively in Europe. Clubs are likely to make firm offers in the coming weeks, with a decision expected before the end of the transfer window.

The move also raises questions about PSG’s next steps. With Earps gone, they’ll need to find a replacement who can step into her shoes. Meanwhile, Earps’ potential return to England could reignite interest in women’s football, especially with the Euros coming up in 2025. Fans are already buzzing about the possibility of seeing her back in a WSL kit, and clubs know she’d be a game-changer in goal.

For now, Earps remains focused on finalizing her move. If all goes to plan, she could be back in an England shirt for Manchester United or another WSL side within weeks. The timing couldn’t be better for her career, and the league stands to benefit from her experience.
