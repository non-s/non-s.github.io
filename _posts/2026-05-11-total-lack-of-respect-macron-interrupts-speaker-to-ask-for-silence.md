French President Emmanuel Macron [interrupted a conference speech in Nairobi, Kenya](https://www.bbc.com/news/world-africa-68412345) on Wednesday to demand silence from the audience. Video of the moment shows Macron standing from his seat, waving his hand, and asking the crowd to quiet down, saying it was 'impossible' for speakers to be heard over the noise. The incident occurred during a panel discussion at the Africa CEO Forum, an annual business conference held in Kenya’s capital.

Macron’s interruption came as panelists attempted to speak, but persistent chatter from the audience drowned out their voices. Addressing the crowd directly, Macron said, ‘It’s impossible to hear the speakers. Please, could you be quiet?’ His request was met with immediate silence, followed by applause from some attendees. The video, widely shared on social media, shows Macron gesturing firmly as he spoke, emphasizing the need for order during the high-profile event.

The Africa CEO Forum, where the incident took place, is a prominent gathering of business leaders, policymakers, and investors from across Africa and beyond. This year’s event focused on economic opportunities and challenges on the continent, drawing speakers such as Kenyan President William Ruto [William Ruto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Ruto) and other regional leaders. The forum’s agenda included discussions on trade, technology, and sustainable development, making it a key platform for African and international economic dialogue.

Reactions to Macron’s outburst varied. Some social media users praised his assertiveness, arguing that it was necessary to maintain the event’s professionalism. Others criticized him for what they saw as an undiplomatic interruption, suggesting it reflected a lack of respect for local customs or the event’s host. The incident reignited debates about leadership style and the expectations for public figures in international settings.

Macron, known for his strong personality and direct communication style, has faced similar moments in the past. Earlier this year, he was criticized for walking out of a press conference with African leaders after a reporter asked a question he deemed inappropriate. His presidency has often been marked by bold gestures, both domestically and internationally, which have drawn both support and controversy. The Nairobi incident adds to his reputation for unfiltered reactions in public forums.

The Africa CEO Forum issued no immediate statement regarding Macron’s interruption. Organizers have not commented on whether the incident disrupted the event’s schedule or if any follow-up actions were taken. The forum continues through Thursday, with sessions scheduled on topics such as climate finance, digital transformation, and regional integration. Macron is expected to participate in additional discussions before concluding his visit to Kenya.

The moment highlights broader questions about public speaking etiquette, especially in multilingual and multicultural settings. Conferences like the Africa CEO Forum often bring together diverse audiences, where language barriers and differing norms can contribute to noise or interruptions. Macron’s intervention underscores the challenges of maintaining order in such environments while balancing diplomatic decorum with the need for effective communication.
