The Grand Steeple-Chase de Paris at [Auteuil Racecourse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auteuil_Hippodrome) on Sunday is shaping up as a thrilling showdown between Kolokico and Toscana Du Berlais. The Grade One race, worth €200,000, kicks off at 2:15pm and will be broadcast live on Sky Sports Racing. Kolokico, who finished second in last year's edition, has had a tough season but showed promise in recent prep runs behind Kivala Du Berlais. Trainer Emmanuel Clayeux is hoping that race will have sharpened his horse for this rematch. Toscana Du Berlais, last year's fourth-place finisher, has been consistent but never won this prize. His form this season suggests he might finally be peaking at the right time. The bookmakers have both horses near the top of the market, with Kaadem, Gold Tweet, and Kivala Du Berlais also in the mix. The race looks wide open, but Kolokico’s recent work suggests he’s ready to go one better than 2024. Auteuil’s stiff fences and tough conditions make this one for the specialists, and the winner will earn serious bragging rights among jumping fans. The track’s heavy ground in recent days means stamina and jumping ability will be tested like never before. Kolokico has the stamina pedigree—his sire is a proven stayers’ sire—but Toscana Du Berlais has shown he can handle the trip. This is shaping up as a tactical battle between a horse who wants to forget last year’s near-miss and one who’s quietly confident he can turn fourth into first. Expect a tight finish in a race that’s famous for dramatic late challenges at the last fence. Auteuil’s crowd always loves an upset, so don’t rule out an outsider like Kaadem sneaking through if the favorites tire over the final half-mile of stiff fences. The race is part of a crackerjack card at Auteuil, with competitive flat racing also on show at Ripon in the afternoon. ## The British EBF fillies’ handicap at Ripon shapes up tightly too. Three-year-olds Lebron Power and Alpine Girl headline a competitive field in the Premier Fillies’ Handicap. Alpine Girl, fresh off a win at Pontefract, is the one to beat after a seven-pound weight rise didn’t slow her down last time. She’s in great form and could make it two from two in this race if the handicapper hasn’t caught her. Lebron Power, winner of three races last season, is another who’s sharpened up since her last run and should go well for Richard Hannon. The fillies’ race is run over a mile and a quarter on the all-weather, so speed and stamina will both matter. The weather forecast is kind, with no rain due until after the meeting, so the ground should stay good for both the jumps and the flat action. ## The Prix Alain du Breil at Auteuil is another Grade One highlight on Sunday’s card. Willie Mullins’ Selma De Vary is the favorite after a strong debut season in France. The Irish champion trainer has won this race twice before, most recently with Gala Marceau in 2023. Selma De Vary has run well in all three starts this year but unluckily missed winning at Leopardstown and Cheltenham. Her fourth at Aintree last time showed she’s still improving, and Paul Townend’s ride should give her every chance. The race is run over two miles and five furlongs on heavy ground, so stamina and jumping will be key. Mullins has saddled winners here before, and his record at Auteuil is second to none. He’s quietly confident Selma De Vary can add to his tally on Sunday. The Prix Alain du Breil is one of France’s richest staying chases, and Mullins will be desperate to keep his grip on the prize. His stable has dominated this race in recent years, and Selma De Vary looks ready to uphold the tradition. For jumping fans, Sunday’s card at Auteuil is a must-watch.
