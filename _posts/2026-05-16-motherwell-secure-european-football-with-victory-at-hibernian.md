Motherwell secured their fourth-place finish in the Scottish Premiership and a spot in the Europa Conference League qualifiers with a 1-0 victory over Hibernian at Easter Road on Saturday. Lukas Fadinger struck the decisive goal in the 35th minute, capitalizing on a rebound after Hibs goalkeeper [Joe Hart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joe_Hart) parried a shot from [Callum Slattery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Callum_Slattery). The visitors held firm despite late pressure and a numerical disadvantage after [Elliot Watt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elliot_Watt) was shown a second yellow card midway through the second half. Stand-in goalkeeper [Matty Connelly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matty_Connelly) made no saves but his positioning and composure under pressure ensured three points, crucial for Motherwell’s European ambitions.

## VAR controversy overshadows well-earned win
Stephen Welsh thought he had doubled Motherwell’s lead in the 62nd minute, but his effort was ruled out for offside. VAR intervention upheld the assistant referee’s decision, sparking debate among Hibs supporters and pundits alike. The disallowed goal came amid a dominant spell for Motherwell, who created several chances but failed to convert until Fadinger’s strike. The officiating decisions shifted momentum, with Hibs rallying in the final stages but ultimately falling short in their attempt to secure a draw.

The red card to Watt, issued for a second booking just after the hour mark, forced Motherwell to play the remainder of the game with 10 men. Despite the numerical disadvantage, Connelly’s command of the box and defensive organization thwarted Hibs’ late attacks. Hibs’ best chance of the game came in stoppage time when [Martin Boyle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin_Boyle) forced Connelly into a save, but the visitors held on to preserve their lead.

## Celtic’s result casts shadow over Hibs’ European fate
Hibs entered the match with hopes of overtaking Motherwell for fourth place, which would have secured Europa Conference League football. However, the 2-0 win by [Celtic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celtic_F.C.) over [Hearts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heart_of_Midlothian_F.C.) later on Saturday meant the Hoops reached the Scottish Cup final. Hibs’ European aspirations now hinge on Celtic defeating [Dunfermline Athletic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dunfermline_Athletic_F.C.) in the final, a scenario that drew mixed reactions from Hibs supporters. Many celebrated Celtic’s progress, while others acknowledged the irony of their fate resting on another club’s performance.

## Injuries raise concerns for World Cup hopefuls
The match was marred by two concerning injuries for Hibs defenders. Democratic Republic of Congo international [Rocky Bushiri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rocky_Bushiri) was stretchered off without contact in the first half, with physios assisting him off the pitch. His replacement, Scotland defender [Grant Hanley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grant_Hanley), also limped off gingerly after slipping, raising doubts about his World Cup prospects. Bushiri’s injury appeared serious, while Hanley’s exit left Hibs with a defensive reshuffle for their next fixture. Motherwell, meanwhile, were without regular goalkeeper [Calum Ward](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calum_Ward) due to injury, with Aston Oxborough recalled from loan as cover.

Hibs did receive positive news ahead of the match when [Martin Boyle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin_Boyle) signed a one-year contract extension, ending speculation over his future at the club. Boyle, a fan favorite, had been linked with a move away but committed to staying at Easter Road. His performance against Motherwell was solid, though his late save was not enough to prevent defeat. The win for Motherwell, however, means they will compete in European football for the first time since 2015-16, a significant achievement for the Lanarkshire club under manager [Steven Hammell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steven_Hammell).

Looking ahead, Motherwell will prepare for their Europa Conference League qualifiers, while Hibs must regroup for their next challenge. The Scottish Cup final on May 21 will determine whether Hibs can still secure European football via a domestic route. Celtic’s dominance in the cup competition has overshadowed Hibs’ late title push, leaving the Edinburgh club to consider their options for next season.
