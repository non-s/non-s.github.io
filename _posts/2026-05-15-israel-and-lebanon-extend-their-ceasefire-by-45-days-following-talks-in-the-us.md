Israel and Lebanon have agreed to extend their ceasefire by 45 days following high-level talks in the United States, the State Department confirmed on Monday. The temporary truce, which has limited cross-border violence since October, was set to expire at midnight. Diplomats say the extension provides more time for negotiations to secure a lasting solution.

The US State Department announced it will host further negotiations aimed at reaching a permanent political agreement on June 2 and 3. Before those talks, the Pentagon is scheduled to bring together military delegations from Israel and Lebanon on May 29 to discuss de-escalation measures. A senior US official said the meetings aim to reduce tensions and prevent renewed conflict along the shared border.

Israel and Lebanon have fought intermittent clashes since October, primarily involving Hezbollah [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hezbollah], the Iran-backed militant group based in Lebanon. Israel has conducted airstrikes targeting Hezbollah positions in response to rocket attacks and cross-border infiltrations. Lebanon’s caretaker government, led by Prime Minister Najib Mikati [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Najib_Mikati], has sought to mediate while facing internal political divisions and economic instability.

The ceasefire extension comes as regional tensions remain high amid ongoing Israel-Hamas [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel%E2%80%93Hamas_war] hostilities in Gaza. The Biden administration has pushed for a broader diplomatic framework to stabilize Lebanon and prevent wider conflict. Analysts warn that without a permanent agreement, sporadic violence could escalate into a larger confrontation.

## US pushes for long-term de-escalation

The Pentagon’s upcoming military talks signal a coordinated effort to stabilize the border through direct dialogue. US officials have emphasized that both sides must adhere to the ceasefire to avoid miscalculations that could trigger a broader crisis. The State Department described the June negotiations as a critical step toward a durable solution, though no enforceable timeline has been set.

Lebanese officials have repeatedly called for international guarantees to prevent further Israeli military actions in southern Lebanon. Meanwhile, Israeli leaders have insisted any agreement must ensure Hezbollah’s full withdrawal from the border area, in line with UN Security Council Resolution 1701 [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_1701].

## Broader regional implications

The ceasefire extension offers temporary relief to civilians on both sides of the border, where thousands have been displaced by ongoing violence. Humanitarian groups report rising needs for shelter, food, and medical care in southern Lebanon and northern Israel. The UN Interim Force in Lebanon (UNIFIL) [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Interim_Force_in_Lebanon] has maintained a monitoring presence but lacks the mandate to enforce a permanent solution.

Diplomatic observers caution that Lebanon’s fragile state—marked by political paralysis, economic collapse, and rising public unrest—complicates efforts for a sustainable agreement. The country has not held a fully functioning government since October 2022, and its military remains under-resourced amid multiple crises.

Israel’s government, led by Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_Netanyahu], faces domestic pressure to prevent further rocket attacks from Lebanon. At the same time, it must balance military action with efforts to avoid a multi-front war involving Hezbollah and Hamas. The US has reiterated its commitment to brokering a deal that prevents further destabilization in the region.

Analysts say the next 45 days will be critical in determining whether the ceasefire holds or collapses under renewed pressure. Both sides have previously accused each other of violating the truce, raising concerns about trust in the negotiation process. International mediators are expected to push for confidence-building measures, including prisoner exchanges and humanitarian access.

The outcome of the June talks could influence broader regional dynamics, including Iran’s role in supporting proxy groups and the possibility of a wider Israel-Hezbollah conflict. The US has urged restraint while continuing to engage with key stakeholders to prevent miscalculation. A failure to reach an agreement could lead to increased military posturing and a higher risk of escalation in the coming months.
