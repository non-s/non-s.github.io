French director Claire Denis is finally moving ahead with ‘The Soap Maker,’ a cannibal crime drama she will both write and direct. The project, which first surfaced in 2025 at Berlin’s European Film Market (EFM), has now secured producers and is edging closer to production. Denis, celebrated for her bold and unsettling filmmaking, is teaming up with producers Sacha Ben Harroche of Butterfly Jam and Ivy Freeman-Attwood of The Dispute to bring the film to life. The two producers bring experience in film finance and international co-productions, which could help the project reach wider audiences.

## Why this film matters to Denis
Denis has long been drawn to stories that push boundaries, blending genre elements with deep psychological tension. Films like ‘Raw’ (2016) and ‘High Life’ (2018) already show her comfort with body horror and existential dread, making ‘The Soap Maker’ a natural fit for her style. The script, still under wraps, is rumored to explore themes of survival, morality, and the grotesque underbelly of human nature. If Denis’ past work is any indication, the film will mix visceral imagery with slow-burning tension rather than outright shock value.

The project’s journey from pitch to production has been a slow one. After its debut at EFM in 2025, the film struggled to find financing and producers willing to take on its controversial subject matter. Denis’ reputation as an auteur helped, but the unconventional premise likely kept some investors cautious. Now, with Ben Harroche and Freeman-Attwood on board, the project has a clearer path forward. Both producers have worked on films that balance artistic vision with commercial appeal, which could help ‘The Soap Maker’ find its audience.

## What we know about the plot and themes
While details remain scarce, ‘The Soap Maker’ is expected to center on a crime that leads to cannibalism, possibly involving a soap-making business as a cover or metaphor. Denis has spoken in past interviews about her fascination with how people justify extreme actions, and this film seems to lean into that theme. The title itself suggests a dark twist—soap is often associated with cleanliness, but the film’s premise flips that idea on its head. Expect a story that’s as much about human nature as it is about crime.

Denis has also hinted that the film will explore the idea of transformation. In her films, characters often undergo physical or psychological changes that reflect their inner turmoil. ‘The Soap Maker’ could follow a similar path, with the protagonist—or antagonist—facing a transformation that’s both literal and metaphorical. The cannibalism angle isn’t just about horror; it’s a tool to examine how far people will go when pushed to their limits.

## The producers and their role
Sacha Ben Harroche, known for producing films like ‘Butterfly Jam’ and ‘The Wasp Network,’ has a track record of backing projects with strong visual styles and international appeal. Ivy Freeman-Attwood, who produced ‘The Dispute’ and other indie films, brings a keen eye for character-driven stories. Together, they’re likely to help Denis navigate the tricky balance between artistic integrity and marketability. Their involvement suggests the film won’t just be a niche art-house project—it’s aiming for a broader reach.

The producers’ first step will likely be securing financing and finalizing the cast. Denis has worked with actors like Robert Pattinson and Juliette Binoche in the past, but ‘The Soap Maker’ could attract a different kind of talent given its genre-blending nature. The film’s tone—part crime thriller, part horror, part psychological drama—means the cast will need to deliver performances that can handle both subtlety and intensity.

## What happens next
With producers now attached, ‘The Soap Maker’ is expected to enter pre-production soon. That means casting, location scouting, and budget finalization are likely underway. Denis has said in interviews that she prefers to shoot in real locations rather than studios, which could mean filming in France or another European country with a gritty, industrial feel. The film’s dark themes might also push it toward a late 2026 or 2027 release, giving it time to build buzz without overshadowing Denis’ other projects.

For fans of Denis’ work, ‘The Soap Maker’ is shaping up to be another provocative entry in her filmography. If it delivers on the promise of its premise, it could become one of her most talked-about films yet. But even if it doesn’t, its very existence proves that a director with Denis’ vision can still find ways to make bold, unconventional cinema in today’s industry.
