Russian President [Vladimir Putin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir_Putin) will meet Chinese leader [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping) in Beijing next week for two days of talks, the Kremlin said Saturday. The visit comes just days after US President [Donald Trump](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Trump)’s state visit to China, which concluded Friday with high-profile ceremonies but little progress on key issues.

## Putin-Xi talks set after Trump’s China visit
Putin’s trip to Beijing marks a rare high-level meeting between the two leaders since Russia’s invasion of Ukraine. The Kremlin did not disclose the agenda but said economic cooperation and regional security would be discussed. Analysts expect the Ukraine war and US-Israeli tensions to dominate conversations after Trump’s visit failed to resolve trade disputes or geopolitical conflicts.

Trump’s four-day trip to China included meetings with Xi and other top officials but produced no major trade agreements. The US president touted billions in new deals, though details remained vague and no binding contracts were signed. The visit also highlighted ongoing US-China tensions over technology transfers, market access and military posturing in the Pacific.

## Russia-China ties deepen amid global tensions
Putin and Xi have strengthened ties since 2022, aligning on opposition to US influence and support for each other’s policies. China has backed Russia’s stance on Ukraine while avoiding direct military support. The two leaders last met in March 2023 on the sidelines of a regional summit in Moscow, where they reaffirmed their partnership.

The upcoming talks in Beijing follow a pattern of high-level diplomacy between Russia and China as both nations face pressure from Western sanctions and diplomatic isolation. Russia has relied on China for trade, technology and diplomatic cover since its invasion of Ukraine, while China has used the partnership to counter US influence in Asia.

## Global reactions to Trump’s China visit
Trump’s visit included a state dinner and a speech to Chinese students, but no major policy shifts emerged. US officials described the trip as productive, while critics noted the absence of concrete trade resolutions. The visit also overshadowed ongoing US-Israeli tensions following a recent escalation in the Middle East.

Analysts say the Putin-Xi meeting could signal a renewed push by both leaders to present a united front against Western pressure. The talks may also address China’s role in mediating the Ukraine war or Russia’s economic reliance on Beijing. No joint statements were announced ahead of the visit.

Experts warn that while the meeting could strengthen Russia-China ties, it may also deepen rifts with the West. The US and its allies have repeatedly called on China to pressure Russia to end its war in Ukraine, but Beijing has resisted taking a harder line. The next round of sanctions or trade measures could hinge on the outcome of these talks.
