Bulgaria’s Dara made history Saturday night in Liverpool, taking the Eurovision Song Contest crown with a record-smashing 685 points. The 22-year-old singer’s victory marks the first time Bulgaria has ever won the world’s biggest live music competition. Dara’s winning song, “Echo of the Heart,” dominated both jury and public votes, securing a 125-point lead over runner-up Switzerland. The final score shattered the previous record set by Måneskin in 2021, when Italy won with 524 points.\n\nDara’s win was no fluke. She led the leaderboard after every round of voting, including the semi-finals, where Bulgaria topped the charts with 270 points. Her performance in Liverpool’s M&S Bank Arena drew a massive home crowd, with Bulgarian flags waving and fans cheering every note. Judges praised her powerful vocals and stage presence, while viewers at home couldn’t stop talking about her energy.\n\n## How Bulgaria pulled off the win
The win didn’t come easy. Bulgaria’s Eurovision journey started in 2005, but this was its first top-five finish. Dara’s team worked for months on her song, choreography, and visuals, aiming to stand out in a field of 25 other acts. Her rehearsals in Liverpool drew big crowds, and her social media following exploded overnight as fans backed her to win.\n\nSwitzerland’s Nemo finished second with 560 points, scoring big with their gender-bending performance and electronic pop sound. Sweden’s Smash Into Pieces took third place with 474 points, while Ukraine and Israel rounded out the top five. The top 10 also included powerhouses like France and Italy, but Bulgaria’s underdog story stole the show.\n
## What happens next for Dara
Dara’s win guarantees her a spot in next year’s contest as a favorite, but the immediate aftermath is already busy. She’ll perform at major music festivals across Europe, and her newfound fame could open doors in the U.S. and Asia. Eurovision’s organizers, the EBU, already announced that next year’s contest will be held in a Scandinavian country, likely Sweden, after their third-place finish this year.\n\nFor Bulgaria, the win is a huge morale boost. The country hasn’t hosted a major international event like this in years, and Dara’s victory could put it on the map as a rising star in European pop. Fans are already calling for the government to invest more in music education and industry support.\n
The Liverpool crowd erupted when Dara’s score was announced, and the city’s mayor called it a “dream come true” for the UK’s Eurovision community. Dara’s team told reporters they didn’t expect to win by such a big margin. “We just wanted to do our best,” Dara said. “This feels like a dream, but it’s real.”
