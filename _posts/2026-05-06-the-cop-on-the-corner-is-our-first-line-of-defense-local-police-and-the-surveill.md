A routine traffic stop in Wilmington, Delaware, turned into a major counterterrorism operation after New Castle County police discovered a detailed campus attack plot during a property check at Canby Park on November 24, 2025. Officers found a white Toyota Tacoma parked after hours near the University of Delaware’s Wilmington campus, a location not typically associated with nighttime activity. When they approached the vehicle, they detained a 22-year-old student who was in possession of a converted machine gun, more than 100 rounds of ammunition, body armor, and a handwritten notebook outlining a planned attack on the campus police department.

The suspect, whose identity has not been released, had mapped entry points, escape routes, and even named a specific officer for potential targeting. The FBI took custody of the suspect after local police secured the scene. Investigators described the notebook as containing meticulous details of the planned assault, including timelines and tactical considerations. The weapons seized included an AR-15-style rifle modified to fire fully automatically, a configuration that requires federal registration and is illegal for civilian possession without proper permits.

The case has raised concerns about local law enforcement’s ability to detect surveillance activities that precede violent attacks. Experts warn that while federal agencies like the FBI and [Homeland Security](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Homeland_Security) have sophisticated monitoring capabilities, local police often lack the resources to identify suspicious behavior before it escalates. The Wilmington incident underscores the challenges faced by smaller departments in detecting potential threats amid routine patrols.

## Police response and investigation timeline

New Castle County police initiated the check at 11:58 p.m. as part of standard property patrols in Canby Park, a public space adjacent to the university’s satellite campus. Officers reported no initial suspicion beyond the vehicle’s location and time of parking. Upon approaching the Tacoma, they observed the suspect attempting to conceal items inside the vehicle. A search revealed the weapons and notebook, prompting an immediate call to Delaware State Police and the FBI’s Joint Terrorism Task Force.

The FBI’s [Philadelphia Field Office](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FBI_Philadelphia_Field_Office) took lead on the investigation, with agents noting the suspect had researched attack methodologies online and had visited the campus multiple times in the weeks prior. Surveillance footage from nearby businesses confirmed his presence during reconnaissance activities. Authorities have not disclosed whether the suspect acted alone or had connections to broader networks.

## Gaps in local surveillance detection

The incident has highlighted a critical gap in local law enforcement’s ability to detect pre-attack surveillance. Unlike federal agencies, municipal police departments often lack access to advanced surveillance tools or behavioral analysis training. The Wilmington case suggests that even routine patrols can uncover significant threats if officers remain vigilant about unusual activity. However, experts argue that without enhanced training and interagency coordination, many plots may go undetected until it’s too late.

Local officials have begun reviewing their surveillance detection protocols in the wake of the arrest. Wilmington Police Chief [Robert Tracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilmington,_Delaware) stated that while his department maintains strong community policing efforts, they are reevaluating how to better integrate threat detection into daily patrols. Delaware’s congressional delegation has also requested a briefing from federal authorities on improving local counterterrorism partnerships.

The suspect remains in federal custody without bond. The FBI continues to analyze digital evidence recovered from his electronic devices, including encrypted communications that may reveal additional details about his planning process. The University of Delaware has increased security measures around its Wilmington campus, including additional patrols and visible police presence. No charges have been filed publicly as of this report, as the investigation remains active.
