U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM) confirmed today that American forces disabled two Iranian oil tankers attempting to break a U.S. blockade near the Strait of Hormuz. The strikes targeted the M/T Sea Star III and M/T Sevda, both of which were stopped before entering an Iranian port in the Gulf of Oman. A U.S. Navy F/A-18 Super Hornet from the USS George H.W. Bush (CVN 77) fired precision munitions into the vessels' smokestacks, rendering them inoperable and preventing their entry into Iran.

CENTCOM released video footage of the strikes, showing the moments before and after the aircraft targeted the tankers. The operation occurred amid escalating tensions in the region, with the U.S. and Iran exchanging fire earlier in and around the Strait of Hormuz. The Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for global oil shipments, has been a flashpoint for recent military confrontations between the two nations.

The UAE also reported another attack by Iran today, adding to the growing list of incidents in the region. This follows a pattern of Iranian aggression in the Gulf, including drone strikes and attempted seizures of commercial vessels. The U.S., which has maintained a naval presence to enforce its blockade, has warned Iran against violating international maritime laws.

## Iran-U.S. tensions escalate in Strait of Hormuz

The latest strikes come just hours after the U.S. and Iran engaged in direct confrontations near the Strait of Hormuz, a vital shipping lane for global oil supplies. Iran has accused the U.S. of provoking tensions, while Washington has framed its actions as necessary to counter Iranian threats to regional stability. The Strait of Hormuz has seen multiple incidents in recent months, including drone attacks, missile strikes, and seizures of commercial ships by both sides.

CENTCOM stated that the targeted tankers were in violation of the U.S. blockade, which aims to prevent Iran from exporting oil in defiance of international sanctions. The agency emphasized that the strikes were conducted to enforce existing sanctions and maintain regional security. The operation reflects a broader U.S. strategy to counter Iranian maritime aggression and protect freedom of navigation in the Gulf.

## International response to rising tensions

The international community has expressed concern over the escalating tensions in the Strait of Hormuz. The UAE, a key U.S. ally in the region, condemned Iran's latest attack and reiterated its support for freedom of navigation. European nations have also urged restraint, emphasizing the need for diplomatic solutions to avoid further escalation.

Analysts warn that continued military confrontations could disrupt global oil supplies and destabilize the region. The Strait of Hormuz sees roughly 20% of the world's oil pass through its waters daily, making it a critical economic artery. Any disruption could have far-reaching consequences for global energy markets and geopolitical stability.

The U.S. has not signaled plans for additional strikes but has warned Iran against further provocations. CENTCOM's statement suggests that future actions will depend on Iran's adherence to international maritime laws and its compliance with existing sanctions. Meanwhile, the UAE and other regional partners continue to monitor the situation closely as tensions remain high.
