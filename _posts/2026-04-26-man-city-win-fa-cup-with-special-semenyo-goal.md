Manchester City claimed the FA Cup with a dramatic stoppage-time winner from Antoine Semenyo, who beat Chelsea 1-0 at Wembley on Saturday. The winger, who joined City from Bournemouth for £64 million in January, produced the decisive moment in the 91st minute, flicking in a cross from Erling Haaland to secure the trophy. The goal capped a resilient display from City, who struggled to break down Chelsea’s disciplined defense for much of the match.

Chelsea had chances to equalize early on, including a header from Moises Caicedo that was cleared off the line by Rodri, and a volley from Enzo Fernández that rattled the crossbar. However, both teams were unable to find a breakthrough until Semenyo’s decisive intervention. The result means Chelsea miss out on a Europa League spot, which would have been guaranteed with an FA Cup win, and leaves them fighting for a top-seven finish in the Premier League.

For City, the victory adds to their Carabao Cup triumph in March, completing a domestic double under manager Pep Guardiola. The Spanish coach now has 20 major trophies in his time at the club, continuing his legacy as one of the most successful managers in English football history. City’s players celebrated on the pitch at Wembley, though their focus now shifts to the Premier League title race, where an Arsenal slip-up could still hand them the championship.

## Chelsea’s frustration continues at Wembley
Chelsea’s interim manager, Calum McFarlane, oversaw a frustrating afternoon at Wembley, with his team unable to capitalize on their limited chances. The Blues had earlier seen Caicedo’s header cleared by Rodri, and Fernández’s effort hit the roof of the net, but they were unable to find a way past City goalkeeper Ederson. Chelsea are now left with a top-seven finish as their only remaining objective in the Premier League, with a Conference League spot also within reach.

Semenyo, who has been a revelation since his move from Bournemouth, provided the spark City needed after back-to-back FA Cup final losses. His goal was the fifth time in five straight Wembley finals that no team scored in the first half, highlighting the defensive solidity on display. City finished strongly, with Matheus Nunes hitting the post and Rayan Cherki’s shot saved by Robert Sánchez, but it was Semenyo who delivered the winning moment in stoppage time.

Guardiola, who has overseen a period of dominance for City, will now turn his attention to the Premier League title race. Arsenal currently lead the table, but City’s victory in the FA Cup adds to their confidence as they push for a historic quadruple. The club’s supporters celebrated at Wembley, while Chelsea’s fans left disappointed after another major final defeat. For City, the focus remains on adding more silverware to their collection in what has been a remarkable season.
