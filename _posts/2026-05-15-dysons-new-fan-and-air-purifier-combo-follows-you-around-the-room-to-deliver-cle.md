Dyson just dropped a gadget that actually pays attention to where you are. The Find+Follow is a tower-style fan and air purifier that rolls around the room, tracks your movement with AI, and aims its airflow directly at you. It’s the first device to merge cooling, heating, and purification into one moving unit, not just a stationary tower that wobbles side to side like old-school oscillating fans. Think of it as a personal climate bubble that follows you instead of making you chase the best breeze.

The tech behind it isn’t subtle. Six built-in sensors—including a 360-degree camera and laser sensors—map your room in real time and predict where you’ll move next. Dyson says the AI learns your habits over a few days, so it doesn’t just react to your current spot. If you stand up from your desk to grab water, it’ll adjust before you even sit back down. The company calls this "3D tracking," and it’s designed to end the daily fight over the couch spot closest to the fan.

## How it actually works in your home

The Find+Follow isn’t just a fancy fan with wheels. It has a HEPA filter that traps 99.95% of particles as small as 0.1 microns, so it’s also an air purifier. That means it pulls double duty: cooling you down while cleaning the air you breathe. The fan side has 20 fan blades angled to push air in a tight jet stream, not the wide sweep you’d get from a regular fan. Dyson claims it can cool a room 5°C faster than a standard fan while using 30% less energy. The heater mode does the opposite—it directs warm air downward to avoid heating the ceiling.

Don’t expect silent operation. Dyson rates the fan noise at 57 decibels on high, which is about the volume of a normal conversation. On low, it drops to 35 decibels, roughly the hum of a quiet library. The purifier side adds a slight white noise when running, but it’s not the loud drone of some older Dyson models. Battery life is the big caveat: it needs to stay plugged in. Dyson says they’re working on a battery pack for future versions, but for now, it’s tethered to the wall.

## What it costs and when you can buy it

Dyson’s pulling no punches on price. The Find+Follow starts at $749 for the black or white version, with the Steel model topping out at $849. That lands it squarely in the "premium appliance" category, where it’ll compete with standalone Dyson fans and purifiers that cost less but don’t move. Preorders start today, and it ships in July 2025. The company’s betting that people tired of arguing over the thermostat or fanning themselves in the same spot all summer will pay the premium for convenience.

## Why this matters beyond the hype

This isn’t just another smart gadget for tech bros. Dyson’s move here reflects a real shift in how we interact with our homes. We’ve gotten used to devices that respond to voice commands or phone apps, but very few actually track us in 3D space. The Find+Follow is testing whether people will trade privacy for comfort. The camera and sensors run locally on the device, so your data doesn’t leave your home, but it’s still a camera watching your living room. For some, that’s a dealbreaker. For others, it’s the price of never having to manually adjust a fan again.

The bigger picture? If this works, expect every major appliance brand to roll out similar tech. Picture a robot vacuum that also cools your kitchen or a microwave that follows you around with its steam vent. Dyson’s not inventing new tech here—they’re repackaging existing sensors into a product that finally makes sense. The question is whether the $749 price tag will convince enough people to take the leap before cheaper imitators flood the market.
