In his closing remarks, Cooper didn’t just say goodbye—he saluted the program itself, calling out the qualities that have kept it relevant for generations of viewers. His parting message was simple: he hopes 60 Minutes stays exactly as it is, holding onto the editorial independence and trust it has earned over decades. The words carried extra weight coming from a reporter who built much of his career on the show’s platform, where he became one of the most recognizable faces in broadcast journalism.## Anderson Cooper’s career on 60 Minutes began in 2003, and he quickly became a fixture on the Sunday night lineup. Over the years, he covered some of the biggest stories of the 21st century, from natural disasters like Hurricane Katrina to political upheavals like the 2016 election. His reporting often took him to war zones and disaster sites, where he balanced empathy with precision. For Cooper, that independence was the show’s defining trait. In his farewell, he made it clear he saw that commitment as rare and worth protecting, especially in an era where media credibility is constantly questioned.## Cooper’s exit leaves a gap on 60 Minutes, but it also marks the end of an era for a generation of viewers who grew up watching him deliver the news.
