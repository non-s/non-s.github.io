A New York jury failed to reach a unanimous verdict Friday in Harvey Weinstein's third sex crimes trial, marking the second mistrial in a year on the same rape charge brought by Jessica Mann. The deliberations lasted three days before jurors notified Judge Curtis Farber they could not reach a decision. Farber then issued a modified deadlock instruction, known as an Allen charge, urging jurors to resume talks. They quickly responded with another note stating their position: "We feel that no one is going to change where they stand." Nine jurors voted not guilty while three supported a guilty verdict, Weinstein's legal team told reporters outside court. The prosecution now has until late June to decide whether to retry the case. Weinstein, the disgraced former Hollywood mogul, returned to court in April for the third New York trial focused solely on Mann's allegations. Mann, a former aspiring actress, testified Weinstein raped her at a Manhattan DoubleTree hotel in 2013. Weinstein was first convicted of assaulting Mann in 2020, but that verdict and another charge were later overturned on appeal. In a second New York trial last summer, Weinstein was found guilty of one count of criminal sexual act in the first degree but acquitted on another charge; the rape charge ended in mistrial after the jury foreperson cited safety concerns. Outside court, 55-year-old juror Rick Treese said the panel diverged on "where we actually had facts." The case has become a flashpoint in the #MeToo movement, which gained momentum after dozens of women came forward with allegations against Weinstein in 2017. Weinstein's legal troubles stem from accusations spanning decades, with multiple accusers testifying in various trials. The third New York trial narrowed the scope to Mann's claims, but the deadlock suggests persistent divisions over credibility and evidence. Legal experts note that repeated mistrials on the same charge could limit prosecutors' willingness to retry the case, given the emotional toll on witnesses and the resources required. The outcome leaves open whether Mann's allegations will ever be resolved in court, though the case has already left a lasting impact on public discourse about sexual misconduct in Hollywood and beyond. Weinstein remains free on bail pending any decision on a potential retrial.
