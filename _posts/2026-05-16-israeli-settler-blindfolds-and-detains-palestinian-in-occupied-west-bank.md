An Israeli settler blindfolded and detained a Palestinian farmer in the occupied West Bank on Wednesday, according to local sources and video evidence. The incident occurred near the village of Beit Iksa, where the farmer was reportedly attempting to access his agricultural land.

Video footage reviewed by Al Jazeera shows the Israeli settler, armed and wearing civilian clothing, approaching the Palestinian man before placing a blindfold over his eyes. The settler then forced the man onto a nearby road as Israeli soldiers stood within 20 meters, observing without apparent intervention. The Palestinian farmer, identified only by local media as Ahmed Hamdan, 42, from Beit Iksa, said he was heading to his land when the settler confronted him.

## Israeli forces’ role questioned
Israeli human rights group B'Tselem [B'Tselem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/B%27Tselem) released a statement calling the incident a "clear violation of international law," noting that settlers are prohibited from detaining Palestinians under any circumstances. The group also questioned the inaction of Israeli forces present at the scene, who did not intervene despite witnessing the confrontation.

Local Palestinian officials reported that Hamdan was held for over an hour before being released without charge. The Israeli military did not immediately respond to requests for comment regarding the incident or the soldiers’ actions. The Palestinian Authority condemned the detention as an "act of terror" by settlers, demanding immediate accountability.

## Rising settler violence in West Bank
This incident is the latest in a series of escalating confrontations between Israeli settlers and Palestinians in the occupied West Bank. According to the United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA) [OCHA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Office_for_the_Coordination_of_Humanitarian_Affairs), settler-related violence has surged by 50% in the first quarter of 2024 compared to the same period last year. Over 500 incidents of settler violence, including physical assaults, property damage, and forced displacements, have been recorded this year alone.

Beit Iksa, located near Jerusalem, has been a flashpoint for such conflicts. The village is surrounded by several Israeli settlements, which have expanded significantly since the Oslo Accords in the 1990s. Palestinians in the area frequently report restrictions on their movement and access to farmland, often citing harassment or violence from settlers.

## Broader implications of settler actions
Analysts warn that unchecked settler violence, combined with the Israeli military’s inaction, is exacerbating tensions in the West Bank. "When settlers act with impunity and the army turns a blind eye, it sends a message that these actions are tolerated," said Yara Hawari, a policy fellow at Al-Shabaka, a Palestinian policy network. "This creates an environment where more Palestinians face harassment, displacement, and violence."

The incident also raises questions about Israel’s compliance with its obligations under international law, including the Fourth Geneva Convention, which prohibits an occupying power from transferring its civilian population into occupied territory. Over 700,000 Israeli settlers now live in the West Bank and East Jerusalem, areas considered illegal under international law.

## What happens next?
The Palestinian Authority has called for an international investigation into the incident, while Israeli authorities have not announced any plans to investigate the settler’s actions or the soldiers’ inaction. Local activists are demanding the video evidence be used to hold the settler accountable, but similar past incidents have often gone unpunished due to lack of evidence or political will.
