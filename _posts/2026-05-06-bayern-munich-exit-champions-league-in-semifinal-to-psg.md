Paris Saint-Germain have booked their spot in the Champions League final after a dramatic 6-5 aggregate win over Bayern Munich in the semifinals. The French giants, chasing back-to-back titles, will face either Real Madrid or Borussia Dortmund in the final on May 30. For Bayern, it’s another near-miss in Europe’s biggest club competition. The German champions haven’t won the trophy since 2020 and have now lost in the semifinals five times in the last seven years.

The first leg in Paris set the tone. PSG raced to a 5-4 lead after a breathless 90 minutes that’s already being called one of the greatest games in Champions League history. Bayern fought back from 2-0 down in the second leg at the Allianz Arena, but it wasn’t enough. Ousmane Dembele, the former Borussia Dortmund winger, put PSG ahead in the third minute with a clinical finish after Khvicha Kvaratskhelia carved open Bayern’s defense. The speed of the goal stunned the home crowd, and PSG never looked back.

## A controversial moment that changed the game

Bayern nearly equalized before halftime, but their frustration grew when referee Michael Oliver awarded PSG a handball against Konrad Laimer. The decision was hotly disputed, with video replays showing the ball hitting Laimer’s shoulder from close range. Bayern coach Vincent Kompany, usually calm under pressure, lost his temper, gesturing angrily toward the officials. PSG’s left-back Nuno Mendes escaped a second yellow card for a similar challenge, adding to the tension.

The second half saw Bayern pile forward, but PSG’s counterattacks were too sharp. Dembele added a second goal in the 67th minute, sealing the win. Bayern pulled one back late through Jamal Musiala, but it was too little, too late. The final whistle sparked wild celebrations in Paris, while Bayern’s players slumped to the turf in disbelief.

## What’s next for Bayern and PSG?

For PSG, the focus shifts to the final. They’ll be favorites to lift the trophy, especially if [Real Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF) wins their semifinal. PSG’s attack, led by [Kylian Mbappé](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kylian_Mbapp%C3%A9) and [Dembele](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ousmane_Dembele), has been unstoppable this season. But they’ll need to stay sharp against a team that’s likely exhausted after their own semifinal battle.

Bayern, meanwhile, can still salvage something from the season. They lead the Bundesliga and will face [Bayer Leverkusen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen) in the German Cup final. A domestic double would soften the blow of another Champions League exit. Kompany will also be under pressure to rebuild for next season, with questions already swirling about Bayern’s aging squad and tactical approach.

The loss marks another chapter in Bayern’s Champions League struggles. Since their last triumph in 2020, they’ve been knocked out in the quarterfinals four times. This time, they at least pushed the holders hard, but their European pedigree is taking a hit. For PSG, the final is another chance to silence critics who question their ability to win the big one. They’ve never lifted the trophy, but this season, they’re closer than ever.
