## Introduction to the Case A federal jury in Chicago awarded $49.5 million to the family of Samya Stumo, a young woman who was killed in the second of two Boeing 737 MAX crashes within months of each other in 2018 and 2019. The verdict resolves one of the last remaining cases stemming from two deadly crashes that killed a total of 346 people and happened within months of each other. Samya Stumo was 24 years old when she died in the second 737 MAX crash. ## Background of the Crash Boeing had already admitted responsibility for the crash, so the trial was only about how much the company should pay in compensatory damages. The family of Samya Stumo pushed for the case, with her mother, Nadia Milleron, stating that her daughter got on the plane completely trusting and never dreamed that there would be any problem with the plane itself. The crash of Ethiopian Airlines Flight 302 in 2019 was a devastating event that raised questions about the safety of the Boeing 737 MAX. ## Aftermath and Implications The verdict is a significant development in the ongoing saga surrounding the Boeing 737 MAX crashes. The company has faced intense scrutiny and criticism for its handling of the situation, and this ruling may have implications for future cases. The family of Samya Stumo hopes that the verdict will bring some measure of closure and justice, and that it will also serve as a reminder of the importance of prioritizing safety in the aviation industry.
