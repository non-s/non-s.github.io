Two US Navy [F/A-18 Super Hornets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McDonnell_Douglas_F/A-18_Hornet) collided Saturday afternoon during a demonstration at the [Patuxent River Air Expo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naval_Air_Station_Patuxent_River) in [Maryland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maryland). The jets were part of the Navy’s flight demonstration team and were performing a routine maneuver when the accident occurred just after 3 p.m. local time. The pilots from both aircraft managed to eject before impact, landing nearby and were quickly recovered by emergency crews on the scene. No one on the ground was injured, according to officials with [Naval Air Station Patuxent River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naval_Air_Station_Patuxent_River). The base has launched an investigation into the cause of the collision. Both aircraft were completely destroyed in the crash, leaving only scattered debris across the crash site. The air show was immediately halted following the incident, and all remaining demonstrations were canceled for the day as a precaution. Local fire and rescue teams secured the area and evacuated nearby spectators. The Navy has not yet released details on the pilots involved or their current medical condition beyond confirming they are all stable and receiving care. The [Patuxent River Air Expo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naval_Air_Station_Patuxent_River) is an annual event that showcases military aviation capabilities and draws thousands of visitors each year. This year’s event featured performances from the Navy’s Blue Angels and other military demonstration teams. Officials say the investigation will focus on whether human error, mechanical failure, or environmental factors contributed to the collision. The Navy has a strong safety record with its jet demonstrations but mid-air collisions, while rare, have happened before during similar events. ## What happened during the collision. The collision occurred as the two [F/A-18 Super Hornets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McDonnell_Douglas_F/A-18_Hornet) were performing a synchronized maneuver. Witnesses described hearing a loud explosion followed by two plumes of smoke spiraling toward the ground. Video footage from the scene shows the jets tumbling out of control before the pilots ejected. The impact point was less than a mile from the main viewing area, but no injuries were reported among spectators. Emergency response teams were on the scene within minutes, and the pilots were taken to nearby hospitals for evaluation. The Navy released a statement late Saturday evening confirming the pilots were all conscious and communicating with medical staff. The statement also thanked local emergency responders for their quick action in securing the scene and ensuring public safety. The investigation is expected to take several weeks, with preliminary findings likely to be shared within days. ## What this means for the air show and military aviation. The accident has raised questions about the safety of large-scale military air demonstrations. The [Patuxent River Air Expo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naval_Air_Station_Patuxent_River) is one of the biggest events of its kind in the region, drawing military personnel, aviation enthusiasts, and families. The Navy has not announced whether future shows will be canceled or modified as a result of the incident. Similar accidents have led to temporary suspensions of flight demonstrations in the past, though none have resulted in fatalities in recent years. Military aviation experts say mid-air collisions are extremely rare but can happen when pilots push the limits of performance. The [F/A-18 Super Hornet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McDonnell_Douglas_F/A-18_Hornet) is a twin-engine multirole fighter used by the Navy for both combat and training. It’s known for its speed, agility, and ability to perform complex aerial maneuvers. The pilots who fly these jets undergo years of rigorous training to handle high-stakes situations like this one. The accident comes just months after another close call during a [Red Bull Air Race](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Bull_Air_Race) event in the United States, where a plane clipped a barrier but the pilot landed safely. ## What happens next. The Navy has formed a mishap investigation board to determine the cause of the collision. This board includes pilots, engineers, and safety experts who will review flight data, witness statements, and video footage. The pilots involved will also be interviewed as part of the process. The board’s findings could lead to changes in training protocols, flight procedures, or even design modifications to the aircraft. Meanwhile, the [Patuxent River Air Expo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naval_Air_Station_Patuxent_River) has canceled its remaining events for the weekend, and organizers are assessing whether to proceed with future shows. Local officials have praised the swift response of emergency teams and credited their preparation for preventing further harm. The Navy has not yet released the names of the pilots involved, citing privacy concerns. Family and friends of the crew have been notified and are being kept updated on their conditions. The broader aviation community will be watching the investigation closely, as the findings could impact how military and civilian air shows operate in the future.
