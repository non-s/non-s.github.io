A new wellbeing garden at Yarm Wellness [Yarm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yarm) will open to the public for the first time this Saturday, offering a free space for outdoor activities aimed at reducing isolation. The outdoor area, part of the Yarm Wellness initiative, will be accessible from 9 a.m. daily, with no admission fee required. Officials say the garden is designed to provide a calming environment for locals to relax, socialize or participate in light exercise programs.

The garden’s launch comes amid growing concerns about loneliness in the UK, particularly among older adults. Local health leaders highlighted the project as a practical step to improve mental wellbeing in the community. Yarm Wellness, a community-focused health hub, has partnered with local councils to fund and develop the space. Volunteers will be on hand during opening hours to guide visitors and organize group activities.

## Features include walking paths, seating areas and small gardening plots

Visitors to the new garden will find landscaped walking trails, shaded seating areas and designated plots for community gardening. Organizers have included quiet zones for reflection and benches positioned to encourage social interaction. The garden also features native plants to support local wildlife, adding an environmental benefit to its social mission. Plans are in place to expand the space with seasonal flower beds and outdoor fitness equipment by next spring.

Yarm Wellness director Sarah Thompson said the garden was designed to be inclusive. "We wanted a space that everyone could use, whether you’re looking for a quiet place to read or a spot to meet friends," she said. Thompson noted that the project had received strong support from local residents during planning. The garden will remain open daily, with staff available to answer questions and assist visitors.

## Officials urge visitors to check local guidelines before arrival

While entry is free, organizers are asking visitors to review current health and safety notices before visiting. Signs will be posted at the entrance with guidelines on social distancing and hygiene. The garden will close at dusk, with lighting installed for early evening visits. Thompson confirmed that the space would be maintained year-round, with winter maintenance scheduled to keep paths clear and safe.

The project adds to a growing number of community gardens across the UK aimed at improving public health. Similar spaces in nearby towns have reported increased foot traffic and positive feedback from users. Yarm Wellness plans to track visitor numbers and gather feedback to guide future improvements. Long-term, the organization hopes to expand the concept to other locations if the project proves successful.
