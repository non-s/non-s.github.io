## Introduction to the Crisis: The number of internal displacements triggered by conflict or violence around the world reached a record high in 2025, surpassing the number of disaster-driven internal displacements for the first time. A report published by the [Internal Displacement Monitoring Centre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internal_Displacement_Monitoring_Centre) shows that by the end of 2025 there were 32.3m conflict-driven internal displacements. That is 60% higher than those recorded the previous year, and – for the first time since data collection began in 2008 – above displacements driven by natural disasters, which reached 29.9m in 2025. ## The Global Impact: The total number of displaced people around the world is now at 82.2m, with the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations) and other organizations working to provide aid and support to those affected. The increase in conflict-driven displacements is a cause for concern, as it highlights the ongoing violence and instability in many parts of the world. ## Response and Support: The [International Committee of the Red Cross](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Committee_of_the_Red_Cross) and other humanitarian organizations are working to provide assistance to those displaced by conflict or disaster. The international community is being called upon to provide support and funding to help address the growing crisis of internal displacement.
