Lainey Wilson didn’t slow down for love—or the ACM Awards. The reigning [Entertainer of the Year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ACM_Awards) took the stage at the 2026 ceremony in Las Vegas to belt out her new single ‘Can’t Sit Still,’ just days after marrying [Caelan Morrison](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caelan_Morrison) in a surprise ceremony in [Franklin, Tennessee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franklin,_Tennessee). The performance was her first major public appearance since tying the knot, and she didn’t just show up—she tore through the number like it was 2019 all over again.

Wilson, who’s been on a tear since winning Entertainer of the Year in 2025, looked every bit the star with her signature blonde curls and a sequined dress that caught every camera flash. The song itself is a high-energy anthem of restless energy, perfectly matching the vibe of her recent singles like ‘Watermelon Moonshine’ and ‘Things a Man Oughta Know.’ Band members traded instruments mid-song, backup dancers spun in coordinated chaos, and Wilson herself paced the stage like she was trying to outrun the spotlight. The crowd erupted when she hit the final chorus, proving she hasn’t lost a step.

Backstage, Wilson told reporters she wrote the song before her wedding but finished it after tying the knot. ‘It’s about that feeling when life’s moving too fast to sit still,’ she said, grinning. ‘Turns out, marriage doesn’t slow you down—it just gives you more to chase.’ The single drops Friday, but the ACM performance gave fans a taste of what’s to come.

The ACM Awards have been good to Wilson. Last year’s Entertainer of the Year win cemented her as country music’s breakout star, and her 2026 performance only reinforced it. Judges praised her ability to blend raw energy with polished showmanship, a rare combo in today’s scene. One insider noted, ‘She’s not just a star—she’s a whole production.’ The performance also gave her team a chance to showcase the new visuals for ‘Can’t Sit Still,’ which drops Friday with a star-studded music video featuring [Kelsea Ballerini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelsea_Ballerini) and [Kane Brown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kane_Brown).

## Wilson’s year keeps getting bigger
Just weeks before the ACM Awards, Wilson headlined [Stagecoach Festival](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stagecoach_Festival) in Palm Springs, drawing a record crowd for the country-focused event. Ticket sales for her upcoming tour are already in the six-figure range, and pre-sale demand crashed websites within minutes. She’s also inked a new endorsement deal with [Coca-Cola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Coca-Cola_Company), her first major non-country partnership.

The timing of ‘Can’t Sit Still’ feels intentional. Wilson’s been vocal about her refusal to take a break, even after her wedding. ‘I don’t do downtime,’ she told *Rolling Stone* last month. ‘If I’m not moving, I’m not living.’ The song’s lyrics—‘I got a heart full of fire and a head full of dreams’—echo that fire, making it a clear statement that her personal life won’t slow down her career.

For Wilson, the ACM Awards weren’t just about the performance. They were a reminder that she’s not just a country star—she’s a brand. From her viral TikTok dances to her sold-out tour dates, she’s building an empire while keeping her music front and center. And if ‘Can’t Sit Still’ is any indication, she’s just getting started.
