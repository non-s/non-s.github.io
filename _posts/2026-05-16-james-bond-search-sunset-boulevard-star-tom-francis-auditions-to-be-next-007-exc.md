A 26-year-old British actor has thrown his hat into the ring to become the next James Bond. Tom Francis, best known for his stage performance in Jamie Lloyd’s unconventional revival of Andrew Lloyd Webber’s Sunset Boulevard, auditioned for the iconic spy role, a source close to the 007 search tells Variety. Francis, who played the part opposite Nicole Scherzinger in the West End production, joins a growing list of actors vying to take over the tuxedo after Daniel Craig’s 15-year run as 007 ended in 2021.


Francis isn’t the first stage actor to eye the Bond role. Previous contenders have included theater heavyweights like Tom Hiddleston and Aidan Turner, both of whom were rumored to be in the mix for earlier Bond films. But Francis’s audition marks a fresh twist: he’s a relative unknown to mainstream audiences, which could make him a bold choice for the franchise. The Bond team has historically favored actors with some Hollywood recognition, though exceptions like Daniel Craig—who was relatively unknown in the U.S. before his casting—have shaken up the formula.
##

The search for 007’s successor has become one of Hollywood’s most closely watched casting dramas. The next Bond film, officially titled *Bond 25*, is in production with a planned 2025 release date, though no lead actor has been announced. Reports suggest the studio is considering a younger actor to refresh the franchise, with names like Regé-Jean Page, Aaron Taylor-Johnson, and even Tom Holland floating around. Francis’s audition adds a wildcard to the mix: a stage-trained actor who could bring a fresh intensity to the role.


If Francis lands the part, he’d be the youngest actor to play Bond—beating Pierce Brosnan’s record by five years. But his lack of film experience might be a hurdle. The Bond franchise has always leaned on leading men with charisma and physicality, and Francis’s stage background is a strength in that regard. His performance in Sunset Boulevard reportedly wowed critics, proving he can carry a demanding role night after night—a skill that could translate well to the grueling schedule of a Bond film.
##

The casting process has reportedly included screen tests with multiple actors, some of whom are already filming other projects while waiting for a decision. The delay has fueled speculation that the studio is taking its time to find the right fit, not just the right face. Francis’s audition suggests the Bond team is still open to unconventional choices, especially if they bring something new to the character.


What happens next? The studio will likely narrow down the list over the coming months, with final decisions made by late 2024. If Francis isn’t chosen, he’ll likely return to the stage or pursue other film roles—he’s already set to star in the upcoming BBC drama *The Long Shadow*. But for now, the 26-year-old is one step closer to living the Bond fantasy, even if it’s just for an audition.
