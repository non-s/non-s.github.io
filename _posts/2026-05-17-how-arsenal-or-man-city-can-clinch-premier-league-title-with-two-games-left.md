The Premier League title race is down to its final two games, and Arsenal [Arsenal F.C.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arsenal_F.C.) are clinging to a slender two-point lead over Manchester City [Manchester City F.C.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C.). The Gunners hold the advantage, but City are breathing down their necks after winning 17 of their last 19 league matches. The season’s final week starts Monday night when Arsenal face Brighton at the Emirates, followed by City’s clash with West Ham on Tuesday. Both clubs then wrap up their campaigns next Sunday, May 24, with Arsenal visiting Liverpool and City hosting Aston Villa.


The current gap is small but meaningful. Arsenal have 82 points to City’s 80, but the Citizens have a +49 goal difference compared to Arsenal’s +48. That one-goal edge means City can still take the title if they finish level on points—and it all comes down to how the goal difference swings. If both teams end level on points, Arsenal would need to overtake City on goal difference, or goals scored, or head-to-head results before the title goes to the Citizens.


## How Arsenal can win the title

Arsenal need just one point from their final two games to secure the Premier League trophy. A win over Brighton on Monday would put them on 85 points—three clear of City—if City don’t win their game on Tuesday. If City also win on Tuesday, Arsenal would need to avoid defeat at Liverpool on Sunday to finish with 85 or more points. That’s because City would then be on 83 points, leaving Arsenal three points ahead. Anything less than a draw for Arsenal would put the pressure back on, forcing them to chase a win at Anfield to stay clear.


City’s recent form gives them a psychological edge, but Arsenal have been the more consistent side all season. Mikel Arteta [Mikel Arteta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mikel_Arteta)’s side has lost just four league games all campaign, while Pep Guardiola [Pep Guardiola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pep_Guard_iola)’s City have dropped six. The Gunners have also been sharper in front of goal, scoring 81 goals to City’s 78. If they keep their nerve in these final games, they could finally end their 22-year trophy drought.


## How City can still win the title

Manchester City still have a mathematical path to the title even with Arsenal two points clear. The simplest route is for City to win both their final games—Tuesday against West Ham and next Sunday against Aston Villa—while Arsenal lose both of theirs. That would put City on 84 points, with Arsenal stuck on 82. If City finish with the same points as Arsenal, their superior goal difference would hand them the title. City’s attack has been relentless under Guardiola, with Erling Haaland [Erling Haaland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erling_Haaland) leading the line, scoring 27 league goals this season.


A less straightforward path involves City finishing level on points and goal difference. In that case, the league looks at goals scored next. Arsenal have scored 81 goals so far; City have 78. If both teams finish with 82 points and a +49 goal difference, Arsenal would win on goals scored. That’s why City need to keep scoring freely. If they can’t overtake Arsenal on goal difference, the next tiebreaker is head-to-head points, where Arsenal currently lead 2-0 after last season’s double. Even that could flip if City take maximum points from their remaining games.


## What happens if it’s a three-way tie?

While unlikely, there’s a scenario where Arsenal, City, and Liverpool—currently third with 73 points—could all finish within a few points of each other. But with Liverpool 11 points behind Arsenal, they’d need an improbable combination of results to force a three-way tie. Even then, the title would come down to the same tiebreakers: goal difference, goals scored, and head-to-head. No team has won the Premier League on away goals in head-to-head matches since the rule was introduced, so the pressure would be on clean wins in the final games.


The final matches are set to be tense. Arsenal’s trip to Liverpool is always tricky, and City’s clash with Aston Villa could be their sternest test in recent weeks. Villa have beaten City twice this season and will be desperate to spoil their rivals’ party. Meanwhile, Brighton and West Ham—both fighting relegation—will push for points knowing their own survival depends on it. The stage is set for a dramatic finish to a season that has kept fans on the edge of their seats.
