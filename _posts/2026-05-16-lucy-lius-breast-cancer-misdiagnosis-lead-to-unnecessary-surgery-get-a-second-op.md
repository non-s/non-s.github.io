Award-winning actress [Lucy Liu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lucy_Liu) has shared how a breast cancer misdiagnosis at 22 led to unnecessary surgery in 1991. The lump in her breast was later confirmed noncancerous, but the experience left a lasting impact on her approach to health care. Speaking to Healthline, Liu described the moment as a turning point in her life, sparking a commitment to advocating for herself and others in medical decisions.


Liu’s revelation comes amid growing awareness of the importance of second opinions in cancer diagnoses. Medical experts increasingly recommend patients seek additional evaluations to confirm test results, particularly for breast cancer screenings. The American Cancer Society notes that while most lumps are benign, diagnostic errors can occur, leading to unnecessary procedures and emotional distress. Liu’s case highlights the potential consequences of misdiagnosis and the need for thorough medical review.


## Misdiagnosis led to surgery Liu still remembers vividly
Liu recalled the lump discovery in 1991, when she was a young actress navigating her early career. After the initial diagnosis and surgery, she learned the lump was not cancerous. Despite the outcome, the experience taught her the value of questioning medical advice and trusting her instincts. "I learned a lot about myself," Liu said. "It was the beginning of advocating for myself, with my health and everything else."


The incident occurred decades before modern advancements in breast cancer detection and treatment. Today, diagnostic tools like mammograms and ultrasounds are more precise, yet misdiagnoses remain a concern in healthcare. Liu’s story underscores the importance of patient empowerment, especially for women who may feel pressured or dismissed in medical settings.


## Liu now advocates for second opinions and cancer screenings
In recent years, Liu has used her platform to encourage others to prioritize their health and seek multiple medical opinions. She emphasizes that advocating for oneself is crucial, particularly when facing potential cancer diagnoses. Her message aligns with trends in patient advocacy, where individuals are encouraged to take an active role in their healthcare decisions.


Healthcare professionals agree that second opinions can prevent unnecessary procedures and provide peace of mind. The [American Cancer Society](https://www.cancer.org) reports that breast cancer remains the most common cancer among women worldwide, with early detection critical to survival. Liu’s experience serves as a reminder of the potential risks of misdiagnosis and the importance of thorough medical review.


## Broader implications for patient advocacy and healthcare
Liu’s story reflects broader discussions about patient rights and the need for transparency in medical diagnoses. O caso de Lucy Liu, conhecida por seus papéis em séries como *Ally McBeal* e *Elementary*, traz à tona um problema que afeta milhões de mulheres no mundo todo, inclusive no Brasil: a importância de buscar uma segunda opinião médica em exames de câncer. Segundo dados do Instituto Nacional de Câncer (INCA), cerca de 70 mil novos casos de câncer de mama são diagnosticados anualmente no país, e erros de interpretação em mamografias ou biópsias podem levar a tratamentos agressivos e desnecessários. A história de Liu, que descobriu o erro décadas depois, serve como um alerta para que brasileiras não dispensem a cautela, especialmente em um cenário onde o acesso a serviços de saúde de qualidade ainda é desigual.

A atriz, que agora apoia campanhas de conscientização sobre saúde da mulher, defende que a medicina não é uma ciência exata e que a humildade para reavaliar diagnósticos pode salvar vidas. No Brasil, a recomendação é clara: sempre que houver dúvida, procurar outro profissional ou até mesmo um centro de referência pode fazer toda a diferença.
