Job seekers can now save money on [TopResume](https://www.topresume.com/), a resume-writing service that promises to help land more interviews. The company rolled out a free resume review for everyone, no strings attached. If you like what you see, you can try its 4-week Career Services Platform trial for free. After the trial, plans start at $149 for a basic resume rewrite, but the free options alone might be worth it for some applicants.

## How TopResume’s free tools work

TopResume’s free resume review gives instant feedback on formatting, keywords, and clarity. You upload your resume, and within minutes, you’ll get a score and a detailed breakdown of strengths and weaknesses. The review isn’t just generic advice—it checks for industry-specific keywords that recruiters scan for. For example, if you’re applying for a project manager role, the system flags missing terms like "Agile" or "Scrum" that could get you past automated applicant tracking systems.

The 4-week trial of the Career Services Platform lets you test drive premium features. You get one resume rewrite, unlimited cover letter reviews, and access to interview prep tools. After the trial, you’ll pay $199 for the full platform if you want to keep using it. But even if you cancel before the trial ends, you still walk away with a free rewritten resume and a clearer idea of where your application stands.

## Who actually benefits from TopResume’s packages

TopResume’s data shows its users get results. In 2023, the company reported that 89% of customers landed more interviews after using its services. That’s a big claim, but it’s backed by user testimonials and third-party reviews. For instance, a user named Maria in Texas said her resume rewrite helped her secure three interviews in two weeks after months of silence. Another user, Jake in New York, landed a $110,000 salary role at a tech startup after using TopResume’s executive package.

The service isn’t for everyone, though. If you already have a strong resume and a network of referrals, you might not need it. But for people re-entering the workforce, changing careers, or applying to competitive roles, TopResume’s tools can fill gaps recruiters notice immediately. The free review alone can spot red flags like generic summaries or outdated job descriptions that make you look lazy.

## What’s included in each package

TopResume’s pricing starts at $149 for a basic resume rewrite. That includes one round of edits and a final polished version. The mid-tier package at $299 adds a cover letter rewrite and a LinkedIn profile update. The top package, $449, includes everything plus a 30-minute career coaching session. Each package comes with a 60-day interview guarantee—if you don’t get interviews within two months, they’ll revise your materials again for free.

The free tools are no joke. The resume review gives you a score out of 100 and highlights specific problems. For example, it might flag that your resume is too long (aim for one page if you have under 10 years of experience) or that your bullet points start with weak verbs like "Responsible for." The trial also lets you test the cover letter builder, which pulls details from your resume to create a tailored letter in minutes.

## How to decide if TopResume is worth it

Before paying, ask yourself a few questions. Are you applying to jobs but getting no responses? Is your industry competitive, like tech or finance? Have you been out of work for over six months? If you answered yes to any of these, TopResume’s tools could help. The free review is a low-risk way to check your resume’s health. If it scores below 70/100, you’ll likely benefit from a rewrite.

But don’t expect miracles. TopResume can’t guarantee a job—it can only optimize your application. If you’re applying to roles that don’t match your skills or experience, no resume service will fix that. The best results come from pairing TopResume’s tools with a strong LinkedIn profile and networking efforts. Use the free trial to see if the platform’s style matches your needs before committing to a paid plan.

## What happens next for job seekers

TopResume isn’t slowing down. The company added AI-powered resume scanning in 2024, which now checks for bias and inclusivity in your language. It also expanded its career coaching services to include video call sessions for users who want more personalized help. If you’re on the fence, try the free review first. If it turns out you need a stronger resume, the 4-week trial gives you a month to test the waters without pressure. For many, that’s enough to land the interviews they’ve been waiting for.
