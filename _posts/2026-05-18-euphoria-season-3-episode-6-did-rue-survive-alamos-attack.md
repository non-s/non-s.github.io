Rue’s (Zendaya) brutal fight with Alamo (Adewale Akinnuoye-Agbaje) finally gets an answer in Euphoria Season 3 Episode 6, titled *Stand Still and See*. After last week’s cliffhanger—where Alamo charged at Rue with a polo mallet in the middle of a crowded party—viewers spent seven days wondering if she’d make it out alive. The episode opens with Rue waking up in a hospital bed, bruised and disoriented, her left eye swollen shut. A nurse tells her she’s lucky to be alive after Alamo’s attack, which left her with a concussion and multiple cuts. The scene cuts to Alamo, handcuffed in a police car, screaming that Rue deserved it. The juxtaposition is chilling: Rue’s fragile survival against Alamo’s unhinged rage.

## Rue’s fragile recovery and Alamo’s arrest

The episode spends time showing Rue’s physical and emotional state as she lies in the hospital. Her friends, Lexi (Maude Apatow) and Cassie (Sydney Sweeney), sit by her bedside, but Rue barely speaks. Flashbacks reveal she’s been hiding her relapse from them, and the attack forces her to confront her addiction again. Meanwhile, Alamo is arrested and held without bail, but the episode ends with his lawyer visiting him in jail. He mutters to the lawyer, *“She had it coming.”* The implication? This isn’t over. Fans are already speculating whether Alamo will return for revenge or if Rue’s survival will push her toward a darker place.

## The fallout for Rue’s relationships and addiction

Rue’s relapse has been a slow burn this season, but Alamo’s attack forces her to face reality. In one scene, she begs Lexi to stay with her, whispering, *“I don’t want to be alone.”* Lexi, who’s been trying to reconcile with her sister, agrees—only for Rue to push her away moments later, saying, *“I don’t deserve help.”* It’s a raw moment that highlights Rue’s self-destructive spiral. Meanwhile, Nate (Jacob Elordi) watches from a distance, his expression unreadable. His role in Rue’s life has been complicated, and this attack might push him to finally confront his own issues.

## The episode’s shocking ending sets up major questions

The episode closes with Rue discharged from the hospital, but her future is anything but clear. She walks out into the sunlight, squinting, as if the world is too bright for her to handle. The final shot is of her standing in the middle of a quiet street, staring at her hands—shaking, unstable. The message? Rue’s survival doesn’t mean she’s safe. Fans are already theorizing whether this is the moment she’ll finally seek real help or spiral further into addiction. The show’s creator, Sam Levinson, has a history of leaving endings ambiguous, and this one is no different.

The bigger question is what happens next for Alamo. The episode teases his potential return, but it’s unclear if he’ll face consequences for his actions. His arrest is a start, but the legal system moves slowly, and his family’s influence might protect him. Rue’s survival gives her a chance to heal, but the road ahead is brutal. Euphoria has always been about pushing characters to their limits, and this season’s no different.
