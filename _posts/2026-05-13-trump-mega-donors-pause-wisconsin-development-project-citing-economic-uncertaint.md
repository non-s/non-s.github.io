Uline, the Illinois-based shipping and packaging supplies company controlled by billionaire Republicans [Richard Uihlein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Uihlein) and his wife [Elizabeth Uihlein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elizabeth_Uihlein), announced Wednesday it is pausing construction on a new $100 million distribution center in Kenosha, Wisconsin. The company cited "ongoing economic uncertainty" as the reason for the halt, which affects more than 500 planned jobs in the politically critical state.

The Kenosha facility was announced in 2022 as part of Uline’s expansion plans, with construction beginning in early 2023. The project would have added 500 full-time positions to the local economy, according to company statements at the time. Kenosha, located on the western shore of Lake Michigan, sits in a congressional district that has shifted between Democratic and Republican control in recent elections.

## Wisconsin’s political battleground status
Wisconsin remains one of the most competitive states in presidential elections, having voted for the Republican nominee in 2016 and 2024 while supporting the Democratic candidate in 2020. The state’s 10 electoral votes have been decisive in several recent elections, making local economic developments politically significant.

Uline’s decision comes amid broader concerns about commercial real estate and construction nationwide. The commercial real estate sector has faced challenges since the rise of remote work altered office space demand, while high interest rates and inflation have slowed development across multiple industries. The company has not specified when, or if, construction will resume.

The Uihleins, founders of Uline, are among the Republican Party’s most prolific donors. They contributed millions to Trump’s 2020 and 2024 campaigns and have funded conservative organizations and candidates across the country. The family’s political influence extends to state-level races in Wisconsin, where they have supported Republican candidates in recent elections.

## Impact on local economy and politics
The pause in construction affects not only Uline’s expansion plans but also the broader economic outlook for Kenosha County. Local officials had anticipated the facility’s completion to boost tax revenues and provide new opportunities for suppliers and service providers. The project was expected to generate additional business for nearby restaurants, retail stores, and housing markets.

Political analysts say the halt could influence perceptions of Wisconsin’s economic climate ahead of the November election. While the state’s unemployment rate remains low compared to the national average, concerns about inflation and business costs have grown in recent months. The Uihleins’ decision may be seen as a signal of broader economic caution among major donors.

Uline has not announced alternative plans for the Kenosha site but stated it will reassess the project as economic conditions evolve. The company operates more than 12 distribution centers across the U.S. and Canada, with the Wisconsin facility intended to serve the upper Midwest market. The pause leaves the future of the project—and the 500 jobs—uncertain as Wisconsin remains a key focus in the 2024 presidential race.
