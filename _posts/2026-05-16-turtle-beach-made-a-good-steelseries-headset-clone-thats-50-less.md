Gaming headset maker Turtle Beach on Tuesday introduced the Stealth Pro 2, a $349 wireless headset that directly challenges SteelSeries’ $399 Nova Pro Omni. The new model arrives just weeks after SteelSeries refreshed its flagship line with the Nova Pro Omni, which debuted May 5. Both headsets now support 24-bit/96kHz wireless hi-res audio over 2.4GHz, a premium feature once reserved for wired setups. The Stealth Pro 2’s launch underscores a growing trend in audio hardware: budget-friendly alternatives with near-identical performance to premium models.

SteelSeries first gained attention with the Arctis Nova Pro Wireless in 2022, setting a high bar for wireless performance. The Nova Pro Omni, its successor, kept the same design but added wireless hi-res audio, improved microphone clarity, and universal console compatibility across all variants. The $599 Nova Elite model remains the top-tier option, distinguished by sturdier build materials and larger drivers. Turtle Beach, meanwhile, has carved a niche by offering similar features at a lower price point, a strategy that resonates with budget-conscious gamers.

## Key specs and features

The Stealth Pro 2’s standout feature is its 24-bit/96kHz wireless hi-res audio, which matches the Nova Pro Omni’s capabilities. The headset uses a 2.4GHz wireless connection for low-latency performance, a standard in modern gaming audio. Turtle Beach also touts improved mic quality, addressing a common complaint in mid-range headsets. Console compatibility spans PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo Switch, and PC, ensuring broad appeal without additional accessories.

Build quality remains a point of differentiation. SteelSeries’ Nova Pro Omni uses high-end materials, including a metal headband and premium ear cushions, while Turtle Beach opts for a more affordable yet durable construction. The Stealth Pro 2’s lighter weight and slimmer profile may appeal to players who prioritize comfort during long sessions. Both brands emphasize swappable ear cushions and headbands, allowing customization to suit individual preferences.

## Pricing and market impact

At $349, the Stealth Pro 2 undercuts the Nova Pro Omni by $50, a significant margin in the gaming headset market. Turtle Beach’s aggressive pricing strategy reflects broader industry trends, where mid-tier brands increasingly challenge premium players. The Stealth Pro 2 also competes with Turtle Beach’s own high-end models, such as the $299 Recon 70X, which lacks hi-res audio but offers a more affordable entry point.

Analysts suggest the headset market is becoming more competitive, with brands like [SteelSeries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SteelSeries) and Turtle Beach vying for dominance. The Nova Pro Omni’s refresh and the Stealth Pro 2’s launch come as gaming audio evolves beyond basic stereo sound. Hi-res audio, once a luxury, is now a standard feature in mid-range and premium headsets, pressuring brands to innovate or risk obsolescence.

## What’s next for gaming headsets?

Industry observers expect further price compression as brands attempt to differentiate their products. Features like custom EQ settings, Bluetooth multipoint connectivity, and AI-enhanced noise cancellation may become standard in the near future. Turtle Beach’s Stealth Pro 2 signals a shift toward accessibility, making high-end audio more attainable for casual gamers. SteelSeries, for its part, continues to emphasize build quality and proprietary technologies, such as its ChatMix dial for balancing game and chat audio.

For players, the choice between the two headsets may come down to brand preference, comfort, and specific needs like battery life or accessory compatibility. Both headsets promise at least 20 hours of battery life, but real-world performance could vary based on usage patterns. As the market matures, consumers stand to benefit from improved features and lower prices, a trend likely to continue in 2024.
