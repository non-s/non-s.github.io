Bruno Fernandes just did something no Manchester United midfielder has ever done. On Saturday, he set up Alejandro Garnacho’s winner against Newcastle to reach 19 assists in a Premier League season. That ties the record Thierry Henry set way back in 2002-03 for Arsenal. Fernandes didn’t just match it—he did it in 31 fewer games than Henry did. That’s efficiency most attackers can’t touch, let alone midfielders like him. [Thierry Henry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thierry_Henry) was a striker, not a playmaker. Yet here’s Fernandes, a deep-lying midfielder, rewriting what we expect from his position. His assist count isn’t just high—it’s historic, especially for someone who’s also United’s captain and top scorer this season. That’s a rare double in modern football. Most players who rack up assists are wingers or strikers. Fernandes is neither. He’s a box-to-box midfielder who’s become the hub of United’s attack. Watching him is like watching a quarterback who also throws the touchdown passes. He dictates the tempo, picks the pass, and scores himself when needed. This season, he’s done all three at an elite level. His 19 assists aren’t just numbers. They’re the result of a style United have been building under Erik ten Hag. Ten Hag has pushed Fernandes into a more advanced role, letting him drift between midfield and attack. It’s a system that rewards creativity—and Fernandes has thrived in it. But let’s not pretend this is all about the system. Fernandes’ vision and execution are what make the numbers possible. He sees passes others don’t, and he delivers them with a weight that most players can’t match. ## How Fernandes stacks up against the all-time greats It’s not just about the record. It’s about the company he’s keeping. Henry’s 20 assists in 2002-03 came in a league where strikers were the primary creators. Today, wingers and inverted fullbacks dominate the assist charts. Fernandes is bucking that trend. His closest modern rivals? Kevin De Bruyne and Mohamed Salah. De Bruyne holds the single-game assist record in the Premier League (5) and has averaged more assists per game than Fernandes over their careers. But De Bruyne’s best assist season is 16—still three shy of Fernandes’ current total. Salah? He’s scored 10 goals more than Fernandes this season, but his assist numbers lag far behind. This isn’t about dismissing De Bruyne or Salah. It’s about recognizing Fernandes’ unique contribution. He’s not just creating chances—he’s doing it at a rate that rivals the best creators in the league, while also chipping in with goals. ## The numbers behind the magic Fernandes’ assist record is impressive, but his underlying stats tell an even bigger story. He’s averaging 3.2 key passes per game this season, per Opta. That’s higher than both De Bruyne and Salah. His xA (expected assists) is 16.5, which suggests he’s created even more chances than his assist tally shows. That’s a sign of a player who’s not just lucky but consistently excellent. His pass completion rate? 85.4%. That’s elite for a player who attempts 68 passes per game. He’s not just spraying the ball around—he’s doing it with purpose. And when he doesn’t have the ball? He presses relentlessly, winning 12.3 defensive duels per 90 minutes. That’s the work rate of a midfielder who’s just as committed to stopping attacks as he is to starting them. ## What happens next for Fernandes The record is tied, but the story isn’t over. United still have six games left in the season, and Fernandes isn’t done yet. If he adds one more assist, he’ll break the record outright. That’s not just a personal milestone—it’s a statement. It would cement his place among the Premier League’s all-time greats, not just as a scorer but as a creator. For United, it’s another piece of evidence that Ten Hag’s project is taking shape. The team’s attack has been fluid this season, and Fernandes is the engine. His form this season has silenced plenty of doubters who questioned whether a midfielder could carry United’s attack. But records aren’t everything. The bigger question is whether Fernandes can sustain this level of performance. He’s 29 now, in the prime of his career, but injuries and fatigue are real risks. United’s next few games will show if he’s still the same player game after game. ## The bigger picture: where does Fernandes rank all-time? Comparing eras in football is tricky. The Premier League today is faster, more tactical, and more physically demanding than it was in Henry’s time. Fernandes is playing in a league where defenders are smarter, goalkeepers are better, and every inch of space is contested. That makes his assist record even more remarkable. But let’s be clear: records are just one way to measure greatness. Creativity isn’t just about how many assists you get. It’s about the quality of the chances you create. It’s about the moments where a single pass changes the game. Fernandes has done that repeatedly this season. Whether he’s the most creative player ever is subjective. But he’s definitely in the conversation now. And if he breaks this record? That conversation will get a lot louder.
