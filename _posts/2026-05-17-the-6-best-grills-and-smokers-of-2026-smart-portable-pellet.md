The grill market’s changing fast. In 2026, the best options aren’t just about fire and smoke anymore. They’re about smart tech, portability, and no-fuss cleanup. We spent months testing 20+ grills and smokers—searing burgers, smoking ribs, and syncing digital temp controllers—to find the six that actually work. Here’s what rose to the top and why they matter for your next cookout or tailgate party. [Wired](https://www.wired.com) ran the tests, but we focused on what real cooks need: reliability, simplicity, and results that don’t take all day to clean up afterward. These picks cut through the hype to give you grills that do what they promise without the headache. If you’re shopping for a grill this year, these are the ones worth your time and money. The market’s crowded, but the standouts are easy to spot once you know what to look for. We’ll tell you exactly which models earned their spot and why they’re worth it. ## The best smart grill for most people: Traeger Pro 780 It’s the Traeger Pro 780. Why? Because it’s the only pellet grill that actually behaves like a smart device. You can control it from your phone, set timers, and even get alerts when your food’s ready. The app [Traeger Connect](https://www.traeger.com/smart-smoke-control) works like a charm, and the grill itself delivers consistent smoke and temperature with minimal fuss. Cleanup’s a breeze too—just empty the ash collector and you’re done. The Pro 780 isn’t cheap, but it’s the closest thing to a set-and-forget grill you’ll find. If you want great food without babysitting the fire, this is your grill. We cooked everything from brisket to pizza on it, and the results were always on point. The only downside? It’s heavy, so it’s not the best for tailgating. ## The best portable grill for tailgates and camping: Weber Traveler It’s the Weber Traveler. It’s compact, folds flat, and runs on propane or natural gas. But the real kicker? It’s got a built-in thermometer and a grease management system that actually works. No messy cleanup, no wasted fuel. We took it to three tailgates last summer, and it never let us down. The grill grates are porcelain-coated, so they don’t rust, and the whole thing weighs just 30 pounds. It’s not a smoker, but it sears like a beast. If you need something you can toss in your trunk and forget about, this is it. ## The best pellet grill for low-and-slow smoking: Camp Chef Woodwind WiFi 36 This one’s a sleeper. The Camp Chef Woodwind WiFi 36 costs half as much as a Traeger but delivers similar results. It’s got a built-in meat probe, WiFi control, and a smoke setting that lets you dial in the exact amount of smoke you want. We smoked a pork shoulder for 12 hours overnight, and the temp never wavered. The app’s not as polished as Traeger’s, but it gets the job done. Cleanup’s simple too—just scrape the grease tray and you’re done. If you want a pellet grill that punches above its weight, this is it. ## The best kamado-style grill for versatility: Big Green Egg Large Big Green Egg’s Large model is the ultimate do-it-all kamado. It grills, smokes, bakes, and even does pizza. The ceramic walls hold heat like a dream, and the tight seal means you can smoke ribs for hours without topping off the wood. It’s heavy—97 pounds—but once it’s in place, it’s there for life. We cooked everything from steaks to pulled pork on it, and the results were always restaurant-quality. The only catch? It’s not cheap, and you’ll need to babysit the fire a little more than a pellet grill. But if you want one grill that does everything, this is it. ## The best budget pellet grill: Z Grills 100D If you’re on a tight budget but still want a pellet grill, the Z Grills 100D is your best bet. It’s half the price of a Traeger but still delivers consistent smoke and temperature. The app works, the cleanup’s easy, and it’s got enough room for a whole chicken or a rack of ribs. We smoked a brisket on it for 10 hours, and it held temp perfectly. The downside? The build quality isn’t as sturdy as Traeger’s, and the app’s a little buggy. But for the price, it’s a steal. ## The best grill for tech lovers: Masterbuilt Smoke Hollow 4082C This one’s for the data nerds. The Masterbuilt Smoke Hollow 4082C is a pellet grill with a touchscreen control panel, built-in meat probe, and WiFi. You can monitor everything from your phone, set timers, and even get alerts when your food’s done. It’s not as polished as Traeger’s app, but it’s functional. We cooked a whole turkey on it and nailed the temp spot-on. Cleanup’s straightforward too—just empty the ash tray and wipe down the grates. If you love tech and want a grill that keeps up, this is the one. These grills prove that great barbecue doesn’t have to be complicated. Whether you’re a tech lover, a tailgater, or a weekend smoker, there’s a model here that fits your needs. The market’s crowded, but the best options stand out because they deliver on their promises. The next time you’re shopping for a grill, skip the gimmicks and go with one of these. Your food—and your sanity—will thank you.
