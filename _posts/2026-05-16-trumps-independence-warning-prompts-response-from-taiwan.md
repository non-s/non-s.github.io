> 📰 **Continuing coverage:** [Trump urges Taiwan to avoid independence amid rising China tensions](/world/2026/05/16/trump-warns-taiwan-against-declaring-independence-hours-after-summit-with-chinas/)

Taiwan’s foreign ministry issued a formal statement on Wednesday pushing back against former U.S. President [Donald Trump](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Trump)’s latest comments on the island’s sovereignty. The ministry asserted that Beijing has no right to claim jurisdiction over Taiwan, a position that has drawn sharp criticism from Chinese officials in recent days.

Trump, in a campaign speech on Tuesday, suggested that Taiwan should take a stronger stance on independence, a remark that sparked immediate backlash from both Taipei and Beijing. The former president stated that Taiwan’s leadership should feel empowered to declare formal independence from China, a move that Beijing has repeatedly warned could trigger military action. Taiwan’s foreign ministry condemned the remarks, calling them an unnecessary escalation.

## Trump’s remarks complicate U.S.-Taiwan-China tensions

Analysts say Trump’s comments risk inflaming already tense relations between Washington, Taipei, and Beijing. The U.S. has maintained a policy of strategic ambiguity regarding Taiwan’s status, neither fully supporting nor opposing its independence. Trump’s latest remarks, however, appear to challenge that stance, potentially emboldening Taiwanese leaders to push for greater international recognition.

China’s foreign ministry responded swiftly, accusing Trump of interfering in internal affairs. A spokesperson reiterated Beijing’s position that Taiwan is an inalienable part of China and warned against any actions that could undermine its sovereignty. The exchange comes as U.S.-China relations remain strained over trade, technology, and security issues.

Taiwan’s government has long maintained that it is a sovereign nation, despite never having formally declared independence. The island operates as a de facto independent state with its own government, military, and currency, though it is not recognized by the United Nations. Taipei has sought to strengthen ties with democratic allies, including the U.S., to counterbalance China’s growing pressure.

## Global reactions highlight divide on Taiwan’s status

International observers are divided over Trump’s remarks. Some analysts argue that his comments reflect a broader shift in U.S. policy toward Taiwan, while others warn they could provoke a dangerous confrontation. European diplomats have expressed concern over the escalation, urging all parties to avoid actions that could destabilize the region.

The U.S. State Department declined to comment directly on Trump’s speech but reiterated its long-standing policy of supporting peace and stability across the Taiwan Strait. Meanwhile, Taiwanese President [Tsai Ing-wen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tsai_Ing-wen) has yet to publicly address Trump’s remarks, though her administration has emphasized the need for dialogue with Beijing.

Taiwan’s next steps remain unclear, but analysts predict further diplomatic efforts to shore up international support. The island is scheduled to hold local elections later this year, which could influence its approach to cross-strait relations. Beijing, meanwhile, continues to conduct military drills near Taiwan as a show of force.

The broader implications of Trump’s remarks could extend beyond Taiwan, potentially reshaping U.S. foreign policy in the Indo-Pacific. With tensions already high over issues like trade and technology, his comments risk adding another layer of complexity to an already volatile relationship.
