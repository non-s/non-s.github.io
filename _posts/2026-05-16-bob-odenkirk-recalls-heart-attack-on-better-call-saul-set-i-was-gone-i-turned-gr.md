Bob Odenkirk says he was just minutes from dying when he suffered a heart attack on the set of [Better Call Saul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Better_Call_Saul) in 2021. The actor, best known for playing slick lawyer Jimmy McGill, described the ordeal in an interview with *The Times of London* published this week. What should have been a routine workday turned into a life-or-death emergency almost immediately after his collapse. Crew members nearly missed the severity of the situation because they thought his co-stars were joking around when they screamed for help. Odenkirk’s account reveals how close a major production came to losing its lead in the middle of filming the final season of the acclaimed drama series.

During the interview, Odenkirk explained that he was gone for a short time after his heart stopped. “I went down and Rhea [Seehorn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhea_Seehorn) and Patrick [Fabian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patrick_Fabian) grabbed me and they were screaming, but [the crew members who noticed] thought they were laughing,” he said. “So I turned gray. I was gone.” The actor’s vivid description underscores the suddenness of the medical emergency and how easily it could have been misread in the chaos of a busy set. His heart attack happened during a day of filming in Albuquerque, New Mexico, where the show is shot.

## Odenkirk’s health scare was not an isolated incident

The actor’s heart attack wasn’t just a private medical issue—it became a production crisis. The set shut down immediately as crew members sprang into action. Paramedics arrived quickly and performed CPR before rushing Odenkirk to the hospital. The show’s producers kept the incident secret at the time to protect Odenkirk’s privacy and avoid disrupting the final season’s production. The actor was back at work within weeks, but the incident left a lasting impact on everyone involved. Colleagues later described the shock of seeing a vital, energetic star suddenly collapse without warning.

Odenkirk has spoken openly about health struggles in the past, including his battle with alcoholism, which he has said contributed to his heart problems. In the interview, he reflected on how close he came to missing the chance to finish [Better Call Saul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Better_Call_Saul), which many consider one of the best TV dramas ever made. The final season wrapped in May 2022, and the show earned widespread acclaim for its emotional conclusion. Odenkirk’s survival meant he could see the project through to its end, delivering a performance that capped off Jimmy McGill’s transformation into the morally compromised Saul Goodman.

## The aftermath and what changed for Odenkirk

Since the heart attack, Odenkirk has spoken publicly about prioritizing his health and making significant lifestyle changes. He told *The Times* that the experience forced him to confront his mortality in a way nothing else could. “It’s not something you can ignore after that,” he said. “You realize how fragile everything is.” His openness about the incident has also highlighted the risks performers take, often without fanfare, while working on demanding productions. For a show like *Better Call Saul*, where physical stamina and mental focus are critical, the stakes were especially high.

The heart attack also raised questions about how studios and productions handle medical emergencies on set. While many shows have medical personnel on standby, Odenkirk’s case shows how quickly a situation can spiral out of control. His survival depended on a combination of quick thinking by his co-stars and the rapid arrival of emergency responders. The incident serves as a reminder of the hidden dangers behind the scenes of popular television, where actors push themselves to deliver performances that often go unnoticed by audiences.

What happens next for Odenkirk is unclear, but he has hinted that he’s focusing on his health and future projects. With *Better Call Saul* now finished, he may explore new roles or take on different creative challenges. Whatever he chooses, the heart attack has already left a mark—not just on his life, but on the legacy of the show that nearly lost him halfway through its final season.
