OVO Energy customers can keep all existing tariffs under a planned takeover that would create one of Britain’s largest energy suppliers. The deal, pending regulatory approval, guarantees no price changes or service cuts for households already on OVO contracts.

Parent company OVO Group confirmed the acquisition would create a combined business serving over 5 million customers nationwide. All tariffs—including fixed-rate and variable deals—will remain intact under the agreement, easing concerns about sudden price hikes or policy shifts.

## Regulatory and industry reaction
The deal comes as energy firms consolidate across the UK amid volatile market conditions. Industry analysts note the merger could strengthen competition by forming one of the country’s five largest suppliers, potentially reshaping pricing power in the sector.

Regulators including Ofgem are expected to scrutinize the takeover for competition impacts. A formal review process will determine whether the deal meets market fairness standards, with a decision likely within six months.

OVO Group’s CEO [Stephen Fitzpatrick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephen_Fitzpatrick) stressed the deal would preserve customer benefits while expanding service reach. “We’re committed to maintaining all tariffs exactly as they are,” Fitzpatrick said in a statement. “This deal is about growth, not disruption.”

## Customer guidance and next steps
OVO has urged customers not to cancel contracts or switch suppliers prematurely. The company will send direct updates once regulatory approval is secured, though no changes are planned even if the deal is blocked.

Experts advise customers to monitor official communications from OVO and Ofgem for updates. Independent energy comparison sites remain available for those considering future moves, though no immediate need is anticipated.

The merger’s broader impact includes potential job consolidation and supplier network expansions. Analysts predict the combined entity could leverage scale to negotiate better wholesale energy rates, though consumer prices are guaranteed under the current terms.
