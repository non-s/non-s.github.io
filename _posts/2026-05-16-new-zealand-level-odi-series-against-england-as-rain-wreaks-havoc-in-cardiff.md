Rain twice forced the abandonment of England’s ODI chase against New Zealand at Sophia Gardens on Friday, ending the series level at 1-1 after the White Ferns won by 17 runs via the Duckworth-Lewis system. The match, originally scheduled for 50 overs, was reduced to 33 overs per side after a two-and-a-half-hour delay, with England finishing on 181-7. New Zealand’s revised target of 184 was within reach when the weather intervened again, forcing the umpires to call an end to the contest with the hosts 141-4 in 20 overs.

England, put in to bat, started strongly before the first rain delay arrived at 77-3 in 17 overs. Alice Capsey top-scored with a run-a-ball 45 as the hosts pushed to 181-7, but New Zealand’s reply nearly unraveled after Lauren Bell removed three key batters lbw in quick succession. Suzie Bates, Melie Kerr and Georgia Plimmer all fell to Bell’s 3-29, leaving the White Ferns at 40-3. A 57-run partnership between Maddy Green and Brooke Halliday steadied the ship, but the second rain delay at 141-4 sealed New Zealand’s victory.
