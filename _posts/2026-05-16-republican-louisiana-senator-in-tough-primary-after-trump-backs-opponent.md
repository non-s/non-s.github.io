Republican Senator Bill Cassidy of Louisiana faces a tough primary challenge Saturday after former President Donald Trump endorsed his opponent, Julia Letlow. The election tests Trump’s influence in deep-red states and comes after Cassidy’s vote to impeach Trump following the January 6 insurrection.

Cassidy, a three-term incumbent, normally would have been favored in Louisiana’s solidly Republican environment. But his impeachment vote put him at odds with Trump, who declared in January that Letlow, a U.S. representative, should challenge Cassidy. Trump’s endorsement of Letlow underscores his continued sway over the Republican base. Cassidy has tried to mend fences by voting to confirm controversial health official Robert F. Kennedy Jr. as a federal health secretary.

The primary marks the latest test of Trump’s ability to sway primary elections even in states he won handily. In 2020, Trump won Louisiana by nearly 20 points, yet his endorsed candidates have faced mixed results in subsequent races. Letlow, a first-term congresswoman, represents Louisiana’s 5th district and has campaigned on loyalty to Trump’s agenda. Cassidy, who has represented Louisiana since 2015, has emphasized his conservative record despite the impeachment vote.

## Trump’s endorsement power on trial in Louisiana

Trump’s endorsement has become a significant factor in Republican primaries, often determining which candidates receive financial and grassroots support. His backing of Letlow signals a clear preference over Cassidy, who has clashed with Trump on multiple occasions. The primary could signal whether Trump’s influence remains as potent as it was during his presidency or if voters are growing more independent in their choices.

Louisiana’s primary system requires candidates to secure over 50% of the vote to avoid a runoff. If no candidate reaches that threshold, the top two finishers will face each other in a December runoff. Polls show the race is close, reflecting the divided opinions within the state’s Republican electorate. Cassidy’s campaign argues that his conservative voting record and experience outweigh his impeachment vote, while Letlow’s team highlights her alignment with Trump’s priorities.

The outcome could influence how other Republican senators facing tough primaries in 2024 approach their relationships with Trump. Senators up for re-election in states like Texas, Montana, and Ohio are watching closely to see if Trump’s backing still carries the weight it once did. Cassidy’s potential loss would send a strong message about the limits of his political resilience despite his deep roots in Louisiana politics.

## What’s at stake for Louisiana Republicans

The primary also highlights Louisiana’s unique political landscape, where voters often split tickets between federal and state offices. While Louisiana is reliably Republican at the presidential level, down-ballot races frequently feature intraparty disputes. Cassidy’s potential defeat would underscore the growing influence of Trump-aligned factions within the GOP, particularly in the South.

For Letlow, a win would solidify her position as a rising star in the Trump-aligned wing of the Republican Party. Letlow, who won a special election in 2021 after her husband’s death, has positioned herself as a loyal Trump ally. Cassidy, meanwhile, has argued that his impeachment vote was a constitutional duty, not a political statement. His campaign has focused on his work securing funds for Louisiana and his conservative voting record.

As ballots are cast Saturday, the race remains too close to call, reflecting the broader tensions within the Republican Party. The primary outcome will shape the state’s political future and could influence Trump’s role in upcoming elections. For Louisiana voters, the choice represents a test of loyalty versus experience in a rapidly changing GOP. The results will be closely watched as a barometer for Trump’s enduring influence in Republican primaries nationwide.

What happens next depends on whether Cassidy can rally enough support to avoid a runoff or if Letlow’s Trump-backed campaign secures the nomination outright.
