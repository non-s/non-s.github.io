---

## China Approves First Titanium-Copper Implant to Fight Hospital Infections

China has approved the world’s first medical implant made from a titanium-copper alloy, a breakthrough meant to cut post-surgery infections. Researchers spent 10 years developing the material, which resists bacterial growth better than traditional titanium. The implant could soon appear in surgeries for bones and joints [SCMP reports](/world/2026/05/18/china-approves-titanium-copper-implant).

This development matters because hospital-acquired infections sicken millions each year. A new material that naturally fights bacteria may reduce repeat surgeries and save lives.

*Implant approvals don’t always translate to quick adoption, so hospitals will need to test the devices before wide use.*

---

## WHO Declares Ebola Outbreak a Global Health Emergency

The World Health Organization declared the Ebola outbreak in Congo a global emergency after more than 80 deaths and fast-spreading cases. No vaccine is available, and infected patients are appearing in new regions. Health workers are struggling to contain the virus as it moves toward major cities [ScienceAlert reports](/science/2026/05/18/ebola-global-emergency-congo).

This emergency comes as global health systems remain stretched from past outbreaks. A new Ebola surge risks overwhelming clinics and aid groups already stretched thin.

*Without a vaccine and limited medical access, the outbreak could worsen quickly in the coming weeks.*

---

## Two Navy Growlers Crash at Idaho Air Show

Two U.S. Navy E/A-18G Growlers collided Sunday during an air show in Idaho, sending all four crew members to hospitals after they ejected safely. The planes were performing a training maneuver when they struck each other mid-air. Investigators are reviewing footage and flight data [The Drive reports](/war/2026/05/18/navy-growlers-crash-idaho-air-show).

This crash follows a record 11-month deployment for the USS Gerald R. Ford carrier group, which returned home Saturday after the longest Navy deployment in 50 years [The Drive reports](/war/2026/05/17/uss-ford-returns-after-record-deployment).

*Aviation mishaps during public events often lead to stricter training reviews and temporary flight restrictions.*
