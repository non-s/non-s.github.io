JERUSALEM — Israel’s military confirmed Friday that Izz al-Din al-Haddad, the leader of Hamas’ [Izz al-Din al-Qassam Brigades](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Izz_al-Din_al-Qassam_Brigades), was killed in a targeted strike in Gaza City. Haddad, one of the last remaining senior commanders who orchestrated the October 7, 2023 attacks on Israel, had taken command after his predecessor, [Mohammed Sinwar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mohammed_Sinwar), was killed in a previous Israeli operation.

Haddad’s death was confirmed by his family to The Associated Press on Saturday. The Israeli military described the strike as a precision operation aimed at dismantling Hamas’ military leadership. Israeli officials stated Haddad played a central role in planning and executing the deadly assault that killed about 1,200 people and led to Israel’s ongoing military campaign in Gaza.

The killing comes amid stalled ceasefire negotiations between Israel and Hamas. Key sticking points include Hamas’ refusal to disarm and Israel’s demands for the militant group’s dismantling. The fragile truce, which has seen periodic flare-ups, remains under threat as both sides accuse each other of violating terms. Hamas [Palestinian Islamic Resistance Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamas) has yet to issue an official statement regarding Haddad’s death.

## Israel-Hamas conflict timeline: How the war escalated

The October 7, 2023 attacks marked the deadliest single day for Israel in decades, triggering a massive Israeli military response in Gaza. Since then, the war has resulted in tens of thousands of Palestinian deaths and widespread destruction in Gaza. International mediators, including Egypt and Qatar, have struggled to broker a lasting ceasefire, with disarmament and hostage releases remaining major obstacles.

Haddad’s death reduces the number of top Hamas military leaders still alive, following the targeted killings of several key figures. His predecessor, Mohammed Sinwar, was killed in an Israeli strike in late 2024, further weakening Hamas’ command structure. Analysts suggest Haddad’s elimination could temporarily disrupt Hamas operations but may not significantly alter the group’s long-term strategy.

## Ceasefire talks stall as disarmament disputes persist

Ceasefire negotiations have repeatedly collapsed over demands for Hamas to disarm and Israel’s insistence on dismantling the group’s military capabilities. The United Nations and several Arab states have warned that continued violence risks escalating regional instability. The fragile truce, which has seen intermittent calm, remains fragile as both sides exchange fire and accusations of violations.

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu has reiterated his government’s commitment to destroying Hamas, while Hamas leaders insist they will not surrender their weapons. The international community continues to call for restraint, fearing further civilian casualties and a broader regional conflict. Egypt and Qatar, which have mediated past talks, are reportedly working to revive negotiations, but progress remains uncertain.

The killing of Haddad adds another layer of complexity to the already volatile situation. With Hamas’ military leadership decimated, the group may seek to retaliate, potentially triggering a new cycle of violence. Israel’s military has warned of continued operations until its objectives are met, raising concerns about further civilian harm in Gaza.
