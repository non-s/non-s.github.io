Oscar-winning filmmaker [Kenneth Lonergan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenneth_Lonergan) returns to the director’s chair with *Tomorrow Is a Drag*, a drama generating buzz ahead of its debut on the Cannes market. The film marks Lonergan’s first directorial effort since *Manchester by the Sea* in 2016, which earned him two Academy Award nominations and a Best Original Screenplay win. The project is positioned as one of the most anticipated acquisitions at this year’s Cannes Film Festival.

## Star-studded ensemble joins Lonergan’s project
The cast features [Aubrey Plaza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aubrey_Plaza) (*The White Lotus*, *Ingrid Goes West*), [Adam Driver](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adam_Driver) (*Star Wars*, *Marriage Story*), [Vanessa Kirby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanessa_Kirby) (*Pieces of a Woman*, *The Crown*), and [Matthew Broderick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matthew_Broderick) (*Ferris Bueller’s Day Off*, *The Lion King*). Plaza, who gained acclaim for her role in HBO’s *The White Lotus*, will star alongside Driver, a two-time Academy Award nominee known for his work in *Marriage Story* and *Annet*. Kirby, fresh off her Emmy-winning performance in *The Crown*, and Broderick, returning to dramatic roles, round out the ensemble.

## Cannes debut adds to project’s allure
The film’s inclusion in the Cannes market underscores its commercial potential, drawing attention from international distributors seeking prestige projects. Lonergan, known for his sharp character-driven narratives, has assembled a cast capable of delivering layered performances. Industry insiders report that financing is in place, with production set to begin later this year. The Cannes market serves as a prime platform for securing distribution deals ahead of the festival.

## Lonergan’s return draws industry attention
Lonergan’s last directorial outing, *Manchester by the Sea*, remains one of the most critically acclaimed films of the past decade. His signature style—marked by raw dialogue and emotional restraint—has earned him a loyal following among cinephiles. The director’s involvement in *Tomorrow Is a Drag* signals a potential return to form, particularly as he explores new thematic territory. The ensemble cast’s involvement further amplifies expectations for the project’s artistic and commercial success.

## Filming and release plans remain under wraps
Details about the plot, filming locations, and release timeline have not been disclosed. However, industry sources confirm that the project is actively in development, with casting finalized and financing secured. The Cannes market debut suggests that distributors will have early access to materials, including teasers or sizzle reels, to gauge interest. If successful, the film could premiere at a major festival before a wider theatrical release.

The announcement of *Tomorrow Is a Drag* arrives as Lonergan transitions from screenwriting to directing, a shift that has historically yielded high-profile projects. With a cast of this caliber, the film is poised to attract both critics and audiences. Further updates on production and casting are expected in the coming months.
