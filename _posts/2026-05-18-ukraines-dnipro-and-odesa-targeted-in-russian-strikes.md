Russian forces launched fresh drone and missile strikes on the Ukrainian cities of Dnipro and Odesa overnight, injuring at least 20 people, local officials reported. In Odesa, head of the local military administration [Serhiy Lysak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serhiy_Lysak) said the strikes damaged residential buildings, a school, and a kindergarten. Lysak confirmed more attacks on infrastructure but didn't provide details on additional casualties. The Black Sea port city, a frequent target, saw significant damage to civilian areas, raising concerns about the humanitarian impact of the ongoing war. The strikes followed a series of Ukrainian drone attacks over the weekend that targeted multiple regions in Russia, including Moscow. Ukrainian officials claimed hundreds of drones were launched, marking one of the most intense cross-border operations since the war began. These retaliatory strikes came just days after Russia killed at least 24 people and injured 50 in attacks on Kyiv, Ukraine's capital. The escalation highlights the brutal cycle of violence that has defined the conflict for more than two years, with civilians bearing the brunt of the assaults. Both sides have increasingly used long-range drones to strike deep into each other's territory, blurring the lines between frontline combat and home front attacks. The strikes on Dnipro and Odesa also reflect Russia's strategy of targeting economic and logistical hubs to weaken Ukraine's resilience. Odesa, a key port city, has been repeatedly hit since Russia withdrew from a grain export deal last summer, leaving global food markets vulnerable. Dnipro, a major industrial center in southeastern Ukraine, has faced relentless attacks aimed at crippling its infrastructure and morale. Ukrainian air defenses intercepted some of the incoming missiles and drones, but many still caused damage on the ground. The overnight strikes left families scrambling for shelter as sirens wailed across both cities. Emergency services worked through the night to clear debris and treat the wounded, while residents described scenes of chaos and destruction in their neighborhoods. The attacks underscore the brutal reality of a war that shows no signs of ending, with civilians caught in the crossfire. International observers have warned that the escalation risks further destabilizing the region, especially as winter approaches and energy infrastructure becomes a potential target. Ukraine's ability to defend its cities remains a critical factor in the conflict's trajectory, but the strain on its air defense systems is becoming increasingly visible. For now, the people of Dnipro and Odesa face another day of uncertainty, as the sound of explosions replaces the usual rhythms of city life. The human cost of this war continues to rise, with no clear end in sight.
