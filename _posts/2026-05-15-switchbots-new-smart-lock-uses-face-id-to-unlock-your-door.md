SwitchBot just made smart locks even smarter. The company’s new [Smart Lock Pro](https://www.switch-bot.com/) ditches the usual keypad or phone tap. Instead, it uses Apple’s Face ID to unlock your door. That’s right—your face is the key. And unlike other smart locks, you don’t need to pull out your phone. Just walk up and it recognizes you instantly.

The lock costs $149 and works with Apple’s HomeKit, so it fits right into your iPhone ecosystem. If you’re not an Apple user, it also supports fingerprint unlocking and a traditional keypad. Setup takes about 15 minutes: swap out your old deadbolt, screw in the new one, and sync it with the SwitchBot app. The app lets you add up to 200 face profiles for family, roommates, or frequent visitors.

## How it beats other smart locks
SwitchBot isn’t the first to offer facial recognition, but it’s the first to skip the phone requirement. Most smart locks that use Face ID or similar tech still need your phone nearby to confirm your identity. This one doesn’t. It runs on a built-in battery that lasts about six months on a charge—longer if you use it less. When the battery dies, you can still unlock the door with a key or the emergency keypad.

The lock also logs every entry and exit, which you can check in the app. That’s handy if you want to see who’s coming and going or if you suspect someone’s using a spare key without permission. It works with most standard doors too, as long as they’re not double-cylinder or have a smart deadbolt already installed.

## Who should buy this
If you’re already locked into Apple’s ecosystem, this is a no-brainer upgrade. HomeKit users will appreciate the seamless integration, and anyone tired of fumbling for keys or phones will love the convenience. But if you’re on Android or don’t use smart home tech, this lock won’t help much. It’s also not the best choice for renters, since it requires replacing the deadbolt.

SwitchBot says the lock is available now on its website and at select retailers like Best Buy. That’s good news for early adopters, but expect demand to be high. It’s a smart product with a price that matches its polish.

## What’s next for smart locks
This isn’t the first time facial recognition has been used in smart locks, but it’s the first time it’s been this seamless. Other companies like [Yale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yale_&_Towne_Manufacturing_Company) and [Schlage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schlage) have offered face unlocking, but they still rely on your phone to confirm your identity. SwitchBot’s approach cuts out that extra step, which feels like the future of convenience.

We’ll likely see more smart locks adopt this kind of standalone facial recognition in the coming years. For now, SwitchBot’s Smart Lock Pro sets a new bar for ease of use. It’s not cheap, but if you value speed and simplicity, it’s worth every penny.
