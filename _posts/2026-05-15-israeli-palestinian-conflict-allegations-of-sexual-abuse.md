Two new investigations released this week allege that Hamas systematically used sexual violence during the October 7, 2023 attacks on Israel. The reports, one by [The Civil Commission in Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israeli_Civil_Commission) and another by a UN-affiliated body, detail multiple accounts of sexual abuse and gender-based violence. Both Israel and Hamas have denied the claims, calling them politically motivated and lacking credible evidence.

The Civil Commission’s report, led by Cochav Elkayam-Levy, examined thousands of photographs, videos, and survivor testimonies. It concluded that Hamas used sexual and gender-based violence (SGBV) deliberately as a tactic of war. The report was endorsed by prominent figures, including former U.S. Secretary of State [Hillary Clinton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hillary_Clinton) and former Canadian Minister of Justice [Irwin Cotler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irwin_Cotler). It follows methodologies recognized by international criminal tribunals for documenting war crimes.

## UN report echoes allegations amid denials

A separate report, commissioned by the United Nations and other human rights organizations, also alleges systematic sexual violence by Hamas during the October 7 attacks. The investigation, led by a team of forensic experts, found evidence of rape, sexual assault, and mutilation. However, its findings have been disputed by Hamas officials, who called the report biased and unreliable.

Israeli officials have welcomed the investigations, calling them critical to exposing Hamas’ tactics. Prime Minister [Benjamin Netanyahu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_Netanyahu) stated that the reports confirm long-standing claims about Hamas’ use of sexual violence as a weapon of war. Palestinian officials, meanwhile, argue the allegations are part of a broader campaign to demonize Hamas and undermine Palestinian rights.

## International reactions mixed

The allegations have sparked reactions from global human rights groups, with some calling for further independent investigations. The [International Criminal Court (ICC)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Criminal_Court) has not yet commented on the reports but has previously opened investigations into alleged war crimes by both Israeli forces and Hamas. The U.S. and several European countries have expressed concern, urging all parties to cooperate with independent probes.

Critics of the reports argue that the lack of immediate access to crime scenes and the passage of time since the attacks make it difficult to verify the claims. They also point to Israel’s ongoing blockade of Gaza, which has restricted independent investigations. Supporters of the reports counter that the accumulation of survivor testimonies, digital evidence, and forensic analysis strengthens their credibility.

## Broader implications for conflict resolution

The allegations could further complicate efforts to mediate the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, which has seen multiple wars and decades of violence. Human rights organizations warn that unaddressed sexual violence in conflict zones often goes unpunished, emboldening perpetrators. The reports may also influence international perceptions of Hamas and the broader geopolitical dynamics in the Middle East.

The United Nations has called for all allegations to be thoroughly investigated by an independent, impartial body. Meanwhile, both sides continue to trade accusations, with no clear path toward resolution. The reports add another layer of complexity to an already intractable conflict, raising urgent questions about accountability and justice.
