More than 50 employees have left [SpaceXAI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SpaceX), the AI division formed after Elon Musk’s merger of [xAI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/XAI_(company)) and SpaceX’s AI teams, since February. While the exact reasons vary, current and former staffers describe a mix of burnout, leadership instability, and frustration over a lack of clear career paths or financial rewards. The departures highlight growing concerns about retention at a company that’s already under intense scrutiny for its rapid pace and Musk’s hands-on management style.

## What’s happening inside SpaceXAI
The exodus follows the merger of xAI and SpaceX’s AI units, which Musk announced last year to accelerate development of AI systems for SpaceX’s Starship rockets and other projects. But instead of rallying talent, the merger has triggered a wave of exits. Insiders say the company’s culture—already known for long hours and high pressure—has grown more volatile since the merger. Some employees report being reassigned abruptly or told to pivot projects without enough support.

Musk’s leadership style hasn’t helped. He’s known for micromanaging, sudden pivots, and public criticism of staff, which has pushed some to leave. One former engineer described the environment as "constant firefighting" where priorities shift weekly. Others say they were hired for specific roles that no longer exist after the merger, leaving them questioning their future at the company.

## Why staff are walking out
The biggest issue seems to be uncertainty. SpaceXAI hasn’t clarified how the merger benefits employees long-term, and stock options—once a key retention tool—feel less valuable now that the company’s path isn’t clear. Some staffers say they’re being poached by competitors offering better pay or more stable work environments. Others are simply burned out after years of intense workloads under Musk’s companies.

The timing stings, too. SpaceXAI was supposed to be a powerhouse combining AI and aerospace expertise, but the merger has created confusion instead. Teams that once worked in silos now overlap, leading to redundancies and turf wars. One project manager said, "We’re supposed to be building the future, but it feels like we’re just spinning our wheels."

## The bigger picture
This isn’t the first time Musk’s companies have faced retention problems. Tesla, SpaceX, and now SpaceXAI have all seen high-profile departures, often tied to Musk’s unpredictable management. But SpaceXAI’s situation is different because it’s still in its early stages, with less institutional history to hold teams together. If the trend continues, it could slow down the company’s ambitious AI and space projects.

Investors and partners may start asking tougher questions about SpaceXAI’s stability. The company has yet to comment publicly on the departures, but the silence speaks volumes. If key engineers keep leaving, it’ll be harder to deliver on Musk’s grand promises—like autonomous rockets or AI-powered spacecraft.

The merger was supposed to create synergy, but so far, it’s mostly created confusion. Until SpaceXAI clarifies its vision or addresses staff concerns, more employees may follow the exit door.
