Aston Villa secured a top-four finish and Champions League qualification for the first time since 1983 with a 2-0 win over Liverpool at Villa Park on Sunday. Ollie Watkins scored twice as Aston Villa moved above their rivals into fourth place in the Premier League, ending Liverpool’s hopes of Champions League football next season.

Watkins, who has scored 20 league goals this season, opened the scoring in the 11th minute with a low shot past Alisson Becker. His second, a clinical finish in the 58th minute, sealed the win and confirmed Aston Villa’s place in Europe’s elite competition. The victory marked a historic moment for Aston Villa, who last competed in the Champions League 41 years ago.

Liverpool, who had already secured a domestic cup double this season, saw their Champions League hopes dashed by Aston Villa’s performance. Jürgen Klopp’s side, who finished third last season, will now have to settle for Europa League football next term. The defeat leaves Liverpool five points off the top four with just one game remaining in the Premier League season.

For Aston Villa, the win was their latest in a remarkable season under manager Unai Emery. The former Arsenal manager has transformed the club’s fortunes, guiding them to their highest league finish since 1996. Their Champions League qualification is a reward for a consistent campaign that has seen them challenge for a top-four spot throughout the year.

The match was played in front of a packed Villa Park, with fans celebrating a season that has exceeded expectations. Watkins’ brace was the highlight, but the entire team delivered a disciplined performance to secure the crucial points. The victory also means Aston Villa will avoid the Europa Conference League, which they would have entered had they finished fifth.

With one game left in the Premier League, Aston Villa now sit fourth, three points clear of Manchester United in fifth. Their final match against Brighton & Hove Albion could further solidify their position if results go their way. For Liverpool, the focus shifts to the Europa League, where they will aim to add another trophy to their collection.

The Champions League qualification is a major achievement for Aston Villa, who have spent much of the past two decades in the lower half of the Premier League. Their success this season has been built on strong defense, midfield dominance, and clinical finishing. The club’s recent investments in the squad have paid off, with Watkins and midfielder John McGinn among the standout performers.

Looking ahead, Aston Villa will now prepare for their first Champions League campaign in over four decades. The group stage draw will take place in August, with the club hoping to make a strong impression in Europe’s premier competition. Meanwhile, Liverpool will reflect on a season that included domestic cup success but fell short in the league.
