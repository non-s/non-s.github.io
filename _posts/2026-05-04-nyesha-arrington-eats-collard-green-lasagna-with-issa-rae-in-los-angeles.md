Chef and television host [Nyesha Arrington](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nyesha_Arrington) wrapped up her California food tour in her hometown of Los Angeles with a standout collard green lasagna dish cooked alongside actress and producer [Issa Rae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Issa_Rae). The episode, part of the Eater series Plateworthy, features Arrington reconnecting with L.A.’s food scene through nostalgic and innovative dishes.

Arrington’s Los Angeles itinerary began with pastries at [Santa Canela](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santa_Canela) in Highland Park, where pastry chef [Ellen Ramos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ellen_Ramos) demonstrated how to make champurrado doughnuts using fresh masa, burnt-vanilla cream-stuffed conchas, and L.A.-shaped churros. After a sweets-filled breakfast, Arrington took her dog, Bleu Ginger, for a bike ride along Santa Monica’s Main Street before stopping at [Augie’s on Main](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Augie%27s_on_Main), a casual restaurant owned by her mentor, chef [Josiah Citrin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Josiah_Citrin). There, she enjoyed a fried dirty chicken sandwich before heading to the beach.

## Collard Green Lasagna with Issa Rae

The episode’s highlight came at Somerville in South Central Los Angeles, where Arrington joined Issa Rae to prepare a collard green lasagna. The dish blends traditional Southern flavors with Italian techniques, showcasing a creative twist on classic comfort food. Rae, known for her role in *Insecure*, shared her enthusiasm for the recipe while collaborating with Arrington in the kitchen.

Arrington also visited Somerville’s culinary director, [Geter Atienza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geter_Atienza), who introduced her to innovative dishes that highlight the restaurant’s modern approach to Southern cuisine. The segment underscores the growing influence of South Central L.A.’s food scene, blending heritage recipes with contemporary techniques.

The Plateworthy episode emphasizes Arrington’s connection to her roots while exploring Los Angeles’ diverse culinary landscape. From sweet pastries to savory comfort food, the series highlights the city’s rich food culture through Arrington’s personal journey. The final episode concludes her California tour, offering viewers a taste of L.A.’s most iconic and emerging food destinations.

For food enthusiasts, the episode serves as both inspiration and entertainment, showcasing the creativity of L.A.’s chefs and the city’s evolving dining scene.
