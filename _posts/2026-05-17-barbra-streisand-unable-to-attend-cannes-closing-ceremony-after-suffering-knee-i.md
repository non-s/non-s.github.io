Legendary singer and actress Barbra Streisand [Barbra Streisand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barbra_Streisand) won’t be at the closing ceremony of the 2024 Cannes Film Festival after sustaining a knee injury. The 82-year-old confirmed the news in a statement released Sunday, saying she was disappointed to miss the event she’s attended before. "I was also very much looking forward to spending time with colleagues whom I so admire—and, of course, returning to France, a place I have always loved," she wrote. The festival runs through May 26, with the closing ceremony marking the end of the 12-day event in [Cannes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cannes), France.

## Streisand’s statement reveals frustration and fondness for France
Streisand’s words carried a mix of regret and warmth for the country where she’s performed and filmed over the decades. She thanked fans for their support and didn’t specify how severe the injury is, but said she’s working with doctors to recover quickly. Cannes is one of the film industry’s biggest stages, and her absence will be noticeable. The closing ceremony typically features a red carpet arrival, speeches, and the announcement of the Palme d’Or winner—the festival’s top prize.

## Cannes organizers confirm her withdrawal
A spokesperson for the [Festival de Cannes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Festival_de_Cannes) told The Hollywood Reporter that Streisand’s team notified them of her injury and confirmed she wouldn’t attend. Organizers said they respected her decision and wished her a speedy recovery. Cannes is a high-profile event, and celebrities often make last-minute changes to their plans. This year’s lineup includes films like *The Second Act* and *Anora*, with stars like Léa Seydoux and Sean Penn expected to walk the red carpet on Wednesday.

## Streisand’s health takes center stage amid festival buzz
Her injury comes as the festival is in full swing, with daily screenings, parties, and media coverage dominating headlines. Streisand, who last attended Cannes in 2019 for the premiere of *The King and I*, has been a fixture in the entertainment world for over 60 years. She’s known for her vocal performances, acting roles, and directing credits, including *Yentl* and *The Prince of Tides*. Fans and colleagues have already taken to social media to send well-wishes, with many hoping she’ll recover in time for future projects.

## What happens next for Streisand and Cannes
Streisand’s team hasn’t announced whether she’ll return to France for later festival events, but her absence from the closing ceremony is final. The festival wraps up on May 26 with the awards and closing film. Meanwhile, the film industry will have to adjust to her temporary absence, though her legacy in music and film remains untouched. Cannes continues to draw global attention, and organizers are focused on delivering a memorable finale.

Streisand’s fans can still catch her work on streaming platforms, where her films and music catalogs are widely available. The injury is a reminder that even icons aren’t immune to setbacks, but her influence in Hollywood shows no signs of fading.
