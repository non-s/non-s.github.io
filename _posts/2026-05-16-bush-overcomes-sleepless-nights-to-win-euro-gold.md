Great Britain’s Matt Bush claimed Paralympic-tkdw European gold in para-taekwondo Saturday, overcoming exhaustion just eight weeks after his second child’s birth to deliver a commanding performance.

Bush, 32, defeated Turkey’s [Burak Gürpınar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burak_G%C3%BCrp%C3%BCnar) 24-5 in the men’s K44 +75kg final at the Manchester Regional Arena, securing the first-ever British gold in para-taekwondo at the European championships. The victory followed months of disrupted sleep and training adjustments, as Bush balanced fatherhood with elite competition.

## ‘Sleep no longer a luxury’

Speaking after the final, Bush admitted the past two months had been the hardest of his career. “Sleep is no longer a luxury I can afford,” he said. “I trained when I could, often in 20-minute bursts between nappy changes and feeds. The level of focus required was immense, but the motivation was simple: to give my family a reason to be proud.”

His wife, Sarah, watched from the stands as Bush secured gold, holding their six-week-old son. The family had initially planned to travel to Manchester together, but Bush’s selection led to a late change of plans. “She told me to go and win it for us,” Bush said. “That’s exactly what I did.”

## Para-taekwondo’s growing profile

The sport, governed by [World Taekwondo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Taekwondo), has seen increased investment and visibility in recent years, with para categories introduced in 2013. Bush’s win marks a milestone for British para-athletes, who have historically lagged behind nations like Turkey and Iran in the discipline.

British Paralympic Association performance director [Tony Way](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tony_Way) praised Bush’s resilience. “Matt’s journey shows what can be achieved with determination and support,” Way said. “His gold is a testament to his work ethic and the growing strength of para-sport in this country.”

## Next stop: Paris Paralympics

Bush’s victory secures his qualification for the Paris 2024 Paralympic Games, where he will compete in the K44 +75kg category. He faces immediate pressure to defend his title on the world stage, but Bush remains focused on the process. “Paris is the target, but I’m taking it one step at a time,” he said. “Right now, I’m just enjoying this moment with my family.”

The European championships in Manchester drew over 300 athletes from 30 nations, with para-taekwondo making its third appearance as a medal sport. Bush’s gold follows silver in the same category at the 2022 World Championships, signaling his progression toward podium contention on the global stage.
