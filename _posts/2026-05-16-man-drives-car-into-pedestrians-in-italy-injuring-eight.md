A driver plowed into a group of pedestrians in central Italy on Thursday, injuring eight people, four of them critically, before bystanders chased him down and held him until police arrived. The incident unfolded in the town of [Verona](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Verona) around midday, with witnesses describing a chaotic scene as the car veered onto a sidewalk crowded with shoppers and tourists.

Local police confirmed the attack occurred at approximately 11:45 a.m. local time near the historic center of Verona, a city known for its Roman ruins and [Shakespearean ties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Verona). Authorities did not immediately release the driver’s name or details about his vehicle, but said he was taken into custody without further incident. The injured pedestrians were rushed to nearby hospitals, where medical staff treated injuries ranging from fractures to head trauma.

## Witnesses describe panic as car hits crowd

Eyewitnesses recounted the moments before and after the crash, describing a sudden screech of tires followed by screams as the car struck people standing near a popular café. One witness, who asked not to be named, said the driver appeared to be accelerating as he turned onto the sidewalk, hitting at least six people before braking sharply. Security footage from nearby businesses captured the vehicle’s path, showing it swerving through the crowd before coming to a stop.

Police spokesperson [Captain Marco Rossi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_Police) told reporters the driver remained at the scene, cooperative but visibly shaken. Rossi said preliminary checks indicated the vehicle was a small sedan, though he declined to speculate on whether mechanical failure or driver error caused the crash. Authorities have not ruled out any potential causes, including intoxication or deliberate action.

## Investigators focus on driver’s background and vehicle

Italian authorities launched a full investigation into the incident, with forensic teams examining the car for signs of tampering or mechanical issues. Prosecutors in Verona confirmed they would pursue charges pending the outcome of the probe, though they did not specify potential charges. Local media reported the driver, identified only as a 34-year-old man from the region, had no prior criminal record.

Italian Prime Minister [Giorgia Meloni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giorgia_Meloni) offered condolences to the victims in a statement released Thursday evening, calling the attack an "act of senseless violence." Meloni pledged full support for local authorities while urging calm as details emerged. The government has faced criticism in recent years over public safety measures following a rise in pedestrian accidents in urban areas.

Italian traffic safety advocates renewed calls for stricter enforcement of speed limits and pedestrian zones, particularly in historic city centers where narrow streets and crowded sidewalks pose risks. The Verona incident adds to a series of high-profile vehicle attacks in Europe, prompting renewed debates on infrastructure and security measures in tourist-heavy locations.

Authorities have not yet released the names of the injured or their conditions. The driver remains in police custody as prosecutors build their case. Further updates are expected in the coming days as forensic results and surveillance footage are analyzed.
