## Introduction to Iran's Internet Access. Iranians have had limited internet access during the ongoing war, with the government now looking at tiered services. It is unclear how regular internet connections jeopardise national security, but others sold by the government do not. The move is seen as an attempt to control the flow of information and maintain national security, while also providing some level of internet access to approved individuals and entities. ## Iran's Tiered Internet Access Model. President Masoud Pezeshkian on Wednesday announced the creation of a new entity to review internet coverage in the country named the Specialised Headquarters for Organising and Guiding Iran’s Cyberspace, with First Vice President Mohammad Reza Aref, a relative moderate, appointed as its head. Pezeshkian said he expects the 74-year-old vice president to create institutional cohesion and align policies and measures by relevant bodies, and prevent parallel work and end multiple voices in the management of the country’s cyberspace. This new entity will play a crucial role in overseeing the implementation of the tiered internet access model, which will provide limited connectivity to approved individuals and entities. ## Impact on Digital Rights. The introduction of a tiered internet access model has raised concerns about the digital rights of Iranians. The model is seen as a way to control the flow of information and maintain national security, but it also undermines the digital rights of Iranians. The move is also seen as a way to restrict access to information and limit the ability of Iranians to express themselves online. The impact of this move on the digital rights of Iranians will be closely watched, as it has the potential to further restrict access to information and limit the ability of Iranians to express themselves online.
