KYIV, Ukraine — A coordinated Russian drone and missile assault struck Ukraine’s capital Kyiv early Thursday, leaving at least one dead and 31 injured, according to local authorities. The attack triggered widespread air raid alerts and sent residents scrambling for shelter as explosions echoed across the city. Damage assessments were still underway late Thursday, with officials warning the toll could rise.

Ukraine’s Air Force reported intercepting multiple incoming threats, including cruise missiles and drones, but confirmed several strikes hit civilian and industrial zones. Kyiv Mayor [Vitali Klitschko](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitali_Klitschko) said emergency services were responding to multiple incidents across the city, including fires and structural damage. “We are assessing the full scale of destruction,” Klitschko told a press briefing. “Safety of civilians remains our top priority.”

## Strikes target critical infrastructure and residential areas

Witnesses described powerful blasts near energy facilities, residential buildings, and transportation hubs. Ukrainian energy operator [Ukrenergo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ukrenergo) reported localized power outages in several districts but said the grid remained stable. Traffic lights and street lighting were knocked out in some areas, causing delays and gridlock.

Russia’s Defense Ministry claimed the strikes targeted “military command centers and weapons depots,” but independent verification was not immediately available. Ukraine’s Air Force dismissed the claim as propaganda, stating the attack was designed to terrorize civilians and disrupt daily life.

## International response and humanitarian impact

The United Nations condemned the attack, calling it a violation of international law. UN High Commissioner for Human Rights spokesperson [Ravina Shamdasani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ravina_Shamdasani) urged an immediate end to attacks on civilian infrastructure. The European Union’s foreign policy chief [Josep Borrell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Josep_Borrell) expressed solidarity with Ukraine, reaffirming EU support for Kyiv’s defense against Russian aggression.

Local hospitals reported treating 31 wounded, including children. Doctors described shrapnel wounds and blast injuries, with some patients in critical condition. Civil defense teams continued clearing debris and searching for survivors in rubble-strewn neighborhoods.

## What happens next?

Ukraine’s Air Force vowed to maintain vigilance, warning of potential follow-up strikes. President [Volodymyr Zelenskyy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volodymyr_Zelensky) is expected to address the nation later Thursday. The attack underscores the escalating intensity of Russia’s aerial campaign as winter approaches, raising concerns about energy supply disruptions and civilian safety.
