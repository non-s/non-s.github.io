Billionaire investor Bill Ackman’s Pershing Square Capital Management disclosed a new position in Microsoft on Friday, revealing the purchase was made during the first quarter amid broader market declines. The move signals Ackman’s confidence in Microsoft’s artificial intelligence and cloud computing segments, which have driven recent earnings strength despite macroeconomic headwinds.

## Microsoft remains top cloud player despite stock volatility
Microsoft’s [cloud division](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/investor) continues to dominate the market, with Azure maintaining a lead over Amazon Web Services and Google Cloud. The company reported 31% year-over-year revenue growth in its cloud segment last quarter, fueled by enterprise demand for AI tools and enterprise software. Despite recent stock fluctuations tied to broader tech valuations, Microsoft’s long-term growth narrative remains intact, analysts say.

Ackman, known for activist investing and concentrated bets, has historically favored stable, cash-flow-generative businesses. His investment in Microsoft aligns with that strategy, given the company’s recurring revenue model through cloud subscriptions and software licenses. The stake comes as tech stocks rebounded in late 2023 and early 2024 following a 2022 sell-off, with Microsoft among the top beneficiaries.

## First-quarter buys amid broader market downturn
Pershing Square purchased Microsoft shares during a period of market stress, when many investors pulled back from growth-oriented tech stocks. The first quarter saw increased volatility driven by inflation concerns, rising interest rates, and geopolitical tensions. Despite the backdrop, Ackman’s firm added to its position, suggesting a belief that Microsoft’s fundamentals outweigh short-term macro pressures.

Microsoft’s recent earnings report highlighted continued strength in its commercial cloud business, which now generates over $110 billion in annualized revenue. The company’s AI integration across products like Office 365 and Azure has also positioned it as a leader in enterprise AI adoption. These factors likely influenced Ackman’s decision to build a stake during the downturn.

## Investor confidence in Microsoft persists
Ackman is not alone in backing Microsoft. Other prominent investors, including [T. Rowe Price](https://www.troweprice.com) and [Capital Group](https://www.capitalgroup.com), have also increased their holdings in recent quarters. The company’s market cap recently surpassed $3 trillion, making it one of the most valuable publicly traded companies in the world.

The tech giant’s dominance in cloud infrastructure and enterprise software has made it a core holding for many institutional investors. Its ability to monetize AI through existing product suites—such as Copilot and Azure AI services—further cements its role as a long-term growth engine for the broader market.

## What’s next for Microsoft and Pershing Square
Looking ahead, Microsoft is expected to continue expanding its AI capabilities, with potential new product launches and integrations across its ecosystem. Analysts anticipate further cloud growth, driven by global digital transformation initiatives. For Pershing Square, the Microsoft stake reflects a broader shift toward high-quality tech names that can weather economic uncertainty while benefiting from structural trends like AI adoption.

The investment also underscores Ackman’s willingness to go against market sentiment during periods of volatility, a strategy that has paid off in past cycles. Whether this bet will outperform depends on Microsoft’s execution in AI and cloud, as well as broader macroeconomic conditions. Investors will closely watch the company’s next earnings report, due in late April, for updates on growth trends.
