A brief diet change may slow or even reverse aging at the cellular level, according to findings published in *Aging* journal. Researchers tracked 40 adults over four weeks and found their biological ages dropped by an average of 2.5 years. The diet focused on whole foods, high fiber, and minimal processed items. Lead author [Dr. Kara Fitzgerald](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Fitzgerald) said the results exceeded expectations, calling the shift ‘unprecedented’ in human studies of aging.

The study, led by [The Institute for Functional Medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institute_for_Functional_Medicine), measured epigenetic markers tied to aging. Participants followed a strict plant-based protocol rich in vegetables, nuts, seeds, and limited animal products. After the trial, tests showed reduced DNA methylation—an indicator of slowed cellular aging. Fitzgerald emphasized the diet’s simplicity, noting no supplements or extreme restrictions were required to achieve measurable results.

## Dietary Changes Show Unexpected Longevity Benefits
The findings challenge prior assumptions that aging reversal requires decades-long lifestyle overhauls. Instead, the four-week intervention produced rapid biological changes. Researchers observed improvements in inflammation markers, gut health, and metabolic function among participants. Some individuals saw their biological age drop by as much as four years, though results varied by person.

The diet mirrored regimens linked to longevity in centenarian populations, such as those in [Okinawa, Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Okinawa_Prefecture), known for high life expectancy. Fitzgerald’s team plans to expand the study to 100 participants, testing whether longer adherence yields greater benefits. Early feedback suggests the diet’s flexibility—allowing moderate animal products—improved compliance compared to stricter vegan plans.

## What’s Next for Aging Research?
While promising, experts caution the study’s small sample size limits broader conclusions. The team is now exploring whether similar diets could benefit older adults or those with chronic conditions. Fitzgerald stressed the need for replication in larger, diverse groups to confirm the findings. If validated, the approach could offer a low-cost, accessible anti-aging strategy.

The research adds to growing evidence that diet shapes biological age faster than previously thought. Unlike genetic factors, which account for only 20-30% of aging, lifestyle choices appear to play a dominant role. The study’s simplicity—requiring no expensive interventions—could make it widely adoptable. For now, Fitzgerald advises gradual dietary adjustments over strict overhauls, emphasizing sustainability over short-term results.

O estudo, que acompanhou 40 adultos durante um mês, mostrou que pequenas mudanças na dieta foram capazes de reduzir sinais de envelhecimento celular, levantando a esperança de que o tempo não precisa ser nosso inimigo incontrolável.

Para os leitores de língua portuguesa, que muitas vezes enfrentam desafios semelhantes em relação à saúde pública e ao acesso a alimentos nutritivos, o estudo reforça a importância de revisitar hábitos à mesa, mesmo que de forma temporária.

## 🇪🇸 Resumen en Español

Un equipo de científicos ha descubierto que modificar la dieta durante solo cuatro semanas podría revertir algunos signos del envejecimiento celular, según un estudio pionero que abre nuevas esperanzas en la lucha contra los efectos del tiempo.

La investigación, realizada con 40 adultos, midió cambios en marcadores biológicos como la longitud de los telómeros —asociados al envejecimiento— y la expresión genética. Aunque los resultados son preliminares, sugieren que una alimentación más saludable, rica en nutrientes y baja en ultraprocesados, podría influir directamente en procesos celulares clave. Para el público hispanohablante, este hallazgo refuerza la importancia de hábitos alimenticios conscientes, no solo como herramienta contra enfermedades crónicas, sino también como posible estrategia antienvejecimiento accesible y al alcance de la mano.
