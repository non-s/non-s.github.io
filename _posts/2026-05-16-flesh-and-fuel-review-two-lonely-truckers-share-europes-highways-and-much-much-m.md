French director Pierre Le Gall’s debut feature ‘Flesh and Fuel’ premiered as a special screening in Cannes Critics’ Week on May 15. The film stars Alexis Manenti, known for his role in [Les Misérables](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Les_Misérables_(2019_film)), as one of the two central characters navigating Europe’s highways in an unlikely road romance.

The film explores themes of loneliness and connection through the unlikely bond between two truckers. Set against the backdrop of Europe’s vast network of highways, ‘Flesh and Fuel’ blends romance with the gritty realities of long-haul trucking. Critics at Cannes praised the film’s emotional depth and Manenti’s performance, highlighting his ability to convey vulnerability and strength.

## A fresh voice in French cinema
Pierre Le Gall, a former cinematographer, makes his directorial debut with ‘Flesh and Fuel.’ The film marks a departure from his previous work, which primarily focused on documentary-style storytelling. Le Gall’s transition to fiction filmmaking has drawn attention for its bold visual style and intimate portrayal of its characters.

The film’s title, ‘Flesh and Fuel,’ reflects its dual focus on human connection and the mechanical world of trucking. Critics noted the film’s balance between raw emotion and the physical demands of the road, creating a narrative that feels both personal and universal.

## Cannes reception and audience response
Early screenings of ‘Flesh and Fuel’ in Cannes Critics’ Week generated positive buzz, with many praising its authentic depiction of trucking culture. Audiences and critics alike highlighted the chemistry between Manenti and his co-star, whose portrayal of a veteran trucker adds layers to the film’s central relationship.

The film’s release comes at a time when European cinema continues to explore themes of isolation and community. ‘Flesh and Fuel’ joins a growing lineup of films that use road narratives to examine human connections across vast distances.

French distributor Wild Bunch will handle the film’s international sales, with discussions underway for a wider theatrical release later this year. The film’s Cannes premiere signals a potential breakout for Le Gall and Manenti, who are both poised for greater recognition in international cinema.
