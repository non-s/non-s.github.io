Russia struck Ukraine with one of its most extensive aerial assaults in years on Thursday, killing at least 24 civilians and injuring dozens more as missiles and drones rained down on Kyiv and nearby regions. The barrage marked Moscow’s largest coordinated attack since 2020, according to Ukrainian officials, and came amid escalating frontline fighting and stalled peace negotiations. Ukrainian President [Volodymyr Zelenskyy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volodymyr_Zelensky) called the attack a deliberate attempt to terrorize civilians, while Kyiv’s mayor reported widespread power outages and damage to critical infrastructure.

## Heavy civilian toll in Kyiv and beyond
The assault began before dawn, with air raid sirens blaring across the capital as explosions rocked residential neighborhoods and commercial districts. Emergency services reported at least 24 dead and 70 injured, with numbers expected to rise as rescue teams combed through rubble for survivors. Hospitals in Kyiv were overwhelmed, and temporary morgues were set up to handle the dead. In neighboring [Lviv](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lviv), authorities said drones struck an industrial zone, causing fires and forcing evacuations. Regional governors reported similar damage in [Dnipropetrovsk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dnipropetrovsk_Oblast) and [Odesa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odesa), where power grids were damaged.

Ukrainian authorities described the attack as a mix of high-precision missiles and Soviet-era drones, some of which were intercepted by air defenses. [Kyiv’s mayor, Vitali Klitschko](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitali_Klitschko), said at least three residential buildings were destroyed, including a high-rise apartment complex in the [Darnytskyi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Darnytsia) district. Videos shared on social media showed smoldering wreckage and residents sifting through debris for belongings. The attack disrupted train services and forced temporary closures of schools and businesses.

## International response: EU rallies behind Ukraine
European Union leaders swiftly condemned the strike, with [Ursula von der Leyen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ursula_von_der_Leyen), president of the [European Commission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Commission), calling it a "brutal escalation." She pledged further military and humanitarian aid, including air defense systems. [NATO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NATO) Secretary General [Jens Stoltenberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jens_Stoltenberg) reiterated the alliance’s backing for Ukraine, warning Moscow of "severe consequences" for targeting civilians. The U.S. State Department also issued a statement supporting Kyiv and urging de-escalation.

## Escalating conflict amid stalled talks
The attack follows months of intensified fighting along the front lines, where Ukrainian forces have struggled to push back Russian advances. Peace talks have stalled, with both sides accusing the other of violating ceasefire agreements. Analysts say Moscow may be attempting to pressure Ukraine into concessions ahead of winter, when energy shortages could further strain Kyiv’s defenses. Ukrainian officials accused Russia of targeting civilian infrastructure to undermine morale, while Moscow claimed the strikes were aimed at military logistics.

As the death toll rises, Ukrainian rescue teams and volunteers continue clearing rubble and assisting survivors. Power restoration efforts are underway, but officials warn of prolonged blackouts as repair crews work through damaged networks. The international community is expected to hold an emergency session at the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations) to address the escalation. For now, Kyiv and other affected regions brace for further attacks as tensions show no signs of easing.
