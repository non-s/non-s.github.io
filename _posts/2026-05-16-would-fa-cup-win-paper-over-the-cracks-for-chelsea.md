Chelsea arrive at Wembley Saturday for the FA Cup final against Leeds United after one of their most turbulent seasons in recent memory. The club has cycled through three managers—Thomas Tuchel, Frank Lampard, and now Graham Potter—while enduring persistent fan protests over the team’s direction and ownership decisions. A victory over Leeds would deliver tangible success and could quiet growing unrest in the stands.


Fan discontent has been a recurring theme this season, with supporters staging protests at both home and away matches. Chants criticizing owner [Roman Abramovich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Abramovich) and calling for board changes have grown louder, reflecting frustration with the club’s stagnant league form and perceived lack of ambition. Despite finishing fifth last season, Chelsea have hovered mid-table in the Premier League this term, with just 42 points from 32 games as of April 2023.


The managerial carousel added to the instability. Tuchel was sacked in September 2022 after a poor start, with Lampard—Chelsea’s caretaker-turned-permanent boss—lasting only seven months before his own dismissal in April. Potter, brought in from Brighton, has stabilized the team but faces immense pressure to deliver silverware.


A win against Leeds would provide Potter his first major trophy as Chelsea manager and a morale boost for a club in need of stability. For Leeds, managed by [Jesse Marsch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesse_Marsch), the final offers a chance to prove their Premier League status and add another trophy to their history.


The stakes extend beyond the trophy itself. A victory for Chelsea could ease fan protests and restore confidence in the club’s leadership. Failure, however, risks deepening the divide between supporters and the board, potentially accelerating calls for change in the summer transfer window.


For both clubs, the financial and reputational benefits are significant. The FA Cup winner earns £2 million in prize money and qualification for next season’s Europa League group stage. More importantly, it offers a chance to end the season on a high note after months of uncertainty.
