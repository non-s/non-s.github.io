AI-powered marketing platform Nectar Social announced Thursday it raised $30 million in Series A funding led by [Menlo Ventures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Menlo_Ventures). The round includes participation from the Anthology Fund, a venture capital fund created alongside artificial intelligence lab Anthropic.

The funding will accelerate development of Nectar Social’s AI-driven marketing tools, which automate customer engagement and campaign management for enterprises. The platform uses natural language processing and machine learning to personalize interactions at scale across email, social media and chat channels.

Menlo Ventures partner Venky Ganesan will join Nectar Social’s board following the investment. Ganesan has backed companies including [Zendesk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zendesk), [Roblox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roblox) and [Guardant Health](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guardant_Health). The firm led the round with participation from existing investors and new backers.

## AI tools target enterprise marketing teams

Nectar Social’s platform automates repetitive marketing tasks such as email drafting, social media scheduling and lead qualification. The company claims its AI reduces campaign setup time by 70% while improving conversion rates through hyper-personalized messaging.

The startup serves primarily mid-market and enterprise clients, including [Shopify](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shopify) merchants and [Salesforce](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salesforce) users. Competitors in the space include Adobe’s Marketo and Salesforce’s Pardot, but Nectar Social emphasizes its AI-first approach as a differentiator.

## Menlo’s Anthology Fund invests in AI collaboration

The Anthology Fund, launched in 2023 by Menlo Ventures and Anthropic, focuses on AI infrastructure and enterprise applications. This investment marks one of the fund’s first major deployments in marketing technology.

Anthropic, creator of the AI model [Claude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Claude_(AI_assistant)), has increasingly invested in companies applying its technology to business use cases. The fund’s collaboration with Menlo Ventures aims to bridge AI research with practical enterprise tools.

## Next steps for Nectar Social

The company plans to expand its engineering team and scale customer acquisition in North America and Europe. Additional funding may be directed toward expanding language model integrations to support more international markets.

Nectar Social co-founder and CEO Adam Guild emphasized the need for AI to reduce marketing workloads. “Teams spend 80% of their time on repetitive tasks,” Guild said. “Our goal is to let marketers focus on strategy, not spreadsheets.”

The Series A brings Nectar Social’s total funding to $42 million since its 2022 launch. The company competes with startups like [Jasper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jasper_AI) and [Copy.ai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copy.ai) in the AI marketing space but targets larger organizations with its enterprise-grade platform.
