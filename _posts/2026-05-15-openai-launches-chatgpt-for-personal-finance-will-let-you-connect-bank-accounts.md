OpenAI has expanded [ChatGPT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT) with new personal finance capabilities, allowing users to link bank accounts for real-time tracking of spending, subscriptions and upcoming bills. The feature, available immediately, provides a unified dashboard showing portfolio performance, monthly cash flow and transaction histories across linked financial accounts.

The update follows OpenAI’s broader push to integrate ChatGPT into everyday workflows beyond text generation. Users can now authorize secure connections to bank accounts through third-party financial data providers, enabling the AI to analyze spending patterns and highlight recurring expenses. The system flags subscriptions set to renew and warns about upcoming payments to prevent overdrafts or missed deadlines.

## How the new finance tool works
Once a user links a checking, savings or investment account, ChatGPT processes transactions and categorizes them automatically. Spending is grouped by type—groceries, utilities, entertainment—and compared against monthly budgets. The AI generates plain-language summaries like, “You spent 15% more on dining out this month than last month,” and suggests ways to cut costs based on spending trends.

OpenAI says the tool prioritizes privacy and security. Bank connections use encrypted APIs compliant with [PSD2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Payment_Services_Directive) standards in Europe and [Regulation E](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regulation_E) in the U.S. No login credentials are stored; instead, data flows directly from financial institutions to ChatGPT through authorized aggregators such as [Plaid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plaid_(company)) or [Yodlee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yodlee).

## What users can expect
Early testers report the tool helps identify forgotten subscriptions and duplicate charges. One user in California discovered a $29 monthly gym membership still charging her two years after canceling in person. Another found three overlapping streaming services totaling $48 per month. The AI flagged both cases within hours of syncing accounts.

## Broader implications for AI and finance
The move positions ChatGPT as a central hub for financial oversight, competing with apps like [Mint](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mint_(personal_finance_software)) and [Personal Capital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_Capital). While these platforms offer similar dashboards, OpenAI’s conversational interface allows users to ask questions like, “When is my rent due?” or “How much did I spend on groceries last quarter?” and receive instant, natural-language answers.

Industry watchers say the feature could accelerate adoption of AI-driven financial assistants. Banks and credit unions may soon integrate ChatGPT directly into their own apps, turning AI into a first-line customer service tool for fraud alerts and budgeting tips. Analysts at [CB Insights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CB_Insights) project the global market for AI financial assistants will reach $10 billion by 2028, driven by demand for real-time insights and proactive money management.
