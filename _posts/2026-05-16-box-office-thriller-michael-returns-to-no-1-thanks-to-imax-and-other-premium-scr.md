Lionsgate’s *Michael*, the biopic about [Michael Jackson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Jackson), reclaimed the top spot at the North American box office over the weekend, buoyed by demand for Imax and premium large-format screenings. The film’s resurgence pushed it back to the No. 1 position, overtaking *The Devil Wears Prada 2*, which had held the lead for two consecutive weekends. Globally, *The Devil Wears Prada 2* crossed the $500 million mark on Friday, solidifying its strong performance worldwide.

## Premium screens drive ‘Michael’ back to top
The return of *Michael* to the No. 1 spot was fueled by a 47% jump in Imax and premium screen ticket sales, according to studio data. Analysts noted that the film’s nostalgic appeal and expanded screenings contributed to its rebound. ‘Michael’ had initially slipped to No. 2 last weekend before staging a comeback, proving its enduring draw among fans of the late pop icon.

Curry Barker’s horror film *Obsession* opened in third place with a weekend gross of $15 million, marking a competitive debut for the genre in a crowded summer slate. The film’s opening was bolstered by strong word-of-mouth and strategic marketing targeting horror audiences. Industry trackers reported that *Obsession* outperformed expectations, securing a solid foothold in the top three.

*Obsession* stars rising talent and marks director Curry Barker’s follow-up to his 2022 horror hit *Disturbing Behavior*. The film’s marketing emphasized its twist-heavy plot and high-stakes suspense, resonating with fans of the genre. Box office analysts said the strong opening reflected pent-up demand for horror content after a slower-than-expected summer season.

## Global box office milestones
*The Devil Wears Prada 2*, the sequel to the 2006 fashion industry satire, surpassed $500 million in global ticket sales on Friday, becoming one of the summer’s most profitable releases. The film’s international appeal, particularly in Europe and Asia, has driven its sustained box office momentum. Studio executives credited a mix of star power, franchise nostalgia, and broad market appeal for the milestone.

The weekend’s box office results highlighted the divergent fortunes of different genres. While *Michael* and *Obsession* leaned into niche audiences, *The Devil Wears Prada 2* broadened its appeal across demographic lines. Industry insiders said the contrast underscored the importance of targeted marketing and screen availability in determining box office success.

Looking ahead, *Michael* is expected to maintain its lead through the week, supported by continued premium screenings. *Obsession* will face competition from new releases, including a family film and a sci-fi sequel, in the coming weeks. Analysts said the holiday weekend could further shake up the rankings as audiences weigh their summer options.
