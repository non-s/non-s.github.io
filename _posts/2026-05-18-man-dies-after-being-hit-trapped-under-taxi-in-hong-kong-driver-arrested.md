A 60- to 70-year-old man died Monday after a taxi struck him and trapped him beneath the vehicle in Hong Kong’s Tung Chung district. The driver was arrested on suspicion of dangerous driving causing death, police confirmed Tuesday. This marks the second fatal crash involving a taxi in Hong Kong in less than a week, raising fresh concerns about road safety in the city. Police received the first report at 6:23am Monday at the intersection of Man Tung Road and Kin Tung Road, just outside the Caribbean Coast [residential estate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caribbean_Coast).

Officers arrived to find the victim trapped under the taxi’s wheels. Emergency services worked to free him, but he was pronounced dead at the scene. The taxi driver, a 52-year-old man, remained at the site and was taken into custody without resistance. Police did not release his name, and no further details about the driver’s background were provided.

## Police confirm investigation into driver’s actions
The case has been handed to Hong Kong’s Commercial Crime Bureau, which will investigate whether the driver’s actions met the threshold for dangerous driving causing death. Authorities say the investigation could take weeks, depending on witness statements and forensic evidence. Neighbors near the crash site told reporters the area is usually busy with morning traffic, especially around schools and commuter routes.

## Second taxi fatality in days
This is the second fatal crash involving a taxi in Hong Kong in less than a week. Last Wednesday, a 72-year-old woman died after being hit by a taxi in Tsuen Wan. In that case, the driver was also arrested on suspicion of dangerous driving. The back-to-back incidents have prompted calls from local advocacy groups for stricter oversight of taxi drivers and better road safety measures in high-traffic areas.

## Community reaction and calls for change
Residents near the Tung Chung crash site expressed shock and sadness. "This street is always busy in the morning," said one local shopkeeper who asked not to be named. "People walk to the MTR station, kids go to school. Something has to change." Hong Kong’s taxi drivers’ union issued a statement expressing condolences to the victim’s family and said it would cooperate with authorities. The union also urged drivers to slow down in residential and school zones.

Authorities have not announced any immediate changes to traffic rules following the crash. But transport officials say they are reviewing camera footage from the area to assess whether additional safety measures are needed. The investigation continues, and no charges have been filed yet. The taxi driver remains in police custody as detectives piece together what happened that morning.
