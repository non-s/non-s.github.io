The lights dimmed in Amsterdam’s Johan Cruijff Arena Wednesday night, and Harry Styles strolled onstage to screams that rattled the rafters. “I got older and the stage got bigger,” he deadpanned, a line that summed up the night’s energy. His first full stadium tour since 2019—[Together, Together Tour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Styles:_Live_on_Tour)—opened with a bang, packing 50,000 fans into an arena that felt tailor-made for his soaring vocals and playful stage presence. The show didn’t just feel like a comeback. It felt like a coronation.

## A night of hits, surprises, and style

Styles didn’t waste time. He opened with “Golden,” a song that’s become a live staple, its brass-heavy beat kicking off a set that balanced hits like “Watermelon Sugar” and “As It Was” with deeper cuts such as “Treat People With Kindness.” The crowd sang every word, a testament to how deeply his music has embedded itself in pop culture. Between songs, he cracked jokes, swapped stories, and even paused to fix a fan’s phone after it flew into the audience mid-performance. It was the kind of moment that made the spectacle feel intimate, even in a space this large.

The real surprise came during the encore. After a rousing rendition of “Late Night Talking,” the band segued into an unexpected “Watermelon Sugar.” The crowd erupted, and Styles grinned, clearly delighted by the reaction. It was a reminder that even after years on the road, he still loves pulling rabbits out of his hat. The Amsterdam crowd got an extra treat when he segued into a stripped-down acoustic version of “Kiwi,” a song that rarely gets the full treatment in stadiums.

## The stage that grew with him

Styles’ last big tour, [Love On Tour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Styles:_Love_On_Tour), played arenas. Tonight’s show in Amsterdam was a full stadium affair, with a massive LED screen above the stage that flickered with vibrant colors and psychedelic visuals. The staging—designed by [Es Devlin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Es_Devlin)—felt like a living collage, shifting with the music’s mood. It was a far cry from the smaller venues where he started his solo career, a point Styles didn’t shy away from.

“I remember playing clubs where the stage was maybe five feet wide,” he told the crowd mid-show. “Now look at this.” The contrast wasn’t just physical. It was emotional. The Amsterdam crowd, many of whom had followed him from his One Direction days, seemed to sense they were watching history—a moment where artistry meets scale in the most public way possible.

## What’s next for Styles and his tour

The Amsterdam opener is just the first of 50+ dates across Europe and North America through 2025. Fans in cities like London, New York, and Los Angeles will get their turn, but the Amsterdam show set the bar high. For Styles, it’s a chance to prove he’s more than a pop star with a flair for fashion. It’s a chance to show he’s a performer who can fill a stadium and still make each person in the crowd feel like he’s singing just for them.

The tour’s setlist changes nightly, but expect more surprises. Styles has a habit of throwing in deep cuts or unreleased tracks when he feels like it. And with a stage this big, the visuals will only get more ambitious. If the Amsterdam crowd is any indication, he’s got a lot to prove—and a lot to celebrate.

O show de abertura da *Together, Together Tour* de Harry Styles em Amsterdã não decepcionou: mais de 10 mil fãs lotaram o Johan Cruijff Arena para testemunhar o início de uma turnê que promete ser histórica, com direito a um palco gigantesco e performances vocais que redefiniram a energia dos concertos pop. Com visual andrógino e hits como *"As It Was"* e *"Watermelon Sugar"*, o artista britânico mostrou por que continua sendo um dos nomes mais influentes da música atual, unindo nostalgia dos anos 1970 a uma estética moderna que conquista audiências mundo afora.

No Brasil, a turnê de Styles ganha ainda mais relevância porque chega em um momento em que o mercado de shows brasileiros busca recuperar o fôlego após a pandemia, com artistas nacionais e internacionais disputando a atenção do público. Além disso, o sucesso do cantor entre os brasileiros — que já lotaram ingressos de suas apresentações anteriores em São Paulo e Rio — reforça a força do pop internacional no país, especialmente entre jovens que consomem não só a música, mas também a moda e o estilo associados a ele. A parada brasileira, prevista para outubro, deve movimentar não só a economia local, mas também as redes sociais, onde os fãs já especulam sobre figurinos e surpresas no repertório.

Se até agora a turnê tem mostrado que Styles não está disposto a perder o posto de superstar, a expectativa é que o Brasil seja palco de um espetáculo à altura — com direito a lotação máxima e, quem sabe, um novo hit viral inspirado na energia tropical do público.
