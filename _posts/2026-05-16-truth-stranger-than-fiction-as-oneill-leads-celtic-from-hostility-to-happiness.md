Martin O'Neill made a bold prediction in March: Celtic would have to win all seven of their remaining games to secure the Scottish Premiership title. On Sunday, he was proven right as his side completed a flawless run to finish nine points ahead of second-placed Aberdeen. The victory marks O'Neill’s first major trophy since joining Celtic in the summer of 2022, capping a remarkable turnaround from early season struggles.

## O'Neill’s plan delivers despite early doubts
The former [Northern Ireland national football team](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Ireland_national_football_team) manager took charge of a side that had started the season poorly, with criticism mounting over his tactics and squad selections. But a tactical shift and improved form in front of goal saw Celtic climb the table. O'Neill’s insistence on a seven-game winning streak was met with skepticism, yet his players delivered exactly that, including victories over Rangers and Hearts.

The run began with a 2-0 win at St Mirren on March 30, sparking a sequence that included draws against Hibs and Motherwell but culminated in dominant displays against top rivals. Celtic’s 3-1 victory over Aberdeen in April extended their lead, while a 2-0 win over Rangers in the Scottish Cup semifinal reinforced their momentum. The title was mathematically secured with two games to spare, ending Aberdeen’s challenge under interim boss Barry Robson.

## Squad depth and key players key to title push
Celtic’s success was built on contributions from multiple players, with forwards Kyogo Furuhashi and Luis Palma leading the line. Midfielders Callum McGregor and Reo Hatate controlled games from deep, while captain Callum McGregor’s leadership steadied the team during pressure periods. O'Neill’s rotation policy also proved crucial, with youngsters like [Adam Idah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adam_Idah) and [Tomoki Iwata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tomoki_Iwata) stepping up when needed.

Defensively, goalkeeper Joe Hart delivered consistent performances, while the backline tightened as the season progressed. O'Neill’s ability to rotate without disrupting the team’s rhythm set Celtic apart from rivals who struggled with squad fatigue. The title win is the latest in a long line for the Glasgow club, extending their domestic dominance under ownership tied to [Celtic F.C.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celtic_F.C.).

## What happens next for Celtic and O'Neill?
With the Premiership secured, Celtic now focus on the Scottish Cup final against Rangers, a chance to complete a domestic double. O'Neill faces a summer of squad overhaul as key players approach contract renewals. The title win solidifies his standing but raises expectations for European progress in the Champions League qualifiers. The club’s hierarchy will also assess whether this season’s tactical approach can sustain future challenges, particularly from Aberdeen and Rangers.
