A Russian missile strike on residential buildings in [Kyiv](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kyiv), Ukraine’s capital, killed 24 people on Thursday, including a 12-year-old girl whose father had died during the war. The attack targeted a densely populated area, flattening multiple apartments and sparking fires that took hours to contain. Ukrainian officials reported the strike used a high-precision missile, likely a [Kh-101](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kh-101) or similar model, launched from Russian airspace.

## Escalation amid ongoing conflict
Ukraine’s military said the strike was the deadliest single attack on Kyiv in months, with rescue workers still searching for survivors under rubble. Among the dead was Lyubava Yakovleva, 12, whose father, a soldier, was killed in 2022. Her mother, Olga Yakovleva, survived but was hospitalized with severe injuries. Ukrainian President [Volodymyr Zelenskyy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volodymyr_Zelenskyy) called the strike a ‘barbaric act’ and vowed retaliation.

The attack came as Ukraine and Russia finalized a prisoner exchange deal, with 94 Ukrainian soldiers and civilians expected to return home. The swap, mediated by [Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey) and [Saudi Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia), includes the release of 88 Russian prisoners. Both sides confirmed the agreement would proceed as scheduled, despite the fresh violence. The swap marks one of the largest exchanges since the war began in 2022.

## Global reactions to the strike
The [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations) and [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union) condemned the attack, calling it a violation of international law. U.S. Secretary of State [Antony Blinken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antony_Blinken) stated the strike demonstrated Russia’s disregard for civilian lives. Meanwhile, Russia’s defense ministry claimed the target was a military facility housing foreign mercenaries, a claim Ukraine and independent analysts dismissed as propaganda.

Kyiv’s mayor, [Vitali Klitschko](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitali_Klitschko), announced a citywide day of mourning on Friday. Residents gathered at makeshift memorials near the strike site, leaving flowers, toys, and handwritten notes. The attack has intensified fears of further escalation, with air raid sirens sounding across the city within hours of the strike. Ukrainian officials warned of potential new offensives targeting energy infrastructure.

The strike follows a pattern of increased Russian airstrikes on Ukrainian cities in recent weeks, as Moscow seeks to pressure Kyiv into negotiations. Analysts suggest the timing may aim to disrupt Ukraine’s wartime morale ahead of a planned counteroffensive. Ukrainian forces, however, have vowed to continue resisting, with Zelenskyy reaffirming commitment to reclaiming occupied territories. The prisoner swap, while a rare moment of progress, offers little relief amid the broader humanitarian crisis.
