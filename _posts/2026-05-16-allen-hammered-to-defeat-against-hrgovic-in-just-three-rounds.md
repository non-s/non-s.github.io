Filip Hrgovic [Hrgovic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Filip_Hrgovi%C4%87) left no doubt about who belonged in the ring Saturday night, dismantling British contender Dave Allen [Dave Allen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_Allen_(boxer)) inside three rounds at Doncaster’s Eco-Power Stadium. The Croatian heavyweight overwhelmed Allen from the opening bell, landing sharp, powerful shots that sent the hometown hero stumbling. Every time Allen tried to fire back, Hrgovic either slipped the punches or countered with a clean right hand that snapped Allen’s head back. By the first round’s end, Allen’s face showed the damage—swollen, split, and unable to keep up with Hrgovic’s relentless pressure.

## Hrgovic dictated the pace from the start
Hrgovic didn’t just win the fight—he made it look easy. He picked Allen apart with a stiff jab before unleashing a right cross that caught Allen flush midway through the first round. The shot staggered Allen, who barely managed to hold himself up against the ropes. Hrgovic smelled blood instantly, stepping in to unload a flurry of power punches. Allen, a slick southpaw, tried looping his own right over the top, but Hrgovic’s timing and footwork made every counter miss. By the second round, Allen was already pulling his gloves up to shield his face, a sign he knew he was in over his head.

## Allen’s corner stepped in to end the suffering
The third round became the final straw. Hrgovic didn’t let up—he walked Allen down and battered him against the ropes. Allen, bloodied and breathing heavily, couldn’t land a clean shot. Referee Chris Williams kept a close eye, but it was Allen’s own corner—[Jamie Moore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_Moore_(boxer)) and Nigel Travis—that finally waved the towel. They pulled Allen out of the fight, a decision Allen immediately supported. ‘If Jamie and Nige say enough’s enough, enough’s enough,’ he told the ringside reporters. ‘Thank you for looking after me.’

## Allen stays in the game but faces tough questions
Despite the brutal loss, Allen made it clear he’s not retiring just yet. ‘A few more fights,’ he told the crowd, his voice shaky but grateful. ‘I just got absolutely hammered, and everyone stayed to the end.’ He acknowledged Hrgovic’s dominance without sugarcoating it: ‘I’m a good fighter but I’m not a great fighter. These people have given me the career of a world champion. No excuses—I tried my heart out. Filip Hrgovic—too good for me.’

## The undercard delivered drama of its own
On the undercard, [Michael Gomez Jr.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Gomez) started fast but ran into trouble early. Before the first bell even rang, Gomez Jr. butted heads with Lee McGregor [Lee McGregor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lee_McGregor), a featherweight moving up to lightweight. The clash set the tone for a scrappy bout, though Gomez Jr. eventually dropped McGregor in the third round before the fight was stopped. McGregor, known for his featherweight skills, showed why moving up in weight is a gamble—his timing and stamina weren’t as sharp against a bigger opponent.

## What’s next for these fighters?
Hrgovic’s next move likely depends on title opportunities. As one of the top contenders in the heavyweight division, a shot at a world title won’t be far off if he keeps performances like this up. For Allen, the loss raises questions about his next steps. He’s 36, with 32 wins and 5 losses on his record, but Saturday’s performance suggests he might need to reassess his competition level. Whether he steps away or finds another opponent remains to be seen. Either way, Saturday night proved that Hrgovic is a force to be reckoned with—and Allen’s hometown crowd got a brutal lesson in just how far ahead the Croatian is.

A luta, que fazia parte de um card de destaque no Eco-Power Stadium, deixou claro que Hrgovic segue firme em sua trajetória rumo ao topo dos pesos-pesados, enquanto Allen, já com 37 anos e 31 vitórias em 34 lutas, mostrou que o tempo de brilhar em grandes palcos pode estar se esgotando.

A vitória de Hrgovic não é apenas mais um capítulo na carreira de um lutador em ascensão, mas um sinal de alerta para o boxe britânico, que vê seus principais nomes perdendo espaço para uma nova geração de gigantes. A performance de Hrgovic, que acumula 15 vitórias, todas por nocaute, e nenhuma derrota, coloca o croata como um dos principais desafiantes para lutas de título ainda este ano.

Com a próxima luta de Hrgovic já sendo negociada, os fãs brasileiros devem ficar de olho: o caminho para uma possível disputa de cinturão mundial pode estar cada vez mais próximo.
