Hamburg SV announced Tuesday that Kathleen Krüger will become the club’s new sport director effective July 1. The appointment makes her the first woman to hold such a permanent position at a Bundesliga club, marking a historic shift in German football leadership.

The club described Krüger as a highly respected figure with extensive experience at the highest international level, including 17 years at [FC Bayern Munich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich), where she most recently served as head of organization and infrastructure. Hamburg SV emphasized her organizational and leadership skills, noting her ability to navigate the emotionally charged environment of elite football.

‘By appointing Kathleen Krüger, HSV is securing the services of a highly respected figure who has worked at the highest international level for many years at FC Bayern München,’ the club stated on its website. ‘Over the course of 17 years, she has gained extensive experience in the sometimes emotionally charged environment of an elite club, helped shape key processes and demonstrated exceptional organizational and leadership skills.’

Krüger, now 40, responded with enthusiasm to the appointment. ‘I’m absolutely delighted by the trust that has been placed in me,’ she said. ‘It is a genuine privilege to help shape Hamburger SV, one of the biggest names in German football, in such a decisive sporting role, building for the future on the sporting level.’

As a player, Krüger made 33 Bundesliga appearances for [SC Freiburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SC_Freiburg), according to club records. Her transition from midfielder to executive highlights the growing pathway for women in football leadership roles.

## Gender milestone in Bundesliga leadership

Hamburg SV’s decision reflects broader changes in German football, where women are increasingly taking on senior executive positions. While other Bundesliga clubs have appointed women to advisory or temporary roles, Krüger’s appointment is the first permanent, board-level sport director position filled by a woman in the league’s history.

The move follows a trend in European football where clubs seek diverse leadership to address modern challenges in governance, finance, and sporting strategy. Hamburg SV’s statement underscored Krüger’s track record in operational excellence and her familiarity with high-pressure environments.

## What’s next for Hamburg SV and Krüger

Krüger will officially begin her role on July 1, taking charge of sporting decisions, strategy, and club development. Her appointment comes as Hamburg SV aims to return to the Bundesliga after a period in the 2. Bundesliga following relegation in 2018. The club, one of Germany’s most historic, is seeking to rebuild its football operations under new leadership.

The appointment also sends a signal to other clubs about the value of diverse leadership in football governance. Krüger’s integration will be closely watched as a test case for whether elite football in Germany can sustain long-term progress in gender representation at the executive level.

Hamburg SV’s leadership expressed confidence in Krüger’s ability to deliver results. ‘We are convinced that Kathleen Krüger’s expertise and leadership will significantly strengthen our sporting structure,’ the club stated. The broader football community will be watching to see how her appointment influences both on-field performance and off-field governance in the coming seasons.
