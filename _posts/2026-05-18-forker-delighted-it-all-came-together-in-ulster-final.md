Armagh’s senior footballers finally ended their Ulster Championship drought last night, lifting the title for the first time since 2006 with a dramatic 2-28 to 0-25 extra-time win over [Monaghan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monaghan_GAA). Captain Aidan Forker admitted his side felt they were long overdue a breakthrough after years of near-misses and narrow defeats in the provincial decider. 'We were due one,' Forker said after the final whistle, reflecting the relief and joy across the Armagh camp. 'It’s been a long time coming, and tonight it all came together for us.' The game stayed tight for long stretches, but Armagh’s resilience shone through in extra time when they pulled clear with a series of clinical scores. Forker finished with 1-04 himself, including a crucial goal midway through the second half that switched the momentum back in Armagh’s favor. His side had led by just a point at the break, but Monaghan fought back hard in the third quarter, keeping the deficit within five points heading into stoppage time. That set the stage for a frantic final three minutes and the inevitable extra period, where Armagh’s experience told in the end. The win means Armagh advance to the All-Ireland series, where they’ll face a qualifier tie in the coming weeks. Their route to the final wasn’t easy either. They needed a replay to overcome [Donegal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/County_Donegal_GAA) in the semi-final, proving their depth and character under pressure. Monaghan, meanwhile, pushed hard for their first Ulster title since 2013 but fell just short, missing several long-range frees in the dying minutes that could have changed the outcome. Manager [Éamonn McEneaney](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89amonn_McEneaney) kept his side compact defensively, but Armagh’s forward line was too sharp too often, particularly in transition play where they carved out multiple goal chances. Forker, who has been a near-ever-present for Armagh over the past decade, said the victory was a reward for a group that has stuck together through tough years. 'There were times when it didn’t look like this day would come, but we never gave up,' he added. 'The lads deserve every bit of this.' The win also marks a personal milestone for Forker, who played his first Ulster final back in 2013 and has been part of several heartbreaking defeats since. Tonight’s performance, especially in extra time, showed why he’s one of Armagh’s most respected leaders. Armagh’s next challenge is the All-Ireland qualifiers, where they’ll likely face a team from Leinster or Munster. Their form in Ulster suggests they’ll fancy their chances, but the championship’s unpredictability means nothing’s guaranteed. For Monaghan, there’s disappointment but also pride in a strong campaign that nearly delivered silverware. They’ll now regroup for the league next year, aiming to build on this run and push for another tilt at the title. The Ulster final lived up to the hype, delivering drama, skill, and a result that will be remembered in both counties for a long time. For Armagh, it’s a night to celebrate. For Monaghan, it’s a lesson in what could have been.
