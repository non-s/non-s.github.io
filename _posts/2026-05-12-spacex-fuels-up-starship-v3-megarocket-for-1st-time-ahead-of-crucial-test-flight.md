SpaceX has completed the first fueling test of its upgraded Starship V3 megarocket, a milestone ahead of its next orbital flight attempt scheduled for May 19. The company shared images of the fully stacked rocket, showing the integration of the Ship upper stage with the Super Heavy booster. This marks the first time the new V3 configuration has undergone a full fueling rehearsal, a key step in validating its readiness for launch.

The Starship V3 represents an incremental but significant upgrade to SpaceX’s [Starship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SpaceX_Starship) program, which aims to reduce costs and improve reliability. The latest version includes structural refinements and performance enhancements over previous iterations, though specifics remain undisclosed. The fueling test involved loading thousands of gallons of liquid methane and liquid oxygen propellants into both the Ship and Super Heavy stages, simulating pre-launch conditions.

## Starship V3 Prepares for Flight After Successful Fueling

Photos released by SpaceX show the rocket in its launch configuration, with the Ship upper stage positioned atop the Super Heavy booster. The fueling test follows earlier static fire tests of individual components, including the Raptor engines designed to power the vehicle. Engineers have conducted multiple sub-system checks to ensure all systems are operational before the final countdown.

SpaceX has not confirmed the exact launch window for May 19, but regulatory filings with the [Federal Aviation Administration](https://www.faa.gov/about) suggest the company is targeting this date barring any technical delays. The upcoming flight will test the rocket’s ascent, stage separation, and potential reentry maneuvers, building on lessons from previous test flights that ended in explosions.

## Starship’s Role in SpaceX’s Long-Term Plans

The Starship program is central to SpaceX’s ambitions, including crewed missions to [Mars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mars) and rapid point-to-point Earth travel. The V3 iteration introduces changes aimed at increasing payload capacity and reducing refurbishment time between flights. SpaceX CEO [Elon Musk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elon_Musk) has previously stated that the company aims to achieve rapid reusability, a goal that could drastically lower the cost of space travel.

The May 19 test flight will also serve as a critical validation step for NASA’s [Artemis program](https://www.nasa.gov/specials/artemis/), which plans to use a modified Starship as the lunar lander for astronaut missions. A successful flight would boost confidence in the vehicle’s reliability ahead of its role in returning humans to the moon.

## What’s Next for SpaceX’s Starship Program

Following the fueling test, SpaceX will review data to address any anomalies before proceeding with the launch. If the May 19 attempt is scrubbed, backup dates have been identified, though none have been publicly confirmed. The company has emphasized a cautious but aggressive timeline, aiming to demonstrate consistent progress toward full operational status.

The broader implications of a successful Starship V3 flight extend beyond SpaceX’s immediate goals. It could accelerate the commercial space industry’s shift toward larger, more capable rockets, while also serving as a testbed for technologies that may be adapted for other deep-space missions. For now, all eyes are on the launch pad as SpaceX prepares for what could be a defining moment in its rocket development program.
