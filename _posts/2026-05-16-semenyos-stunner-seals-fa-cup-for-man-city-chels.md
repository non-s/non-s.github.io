Manchester City claimed the FA Cup on Saturday after Antoine Semenyo’s [Antoine Semenyo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antoine_Semenyo) produced a moment of magic in stoppage time. His audacious back-heel sealed a 2-1 victory over Chelsea at Wembley, ending the Blues’ hopes of a domestic double. The goal, later voted the tournament’s best moment, gave Pep Guardiola’s side their 12th FA Cup, extending their dominance in English football’s oldest competition.

Chelsea, who had equalized through [Conor Gallagher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conor_Gallagher_(footballer)) after [Bernardo Silva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernardo_Silva)’s early strike, now face an uncertain future in European competitions. A sixth-place finish this season would leave them below the Europa League qualification threshold, meaning they could miss out entirely next campaign if other results go against them. The loss means Chelsea have now failed to win a major trophy for three straight seasons under [Mauricio Pochettino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauricio_Pochettino).

## Semenyo’s goal cements place in FA Cup history

Semenyo, on loan from [Bournemouth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A.F.C._Bournemouth), became the first player to score a back-heel in an FA Cup final since 1956. His 90th-minute strike was the latest in a string of dramatic late winners for Manchester City, who have now claimed six major trophies under Guardiola. The goal also extended City’s unbeaten run in finals to 10, a streak that includes league titles and domestic cups.

Chelsea began brightly, with Gallagher leveling the score in the 32nd minute after Kevin De Bruyne had put City ahead. But their challenge faded in the second half, and they were punished for defensive lapses that Semenyo exploited. Pochettino’s side now face a summer of scrutiny over their inability to deliver in big games, despite spending over £1 billion on transfers since 2022.

## Guardiola extends City’s trophy haul

For Guardiola, the win further solidifies Manchester City’s status as England’s most successful club of the modern era. The victory comes just days after City secured their fourth consecutive Premier League title, completing a historic domestic double. Their next challenge is the Champions League final, where they face [Real Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF) on June 1 in London.

Chelsea’s European hopes now hinge on their final Premier League fixtures, with a top-six finish still mathematically possible but increasingly unlikely. A seventh-place finish would mean a return to the Europa Conference League, while anything lower could leave them without European football entirely. The Blues’ board is expected to review Pochettino’s future over the coming weeks as pressure mounts for immediate improvement.

The FA Cup victory ensures Manchester City will play in next season’s FA Community Shield and Europa League, while Chelsea’s focus shifts to salvaging their domestic campaign. The contrast in fortunes underscores the growing gap between England’s top clubs and the rest of the Premier League.

A jogada, marcada por uma bicicleta espetacular nos minutos finais, não só deu o título ao City como ainda jogou o Chelsea em uma encruzilhada perigosa: a derrota pode custar a classificação para competições europeias pela primeira vez desde 1997, um baque histórico para um clube de sua tradição.
