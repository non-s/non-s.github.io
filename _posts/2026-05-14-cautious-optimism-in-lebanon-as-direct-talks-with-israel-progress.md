## Introduction to Talks
Lebanese officials are hoping that two-day ambassador-level negotiations in the US will yield a new ceasefire deal. A third round of direct talks between [Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel) and [Lebanon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lebanon) has kicked off in [Washington, DC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington,_D.C.), days before the expiration of a “ceasefire” that hardly halted Israeli attacks and [Hezbollah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hezbollah)'s response to them. 
## Progress and Expectations
The talks, which began on Thursday, represent a step towards more serious negotiations, with higher-level envoys from Lebanon and Israel taking part after the initial preparatory sessions were headed by the ambassadors of the two countries to Washington. Lebanese officials are hoping that the two-day negotiations will yield a new ceasefire deal and pave the way for tackling a series of thorny issues, including the withdrawal of Israeli forces from southern Lebanon and the disarmament of Hezbollah. [US Secretary of State](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_State) Marco Rubio, who attended the first Israel-Lebanon meetings in Washington in April, was with [US President](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_the_United_States) Donald Trump on a visit to [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China) and did not attend Thursday’s session. 
## Implications and Next Steps
Lebanon’s envoy heading up Thursday’s talks, Simon Karam, is an attorney and well-connected former Lebanese ambassador. The outcome of these talks will have significant implications for the region, with the potential to bring about a lasting peace or further escalate tensions. As the talks progress, the international community will be watching closely, hoping for a peaceful resolution to the longstanding conflict between Israel and Lebanon.
