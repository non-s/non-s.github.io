Bruno Fernandes made it 20 assists this season with a pinpoint cross that Bryan Mbeumo converted with a first-time finish in the 96th minute. The goal gave Manchester United [Manchester United F.C.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C.) a 2-1 win over Brighton & Hove Albion [Brighton & Hove Albion F.C.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C.) at Old Trafford, keeping the pressure on the top four chasing Arsenal and Liverpool.

Fernandes has been Manchester United’s creative engine all season, leading the Premier League in assists. His delivery was typical of his work—low, fast, and aimed at the near post where Mbeumo timed his run perfectly to meet the ball. The Portuguese midfielder had already set up Marcus Rashford’s opener, showing his range of passing and vision in tight spaces.

Mbeumo, who joined Brentford in 2018 and has grown into one of the Premier League’s most reliable finishers, made no mistake. He controlled the ball on his chest before slotting it past Bart Verbruggen with the outside of his right foot. The finish was clinical, but the play’s setup by Fernandes was the real highlight—another reminder of why United’s captain is so vital to their attack.

## Fernandes keeps chasing history

This assist ties Fernandes’ personal best from last season when he hit 20 assists in all competitions. He’s now just two behind the Premier League record of 22 set by Thierry Henry in 2002-03 and Kevin De Bruyne in 2019-20. Fernandes has been consistently at the top of the assist charts, proving he’s not just a penalty specialist but a genuine playmaker.

United’s win was scrappy, with Brighton equalizing through Pascal Groß’s deflected effort midway through the second half. But Fernandes’ leadership and Mbeumo’s composure in stoppage time turned a nervy afternoon into three more points. It keeps United alive in the title race while Brighton stay mired in mid-table.

## What’s next for both teams?

United host Nottingham Forest next weekend in a game that could define their top-four push. Brighton, meanwhile, are set for a tricky trip to Arsenal as they fight to avoid relegation battles. Fernandes’ assist machine keeps running, but the real test is consistency—can United grind out results in tight games like this one?

Mbeumo, who’s been in fine form this season, will hope this moment sparks a run of goals. His link-up with Fernandes is starting to look dangerous, and if they keep combining like this, United’s attack could become even more unpredictable.
