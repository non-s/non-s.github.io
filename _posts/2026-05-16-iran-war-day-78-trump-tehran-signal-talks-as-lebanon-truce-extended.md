Iran has indicated that the administration of U.S. President [Donald Trump](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Trump) has signalled openness to renewed negotiations aimed at resolving the standoff over Tehran’s nuclear programme. The announcement came during a [BRICS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BRICS) meeting where Iran’s Foreign Minister [Abbas Araghchi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abbas_Araghchi) stated that Washington had expressed interest in new talks. However, Araghchi emphasized that a deadlock persists over Iran’s stockpile of enriched nuclear material.

Meanwhile, Lebanon and Israel have agreed to extend a fragile ceasefire by 45 days beyond Sunday’s deadline, following talks held in the United States. The extension comes despite continued Israeli military strikes on towns and villages in southern Lebanon, which have escalated in recent days. Lebanese authorities reported at least 12 fatalities on Friday, including three paramedics who were killed in the strikes. The attacks have left communities in southern Lebanon under persistent threat, with infrastructure and civilian areas repeatedly targeted.

The extension of the Lebanon-Israel ceasefire provides a temporary reprieve, but tensions remain high. The fragile agreement reflects the difficulty of sustaining peace in the region, where sporadic violence has persisted for months. The [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations) has played a role in brokering discussions, though observers note that the underlying issues driving the conflict have not been resolved.

On the nuclear front, the reported U.S. willingness to engage in talks represents a potential shift in diplomatic efforts. Trump has suggested that Iran could pause its civilian nuclear programme for two decades if Tehran demonstrates a genuine commitment to a broader agreement. This proposal aims to address concerns over Iran’s nuclear activities while providing a pathway for de-escalation. However, the deadlock over enriched uranium stockpiles continues to pose a significant obstacle.

The situation in southern Lebanon remains volatile, with reports of ongoing Israeli military operations and civilian casualties. The extension of the truce is seen as a cautious step, but its durability depends on the actions of both parties. Analysts warn that any violation of the ceasefire could quickly escalate tensions and undermine the fragile progress made.

The broader implications of these developments extend beyond the immediate conflict zones. A potential U.S.-Iran nuclear deal could reshape regional dynamics, while the Lebanon-Israel ceasefire offers a glimmer of hope amid years of instability. Both sides face pressure to make concessions to prevent further violence, but the path to lasting peace remains uncertain. The coming weeks will be critical in determining whether these diplomatic signals translate into tangible progress.

A proposta de diálogo, ainda nebulosa, surge em meio à estagnação das negociações nucleares e à escalada de violência na região, onde Israel e grupos aliados mantêm confrontos diários. Enquanto Washington e Teerã buscam um ponto de convergência, o Brasil, como observador atento ao equilíbrio geopolítico, precisa monitorar os desdobramentos que podem impactar não só o comércio de energia, mas também a segurança de seus cidadãos no exterior e a estabilidade de aliados como a Arábia Saudita.

Já no Líbano, a trégua frágil entre Israel e o Hezbollah, grupo apoiado pelo Irã, depende de um equilíbrio instável, onde cada tiroteio ou ataque aéreo pode reacender um conflito aberto. Para o Brasil, tradicional mediador em crises internacionais, a situação exige cautela: o país importa cerca de 80% de seu petróleo do Oriente Médio, e qualquer interrupção no fornecimento, agravada por um possível confronto direto entre EUA e Irã, poderia elevar os preços dos combustíveis internamente.
