Pakistan’s Fatima Sana [wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatima_Sana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatima_Sana) has rewritten the record books in women’s T20 international cricket, smashing the fastest half-century ever recorded. The Pakistan captain reached 50 off just 15 deliveries during a dominant innings against Zimbabwe at Karachi’s National Stadium, surpassing the previous mark of 18 balls shared by New Zealand’s Sophie Devine, Australia’s Phoebe Litchfield, and India’s Richa Ghosh.

Sana’s explosive batting set the tone for Pakistan’s innings. She struck 10 fours and two sixes in a 19-ball unbeaten 62 that propelled her team to 223 for four in 20 overs. Zimbabwe’s bowlers struggled to contain her, as she maintained a strike rate of 326.32, the highest of her international career. The visitors were bundled out for 90 in 17.1 overs, handing Pakistan a 133-run victory and a clean 3-0 series sweep.

## Sana’s captaincy fuels batting resurgence

Since taking over as Pakistan’s captain, Sana has transformed the team’s approach at the top of the order. Her aggressive starts have become a hallmark of Pakistan’s white-ball strategy, and her personal form has mirrored the team’s upward trajectory. The 24-year-old has been Pakistan’s standout performer across formats, but her batting has shown marked improvement since she assumed leadership.

Her performance against Zimbabwe follows back-to-back ODI half-centuries against South Africa, who reached the 2022 Women’s ODI World Cup final. Sana’s ability to accelerate the scoring rate while anchoring the innings has made her one of the most feared batters in women’s cricket today.

## Record-breaking innings a product of years of hard work

Sana’s journey to this milestone has been years in the making. A left-arm orthodox spinner and hard-hitting middle-order batter, she has developed into a genuine all-rounder since her international debut in 2019. Her growth has been particularly notable in the shortest format, where her power-hitting and tactical awareness have made her a key asset.

The record-breaking knock in Karachi is the latest chapter in Sana’s rapid rise. It also underscores Pakistan’s emerging strength in women’s cricket, a sport where the national team has historically punched above its weight despite limited resources compared to powerhouses like Australia and England.

## Zimbabwe exposed by pace and spin

Zimbabwe’s bowlers were outclassed from the outset, unable to contain Sana’s onslaught. Both pace and spin struggled, with the visitors’ best figures coming from off-spinner Nomatter Mutasa, who conceded 45 runs in her four overs. Pakistan’s batting depth proved decisive, with Muneeba Ali and Sidra Ameen also contributing solid starts.

In the field, Pakistan’s bowlers complemented Sana’s heroics. Left-arm spinner Nashra Sandhu chipped in with two wickets, while Sana herself claimed a wicket, reinforcing her value as a match-winner. The series whitewash sends a strong message ahead of Pakistan’s upcoming engagements, including next year’s Women’s T20 World Cup qualifier.

The broader implications extend beyond Pakistan’s immediate success. Sana’s record-breaking innings adds to a growing body of evidence that women’s cricket is becoming faster, more competitive, and more entertaining. With more nations investing in women’s programs and exposure to top-tier cricket, milestones like this are likely to become more frequent.
