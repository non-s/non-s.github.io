An Israeli airstrike struck southern Lebanon on Friday, killing six people and escalating tensions with Hezbollah, the militant group linked to Iran. The strike came hours after the U.S. announced a 48-hour extension to a fragile ceasefire agreement brokered last month. The renewed violence marks the deadliest escalation in the region since heavy fighting began in October, with both sides exchanging rocket and artillery fire across the border.

Lebanese officials confirmed the deaths in the strike near the town of Naqoura, close to the Israeli border. Civil defense teams recovered the bodies, including two women and a child, from the rubble of a residential building. Israel’s military did not immediately comment on the attack but stated it was targeting Hezbollah positions it claimed were used to launch rockets into northern Israel earlier in the week. The strike follows a pattern of tit-for-tat exchanges that have left dozens dead on both sides since fighting resumed after the October 7 Hamas attack on Israel.

## U.S. pushes for extended truce as diplomats scramble

U.S. Secretary of State [Antony Blinken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antony_Blinken) confirmed the ceasefire extension late Thursday, calling it a critical step toward de-escalation. The temporary halt to hostilities had been set to expire Friday but was extended after urgent negotiations in Beirut and Jerusalem. Blinken urged both sides to use the pause to pursue a lasting agreement, though neither Israel nor Hezbollah have signaled willingness to halt operations indefinitely. Regional diplomats warn that further delays could push the conflict beyond control, risking spillover into neighboring Syria or Jordan.

Hezbollah, designated a terrorist organization by the U.S. and Israel, has vowed to continue its attacks until Israel ends its military campaign in Gaza and lifts a blockade on Lebanon’s ports. The group, which receives support from Iran, has launched thousands of rockets since October, targeting Israeli military installations and civilian areas. Israel has responded with airstrikes and artillery fire, destroying Hezbollah command centers and weapons depots. The cross-border violence has displaced over 100,000 civilians on both sides, according to UN reports.

## Regional leaders warn of wider conflict

Arab League Secretary-General [Ahmed Aboul Gheit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmed_Aboul_Gheit) condemned the escalation, calling for an immediate international intervention to prevent further destabilization. Egypt and Jordan, both engaged in mediation efforts, have proposed a broader security framework involving Lebanon’s government, though Hezbollah operates independently of the state. Meanwhile, Iran’s foreign minister reiterated support for Hezbollah, stating that any attack on the group would be met with a "firm response." The U.S. has deployed additional naval assets to the Eastern Mediterranean to monitor the situation, while Russia has called for restraint from all parties.

The renewed fighting has drawn global concern, with the United Nations Security Council scheduled to convene an emergency session on Friday. UN High Commissioner for Human Rights [Volker Türk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volker_T%C3%BCrk) urged both sides to protect civilians, highlighting reports of indiscriminate shelling in populated areas. The International Committee of the Red Cross has also warned of a humanitarian crisis, with hospitals and aid groups struggling to operate amid the violence.

Civilian casualties continue to rise as the conflict shows no signs of abating. Aid organizations report severe shortages of food, medicine, and fuel in southern Lebanon, where power grids have collapsed. In Israel, emergency services are on high alert, with sirens sounding daily in cities like Kiryat Shmona and Metula. The Israeli government has begun evacuating residents from border communities, while Hezbollah has reportedly mobilized reservists in southern Lebanon. The next 48 hours will be critical in determining whether the truce holds or if the violence spirals into a broader regional war.
