A car slammed into a crowd in central Modena, Italy on Saturday afternoon, injuring eight people and leaving four in serious condition, police confirmed. Witnesses described the vehicle moving at high speed before crashing into a shop window. The driver then tried to escape on foot but was quickly detained by police after officers spotted him holding a knife near the scene. Modena’s mayor, [Massimo Mezzetti](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massimo_Mezzetti), told reporters the suspect appeared to be targeting people but didn’t manage to stab anyone. 'Whatever the nature, it is a very serious act,' Mezzetti said. 'If it were an attack, it would be even more serious.' Italian Prime Minister [Giorgia Meloni](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giorgia_Meloni) called the incident 'extremely serious' in a public statement, though investigators haven’t yet determined whether the driver acted deliberately or was experiencing a mental health crisis at the time. Security camera footage from the scene shows the car speeding down a pedestrian street before impact, sending debris flying and scattering pedestrians. Authorities have not released the footage publicly but confirmed its authenticity to news agencies. The crash occurred near Modena’s historic center, a busy area with restaurants and shops. Video from the scene shows emergency responders treating the wounded while police cordon off the area with tape. Local media reported that the driver fled on foot after the collision, leaving the car embedded in the shop’s front window. Police tracked him down within minutes, finding him nearby with the knife. The suspect, identified as a 31-year-old second-generation Italian born in [Bergamo](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bergamo), had a documented history of schizoid disorders. [Fabrizia Triolo](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fabrizia_Triolo), the city’s prefect, told reporters the man had been treated at a mental health center but was no longer under observation when the incident occurred. 'We lost track of him after his initial period of care,' Triolo said. Authorities haven’t disclosed a motive but are reviewing his background, social media activity, and any recent interactions with law enforcement. The four critically injured victims were rushed to [Policlinico di Modena](https://www.policlinico.mo.it/), the city’s main hospital, where doctors said their conditions were stable but serious. The other four injured sustained minor injuries and were treated at the scene. Italian media reported the driver had no prior criminal record, though his mental health history is now under scrutiny. Investigators are analyzing the car, the scene, and the suspect’s phone and financial records for clues. The incident has sparked renewed debate in Italy about mental health care access and the responsibilities of treatment facilities. Mental health advocates point out that gaps in long-term care can leave vulnerable individuals without support, while law enforcement faces growing pressure to monitor potential threats without infringing on civil liberties. The Modena prosecutor’s office has opened a case but hasn’t filed formal charges yet. Authorities expect to complete their initial investigation within days, though a final determination of motive could take weeks. For now, the city remains on edge, with local leaders urging calm while reinforcing security in public spaces.
