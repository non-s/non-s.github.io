A freight train plowed into a public bus in Bangkok on Monday, killing eight people and injuring 25 others, authorities said. The collision occurred near the historic [Hua Lamphong railway station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hua_Lamphong_railway_station), a major transit hub in Thailand’s capital. Emergency responders rushed to the scene, where they found multiple victims trapped in wreckage. Hospitals in the area reported receiving the injured, with some in critical condition.

The crash happened during the afternoon rush hour, disrupting traffic in one of Asia’s busiest cities. Witnesses described hearing a loud crash followed by screams as the train struck the bus at a rail crossing. Video footage circulating on social media showed the bus mangled under the train’s wheels, with debris scattered across the tracks. The [State Railway of Thailand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_Railway_of_Thailand) confirmed the incident and said an investigation had begun.

Thai Prime Minister [Srettha Thavisin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Srettha_Thavisin) ordered an immediate review of rail crossing safety nationwide. “The government will take action to prevent such tragedies from happening again,” he said in a statement. The prime minister also expressed condolences to the victims’ families and pledged support for the injured. The State Railway of Thailand suspended operations on the affected line pending the investigation.

Experts noted that Bangkok’s aging rail infrastructure may have contributed to the crash. The city’s rapid urban expansion has strained transport systems, leading to frequent accidents at unprotected crossings. A 2022 report by Thailand’s Office of Transport and Traffic Policy and Planning highlighted 157 rail-related accidents in the past five years, resulting in 42 deaths. Critics have long called for better safety measures, including barriers and warning systems at crossings.

The bus involved in the crash was part of the city’s public transit system, which serves thousands daily. Authorities have not yet determined why the train failed to stop before the collision. Investigators are reviewing surveillance footage and interviewing witnesses to reconstruct the sequence of events. The train’s engineer and conductor are cooperating with authorities, according to officials.

Relatives of the victims gathered outside the hospital where the injured were taken, demanding answers. “We want to know why this happened,” said one family member. “No one should have to go through this.” The government has set up a compensation fund for victims and announced plans to strengthen rail safety regulations. The State Railway of Thailand has also pledged to install more warning signals at high-risk crossings.

This incident adds to growing concerns about Thailand’s transport safety record. Earlier this year, a bus plunged off a bridge in Chiang Mai province, killing 14. Local activists have criticized the lack of enforcement of traffic laws and poor maintenance of public infrastructure. The government has promised reforms but faces pressure to act quickly to prevent further tragedies.
