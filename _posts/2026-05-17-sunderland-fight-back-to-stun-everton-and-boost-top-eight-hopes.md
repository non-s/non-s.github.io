Sunderland pulled off one of the biggest upsets of the Premier League season on Sunday, storming back from a first-half deficit to beat Everton 3-1 and keep their top-eight dreams alive. The Black Cats, tipped for an immediate return to the Championship just a year ago, now sit just one point behind eighth-placed Brentford with a game in hand. Their final-day clash with Chelsea could decide whether they secure a Europa League spot. For Everton, it was a brutal end to a miserable run. They haven’t won in six games and now trail eighth-placed Brighton by three points with just one match left. Their fans made their displeasure clear, booing the players off at full-time, especially after a stoppage-time goal by Sunderland’s Wilson Isidor sealed the win. Even [Seamus Coleman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seamus_Coleman), playing his final home game before retirement, couldn’t inspire a response. He came on as a late substitute but couldn’t prevent the Toffees from suffering their sixth straight league defeat, a run that has killed their hopes of European football next season.

## How Sunderland turned the game around
The match looked set to be another dull stalemate until [Everton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Everton_FC) took the lead in the 35th minute. [Merlin Rohl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Merlin_Rohl), making his first start for the club, scored his first goal after a deflection off [James Tarkowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Tarkowski) found the net. But the Toffees’ joy was short-lived. Just before the hour mark, [Brian Brobbey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_Brobbey) equalized after outmuscling Tarkowski following a misplaced pass from Sunderland’s [Jake O’Brien](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jake_O%27Brien). The Dutch striker showed why he’s one of the league’s most dangerous forwards, finishing emphatically at the near post. Everton nearly regained the lead minutes later when O’Brien headed straight at [Robin Roefs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robin_Roefs) from a Tyrique George cross, but the chance slipped away. 

## The killer blows come late
Sunderland didn’t need much time to make their move. In the 81st minute, [Enzo Le Fee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enzo_Le_Fee) turned home from close range after being played in by [Chris Rigg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chris_Rigg). The French midfielder’s goal put Regis Le Bris’ side ahead for the first time. But they weren’t done. Just seconds after the restart, [Wilson Isidor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilson_Isidor) slotted home a brilliant low cross from substitute [Habib Diarra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habib_Diarra) to make it 3-1. The goal was a fitting end to a game that summed up Sunderland’s remarkable season. They were written off before it started but now have a real chance to finish in the top half of the table. For Everton, it’s another disappointing finale to a campaign that promised so much but delivered so little. Their fans left frustrated, their captain soon to depart, and their European dreams in tatters.
