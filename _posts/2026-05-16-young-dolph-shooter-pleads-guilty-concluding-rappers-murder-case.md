Cornelius Smith Jr., the man convicted of fatally shooting rapper Young Dolph in 2021, was sentenced Friday to 20 years in prison after pleading guilty to second-degree murder. Smith’s confession came during the trial of a co-defendant, solidifying his role in the high-profile killing that shocked the hip-hop world. The sentence, handed down in [Shelby County Criminal Court](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shelby_County,_Tennessee), marks the end of a case that drew national attention and left fans mourning the loss of an influential artist.

The shooting occurred on November 17, 2021, outside a bakery in Memphis, Tennessee. [Young Dolph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Young_Dolph), whose real name was Adolph Thornton Jr., was 36 years old. His death sparked a wave of tributes from fellow musicians and fans, highlighting his impact on Southern hip-hop. Authorities arrested Smith and several others in connection with the killing, with prosecutors alleging a premeditated act tied to ongoing disputes.

Prosecutors said Smith’s guilty plea during the co-defendant’s trial provided critical evidence that secured convictions in the case. The trial revealed details about the shooting, including the involvement of multiple suspects allegedly linked to a Memphis-based rap feud. Smith’s sentencing Friday closes one chapter of the investigation, but authorities continue searching for remaining suspects connected to the crime.

## Memphis community reacts to Young Dolph’s legacy

Fans and local leaders gathered outside the courthouse Friday to honor Young Dolph’s memory after the sentencing. The rapper, known for hits like *Get Rich* and *Preach*, had built a reputation as a philanthropist, donating to community programs in Memphis. His murder left a void in the city’s music scene and fueled discussions about gun violence and unresolved conflicts in the hip-hop industry.

Memphis police have not ruled out additional charges against other suspects still at large. Investigators allege the shooting stemmed from personal and professional disputes that escalated over time. The case has drawn scrutiny from national media, with critics questioning the city’s efforts to address systemic violence affecting artists and residents alike.

## What happens next in the Young Dolph investigation

While Smith’s 20-year sentence provides a measure of justice, authorities say the investigation remains active. Detectives are pursuing leads related to other individuals allegedly involved in the planning or execution of the murder. The case has also prompted discussions about the safety of hip-hop artists, particularly those who become targets due to public disputes.

Young Dolph’s family has previously called for accountability, and Friday’s sentencing offers a partial resolution. However, the broader implications of his death—including its impact on Memphis’s music scene and efforts to curb street violence—continue to unfold. Prosecutors have not indicated whether additional indictments will be filed.

The case underscores the risks faced by artists whose public personas intersect with real-world conflicts. As Memphis mourns the loss of one of its most beloved musicians, the sentencing of Smith serves as a reminder of the unresolved issues that contributed to the tragedy.
