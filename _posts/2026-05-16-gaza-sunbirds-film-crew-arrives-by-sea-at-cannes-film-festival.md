A team of Palestinian amputee cyclists arrived at the Cannes Film Festival on Monday by boat, marking a historic first for the 'Gaza Sunbirds' documentary premiere. The crew, composed entirely of athletes from Gaza, sailed into the French Riviera to symbolize their journey from blockade to global representation.

The documentary, titled 'Gaza Sunbirds,' follows the lives of six members of the Palestinian paracycling team, all amputees from Gaza. Their story centers on survival, resilience, and the transformative power of sport despite the challenges of living in a conflict zone. The film captures their training sessions, personal struggles, and the barriers they overcame to compete internationally.

## Gaza team faces obstacles to compete

Despite securing funding for international travel, the team faced last-minute restrictions from Egyptian authorities, forcing them to reroute their journey to Cannes by sea. The 12-hour journey from Egypt to France aboard a small fishing vessel underscored the logistical hurdles Palestinians face in accessing global platforms.

Team captain Raed al-Baba, a double amputee, told reporters in Cannes that the journey was not just about cycling but about sending a message of hope. 'We came here not just to race but to show the world that people with disabilities in Gaza can achieve great things,' al-Baba said.

## Cannes debut highlights Palestinian stories

The team’s arrival coincides with broader efforts to amplify Palestinian voices in international cultural spaces. Palestinian filmmaker Hadi Zaher, who directed 'Gaza Sunbirds,' emphasized the film’s role in challenging stereotypes. 'This is not a story of pity but of empowerment,' Zaher said. 'These athletes are breaking barriers in a place where disability often means exclusion.'

The documentary’s screening at Cannes follows a series of high-profile Palestinian cultural events in Europe, including recent exhibitions and film screenings in Berlin and Brussels. The attention reflects growing international interest in stories from Gaza beyond conflict narratives.

## Broader implications for paracycling and Palestine

The Gaza Sunbirds’ participation at Cannes could pave the way for more Palestinian athletes to compete in international sports events. Paracycling associations worldwide have praised the team’s determination, with some calling for increased support for disabled athletes from conflict zones.

For the athletes, the trip to Cannes represents more than a film premiere—it’s a step toward normalizing their presence on the global stage. Team member Mona Abu Al-Qumsan, who lost her leg in an Israeli airstrike, said, 'We want people to see us as athletes, not as victims.' The team plans to participate in paracycling competitions in Europe later this year, aiming to qualify for the Paralympic Games.
