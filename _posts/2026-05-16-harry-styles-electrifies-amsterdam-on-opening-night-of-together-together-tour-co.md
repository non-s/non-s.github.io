The city that gave the world Van Gogh and Rembrandt got a different kind of masterclass Friday night when Harry Styles took the stage at AFAS Live for the first of four sold-out Amsterdam shows. The venue, usually a pit stop for big acts, felt electric from the first note of “As It Was.” Styles walked on wearing a shimmering silver jacket, the crowd erupted, and for the next three hours, the 29-year-old British singer turned the Dutch capital into his personal stage.

## A night of surprises and deep cuts
The setlist wasn’t just a greatest hits parade. Styles mixed fan favorites like “Watermelon Sugar,” “Adore You,” and “Kiwi” with deep cuts such as “Treat People With Kindness” and a stripped-down acoustic version of “Falling.” The Amsterdam crowd sang every word, even on the less obvious tracks. The biggest surprise came mid-set when he launched into “Late Night Talking,” the 2022 single that had been absent from most setlists. The crowd lost it — literally. Phones lit up the room as fans recorded the moment.

## Fashion as spectacle
Styles’ wardrobe deserves its own review. He cycled through at least six outfits, from a feathered top hat to a sequined jumpsuit, each change met with cheers. The Amsterdam crowd got a first look at a new metallic silver jacket that’s become a signature of this tour. Backstage reports said he changed costumes between songs, an impressive feat given the tight schedule.

## The crowd that wouldn’t quit
The energy never dropped, even after the 11 p.m. finish. Fans hung around the venue for hours, some camping outside in the cold Dutch spring weather just to catch a glimpse of Styles leaving. Security had to manage the crowd as he exited through a side door, avoiding the main crowd path. The devotion matches the tour’s reputation — these aren’t just shows, they’re pilgrimages.

## Why Amsterdam mattered
Amsterdam’s selection as the sole European city for this leg wasn’t random. The city’s fanbase is known for its loyalty and vocal support, a perfect test market for a tour that’s playing one city per continent this year. The sold-out shows proved Styles’ global draw hasn’t faded. With only four nights here, demand outstripped supply by tens of thousands, mirroring the pattern seen in Tokyo, Sydney, and Buenos Aires.

## What’s next for the tour
Styles moves to Berlin next week for another four-night run at Mercedes-Benz Arena before hitting North America in July. The tour’s structure — one city per continent — makes each stop feel like an event, not just another date on a spreadsheet. For fans who missed Amsterdam, Japan and Australia offer the next chances to catch the show in 2023.

The Amsterdam run set a high bar. If the opening night is any indication, this tour will be remembered for its energy, surprises, and the way Styles turns a concert into a shared experience. The rest of the world will get their turn soon.
