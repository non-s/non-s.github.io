Rangers chairman Andrew Cavenagh has thrown his full support behind manager Danny Rohl as the club prepares for a summer of squad overhaul. After a season that saw Rangers finish well off the Scottish Premiership title race, Cavenagh admitted major changes were on the way. "We need to make some substantial changes," Cavenagh told BBC Sport. "It’s clear we didn’t get it right this year, and we’ll be making some big decisions in the summer."

The first casualty of the shake-up will be assistant coach John McMahon, who is set to leave Ibrox after five seasons in the dugout. McMahon, who joined from Manchester City in 2019, has been a key figure under both previous managers Steven Gerrard and Giovanni van Bronckhorst. His departure signals a wider housekeeping effort as Rangers look to reset following another trophy-less campaign.

Rangers finished sixth in the Premiership this season, their lowest league position since 2016, and were knocked out of the Scottish Cup in the fourth round. Cavenagh pointed to the club’s recent struggles as the reason for the upcoming changes. "We’ve got to be honest with ourselves," he said. "We’re not where we want to be, and we need to make some tough decisions to get back on track."

The summer transfer window now looms large for Rangers, with supporters expecting big-money signings to reinvigorate a squad that has looked short on fight and creativity. The club’s hierarchy has already started sounding out agents and scouting targets, with reports linking them to several high-profile players. Cavenagh stressed that the focus would be on bringing in talent that fits Rohl’s vision for the team.

## What’s next for Rangers and Rohl?

Danny Rohl now faces his third full season in charge, with the pressure mounting to deliver silverware. The German manager, a former youth coach at Bayern Munich, has divided opinion among the Ibrox faithful, with some fans questioning his tactical approach. However, Cavenagh’s public backing suggests the boardroom isn’t ready to pull the plug just yet.

The club’s transfer strategy will be closely watched, especially after last summer’s high-profile flops like [Fábio Silva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/F%C3%A1bio_Silva). Rangers splashed out over £10 million on the Portuguese striker, who struggled to make an impact. This time, the club’s recruitment team will be under more scrutiny than ever to get their moves right.

For McMahon, his exit marks the end of a long run in Glasgow. The 52-year-old has been a familiar face in the dugout, working alongside Rohl since he was appointed in 2021. His departure could open the door for a more experienced coach to join the backroom staff, though no replacements have been named yet.

The broader implications of this summer’s changes extend beyond just results on the pitch. Rangers are under pressure to justify their status as one of Scotland’s biggest clubs, especially after rivals Celtic strengthened their squad with major signings. The Ibrox faithful will be hoping for a quick turnaround, with Europe also looming as a potential banana skin if performances don’t improve.

What happens next depends largely on how the transfer window plays out. If Rangers land the right players, Rohl could silence his critics. If they don’t, the board may face tough questions about whether it’s time for a fresh start—with or without the current manager.
