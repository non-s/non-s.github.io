Former British health secretary [Wes Streeting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wes_Streeting) said Saturday he will run to replace Prime Minister [Keir Starmer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keir_Starmer) as leader of the UK’s Labour Party, escalating pressure on the prime minister amid internal party unrest. Streeting, who resigned from Starmer’s cabinet earlier this week, announced his candidacy at a Labour-affiliated think tank event in London. His move comes as multiple Labour MPs signal support for a leadership challenge following the party’s poor performance in recent local elections, which saw the Conservatives gain hundreds of council seats across England.

Streeting urged Starmer to set a clear timetable for his departure, arguing the party needs a competitive contest to select its next leader. “We need a proper contest with the best candidates on the field, and I’ll be standing,” Streeting told attendees at the Progress group gathering. The former health secretary, who served in Starmer’s government since 2021, framed his bid as a chance to reset Labour’s direction amid voter dissatisfaction. Polls show Starmer’s approval ratings have slipped since Labour’s 2024 election victory, with critics citing unpopular policies and perceived weak leadership.

## Starmer’s Response and Party Divisions

Prime Minister Starmer has so far dismissed calls for his resignation, insisting his government remains focused on delivering its agenda. A spokesperson said Saturday that Starmer has no plans to step down, noting Labour’s large parliamentary majority gives it a mandate to govern. However, internal party divisions have deepened, with backbench MPs and former cabinet members privately expressing frustration over Starmer’s management style and policy decisions, including economic reforms and foreign policy stances.

Streeting’s challenge reflects broader unease within Labour’s centrist and left-wing factions. While Starmer’s victory in the 2024 general election secured Labour’s largest majority since 1997, local election losses in May exposed vulnerabilities ahead of the next national vote. Analysts say a prolonged leadership contest could further destabilize the party and weaken its electoral prospects. Streeting, a rising star in Labour’s parliamentary party, has positioned himself as a unifying figure capable of bridging divides within the party.

## What Happens Next in the Leadership Race

Streeting’s formal declaration of candidacy signals the start of what could be a short but intense leadership race. Labour Party rules require candidates to secure nominations from at least 10% of Labour MPs before a ballot can be triggered. Streeting is expected to meet this threshold quickly, given his support among centrist and soft-left MPs. The contest, if it materializes, would likely take several weeks to resolve, with a new leader installed by autumn.

Whoever emerges as Labour’s leader will automatically become UK prime minister, as the party holds a commanding majority in Parliament. Streeting’s bid could force Starmer to face a confidence vote among Labour MPs, though such a move remains unlikely given his current parliamentary backing. The prime minister has vowed to press ahead with his legislative agenda, including plans to overhaul public services and address economic stagnation. However, the threat of a leadership challenge underscores the fragility of his position within his own party.

## Broader Implications for UK Politics

A leadership contest within Labour Party could reshape the UK’s political landscape, with potential ripple effects across Westminster. Streeting’s candidacy highlights growing discontent among Labour MPs who believe the party must adopt bolder policies to retain voter trust. His challenge also reflects wider dissatisfaction with Starmer’s cautious governing style, which critics argue has failed to deliver on key promises such as NHS reform and economic growth.

Meanwhile, the Conservative Party, which suffered heavy losses in the 2024 election, is seizing on Labour’s internal strife to regain political momentum. Party strategists are preparing for a potential snap election, expected to be held by 2029 but possibly sooner if Labour’s divisions persist. Political observers warn that prolonged instability could undermine public confidence in the government’s ability to address pressing issues like inflation, healthcare and national security.

Streeting’s bid marks a critical juncture for Labour and the UK government, with the outcome likely to determine the party’s direction for years to come. As MPs prepare for potential nominations and debates, the pressure on Starmer to either regroup or step aside will intensify, setting the stage for a high-stakes political showdown.
