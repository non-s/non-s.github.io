Chelsea have finalized an agreement with Xabi Alonso to take over as the club’s head coach, ending weeks of speculation over the Blues’ managerial vacancy. The former Real Madrid [manager](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xabi_Alonso) was the club’s top target after Liam Rosenior was dismissed in April following a dismal start to his tenure. Rosenior lasted just four months in charge, with Chelsea languishing in ninth place after picking up one point from their last seven Premier League matches. Alonso will now oversee the club’s summer transfer strategy, including potential signings to bolster the squad ahead of the new season. The announcement is expected to be made formally early next week, likely following the conclusion of the FA Cup final involving Manchester City and Chelsea, which concluded with a 2-1 defeat for the Blues on Saturday. Alonso’s appointment marks the fifth permanent managerial change under Chelsea’s BlueCo ownership, following Graham Potter, Mauricio Pochettino, Enzo Maresca and Rosenior. The club has endured two consecutive seasons without European competition, and defeat in the FA Cup final means they face a second straight campaign outside UEFA tournaments. ## Xabi Alonso’s coaching rise and Chelsea’s urgency Alonso’s rapid ascent in management began with Bayer Leverkusen, where he guided the club to an unbeaten Bundesliga title in his debut 2023/24 season. His Champions League-winning pedigree as a player with Liverpool and Real Madrid further elevated his profile, making him a prime candidate for top managerial roles. Chelsea’s urgency in securing his signature reflects their struggles under Rosenior, who oversaw just two wins in 14 league matches before his dismissal. The club’s ninth-place finish in the Premier League underscores the need for a fresh tactical approach and stronger leadership. Alonso’s arrival signals a shift toward experienced leadership, with the club aiming to restore stability and competitiveness. ## Competing candidates and transfer implications Chelsea had previously considered several other high-profile candidates, including Bournemouth’s Andoni Iraola, Fulham’s Marco Silva, Crystal Palace’s Oliver Glasner, Como’s Cesc Fàbregas and ex-Flamengo boss Felipe Luis. However, Alonso’s Champions League-winning legacy and proven success in Germany made him the standout choice. His involvement in transfer business aligns with Chelsea’s strategy to sign experienced first-team players this summer, though the club’s financial constraints and lack of European football may limit their options. The Blues’ ongoing ownership transition under BlueCo continues to shape the club’s direction, with Alonso’s appointment representing a significant step in their rebuilding process. ## What’s next for Chelsea and Alonso Alonso’s first tasks will include assessing the current squad, evaluating Rosenior’s remaining players and preparing for pre-season training. With European football out of reach for a second straight season, Chelsea’s focus will shift to domestic competitions and potential cup runs. The club’s hierarchy, led by co-owners Todd Boehly and Clearlake Capital, will need to balance ambition with financial prudence in the transfer market. Alonso’s managerial debut could come in the Premier League opener, with the timing of his official introduction yet to be confirmed. For the Spaniard, the challenge represents a new chapter after his abrupt exit from Real Madrid, where he was sacked in February following a disappointing first half of the season. His ability to replicate his Leverkusen success in the Premier League will be closely scrutinized as Chelsea seek to regain their status among England’s elite.
