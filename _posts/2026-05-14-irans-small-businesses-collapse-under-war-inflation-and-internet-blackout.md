## Introduction to Iran's Economic Crisis: Iran's economy is facing an unprecedented crisis, with small businesses being the hardest hit. Prices for some goods have tripled or quadrupled within a matter of months, leaving many Iranians struggling to make ends meet. The currency has lost 60% of its value, and the international internet has been shut down for more than 75 days, making it difficult for businesses to operate. ## Impact on Small Businesses: We spoke to the owner of an e-shop, a small business, and a major tech startup to capture what life is like for Iranians today. The owner of the e-shop told us that she has had to lay off most of her employees due to the lack of sales, and is struggling to keep her business afloat. The tech startup, which was once thriving, is now on the verge of collapse due to the lack of access to international markets and funding. ## Future of Iran's Economy: The future of Iran's economy looks bleak, with no end in sight to the war, ceasefire, and blockade. At least two million people have lost their jobs, and the economy is expected to contract further in the coming months. The Iranian government is under pressure to find a solution to the crisis, but so far, no concrete steps have been taken to address the issues facing small businesses and the wider economy.
