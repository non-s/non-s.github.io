Fujifilm’s X Half digital camera isn’t trying to be your daily driver. It’s a tiny, feature-light shooter designed to mimic the charm of half-frame film cameras from the 1960s. The catch? It used to cost $850, which was hard to justify even for enthusiasts. Now, the company’s cutting the price twice in quick succession: first to $649.99, then knocking another $100 off through June 28. That brings the base X Half down to $549.99 at Amazon, Best Buy, and Adorama—with Adorama sweetening the deal by including a 64GB SanDisk SD card and a soft camera case at no extra cost.

The X Half’s design is intentionally playful, resembling a vintage half-frame camera like the [Olympus Pen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olympus_Pen_series) series. It shoots 12-megapixel photos with a fixed 28mm f/2.8 lens, which is about as simple as it gets. There’s no viewfinder, no fancy autofocus modes—just a pop-up flash, a three-inch screen, and a single shutter button. It’s not built for pro work, but it’s perfect for casual shooters who want something light, fun, and nostalgic.

## Where to buy the X Half right now
The price cut is available at three major retailers, but Adorama’s bundle stands out. Here’s the breakdown:

- **Amazon**: $549.99 with free shipping for Prime members.
- **Best Buy**: $549.99 in-store or online, often with same-day pickup in major cities.
- **Adorama**: $549.99 plus a free accessory pack (64GB SD card and camera case) while supplies last.

The deal ends June 28, so if you’re on the fence, now’s the time to pull the trigger. Fujifilm hasn’t announced future discounts, and given the camera’s niche appeal, it’s unlikely to drop much further.

## Why would anyone buy a half-frame camera today?
Half-frame cameras were popular in the mid-20th century because they let photographers shoot twice as many photos on a roll of film. The X Half mimics this by cramming two 12MP images into a single shot, effectively doubling the number of photos you can take on a 64GB card. It’s a gimmick, sure, but it’s a fun one—especially for travel or street photography where you want to shoot a lot without changing cards.

The camera’s fixed lens and lack of advanced features also make it foolproof. No menus to navigate, no settings to tweak—just point and shoot. It’s the kind of camera you can toss in a bag and forget about until you need it, which is exactly why it’s appealing to casual photographers and collectors alike.

## What’s missing—and who should still wait
If you’re a professional or even a serious hobbyist, the X Half isn’t for you. It lacks 4K video, interchangeable lenses, and even a proper viewfinder. The autofocus is basic, and the image quality, while decent for its price, won’t compete with modern APS-C or full-frame cameras. Even Fujifilm markets it as a secondary camera, not a primary one.

That said, if you’re into analog-inspired photography or just want a fun, no-fuss camera for vacations or family events, the X Half is a solid pick. It’s also a great conversation starter—imagine pulling this out at a wedding and people asking, “Wait, is that a real camera?”

## The bigger picture: Fujifilm’s niche camera strategy
Fujifilm has been quietly pushing niche cameras like the X Half, the X-Pro3’s hybrid viewfinder, and the Instax line to carve out space in a market dominated by smartphones. These cameras aren’t meant to replace your iPhone or even a DSLR—they’re designed to fill gaps that big sensors and computational photography can’t. For Fujifilm, it’s a way to keep its brand relevant without going head-to-head with Canon or Sony in the pro market.

The X Half’s price cut fits into this strategy. It’s not a money-maker for Fujifilm, but it keeps the brand in the conversation. And for buyers, it’s a low-risk way to experiment with something different. If it doesn’t work out, you’re only out $550—not the $2,000 you’d drop on a full-frame mirrorless setup.

The clock’s ticking on this deal, so if you’ve been curious about the X Half, now’s your chance to try it without breaking the bank.
