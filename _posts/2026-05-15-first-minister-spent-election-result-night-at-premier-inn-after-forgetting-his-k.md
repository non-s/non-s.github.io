Welsh First Minister [Rhun ap Iorwerth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhun_ap_Iorwerth) found himself locked out of his home on election night after celebrating his party’s victory in the Senedd elections. The leader of Plaid Cymru, who declared a ‘new dawn’ for Wales following the results, spent the night at a Premier Inn after realizing he had forgotten his keys during the celebrations.

Ap Iorwerth made the announcement shortly after midnight on Friday, confirming his party’s gains in the Welsh Parliament. However, the euphoria of the moment was quickly dampened when he discovered he could not return to his residence. A spokesperson for Plaid Cymru confirmed the incident, stating that the first minister had no choice but to stay at the budget hotel chain after the mishap.

## Election night mishap overshadows victory
The incident occurred as ap Iorwerth and his team were finalizing the results of the Senedd election, where Plaid Cymru secured 40% of the vote, winning 15 seats—a historic high for the party. The victory marked a significant shift in Welsh politics, ending years of Labour dominance in the Senedd. However, the celebratory mood was interrupted when ap Iorwerth realized he had left his keys behind.

Social media users quickly picked up on the story, with some joking about the irony of a political leader who had just secured a major electoral win spending the night at a budget hotel. Others questioned whether the keys were a metaphor for the challenges ahead. The first minister’s office declined to comment further on the incident, emphasizing instead the party’s focus on forming the next Welsh government.

## Welsh politics in flux after election results
The Senedd election reshaped the political landscape in Wales, with Plaid Cymru’s strong performance forcing Labour into a coalition or confidence-and-supply arrangement. Analysts suggest that ap Iorwerth’s leadership will now face intense scrutiny as he navigates the complexities of governing with a slim majority. The incident at the Premier Inn, while minor in the grand scheme of Welsh politics, has already become a talking point among voters and commentators alike.

Ap Iorwerth, a former journalist and longtime advocate for Welsh independence, has promised to deliver a government focused on economic growth and public services. However, the immediate challenge of securing a home address for the first minister may now take on symbolic importance. His office has not indicated whether alternative arrangements were made for the following night.

The story of the lost keys has overshadowed what was otherwise a historic night for Plaid Cymru and Welsh devolution. While the party celebrates its electoral success, the practical challenges of governance have already begun to surface. Whether this incident will become a lasting footnote or a cautionary tale remains to be seen.
