The Israeli military announced it carried out an airstrike in Gaza City targeting Izz al-Din al-Haddad, identified as Hamas’s military chief in the area. [Izz al-Din al-Haddad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Izz_al-Din_al-Qassam_Brigades) is a senior figure within Hamas, though the militant group has not yet confirmed his status or whether he was hit in the strike.

The Israeli Defense Forces (IDF) stated the operation was aimed at disrupting Hamas’s military infrastructure. A spokesperson for the IDF said the strike was conducted with precision to minimize civilian casualties, but details on the outcome remain unconfirmed. Gaza City has been a focal point of intense airstrikes and ground operations in recent weeks, with both sides exchanging fire amid rising casualties.

## Hamas silent on reports of al-Haddad’s status

Hamas has not issued an immediate statement regarding the airstrike or the fate of al-Haddad. The militant group typically responds swiftly to such reports, but no official confirmation or denial has been provided. The lack of response has fueled speculation, though unverified at this time.

Gaza health officials report at least 15 civilians killed in Israeli airstrikes across the Gaza Strip in the past 24 hours, bringing the death toll in the current conflict to over 3,000 since October. The IDF has not commented on the civilian casualties but maintains its operations are targeting Hamas military assets.

## Regional tensions escalate amid unconfirmed reports

The airstrike comes as regional tensions continue to rise, with calls for a ceasefire growing louder from international diplomats. The United Nations has warned of a humanitarian crisis in Gaza, where hospitals and infrastructure have been severely damaged. Egypt and Qatar are mediating talks between Israel and Hamas, but no breakthrough has been achieved.

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu reiterated his government’s commitment to dismantling Hamas’s military capabilities, calling the airstrike a necessary step in the ongoing conflict. Meanwhile, Hamas officials in Gaza have accused Israel of targeting civilians, a claim the IDF denies.

The next 24 hours are critical as observers await further developments, including potential statements from Hamas or independent confirmation of al-Haddad’s status. The international community continues to pressure for a de-escalation, but both sides remain entrenched in their positions.

Efforts to broker a truce have stalled, with both Israel and Hamas blaming the other for the breakdown in negotiations. The humanitarian situation in Gaza is deteriorating, with reports of food and medical supply shortages worsening by the day. Aid organizations are struggling to deliver assistance amid the ongoing violence.
