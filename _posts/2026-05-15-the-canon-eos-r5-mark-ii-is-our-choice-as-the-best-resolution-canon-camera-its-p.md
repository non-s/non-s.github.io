Canon’s flagship mirrorless camera, the [EOS R5 Mark II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canon_EOS_R5_Mark_II), just hit its lowest price ever at Walmart. The deal drops the camera from $4,800 to $4,299, a $501 discount that’s hard to ignore for serious photographers. The price cut comes months after the camera’s 2023 launch, finally making it more accessible outside professional studios and wealthy hobbyists. For anyone eyeing a high-end camera with top-tier resolution and astrophotography chops, this is the moment to act. Other retailers still list the R5 Mark II near $4,800, so Walmart’s offer stands out as the best deal on the market right now. Even used models can’t match this price for the camera’s specs and condition, according to recent listings on photography forums. The discount isn’t a fluke either — it’s part of Walmart’s seasonal sale push, which often includes tech like cameras, TVs, and laptops. But the R5 Mark II’s price drop is the standout, given how long the camera’s been on the market. Canon hasn’t announced any new firmware updates or accessories to go with the sale, so the discount is purely about pricing, not added features. That means buyers are getting the same camera pros have been raving about since its release. The camera’s 45-megapixel sensor and low-light performance make it a favorite for astrophotographers, even without extra lenses or filters. For comparison, the original EOS R5 launched in 2020 with 45 megapixels, but the Mark II improved on autofocus, dynamic range, and video capabilities. It’s now the go-to choice for photographers who need both stills and video without compromise. The Walmart deal also bundles the camera body only, so buyers will need to factor in the cost of lenses separately. A basic RF 24-70mm f/2.8 lens retails for around $2,400, pushing the total setup cost closer to $6,700. That’s still cheaper than buying the camera and lens at full price elsewhere, but it’s a reminder that the real investment starts after the body. For those willing to stretch their budget, the R5 Mark II’s price drop makes it easier to justify. Even photographers who don’t shoot astrophotography find the camera useful for wildlife, sports, and studio work. The 8K video capability alone is a game-changer for creators who need ultra-high-resolution footage. The camera’s dual card slots and improved battery life also make it a favorite for long shoots. ## Why the R5 Mark II stands out for astrophotography The R5 Mark II isn’t just a high-megapixel camera — it’s designed to handle the challenges of night sky photography. Its sensor captures more detail in low light than many competitors, and the improved noise reduction means cleaner images at high ISOs. Astrophotographers often use wide apertures and long exposures, so the camera’s in-body stabilization helps prevent blurry shots. The flip-out touchscreen is another bonus, letting photographers frame shots from tricky angles without contorting themselves. Canon’s own [RF 100-400mm f/5.6-8 IS USM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canon_EF_lens_mount#RF_mount) lens pairs perfectly with the R5 Mark II for deep-sky shots. The lens’s image stabilization works with the camera’s to deliver sharp results even when shooting handheld. That combo has made the R5 Mark II a favorite among amateur astronomers and professional astrophotographers alike. The camera’s menu system is also more intuitive than older Canon models, which matters when you’re adjusting settings in the dark. ## Who should buy the R5 Mark II at this price? The R5 Mark II isn’t for everyone. If you’re a casual photographer, a used Canon R6 or even a budget mirrorless like the [Sony a7 IV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sony_Alpha_7_IV) might be a better fit. But for professionals or serious enthusiasts, the R5 Mark II’s resolution and versatility are hard to beat. Wedding photographers, sports shooters, and video creators all benefit from its high frame rates and 4K/8K recording. The camera’s weight — around 1.8 pounds without a lens — is also manageable for all-day shoots. The Walmart deal makes it especially appealing for those upgrading from older Canon DSLRs or mirrorless models. The R5 Mark II’s price drop could also pressure other retailers to follow suit. Amazon and B&H Photo have historically matched Walmart’s deals on high-demand cameras, so shoppers might see further discounts soon. For now, Walmart’s offer is the most aggressive, and it’s unlikely to last more than a few weeks. ## What’s next for Canon’s high-end cameras Canon has already teased the next generation of mirrorless cameras, but the R5 Mark II’s price drop suggests the company is clearing inventory to make room for new models. The R5 Mark III is rumored to arrive in late 2025, though details are scarce. Until then, the R5 Mark II remains Canon’s top-tier mirrorless camera. The discount at Walmart is a rare opportunity to own a pro-grade camera without the pro-grade price tag. For photographers who’ve been waiting for the right moment to upgrade, this could be it. Just don’t expect the deal to stick around forever — Walmart’s sales rarely do.
