John Travolta, 77, received a surprise honorary Palme d'Or on Friday at the [Cannes Film Festival](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cannes_Film_Festival) before the premiere of his directorial debut, 'Propeller One-Way Night Coach.' The award honors Travolta's five-decade career in Hollywood, spanning hits like 'Grease,' 'Saturday Night Fever' and 'Pulp Fiction.'

Travolta, flanked by French actor [Jean Reno](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_Reno), accepted the award on stage at the Palais des Festivals in Cannes. The Cannes jury praised Travolta for his contributions to cinema, calling him 'a true icon of American film.' The ceremony took place minutes before the festival screening of Travolta's animated feature, marking his first directorial effort.

## Travolta's Cannes legacy and career highlights

Travolta first rose to fame in the 1970s with 'Saturday Night Fever,' earning an Oscar nomination. His role as Danny Zuko in the 1978 musical 'Grease' cemented his global stardom. In 1994, he earned an Oscar nomination for his performance in 'Pulp Fiction,' directed by [Quentin Tarantino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino).

The honorary Palme d'Or follows similar honors bestowed on actors like [Alain Delon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alain_Delon) and [Jane Fonda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jane_Fonda). Travolta joins an exclusive list of international stars recognized by the prestigious festival.

## Cannes crowd reacts to Travolta's honor

Festival attendees gave Travolta a standing ovation as he accepted the award. French media reported an emotional moment when Travolta referenced his late wife, [Kelly Preston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelly_Preston), who died in 2020. The actor has kept a lower public profile since her passing.

Travolta's directorial debut, 'Propeller One-Way Night Coach,' is an animated short film based on his own life experiences. The film explores themes of grief and resilience through an aviation metaphor.

## Festival continues with premieres and awards

The Cannes Film Festival continues through May 25, showcasing 22 films in competition for the Palme d'Or. This year's festival has drawn stars including [Margot Robbie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Margot_Robbie) and [Ryan Gosling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ryan_Gosling), who premiered their film 'Furiosa: A Mad Max Saga' on Wednesday.

Travolta's honor adds to a week of surprises at Cannes, including the announcement of a special prize for [Jean-Luc Godard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Luc_Godard)'s posthumous contributions to cinema.

The honorary award carries no prize money but represents one of the highest honors in international film.
