Two men were taken into custody Friday at London’s Euston station in connection with a fatal incident in Birmingham the previous day. A man died after being struck by a van driven by a motorist who allegedly reacted to the removal of flags from lamp-posts, police said. The arrests occurred near the gathering point for the 'Unite the Kingdom' march, a rally organized in response to recent political events in [Northern Ireland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Ireland). The [Metropolitan Police](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metropolitan_Police) confirmed the detentions but did not release the identities or charges against the suspects. The victims’ name and circumstances have not been made public pending next of kin notification. The incident followed reports of flags being taken down from lamp-posts, a politically charged act in some areas of the UK. Flags on public property often symbolize community allegiances and disputes over their removal have sparked tensions in the past. The [West Midlands Police](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Midlands_Police) are leading the investigation into the fatal collision, which occurred on Thursday evening in the [Birmingham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Birmingham) area. Authorities have not disclosed whether the flag dispute directly led to the collision or if it was a contributing factor. Responding officers found the victim on the scene and pronounced him dead shortly after. The van driver was questioned and released pending further investigation. Police have appealed for witnesses to come forward with any information related to the incident or the flag removals. The 'Unite the Kingdom' march, organized by [UKIP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UKIP) and other groups, aims to advocate for national unity amid political divisions. The event’s organizers have not commented on the arrests or their connection to the rally. The arrests raise questions about the role of political gatherings in local disputes. Historically, such events have sometimes escalated into confrontations, particularly in areas with deep-seated community divisions. Authorities are reviewing footage from nearby cameras and reviewing statements from potential witnesses. The investigation is ongoing, with no further arrests anticipated at this time. Authorities urge anyone with information to contact West Midlands Police or Crime Stoppers anonymously.
