Russian authorities confirmed at least four deaths Sunday after Ukraine launched its largest drone strike on Russia in more than a year. The assault targeted multiple regions, including the Moscow area and Belgorod near the Ukrainian border. Governor Andrei Vorobyov of the Moscow region said a woman died when a drone hit a house in Khimki, north of the capital. Rescue teams continued searching the rubble for additional victims. Two more men were killed in the village of Pogorelki within the Mytishchi district. Vorobyov added that apartment buildings and infrastructure sites sustained damage during the strikes.

In the capital, Moscow Mayor Sergei Sobyanin reported that air defense systems shot down 81 drones overnight. Twelve people were injured, most near the city’s oil refinery, though operations at the facility continued without disruption. Three homes were damaged in the attacks. Russia’s state news agency TASS quoted Sobyanin as saying the strikes posed no threat to the refinery’s safety or the surrounding area.

The Belgorod region, close to the Ukrainian border, also reported a fatality. Officials did not provide details about the victim or the specific location of the attack. Belgorod has been a frequent target of Ukrainian drone and missile strikes in recent months, given its proximity to the front lines in the ongoing war.

Russia’s Ministry of Defense confirmed the scale of the drone assault but did not release further details about the operation’s origin or targets beyond the civilian casualties and damage. Ukrainian officials have not immediately commented on the strikes, though the country has repeatedly targeted Russian military and energy infrastructure in similar operations.

The timing of the attack aligns with Ukraine’s recent focus on disrupting Russian logistics and energy supplies. Large-scale drone strikes have become a regular feature of the conflict, but Sunday’s assault stands out for its scale and reach. The last comparable strike occurred in mid-2023, when Ukraine hit multiple Russian regions in a single night.

Residents in the affected areas described scenes of panic and confusion as air raid sirens blared and explosions echoed through the night. Social media videos showed emergency crews working amid damaged buildings and scattered debris. The strikes come as both sides continue to escalate attacks, raising concerns about further civilian casualties and infrastructure damage.

For now, Russian authorities are assessing the full extent of the damage and coordinating recovery efforts. The strikes underscore the ongoing volatility of the war, even as frontline fighting remains the primary focus for both militaries.
