Ncuti Gatwa’s [hosting gig](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ncuti_Gatwa) on Saturday Night Live UK’s season finale wasn’t just any gig. The actor, who stars as the Fifteenth Doctor in [Doctor Who](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doctor_Who), used the platform to poke fun at the sci-fi show’s fanbase—and his own role in it. During his monologue, he leaned into the criticism he’s heard about the latest seasons, including the perceived lack of excitement from longtime fans. “About 12 of you watched me,” he quipped, referring to himself in the third person. “Maybe that’s why I kept crying on set.” The crowd ate it up, laughing hard as Gatwa nodded along with the joke’s self-aware edge. It wasn’t just a roast of the fans, though. It was a moment of vulnerability, wrapped in humor, that made the audience feel like they were in on the joke with him. The monologue aired live from London, where SNL UK has built a reputation for sharp, current humor that doesn’t shy away from self-deprecation or cultural commentary. Gatwa’s appearance capped off a season that’s leaned into its roots while also trying to modernize the format, something he fit right into with his natural charisma and quick wit. The actor’s rise to fame with Doctor Who has been meteoric. After taking over the iconic role from [David Tennant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Tennant), Gatwa’s first season saw a mix of praise and skepticism, a pattern that’s followed many Who stars over the decades. Fans of the show are famously passionate, sometimes to a fault, and Gatwa’s joke about the “12 of you” likely struck a chord with anyone who’s ever felt like their favorite show is underappreciated. His monologue didn’t just target the fans, though. He also took aim at the show’s production choices, including the controversial finale of Season 14, which left some viewers scratching their heads. “I don’t know what happened in that finale,” he admitted, before adding, “But I do know it started with a spaceship crashing into a planet.” The line drew big laughs, proving that Gatwa’s humor isn’t just about mocking the show—it’s about celebrating it, warts and all. The SNL UK finale wasn’t just a platform for jokes. It was also a chance for Gatwa to connect with a new audience. While Doctor Who has always had a dedicated fanbase, Gatwa’s hosting gig introduced him to viewers who might not tune in to the show every Saturday. His monologue, which blended humor about the show with broader cultural references, showed why he’s become such a compelling figure in entertainment. For Gatwa, the monologue was a chance to show he can take a joke—and dish one out—while still being the Fifteenth Doctor fans fell in love with. It’s a balancing act that not every actor playing the role has pulled off, but Gatwa’s performance in London suggests he’s up for it. What’s next for Gatwa? He’s still the star of Doctor Who, and his second season is already in the works. But the SNL UK gig proves he’s more than just a TV doctor—he’s a comedic talent with a sharp eye for the absurdities of fandom. Fans of the show and comedy lovers alike will be watching closely to see what he does next, both on and off the TARDIS.
