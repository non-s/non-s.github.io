Israel’s Defense Ministry confirmed Thursday a $34 million contract to equip its fleet of F-35I Adir stealth fighters with external fuel tanks, expanding their operational range for long-distance strikes. The upgrade, awarded to Elbit Systems’ subsidiary Cyclone, marks a direct response to the intensive deployment of the jets in recent campaigns targeting sites in Iran during 2023 and 2024. The Israeli Air Force’s [F-35I Adir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elbit_Systems) fleet has operated at an elevated tempo, prompting concerns about refueling capacity and mission sustainability for deep-strike missions. The new external tanks will reduce dependence on mid-air refueling, a critical limitation in long-range operations over hostile territory. Military analysts note that Israel’s recent strikes in Iran required multiple refueling aircraft, straining the air force’s logistical pipeline. The F-35I’s stealth features remain unchanged, preserving its ability to penetrate defended airspace undetected. ## External fuel tanks extend range without compromising stealth The external fuel tanks, based on an existing design used by other modern fighters, will be integrated without altering the jet’s internal structure or avionics. Elbit Systems stated the tanks are compatible with the F-35I’s existing hardpoints, allowing for rapid installation and deployment. The upgrade is expected to enter service within 18 to 24 months, according to defense procurement officials. Israel has operated the F-35I since 2017, becoming the first international customer to field the jet. The aircraft has been central to Israel’s strategy of maintaining qualitative military superiority in the region. ## Israel prioritizes long-range strike capability amid regional tensions Israel’s decision follows a series of direct strikes on Iranian soil, including attacks on nuclear and military facilities. The high operational tempo has raised questions about the sustainability of such missions given limited aerial refueling assets. External fuel tanks provide a cost-effective solution compared to developing entirely new aircraft or increasing the number of refueling planes. The upgrade also aligns with Israel’s broader modernization efforts, including the planned acquisition of more F-35Is. The Defense Ministry emphasized the tanks will enhance the jets’ flexibility in responding to emerging threats across the Middle East. ## Broader implications for regional air power The move underscores Israel’s commitment to maintaining air dominance amid escalating tensions with Iran and its proxies. The F-35I’s enhanced range could enable Israel to project power deeper into enemy territory without relying on regional bases. Military experts suggest this could shift the balance in future conflicts, particularly in scenarios involving Iran or its allied militias in Syria and Lebanon. The upgrade also positions Israel as a leader among F-35 operators, driving innovation in fourth-generation-plus stealth technology. Elbit Systems, a key defense contractor, gains a new revenue stream while reinforcing its role in Israel’s military-industrial complex. The contract follows a trend of Israeli defense firms adapting Western systems to meet specific operational needs. Within five years, the upgraded F-35Is are expected to play a central role in Israel’s layered deterrence strategy.
