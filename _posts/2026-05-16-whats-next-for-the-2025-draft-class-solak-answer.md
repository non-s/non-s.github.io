The 2025 NFL Draft is less than a year away, but teams are already dissecting how prospects like [Travis Hunter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travis_Hunter) fit their schemes. Hunter, a dual-threat from Jackson State, has drawn interest as a full-time cornerback or safety, ending speculation about his future on offense. Scouts say his versatility and ball skills make him a top-10 candidate, but teams must decide whether to maximize his defensive impact.

## Hunter’s draft stock hinges on defensive role

Hunter’s transition to defense could reshape his draft position. The 2024 season saw him split time between offense and defense, but scouts report that NFL teams are leaning toward using him primarily as a defensive back. His 2023 tape shows elite coverage skills, and analysts project him as a Day 1 starter if he commits to defense. However, some teams still consider him an offensive asset, creating debate in war rooms.

Ashton Jeanty, a running back from Texas A&M, appears poised for a breakout season. Scouts highlight his explosiveness and receiving ability, traits that could push him into the first round. His combine performance in 2024—where he ran a 4.38-second 40-yard dash—cemented his status as a top-tier talent. Teams with pass-catching needs are monitoring his development closely.

## Jeanty and Egbuka could redefine draft boards

Emeka Egbuka, a wide receiver from Ohio State, has drawn comparisons to former Buckeyes like Chris Olave. His route-running and contested-catch ability make him a Day 2 target, but some scouts believe he could crack the top 20 with a strong senior year. His chemistry with quarterback Kyle McCord has drawn early praise, fueling draft hype.

Meanwhile, [Will Campbell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Will_Campbell_(American_football)), a guard from LSU, faces uncertainty after his team added multiple linemen in the 2024 draft. Scouts say his technique and strength remain strong, but his draft stock could slip if teams question his fit in certain schemes. His combine performance will be critical to regaining momentum.

## Campbell’s draft outlook shifts after 2024 draft moves

Nick Emmanwori, a linebacker from Kansas, remains a wildcard in the 2025 class. His size and speed make him a prototypical edge rusher, but scouts say his draft stock depends on his off-field work ethic and combine performance. Some teams see him as a late first-rounder, while others project him to Day 2. His role in Kansas’s defense this season will be closely watched.

Teams are also monitoring smaller-school prospects who could rise in the rankings. Players like [Javon Bullard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Javon_Bullard) from Georgia and [Jordan Morgan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jordan_Morgan_(offensive_lineman)) from Arizona have drawn late-round buzz. Their combine invitations could elevate their profiles significantly.

## Late-round sleepers could shake up 2025 draft

The 2025 class is still fluid, with rankings shifting weekly. Scouts emphasize that injuries, combine performances and pro days will play major roles in final evaluations. Teams drafting in the top 10 will prioritize positional need, but versatility could be the ultimate tiebreaker. The draft order could look entirely different by April 2025.

As the season progresses, expect more prospects to rise—or fall—based on game tape and workout numbers. The 2025 draft is shaping up to be one of the most unpredictable in recent years, with teams scrambling to fill roster gaps before the April selections.
