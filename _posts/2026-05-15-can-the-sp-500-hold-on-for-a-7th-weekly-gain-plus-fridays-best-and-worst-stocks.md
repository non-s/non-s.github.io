The S&P 500 [Standard & Poor's 500 Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%26P_500) edged higher Friday, staying within striking distance of a historic seven-week winning streak. The index closed just 0.2% below its record, as tech and consumer stocks led gains. A late-day surge pushed the benchmark closer to the milestone after a choppy morning of trading.

## Weekly gains hinge on economic data and earnings
Investors gauged fresh economic reports and corporate earnings as the week wound down. The latest [U.S. jobs report](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_employment) showed steady hiring, while inflation data signaled mixed progress. Analysts say the S&P 500’s streak reflects cautious optimism, with traders balancing growth hopes against recession fears.

## Friday’s top and bottom performers
Tech giants dominated the day’s winners, with [Nvidia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nvidia) and [Apple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc.) leading gains. Nvidia surged 3% after a brokerage upgrade, while Apple rose 1.5% on strong iPhone sales data. On the losing side, [Tesla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tesla,_Inc.) fell 2.5% after a downgrade from a major bank, and [Dow Inc.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dow_Inc.) slipped 1.8% on weak earnings.

## Market watchers eye Fed signals
All eyes remain on the [Federal Reserve](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Reserve) as it weighs interest rate decisions. Minutes from the Fed’s last meeting hinted at a possible pause, but traders are split on whether further hikes are needed. Some economists warn that prolonged rate pressures could cool the S&P 500’s momentum.

## What’s next for the S&P 500?
The index’s seven-week streak would mark its longest run since 2017, a rare feat in volatile markets. Next week’s [CPI inflation report](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consumer_Price_Index) and [retail sales data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retail_sales) could sway traders. If the streak holds, it would signal sustained investor confidence, but risks like geopolitical tensions or earnings misses could derail the advance.
