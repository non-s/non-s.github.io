Aaron Rodgers will play for the Pittsburgh Steelers in 2025 after agreeing to a one-year contract on Tuesday, according to multiple reports. The deal comes after months of speculation about Rodgers' future following the Packers' decision last season to move on from the future Hall of Famer. Rodgers, who turns 42 in December, had been weighing retirement versus one final NFL run, but the Steelers convinced him to continue his career in Pittsburgh.

The agreement marks a major shift for Rodgers, who spent his entire 18-year career with the [Green Bay Packers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Bay_Packers) before becoming a free agent this offseason. Pittsburgh’s roster already features a strong young core led by [Minkah Fitzpatrick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minkah_Fitzpatrick) and [T.J. Watt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T.J._Watt), and adding Rodgers gives the Steelers a veteran presence in the locker room and on the field. The team has struggled to find consistent quarterback play since [Ben Roethlisberger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ben_Roethlisberger) retired after the 2021 season.

## Why Pittsburgh won Rodgers over
Rodgers reportedly visited Pittsburgh twice in the offseason, including a meeting with Steelers owner [Art Rooney II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Art_Rooney_II) and head coach [Mike Tomlin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mike_Tomlin). The Steelers offered a one-year deal worth around $10 million, a figure that reflects both Rodgers’ market value and Pittsburgh’s need to balance veteran leadership with financial flexibility. The contract includes incentives tied to Rodgers’ playing time and performance, giving the team an easy out if he declines physically during the season.

For Rodgers, the move to Pittsburgh provides a fresh start after years of tension with Packers management. His relationship with former Packers general manager [Brian Gutekunst](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_Gutekunst) had become strained, and Rodgers felt the team wasn’t committed to building a competitive roster around him. The Steelers, meanwhile, are hoping Rodgers can stabilize their offense, which ranked 24th in the NFL in scoring last season. They’re also betting he can mentor young quarterback [Kyle Shanahan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kyle_Shanahan) or push [Russell Wilson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russell_Wilson) (who’s on a one-year deal himself) to perform at a higher level.

## What this means for the Steelers
The addition of Rodgers immediately makes the Steelers contenders in the [AFC North](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AFC_North), a division where the [Cleveland Browns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cleveland_Browns) and [Baltimore Ravens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltimore_Ravens) are perennial playoff threats. Pittsburgh’s defense, which ranked sixth in the NFL last season, remains one of the league’s best, and Rodgers’ presence could push the team into the playoffs for the first time since 2021. However, age is a major concern: Rodgers hasn’t thrown for 4,000 yards in a season since 2020, and his completion percentage has dipped below 65% in three of the last four years.

The Steelers’ front office also had to consider the long-term future. Rodgers is unlikely to play beyond 2025, so this isn’t a signing that locks Pittsburgh into a decade-long QB contract. Instead, it buys the team time to evaluate younger options like [Mason Rudolph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mason_Rudolph) or draft a successor in 2025. If Rodgers struggles or gets hurt, the Steelers can pivot quickly without major financial consequences.

## What’s next for Rodgers
Rodgers has said repeatedly that 2024 would likely be his final season, but his decision to sign with Pittsburgh suggests he’s not ready to call it quits just yet. The timing of his retirement announcement will depend on how the season goes—if he stays healthy and performs well, he could ride off into the sunset on his own terms. If injuries or poor play force his hand, he might leave earlier than planned.

For now, Rodgers is focused on helping Pittsburgh win now. He’s already begun working with the team’s offensive staff, and his first preseason game could come as early as August. The Steelers open their season on September 7 against the [Chicago Bears](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago_Bears).
