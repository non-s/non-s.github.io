A U.S. Navy F/A-18 Super Hornet launched from [USS Abraham Lincoln](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Abraham_Lincoln_(CVN-72)) disabled an Iranian-flagged oil tanker’s rudder with 20mm cannon fire in the Gulf of Oman Thursday morning, U.S. Central Command announced on X. The strike, which occurred at 9 a.m. EDT, prevented the vessel from reaching Iranian waters as part of the ongoing U.S.-led blockade of Iranian ports.

Central Command stated the aircraft was scrambled in response to the tanker M/T Hasna, which was transiting international waters en route to an Iranian port when American forces intervened. “American forces issued multiple warnings to the vessel before the jet engaged,” the command noted, emphasizing adherence to rules of engagement.

The incident follows President Trump’s pause of [Operation Sentinel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Sentinel) Wednesday, a short-lived initiative aimed at protecting commercial shipping in the Persian Gulf. The operation was suspended amid new diplomatic efforts to broker peace between the U.S. and Iran, though regional tensions remain high.

Maritime security analysts note the strike reflects the Pentagon’s continued enforcement of sanctions against Iran, despite recent diplomatic overtures. The Gulf of Oman has become a flashpoint for confrontations between Western naval forces and Iranian vessels attempting to bypass maritime restrictions.

Aircraft carrier USS Abraham Lincoln has been deployed in the region since late 2024 as part of the U.S. Navy’s Fifth Fleet operations. The carrier’s air wing includes F/A-18 Super Hornets, which have been used in multiple maritime interdiction missions in recent months.

The disabled tanker remains afloat but is effectively immobilized in international waters. U.S. forces are monitoring the situation while evaluating next steps, which may include boarding or towing the vessel. The incident underscores the ongoing risks to commercial shipping in one of the world’s most strategically vital waterways.

Regional observers warn the blockade enforcement could escalate tensions, particularly as Iran seeks alternative routes to export oil. The latest confrontation comes just days after Iranian officials reiterated demands for sanctions relief amid stalled nuclear negotiations.
