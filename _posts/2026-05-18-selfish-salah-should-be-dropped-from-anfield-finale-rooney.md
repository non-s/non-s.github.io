Wayne Rooney has taken a strong public stance against Liverpool’s star forward Mohamed Salah, urging the club to bench him for Sunday’s season finale against Wolves at Anfield. The former England captain and now TV pundit called Salah’s recent criticism of the club “selfish,” arguing it undermines team morale just as the season winds down. “He’s got to look at himself and think about the team,” Rooney told Sky Sports. “If you’re not happy, fine, but you’ve got to respect the club and the supporters.”

Liverpool head into the match knowing a win would secure second place in the Premier League, just two points behind champions Man City. Salah, the club’s top scorer with 21 goals this season, has been a key figure in their push for the title. But his public comments about wanting a new challenge have put him in the spotlight for all the wrong reasons. Many fans see them as a distraction ahead of a crucial game.

Rooney’s remarks add fresh fuel to the debate over Salah’s future at Anfield. The Egyptian joined Liverpool in 2017 for £36.9m from Roma and has since become a club legend, winning the Champions League, Premier League, and FA Cup. But his recent contract talks have stalled, and his comments about wanting a new experience have left supporters divided. Some see him as a symbol of loyalty; others wonder if he’s lost his passion for the club.

Liverpool manager Jürgen Klopp [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_Klopp] has so far avoided direct criticism of Salah, but the pressure is on. The German has built his squad around high-energy players who buy into his philosophy. If Klopp decides to drop Salah, it would send a strong message to the dressing room—and to the fans. But it would also risk weakening the team on the pitch just as they chase a top-four finish.

Salah’s next move is unclear. Reports suggest he’s keen to test himself elsewhere, possibly in Saudi Arabia or Europe. But Liverpool have made it clear they want to keep him. The club’s hierarchy, including sporting director Julian Ward [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julian_Ward], will be watching closely. Ward joined Liverpool in 2021 and has overseen transfers like Darwin Núñez, but Salah’s future may be his biggest challenge yet.

For now, all eyes are on Sunday’s game. Can Liverpool finish second? Will Salah play? And what does this mean for his future? One thing’s for sure: this won’t be the last we hear about it. The season’s not over, and neither is this story.

## What’s next for Salah and Liverpool?

Liverpool’s season finale is set for Sunday at 4:30 PM BST against Wolves. The result will shape their summer transfer plans. If Salah plays, fans will expect fireworks. If he’s benched, the message will be clear. Either way, his contract situation dominates the conversation. The club has until June 30 to decide his future—and they’re running out of time.
