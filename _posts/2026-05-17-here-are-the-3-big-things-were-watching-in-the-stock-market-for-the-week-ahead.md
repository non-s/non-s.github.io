Nvidia’s blowout quarter last spring set off a tech stock frenzy, and this week’s earnings report could show whether that momentum is fading or still growing. Analysts expect Nvidia to post another strong quarter, but investors will scrutinize its guidance for signs of slowing demand. The stock has surged over 200% in the past year, making its report one of the most anticipated of the earnings season. Any miss on revenue or guidance could rattle the broader market, which has relied on Nvidia’s AI chips to drive growth. Traders will also watch for updates on Nvidia’s data center business, its biggest moneymaker, and any hints about new product launches. The stock’s performance this week could set the tone for tech and the S&P 500 for months to come. \n\n\n## Google’s AI push takes center stage at developer conference

Google is set to unveil its latest artificial intelligence tools at its annual developer conference on Monday, with AI expected to dominate the keynote. The event, called Google I/O, will likely feature updates to its [Gemini AI model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gemini_(language_model)), new features for its search engine, and partnerships with cloud computing providers. Google has been playing catch-up to Microsoft and Nvidia in the AI race, but recent advances in its AI models have narrowed the gap. The conference will also showcase how Google plans to integrate AI into its core products like Gmail, Google Docs, and YouTube. Investors will look for signs that Google’s AI investments are paying off, especially as ad revenue growth slows. A strong showing could boost the company’s stock, which has underperformed its peers in recent months. \n\n\nThe timing of Google’s event couldn’t be better—or riskier—for the stock market. Coming just days after Nvidia’s earnings, it gives investors a second chance to judge the staying power of the AI trade. If both companies deliver strong updates, it could extend the tech rally. But if either stumbles, the fallout could ripple across the market. Either way, AI isn’t just a tech story anymore. It’s driving moves in everything from semiconductor stocks to cloud computing and even traditional industries like retail and banking. \n\n\n## Earnings season ramps up with 150+ S&P 500 reports due

This isn’t just about tech. Over 150 companies in the S&P 500 will report earnings this week, including heavyweights like [Costco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Costco), [Home Depot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Home_Depot), and [Target](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Target_Corporation). These reports will give a clearer picture of whether consumer spending is holding up despite higher borrowing costs. Analysts expect a mixed bag: some retailers are bracing for weaker sales, while others, like Costco, have seen steady demand. \n\n\nThe earnings reports will also test the Federal Reserve’s outlook for inflation and economic growth. If companies report strong profits, it could ease concerns about a recession. But if earnings disappoint, it might force the Fed to reconsider its plan to cut interest rates later this year. Traders are already pricing in a 60% chance of a rate cut in September, so any surprises in the earnings data could shift those odds. \n\n\nBeyond the numbers, companies are using earnings calls to talk about AI’s impact on their businesses. From Walmart using AI to manage inventory to banks like JPMorgan deploying chatbots, artificial intelligence is becoming a key part of corporate strategies. Investors will listen closely for how much companies are spending on AI—and whether it’s translating into real profits. \n\n\nThe week’s earnings calendar is packed with surprises. [Deere & Co.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deere_%26_Company), the farm equipment giant, reports on Wednesday, and its results will signal whether the agricultural sector is still struggling with high costs. Meanwhile, [Cisco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cisco_Systems) will give an update on its push into AI-driven networking, a business it hopes will offset slowing sales in traditional hardware. All eyes will also be on [Tesla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tesla,_Inc.), which reports on Tuesday and faces questions about demand for its electric vehicles and energy products. \n\n\nWhat happens next depends on how these reports stack up against expectations. If Nvidia and Google deliver, it could extend the tech rally. If the broader earnings reports surprise to the upside, it might convince the Fed to hold off on rate cuts. But if any of these stories disappoint, it could spark a pullback in the market. Either way, this week’s events will shape how investors view the economy—and where they put their money—for the rest of the year.
