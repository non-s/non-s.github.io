The UK’s Parliamentary Commissioner for Standards has opened a formal inquiry into Reform UK leader [Nigel Farage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigel_Farage) after he failed to declare a £5 million donation from a cryptocurrency billionaire received in 2023. The investigation, confirmed on Friday, will determine whether Farage breached rules requiring MPs and peers to register financial gifts within 28 days of receipt.

The £5 million gift, the largest ever recorded in UK political donations, was reportedly made by a donor whose identity has not been disclosed. Farage, who led the Brexit campaign and now heads the Reform UK party, has previously stated he would comply with all registration requirements. However, the commissioner’s office has confirmed it will review whether the donation should have been declared under the [House of Commons’ Code of Conduct](https://www.parliament.uk/mps-lords-and-offices/standards-and-financial-interests/).

## Reform UK responds to standards probe

Reform UK said in a statement that Farage had acted in accordance with legal advice at the time the donation was received. The party added that it was cooperating fully with the investigation and would provide all necessary documentation. A spokesperson declined to comment on the donor’s identity, citing privacy concerns.

The donation, made in Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies, has raised questions about the transparency of digital asset gifts in UK politics. Unlike traditional bank transfers, cryptocurrency transactions can be harder to trace, complicating oversight by regulators and watchdogs. The investigation will examine whether Farage’s failure to declare the gift was a procedural oversight or a breach of parliamentary rules.

## Watchdog examines gift disclosure rules

The probe comes amid growing scrutiny of political donations, particularly those involving cryptocurrencies. The Electoral Commission has previously warned that digital asset gifts could pose risks to transparency due to their anonymous nature. The standards commissioner’s office has not indicated whether criminal charges are possible, but breaches of the [Code of Conduct](https://www.parliament.uk/mps-lords-and-offices/standards-and-financial-interests/) can result in public reprimands or suspension from parliamentary duties.

Farage, a prominent Eurosceptic figure, has been a vocal critic of the UK’s political establishment. His Reform UK party has gained traction in recent polls, partly due to its opposition to net-zero policies and calls for stricter immigration controls. The standards probe could affect his party’s credibility as it prepares for the next general election.

The investigation is expected to take several weeks, with a preliminary report likely to be published within two months. If the commissioner finds a breach, Farage may face disciplinary action, including a formal apology or sanctions. The outcome could set a precedent for how cryptocurrency donations are handled in future political campaigns.

Campaign groups have already called for stricter rules on digital asset donations, arguing that current regulations are ill-equipped to handle the rapid growth of cryptocurrencies. The standards probe underscores the challenges faced by watchdogs in an era of evolving financial technologies.
