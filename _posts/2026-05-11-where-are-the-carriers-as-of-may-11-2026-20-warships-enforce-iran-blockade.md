The U.S. Navy has deployed more than 20 warships, including two carrier strike groups, to enforce a blockade against Iran as of May 11, 2026. The operation, conducted under U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM), involves multiple naval assets in the Arabian Sea and broader CENTCOM area of responsibility. Among the ships active in the blockade are the USS George H.W. Bush (CVN 77), a [Nimitz-class aircraft carrier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nimitz-class_aircraft_carrier), and the guided-missile destroyer USS John Finn (DDG 113).

## CENTCOM Reports 61 Vessels Redirected, Four Disabled

CENTCOM forces have redirected 61 commercial vessels believed to be linked to Iran since the blockade enforcement began. At least four vessels attempting to evade the blockade have been disabled, according to publicly available reports. The blockade is part of broader U.S. efforts to curb Iran’s alleged support for proxy groups and violations of international sanctions. The operation reflects heightened tensions in the region and the U.S. military’s expanded presence in the Middle East.

## Carrier Strike Groups and Amphibious Ready Groups Deployed

The two carrier strike groups currently enforcing the blockade include the USS George H.W. Bush Carrier Strike Group and another [Nimitz-class carrier group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nimitz-class_aircraft_carrier). These groups typically consist of an aircraft carrier, guided-missile cruisers, destroyers, and support ships. Amphibious Ready Groups (ARGs) are also part of the deployment, providing additional flexibility and firepower in the region. The presence of these groups underscores the U.S. Navy’s commitment to maintaining maritime security and enforcing sanctions.

The blockade enforcement comes amid escalating tensions between the U.S. and Iran, with both sides accusing the other of provocative actions. The deployment of over 20 warships signals a significant increase in U.S. military posture in the Middle East.
