Israeli troops killed a 34-year-old Palestinian man Saturday during a military raid on Jenin refugee camp in the occupied West Bank, the Palestinian Health Ministry said. Forces identified the victim as Nour al-Din Kamal Hassan Fayyad, claiming he attempted to infiltrate a restricted area near the camp. The Israeli military stated soldiers opened fire after Fayyad approached the Jenin camp perimeter, where entry is prohibited due to ongoing operations. Witnesses reported heavy gunfire and armored vehicle deployments during the raid, which also included home searches.

## Escalation in Jenin camp follows year of intensified Israeli operations
The deadly raid marks the latest in a series of Israeli military operations targeting Jenin and Tulkarem refugee camps. Since January 2023, these operations have displaced more than 40,000 Palestinians, according to the United Nations Relief and Works Agency for Palestine Refugees (UNRWA) [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Relief_and_Works_Agency_for_Palestine_Refugees_in_the_Near_East]. The agency warned that continued raids exacerbate humanitarian conditions in densely populated camps. Israeli authorities cite security concerns, alleging armed groups operate within the camps.

## Home raids and settler attacks reported amid West Bank violence
Israeli forces conducted additional raids in Shu’fat refugee camp northeast of Jerusalem Saturday, where troops arrested a Palestinian man after assaulting him, according to the Wafa news agency. The incidents coincide with rising settler violence in the West Bank, where Palestinian and Israeli rights groups report increased attacks on Palestinian communities. Israeli human rights group [B'Tselem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/B%27Tselem) documented 140 settler attacks on Palestinians in the first quarter of 2024 alone. The United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA) [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Office_for_the_Coordination_of_Humanitarian_Affairs] reports a 20% increase in settler-related incidents compared to 2023.

The Palestinian Authority condemned the Jenin raid, calling it a "flagrant violation" of international law. Spokesperson Ibrahim Melhem stated Israeli actions violate ceasefire agreements and escalate tensions. The Israeli military did not immediately respond to requests for comment on the broader operations or settler violence.

## Broader context: West Bank tensions and regional instability
The West Bank remains a flashpoint amid stalled peace negotiations and rising violence. Since October 7, 2023, Israeli military operations in the West Bank have killed at least 426 Palestinians, including 100 children, according to Palestinian officials. Israeli forces have also arrested more than 7,500 Palestinians during the same period. The surge in violence follows the October 7 Hamas-led attack on southern Israel, which killed 1,200 people and triggered a sustained military response in Gaza.

International observers warn that continued raids in refugee camps risk deeper instability. UNRWA Commissioner-General Philippe Lazzarini stated that "the cycle of violence and displacement must end." The agency has called for increased humanitarian access and protection for civilians in conflict zones. Human rights organizations urge both sides to adhere to international law and de-escalate tensions.

With no immediate signs of a ceasefire, analysts predict further clashes in the West Bank. Israeli settlers continue expanding settlements, while Palestinian factions warn of retaliatory attacks. The Biden administration has reiterated calls for restraint, urging both parties to avoid further escalation.
