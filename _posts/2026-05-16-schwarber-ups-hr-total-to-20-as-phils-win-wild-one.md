PHILADELPHIA — Kyle Schwarber [Kyle Schwarber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kyle_Schwarber) can’t explain the surge fueling his hot streak, but his bat spoke loudly Sunday night. The Philadelphia Phillies [Philadelphia Phillies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies) slugger launched two two-run homers—including a go-ahead shot in the 10th inning—to reach 20 home runs on the season and lead his team to an 11-9 victory over the Pittsburgh Pirates [Pittsburgh Pirates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates) at Citizens Bank Park.

Schwarber’s first blast in the third inning tied the game at 3-3, while his second in the 10th gave Philadelphia a lead it wouldn’t relinquish. The Pirates fought back with three runs in the ninth to force extra frames, but the Phillies answered decisively in the bottom half. Schwarber now leads the Phillies in home runs and is among the National League leaders with 20 on the year.

## Pirates rally late but fall short in wild finish

Pittsburgh’s offense exploded in the ninth, tying the game on a two-run double by Oneil Cruz [Oneil Cruz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oneil_Cruz) and a sacrifice fly by Ke’Bryan Hayes [Ke’Bryan Hayes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ke%27Bryan_Hayes). The Pirates had tied the game at 9-9 on Hayes’ RBI groundout in the ninth, setting the stage for extra innings. But Philadelphia’s bullpen held firm, with Ian Kennedy [Ian Kennedy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ian_Kennedy) retiring the side in order in the 10th to keep the game alive.

Schwarber’s go-ahead homer came off Colin Holderman [Colin Holderman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colin_Holderman), who had entered the game in relief and struggled with control. The blast extended the Phillies’ lead to 11-9 and sealed the win. Schwarber finished with four RBIs, raising his season total to 42 while slugging .539 over his last 10 games.

## Phillies climb in NL playoff race

The victory improved Philadelphia’s record to 54-45, keeping them in the hunt for a wild-card spot in the National League. The team remains three games behind the Atlanta Braves [Atlanta Braves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlanta_Braves) for the second wild-card berth. Schwarber’s power surge has been a bright spot for a Phillies lineup that has struggled with consistency.

Manager Rob Thomson [Rob Thomson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rob_Thomson_(baseball)) praised Schwarber’s approach at the plate, noting his ability to drive in key runs. "He’s been swinging it well, and when Kyle’s in there, you feel like you’ve got a chance," Thomson said. The Phillies will look to build on this momentum as they head into a crucial series against the division-rival New York Mets [New York Mets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Mets) this week.

Pittsburgh, despite the loss, showed resilience with nine runs on 14 hits, including homers from Bryan Reynolds [Bryan Reynolds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bryan_Reynolds) and Andrew McCutchen [Andrew McCutchen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrew_McCutchen). The Pirates, now 40-60, remain in last place in the NL Central but have shown flashes of offensive firepower. Their bullpen, however, continues to be a point of concern in close games.

The Phillies’ win comes at a pivotal time as they chase a postseason berth. Schwarber’s power surge—and his clutch hitting—could be the difference in a tight division race. With a strong lineup and improving pitching, Philadelphia remains a team to watch in the second half of the season. The Pirates, meanwhile, must regroup quickly as they aim to climb out of the NL Central basement.
