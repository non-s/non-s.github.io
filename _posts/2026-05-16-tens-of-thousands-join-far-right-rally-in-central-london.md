Tens of thousands of people joined a far-right march in central London on Saturday, led by activist [Tommy Robinson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tommy_Robinson_(activist)) and focused on stricter immigration policies. The demonstration prompted one of the Metropolitan Police’s largest public order operations in recent years, with authorities deploying significant resources to manage potential unrest. A pro-Palestinian counter-protest also unfolded in the capital, adding to security concerns as tensions flared between opposing groups. Police reported no major incidents by late afternoon, though officers remained on high alert throughout the day. Robinson, a controversial figure known for his anti-immigration stance, addressed the crowd near Parliament Square before the march proceeded through key areas of the city. The Metropolitan Police confirmed deploying thousands of officers, including mounted units and officers in riot gear, to prevent clashes between demonstrators and counter-protesters. City officials had earlier warned of disruption to public transport and road closures in central London, urging residents and commuters to avoid the area. The rally comes amid rising public debate over immigration policies in the UK, with far-right groups gaining visibility in recent months. Police sources stated that intelligence suggested the possibility of isolated confrontations, though the overall atmosphere remained largely peaceful. Robinson, who has a history of organizing similar events, framed the march as a protest against what he described as government failure to control borders. The counter-protest organized by pro-Palestinian groups drew a smaller but vocal crowd, criticizing the far-right demonstration for its perceived discriminatory undertones. By evening, the Metropolitan Police reported 12 arrests, primarily for public order offenses and minor disturbances. Authorities urged calm as both protests disbanded without major incidents, though tensions lingered in affected neighborhoods. The events highlighted ongoing societal divisions in the UK over immigration and national identity, with political leaders calling for restraint on all sides. Police are reviewing footage and statements to assess whether further action is needed against individuals involved in disorder. The Metropolitan Police said they would continue monitoring social media for calls to escalate tensions in the coming days. Experts noted that such demonstrations often serve as a barometer for broader public frustrations over economic and social issues tied to migration.
