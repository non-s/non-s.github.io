French authorities have initiated deportation proceedings against Palestinian activist Ramy Shaath, labeling him a 'serious threat to public order,' his lawyer confirmed on Friday. The move comes amid heightened scrutiny of pro-Palestinian advocacy in Europe following the Israel-Hamas war in Gaza. Shaath, a co-founder of the Paris-based organization Urgence Palestine, was detained without charge in Egypt for nearly three years from 2019 to 2022 before being released and permitted to join his wife in France.

Shaath’s legal team has denounced the decision, calling it politically motivated and an attempt to silence Palestinian voices. "This is a clear violation of international human rights standards," said one of his lawyers, who requested anonymity due to the sensitivity of the case. French immigration authorities have not publicly detailed the specific reasons behind the deportation order, citing confidentiality rules. However, the classification of 'serious threat to public order' is a rarely used legal designation that allows for expedited removal.

## Urgence Palestine and Shaath’s advocacy work

Urgence Palestine, co-founded by Shaath in 2018, describes itself as a solidarity movement supporting Palestinian rights through demonstrations and lobbying efforts. The group has organized numerous protests in Paris, including marches condemning Israeli military actions in Gaza. French officials have previously monitored such demonstrations under anti-terrorism and public order laws, though no formal charges have been filed against Urgence Palestine.

Shaath, who holds both Palestinian and French residency permits, had been living in Paris with his wife since his release from Egyptian detention in 2022. His lawyer stated that Shaath was granted refugee status in France, though immigration officials have now moved to revoke that status based on the new allegations. The activist has denied any wrongdoing, calling the accusations baseless and an attempt to suppress Palestinian solidarity movements.

## International response and legal challenges

Human rights organizations, including Amnesty International and the European Center for Constitutional and Human Rights, have criticized France’s decision. "France must not become a tool for silencing legitimate political dissent," said a spokesperson for Amnesty International France. The groups are urging French courts to block the deportation, arguing that the evidence against Shaath has not been made public and that his detention in Egypt suggests prior political targeting.

France’s deportation process could take weeks or months, depending on legal appeals. Shaath’s legal team has filed an urgent motion to suspend the removal order while challenging its legality. If the motion fails, he may be detained in an immigration facility before being flown to a third country. His lawyer confirmed that Shaath’s wife, a French citizen, remains in Paris and has not been targeted in the proceedings.

## Broader implications for activism in Europe

The case reflects growing tensions across Europe over the balance between national security and freedom of expression, particularly regarding Palestine-related activism. Several European countries have recently tightened laws on protest and speech, citing concerns over antisemitism and extremism. Critics argue these measures disproportionately target Palestinian advocates and critics of Israel.

Shaath’s situation is not unique. Other Palestinian activists and organizations in France and across the EU have faced similar scrutiny, including frozen assets, canceled events, and travel bans. Human rights groups warn that such measures could chill free speech and solidarity movements globally.
