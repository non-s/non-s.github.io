A dramatic mid-air collision between two U.S. Navy E/A-18G Growler electronic attack aircraft happened Sunday afternoon during the Gunfighter Skies Air Show at Mountain Home Air Force Base in Idaho. The mishap was caught on video and shows one Growler closing in on the other from behind before striking the lead aircraft’s rear with its nose. The two jets became entangled briefly before separating, with both crews ejecting safely moments later.

The incident occurred about two miles northwest of the base, according to the 366th Fighter Wing’s Facebook post. The Growler Airshow Team, part of Electronic Attack Squadron (VAQ) 129 based at Naval Air Station [Whidbey Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naval_Air_Station_Whidbey_Island), was performing a two-jet display when the accident happened. The team is known for precision flying demonstrations with the Growler, a specialized aircraft designed for electronic warfare.

Witnesses and attendees at the air show captured the collision on their phones, posting the chaotic footage online within minutes. The videos show the Growler from behind suddenly banking sharply upward before making contact with the lead aircraft. The impact sent both jets into a brief, unstable tumble before the crews ejected. The exact cause of the collision isn’t yet clear, but investigators will likely examine pilot actions, weather conditions, and the aircraft’s flight paths.

All four crew members—two from each jet—landed safely and were quickly recovered by emergency crews. They were taken to a nearby medical facility for evaluation, though no immediate details about their injuries have been released. The Navy has not confirmed whether all crew members are unharmed or if any were transported to a hospital.

The Growler is a key asset for the Navy, used for suppressing enemy air defenses and jamming radar. The E/A-18G variant is a heavily modified version of the F/A-18 Super Hornet, equipped with advanced electronic warfare systems. The [VAQ-129](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronic_Attack_Squadron_129) Growler Airshow Team is one of the few units that performs public aerial demonstrations with the aircraft, making this incident particularly notable.

Air shows often involve high-risk maneuvers, and mid-air collisions, while rare, have happened before. The Navy will likely review its safety protocols for air show performances in light of this incident. The base and the Navy have not released further details about the investigation or any potential changes to upcoming shows.

This isn’t the first time a Growler has been involved in a high-profile incident. In 2020, a Growler from VAQ-142 crashed during a training exercise in Japan, killing both crew members. That accident was attributed to mechanical failure. Investigators will need to determine if a similar issue played a role here or if the collision was caused by pilot error or other factors.
