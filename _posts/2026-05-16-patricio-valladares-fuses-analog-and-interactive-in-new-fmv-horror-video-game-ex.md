Chilean filmmaker [Patricio Valladares](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patricio_Valladares) is stepping into video games with an untitled FMV horror title that fuses analog filmmaking with interactive elements. The English-language project, produced by Vallastudio Films, marks Valladares’ debut in interactive media after years in film and television. The game will be available on Steam and as a mobile app, expanding its reach beyond traditional gaming platforms.

Valladares, known for his work in horror films such as *The Last International Playboy* and *Terror 5*, is leveraging his cinematic background to create a unique experience. Full motion video (FMV) games use pre-recorded video sequences to drive gameplay, typically featuring branching narratives where players’ choices influence the story’s outcome. Valladares’ project appears to push this format further by integrating live-action footage with interactive decision-making, a hybrid approach rarely seen in mainstream horror gaming.

## Valladares blends analog filmmaking with interactive choices

The developer has not yet confirmed a release date or final title, but Valladares described the project as a "cinematic horror experience where every choice matters." Unlike traditional FMV games that rely on pre-set paths, Valladares’ title reportedly allows players to shape the narrative through their decisions, creating a personalized horror journey. The mobile app release suggests accessibility for casual players who may not own gaming consoles or high-end PCs.

Industry analysts note the growing interest in FMV games, particularly in horror, where atmospheric tension aligns well with the format. Valladares’ entry could attract both film enthusiasts and gamers familiar with interactive storytelling. The project also highlights the expanding role of filmmakers in video game development, a trend seen with creators like [David Lynch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Lynch) and [Quentin Tarantino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino) exploring interactive media.

## Valladares targets Steam and mobile audiences

Vallastudio Films, Valladares’ production company, is handling development, though the team remains small compared to major game studios. The dual-platform release strategy aims to maximize visibility, with Steam offering a core audience of PC gamers and the mobile app catering to broader, casual users. Valladares has not disclosed whether the game will include microtransactions or premium pricing, a common discussion point in modern gaming releases.

The horror genre’s popularity, amplified by streaming platforms and social media trends, provides a ripe market for Valladares’ project. Recent FMV horror titles like *The Quarry* and *Five Nights at Freddy’s* have demonstrated strong player engagement, suggesting Valladares’ game could find a receptive audience. His cinematic approach may also appeal to fans of narrative-driven horror who prefer story over gameplay complexity.

Valladares has teased additional details in interviews, including the possibility of celebrity voice actors and collaborations with horror writers. If successful, the project could inspire more filmmakers to explore interactive media, blurring the lines between cinema and gaming. The upcoming months will reveal whether Valladares’ hybrid approach resonates with audiences beyond his established fanbase.

The game’s development comes as interactive entertainment expands into new formats, from VR to mobile storytelling. Valladares’ FMV horror title could become a test case for whether analog filmmaking can hold its own in an industry dominated by digital graphics and procedural generation. If the project delivers on its promise, it may redefine expectations for horror storytelling in gaming.
