Mexican authorities in [Puebla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puebla) confirmed Sunday that 10 people—six men, three women, and a minor—were shot dead in an early-morning attack on a home in [Tehuitzingo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tehuitzingo), a city of about 11,300 people. The state government said the victims were allegedly attacked by armed individuals, though no further details about the suspects or their motives were provided as of mid-morning. Police arrived at the scene after a neighbor called 911, reporting seeing people who appeared unresponsive inside the house. When officers entered, they found multiple people with gunshot wounds. A woman later died in the ambulance on the way to the hospital.

The [Puebla state government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puebla) released a statement saying municipal police responded to the residence after receiving the call. Authorities have yet to release any names of the victims or suspects, and no arrests have been announced. The motive behind the attack remains unclear, adding to the frustration for locals who are already on edge as Mexico prepares to co-host the [FIFA World Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup) with the U.S. and Canada in less than a month.

## A city where violence isn't new
Tehuitzingo, located about 208 kilometers southwest of Mexico City, has seen its share of violence linked to organized crime. While the state of Puebla has not always been a hotspot for cartel battles like other Mexican states, recent years have seen a rise in localized cartel activity and turf wars. The attack comes as Mexico ramps up security measures ahead of the World Cup, a global event that puts extra pressure on local authorities to prevent high-profile incidents. Authorities haven’t said whether this shooting is connected to organized crime, but they haven’t ruled it out either.

The state’s Attorney General’s Office has opened an investigation, and authorities are appealing to the public for any information that could help identify the attackers. Local residents told reporters they were shocked by the violence, especially so close to their homes. One neighbor, who asked not to be named, said she heard gunshots around 5 a.m. but assumed they were fireworks until police arrived. She described the scene as chaotic when officers and emergency responders flooded the area.

## What happens next
Authorities are still processing the crime scene and interviewing witnesses, though progress has been slow due to limited early evidence. Forensic teams are expected to spend the day collecting ballistics and gathering additional clues. Meanwhile, the state government has pledged to increase patrols in the area, but residents say they’re worried about their safety. The lack of answers so far has only fueled rumors, with some speculating the attack could be tied to local disputes or cartel activity.

This isn’t the first time violence has disrupted life in Puebla ahead of a major event. Earlier this year, authorities boosted security around tourist spots after threats from criminal groups. The World Cup is just weeks away, and while the focus remains on the games, incidents like this one serve as a grim reminder of the challenges Mexico faces in balancing international exposure with ongoing security concerns.
