Five years ago, Cascade was a spy leading a dangerous operation. Today, she’s thawed from stasis, stuck with desk work, and drowning in guilt. Zero Parades: For Dead Spies, the upcoming RPG from the team behind Disco Elysium, throws her into Portofiro, a gritty city where the past refuses to stay buried. The game hits shelves October 2024, and it’s already turning heads for its sharp writing and brutal honesty about redemption. Players don’t just follow a spy’s mission—they walk with her through the cost of forgiveness, both for others and herself.

## A familiar feel with a new story
Zero Parades isn’t a direct sequel, but it smells like the Disco Elysium DNA. The writing stays dense, witty, and packed with choices that feel real. Where Disco Elysium dropped you into the shoes of a depressed detective, Zero Parades swaps the noir for espionage. Cascade’s internal monologue isn’t just a hangover—it’s a lifetime of regret. The game leans into its themes hard: what does it mean to earn forgiveness when you’ve burned bridges? Can you ever truly move on from the people you’ve hurt?

The city of Portofiro isn’t Revachol—no rainy socialist capital here. It’s a Mediterranean port where the salt air mixes with the stink of corruption. The architecture feels lived-in, the people feel real, and the city itself becomes a character. Developers describe it as a place where every alley hides a secret, and every choice Cascade makes ripples through its streets. The art style leans into warm oranges and sharp shadows, a visual shift from Disco Elysium’s moody blues.

## The weight of a spy’s burden
Cascade isn’t a hero. She’s a woman who made costly mistakes, and the game makes sure players feel that weight. The failed operation isn’t just a plot device—it’s a scar that shapes every decision. Dialogue choices aren’t just about success or failure; they’re about guilt, shame, and the messy business of trying to do better. The game’s skill system isn’t about stats—it’s about the stories Cascade tells herself. Each skill isn’t just a tool; it’s a voice in her head, sometimes supportive, sometimes brutal.

The team behind Zero Parades is small but experienced. They’re the same devs who built Disco Elysium’s world, including writer Robert Kurvitz and composer Ludo Nika. Their goal? To prove that a game can be smart, emotional, and still fun without relying on combat as the main driver. Combat exists here, but it’s not the focus. The real battle is internal—with Cascade’s past, her present, and the people she’s let down.

## What’s next for the game
Zero Parades: For Dead Spies launches October 14, 2024, on PC and consoles. The demo’s already out, and players are calling it a must-try for fans of narrative-driven RPGs. Early reviews praise its writing, calling it a rare game that respects the player’s intelligence. The team’s also hinted at post-launch content, though details are scarce. For now, the focus is on Cascade’s story—and whether players will forgive her choices by the end.

This isn’t just a game about spies. It’s about the people behind the missions, the ones who get left behind when things go wrong. It’s about the cost of second chances and the hard work of earning them. And if the trailers are any indication, it’s going to be one hell of a ride.
