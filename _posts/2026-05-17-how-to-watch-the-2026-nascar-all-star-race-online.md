The 2026 NASCAR All-Star Race is the place to see the sport’s biggest stars go all out for cash and glory. The event at [Dover Motor Speedway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dover_Motor_Speedway) tonight pits the best drivers against each other in a no-holds-barred 150-lap sprint. The winner takes home a cool $1 million, but the real prize is the bragging rights that come with beating the best in the business.

## How to watch the All-Star Race without cable

You don’t need a pricey cable package to catch the action. The race streams live on [Fox Sports](https://www.foxsports.com/) starting at 7 p.m. ET. If you’re not a Fox Sports subscriber, you can use the free trial on [Hulu + Live TV](https://www.hulu.com/live-tv) or [Sling TV](https://www.sling.com/) to watch it. Both services let you cancel after the race if you only care about tonight’s event.

## Why tonight’s race matters

This isn’t your average NASCAR race. The All-Star Race guarantees spots to last year’s race winners, so you’re seeing the fastest drivers from 2025 back for another shot. The format includes a mix of street-style races and high-speed oval action, making it one of the most unpredictable events on the calendar. Last year’s winner, [Kyle Larson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kyle_Larson), is back to defend his title, but he’s got company from stars like [Chase Elliott](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chase_Elliott) and [Ryan Blaney](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ryan_Blaney).

## What to expect from the track

Dover Motor Speedway is a 1-mile concrete oval that’s famous for its tight turns and abrasive surface. The track wears down tires fast, forcing drivers to make smart pit stops to stay competitive. The All-Star Race adds another twist: drivers must start the race on the same set of tires they qualify with, which means strategy could decide who wins. If you like your racing with high stakes and no second chances, this is the event to watch.

## How the All-Star Race works

Unlike regular NASCAR races, the All-Star format is all about speed and strategy. There’s no points on the line for the championship, so drivers can push harder without worrying about the season standings. The first 75 laps are a straightforward sprint, but the second half includes segments where drivers can win bonus money for leading laps or winning stages. The final 25 laps are a green-white-checkered finish, meaning a late caution could turn the race upside down.

Tonight’s race is the first major test for NASCAR’s new Next Gen car, which has been on track since 2022. The car’s updated design puts more emphasis on driver skill, and the All-Star Race is where teams often push the limits to see what they can get away with. Keep an eye on how the cars handle the wear and tear at Dover—it could tell us a lot about the car’s durability ahead of the playoffs.

After the checkered flag drops, the focus shifts to Nashville Superspeedway next week, where the regular Cup Series race will take place. But don’t sleep on tonight’s All-Star Race—it’s a chance to see NASCAR at its most entertaining.
