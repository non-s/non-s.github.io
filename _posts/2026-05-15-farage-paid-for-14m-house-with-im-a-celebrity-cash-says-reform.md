Nigel Farage, leader of Reform UK, is facing scrutiny from Parliament’s standards watchdog over a £5m donation from a billionaire and allegations that he used earnings from *I’m a Celebrity* to purchase a £1.4m home. The investigation centers on whether the donations and financial arrangements complied with parliamentary rules on gifts and transparency.

Reform UK confirmed the payments, stating that Farage’s earnings from *I’m a Celebrity* were used to buy the property and that all financial disclosures were made in accordance with parliamentary guidelines. The party did not provide additional details about the billionaire donor or the exact timeline of the transactions.

The watchdog’s investigation follows reports that Farage received the £5m gift, which has raised questions about potential conflicts of interest. The Standards Committee for MPs and Peers is reviewing whether the donations and property purchase align with ethical rules for elected officials. Farage, a prominent Brexit campaigner and former MEP, has not publicly addressed the specifics of the investigation but has previously defended his financial dealings.

## Standards watchdog probes Farage’s finances

The Standards Committee’s review is part of a broader effort to ensure transparency in the finances of public officials. The watchdog has the authority to impose sanctions, including reprimands or suspensions, if it finds that rules were violated. Farage’s case is particularly notable given his high-profile role in UK politics and Reform UK’s growing influence in right-wing discourse.

Reform UK has emphasized that Farage’s financial arrangements were properly declared and that the £5m donation was unrelated to his parliamentary duties. The party also noted that Farage’s earnings from *I’m a Celebrity*—a reality TV show where he finished as runner-up in 2023—were used for personal expenses, including the home purchase. However, critics argue that the timing of the donations and property transaction warrants further scrutiny.

## Farage’s financial history under scrutiny

This is not the first time Farage’s finances have come under the microscope. In 2018, he faced questions about his use of European Parliament funds, leading to a separate investigation. His financial transparency has long been a point of contention among opponents and watchdog groups. The current investigation adds to the ongoing debate about the ethical standards of UK politicians and the accountability of donations.

The Standards Committee is expected to conclude its review in the coming months, with potential implications for Farage’s political career. If violations are found, it could further damage his reputation and influence within Reform UK and the broader political landscape. The case also raises broader questions about the oversight of donations to political figures and the need for stricter financial disclosure rules.

What happens next will depend on the watchdog’s findings and whether Farage or Reform UK faces any formal consequences. In the meantime, the investigation has reignited discussions about the ethical obligations of public officials and the transparency of their financial dealings.
