An astrophotographer has captured a remarkable deep-space image of Markarian’s Chain, a striking alignment of galaxies visible through amateur telescopes. The photo, taken from a backyard setup, shows eight bright galaxies strung together in a visually stunning cosmic chain spanning the constellations [Leo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_(constellation)), [Virgo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgo_(constellation)), and [Coma Berenices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coma_Berenices). This celestial feature is one of the most photographed deep-sky objects by backyard astronomers due to its accessibility and beauty.


## How to find Markarian’s Chain in the night sky

Markarian’s Chain is part of the [Virgo Cluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgo_cluster), a massive grouping of galaxies located 50 to 60 million light-years from Earth. To locate it, stargazers should first find the bright star [Denebola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denebola) in Leo, then scan eastward toward the constellation Virgo. The chain appears as a faint, curved line of smudges in the sky, best observed under dark conditions away from city lights. Spring months offer the clearest views for Northern Hemisphere observers.


## The science behind the galactic chain

Markarian’s Chain is named after Soviet astrophysicist [Benjamin Markarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_Markarian), who studied the galaxies in the 1960s and discovered their unusual motion. The chain includes several massive elliptical galaxies, including [M84](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Messier_84), [M86](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Messier_86), and [NGC 4438](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NGC_4438), which are gravitationally bound in a slow, orbital dance. These galaxies host billions of stars each, with some containing supermassive black holes at their centers that influence their motion.


## Tools for photographing deep-space objects

Capturing images like this requires patience and the right equipment. Most amateur astrophotographers use telescopes with focal lengths between 400mm and 1000mm, paired with cooled CCD or CMOS cameras capable of long exposures. The photographer who took this image used a 10-hour total exposure time to reveal faint details in the galaxies. Light pollution filters and tracking mounts are essential to counteract Earth’s rotation and improve image sharpness.


## Why Markarian’s Chain fascinates astronomers

The chain’s unique structure provides clues about the dynamics of galaxy clusters. Some galaxies in the chain, like NGC 4435 and NGC 4438, show signs of past interactions, with distorted shapes and streams of gas between them. Studying these interactions helps scientists understand how galaxies evolve over billions of years. For amateur astronomers, Markarian’s Chain offers a glimpse into the sheer scale and beauty of the universe from their own backyards.


The next step for many stargazers is to observe or photograph Markarian’s Chain themselves. Local astronomy clubs often host public viewing nights where beginners can try spotting the chain with guidance. As technology improves, even more detailed images of this celestial wonder are expected from amateur astronomers worldwide.
