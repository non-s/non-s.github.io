A 66-year-old former teacher in [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France) surrendered to police after an influencer used artificial intelligence to pose as a 14-year-old girl in an online sting operation. The sting was broadcast live, exposing the suspect’s alleged intent before authorities confirmed his arrest. Prosecutors in France confirmed the man handed himself in following the broadcast, which has sparked debate over the use of AI in self-justice scenarios.

The influencer, known for exposing online predators, created an AI-powered chatbot designed to mimic a teenage girl. The chatbot engaged the suspect in conversation, recording and broadcasting the interaction in real time. Within hours, the broadcast went viral, drawing public attention and prompting authorities to act swiftly. The suspect, identified locally as a retired educator, was located and detained after the livestream.

French police confirmed the man was charged under laws related to child exploitation and online grooming. Authorities stated the investigation is ongoing, with no further details released about the suspect’s background or prior offenses. The case has intensified scrutiny over the use of AI tools in vigilante justice, raising concerns about legality, privacy, and the risk of false accusations.

Legal experts warn that while such stings may expose wrongdoing, they could also violate privacy rights or entrap individuals without due process. The suspect’s decision to surrender suggests he was aware of the public exposure and chose to cooperate, though motives remain unclear. French prosecutors have not commented on whether additional charges may follow based on evidence from the broadcast.

The use of AI in this case reflects a growing trend among online activists using technology to identify and expose suspected criminals. While some applaud such efforts as modern forms of accountability, critics argue they bypass legal safeguards and could lead to abuse. The outcome may influence future use of AI in investigative or vigilante contexts globally.

Authorities are expected to release further details as the investigation develops. Meanwhile, the case has prompted calls for clearer regulations on AI use in public exposure tactics.
