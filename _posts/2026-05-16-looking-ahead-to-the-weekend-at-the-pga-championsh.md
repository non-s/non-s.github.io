Scottie Scheffler [has seized control](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scottie_Scheffler) of the PGA Championship after firing a 5-under 65 on Friday at Aronimink Golf Club in Pennsylvania. The 27-year-old Texan carded a two-round total of 10-under, leading by two strokes over Xander Schauffele. Scheffler’s round included six birdies and just one bogey, showcasing his dominance in the clubhouse.

Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania, hosts the final two rounds of the tournament. Known for its challenging layout and history of tough scoring, the course has already tested the field. Scheffler’s lead is slender but significant, especially with weekend conditions expected to tighten.

## Scheffler’s path to victory: Can anyone catch him?

Scheffler, ranked No. 1 in the world, has been the most consistent player over the past two months. His iron play and putting have been sharp, but the weekend will demand precision under pressure. Schauffele, the 2024 PGA Championship defending champion, sits tied for second at 8 under. Brooks Koepka [chases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brooks_Koepka) at 7 under, while 2022 champion Justin Thomas lurks at 6 under. The chase pack is tightly grouped, making for a compelling weekend.

Weather forecasts indicate wind and rain could play a factor Saturday and Sunday. Aronimink’s tree-lined fairways and undulating greens will reward accuracy, punishing errant shots. Scheffler’s ability to handle wind will be tested, while Schauffele and Koepka bring major-winning experience to the weekend.

Aronimink has hosted PGA tournaments before but not in this decade, adding intrigue to the venue. The club’s reputation for difficult scoring could swing momentum quickly. Scheffler’s lead is fragile, and one bad stretch could erase it entirely.

## Aronimink’s role in the weekend drama

Aronimink Golf Club, a private course near Philadelphia, has a storied history in golf. It has hosted PGA Tour events in the past, most recently the 2003 Buick Open. The course’s architect, William Flynn, designed it to challenge every facet of a player’s game. This weekend, it will serve as the backdrop for a potential first major title for Scheffler—and a chance for others to reclaim the trophy.
