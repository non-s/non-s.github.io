## Introduction to the Issue: The French government's plan to charge students from outside the EU 16 times more than previously has stoked discontent among international students and sparked a debate about access to higher education in Europe. The 'Choose France for Higher Education' scheme aims to remove an opt-out system that French universities often used to keep non-EU students' fees the same as their EU counterparts. [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France) is not the only European country to charge international students higher fees, but the sudden increase has raised concerns about equity and access to education. ## Comparison to Other European Countries: International students often pay a premium to study in Europe, but the fees vary significantly from country to country. While some countries like [Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germany) charge minimal or no tuition fees to international students, others like the [United Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom) charge significantly higher fees. The French government's plan has sparked protests and highlighted the problems faced in the European country's higher education sector. ## Implications and Reactions: The proposed fee hike is expected to net universities an extra €250 million a year, but it has also sparked concerns about the impact on international student enrollment and the diversity of the student body. [Deutsche Welle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deutsche_Welle) reports that the move is expected to affect thousands of students from outside the EU who are planning to study in France. The French government's plan has been criticized by student organizations and advocacy groups, who argue that it will limit access to higher education for students from lower-income countries.
