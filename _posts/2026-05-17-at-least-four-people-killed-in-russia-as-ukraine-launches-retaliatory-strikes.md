Ukraine launched a massive drone attack on Russia overnight, hitting at least 14 regions including the capital city, Moscow, and the Crimean peninsula, Russian authorities said Sunday. The strike involved nearly 600 drones, the largest single wave of the war so far, according to the Russian defense ministry. At least four people were killed, and the attack targeted areas near Moscow, the Black Sea, and the Azov Sea, officials added.

The Ukrainian strike was a direct response to a three-day Russian aerial assault on Ukraine last week that killed 18 people and injured more than 100, according to Ukrainian officials. That attack marked the deadliest Russian strike on Ukrainian cities since the start of the full-scale invasion in 2022. Moscow’s assault included missile barrages and drone strikes, hitting residential areas in multiple cities, including Kyiv, Kharkiv, and Dnipro.

Russian authorities said the Ukrainian drones caused damage to infrastructure and civilian buildings, though they did not specify the exact locations. Videos shared on social media showed fires and explosions in several regions, including near Moscow. The Russian defense ministry claimed to have shot down more than half of the incoming drones, but some managed to hit their targets. The strike disrupted air travel at several regional airports, with flights delayed or canceled as authorities conducted security checks.

Ukrainian officials have not confirmed responsibility for the drone attack, but it follows a pattern of escalating strikes deep inside Russia in recent months. Ukraine has increasingly used long-range drones to target military sites, oil refineries, and logistics hubs in Russia, aiming to disrupt the country’s war effort. The latest strike appears designed to retaliate for last week’s civilian casualties and demonstrate Ukraine’s ability to project force beyond its borders.

The timing of the attack—just days after Russia’s deadly assault on Ukraine—raises questions about the next phase of the war. Analysts suggest Ukraine may be shifting tactics to pressure Russia by hitting high-profile targets inside its territory. Meanwhile, Russia has warned of a "full-scale" response if such attacks continue, though it’s unclear what form that might take. The escalation underscores the growing intensity of the conflict as both sides struggle to gain the upper hand.

For now, the focus is on assessing the damage and preventing further strikes. Russian air defenses are on high alert, and authorities have urged civilians to stay indoors and report any suspicious activity. Ukraine has not commented on the operation beyond acknowledging the broader context of retaliation. The coming days will likely reveal whether this was a one-off strike or the start of a new phase in the war.
