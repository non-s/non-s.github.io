A murder investigation is underway in East Belfast after 52-year-old Nicholas Gordon died from severe injuries sustained during a robbery early Tuesday. Police discovered Gordon inside an apartment on the Newtownards Road just after 2 a.m., suffering multiple trauma injuries, and rushed him to the Royal Victoria Hospital where he later died. Detectives have since classified the case as murder and are appealing for anyone who saw suspicious activity in the area around that time to come forward.

Neighbors described hearing loud noises and what sounded like a struggle coming from the building around 2 a.m., but didn't intervene as they assumed it was just another late-night disturbance common in the area. One resident, who asked not to be named, said the building's security door had been forced open, which is unusual for the usually quiet street. "We heard banging and shouting, then it went quiet. I thought maybe someone was moving furniture or something," the neighbor told local reporters.

## Police appeal for witnesses as murder probe intensifies

Detective Inspector Mark Bell confirmed the investigation is now focused on the circumstances leading up to Gordon's death. "This was a violent and targeted attack," Bell said. "We're treating it as murder and urge anyone with information—no matter how small—to contact us immediately." Police have cordoned off the apartment building and are reviewing CCTV footage from nearby businesses and homes. Forensic teams are also examining the scene for evidence.

Gordon, who went by Nicky, lived alone in the ground-floor apartment. Friends described him as a quiet man who kept to himself but was well-liked in the community. His death has shocked locals, who say violent crime is rare in this part of East Belfast. The area is primarily residential with a mix of social housing and private homes, and violent incidents are uncommon.

## Questions mount over security and response time

Police have faced scrutiny in the past over response times in East Belfast, though this incident appears to have been reported quickly after the attack. The ambulance service confirmed an emergency call was received at 2:07 a.m., and paramedics arrived within eight minutes. The Royal Victoria Hospital, where Gordon was taken, is one of Northern Ireland's major trauma centers, but his injuries were too severe to save him.

Gordon's family has issued a statement asking for privacy during this difficult time. "Nicky was a kind soul who didn't deserve this," the statement read. "We just want justice for him." Police are urging anyone who saw anything unusual near the Newtownards Road around 2 a.m. Tuesday to contact them. The investigation is still in its early stages, and more details are expected to emerge in the coming days.

Local councillor Emma Sheerin called for increased police presence in the area following the attack. "This is a terrible tragedy," Sheerin said. "We need to ensure our communities feel safe and that incidents like this don't happen again." The Police Service of Northern Ireland has not commented on whether additional patrols will be deployed.

The investigation remains active, and detectives are following multiple leads. Anyone with information is asked to contact detectives on 101, quoting reference 2089 of 17/09/24, or anonymously via Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.
