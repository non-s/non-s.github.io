Newcastle United’s community arm has transformed benches in Leazes Park into beacons for mental health awareness. The project, led by the [Newcastle United Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newcastle_United_Foundation), uses brightly colored seats to prompt conversations among fans about emotional well-being. Defender Dan Burn, who has been vocal about mental health, spoke to BBC Sport about the initiative and its role in normalizing open discussions.

The benches, painted in shades of blue and yellow, stand out against the park’s greenery. Each carries a simple message: a reminder to check on friends and family. The foundation chose Leazes Park for its high foot traffic, ensuring visibility among matchday crowds and local residents. Burn, who has publicly discussed his own struggles, emphasized the need for fans to support one another. "It’s about creating a culture where people feel comfortable asking for help," he said. The campaign aligns with broader efforts to reduce stigma around mental health in sports.

## Mental health stigma in football remains a challenge

Football has long been slow to address mental health openly, despite high-profile cases highlighting the issue. Players like Burn are increasingly using their platforms to share personal stories, breaking the silence that once surrounded psychological struggles. The Newcastle United Foundation’s project targets fans beyond the pitch, aiming to foster community-wide dialogue. Research shows that men, particularly in sports cultures, are less likely to seek help due to perceived stigma. Initiatives like this one aim to change that perception.

Burn’s involvement underscores the growing recognition of mental health as a critical part of overall well-being. He noted that small gestures—like checking in on a teammate or friend—can make a significant difference. The foundation’s work complements existing mental health resources, including partnerships with local charities. By placing these benches in a public space, the campaign ensures that the conversation about mental health is visible and accessible to everyone.

## How the initiative fits into broader mental health efforts

The Newcastle United Foundation’s bench project is part of a wider push to integrate mental health support into sports communities. Clubs across the UK have launched similar initiatives, from crisis text lines to mental health first-aid training for staff. Burn’s advocacy reflects a shift in how athletes view their role in public health. "Football can be a powerful platform," he said. "If we can make one person feel less alone, it’s worth it."

Local mental health professionals have praised the effort, calling it a practical step toward reducing isolation. The benches serve as a physical reminder that support is available, even in informal settings. For many fans, seeing these messages in a familiar space—like a park they visit before or after games—could encourage them to reach out. The foundation plans to monitor the initiative’s impact, tracking any increases in engagement with mental health services over the coming months.

The project also aligns with national campaigns, such as the [Heads Together](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heads_Together) initiative, which encourages people to talk about mental health. Newcastle United’s approach is unique in its grassroots method, using everyday spaces to spark conversations. Burn hopes the benches will become a regular part of the city’s landscape, prompting ongoing discussions long after their initial installation. "It’s not just about the paint or the color," he said. "It’s about the conversations that follow."

A iniciativa, que ganhou as redes sociais, veio da Newcastle United Foundation e busca quebrar o estigma em torno do tema, especialmente entre torcedores e moradores da cidade inglesa. Mas o impacto desse tipo de campanha transcende fronteiras, levantando uma questão importante para o Brasil: como estamos lidando com a saúde mental em meio ao cotidiano acelerado e às pressões sociais?

A campanha do Newcastle, ao usar o esporte — paixão nacional em terras brasileiras — como ferramenta de conscientização, mostra que a linguagem do futebol pode ser um canal poderoso para abordar problemas que, muitas vezes, são varridos para debaixo do tapete.
