The San Antonio Spurs secured their place in the Western Conference finals Monday with a dominant 110-97 win over the Minnesota Timberwolves. Stephon Castle led the charge with 28 points, while the Spurs’ defense held Minnesota to just 40 percent shooting. The victory capped a hard-fought series and marked San Antonio’s return to the conference finals for the first time since 2017.

## Spurs ride strong second half to series victory
Castle’s performance was the difference, as he shot 10-of-17 from the field and added six assists. The Spurs outscored the Timberwolves 30-18 in the third quarter to build an insurmountable lead. Minnesota, led by Anthony Edwards’ 32 points, fought back in the fourth but couldn’t overcome the deficit. San Antonio’s bench also contributed 25 points, including key stops down the stretch.

Coach Gregg Popovich praised the team’s resilience after the game. "We knew it wouldn’t be easy," Popovich said. "Minnesota is a tough opponent, but our guys stayed disciplined and executed when it mattered most." The Spurs now face the winner of the Oklahoma City Thunder-Dallas Mavericks series in the Western Conference finals.

## Castle’s breakout game fuels Spurs’ playoff push
Castle, a second-year guard, has steadily increased his production this postseason. His 28-point effort was his highest scoring game of the year, showcasing his growth as a playmaker. Veteran Dejounte Murray added 22 points and 10 rebounds, while Victor Wembanyama contributed 18 points and nine blocks.

The Timberwolves, despite Edwards’ 32-point effort, were outmatched in key moments. Minnesota’s offense stalled in the fourth quarter, allowing San Antonio to pull away. Coach Chris Finch acknowledged his team’s effort but cited missed opportunities as the difference. "We gave ourselves a chance, but San Antonio made the big plays when it counted," Finch said.

## What’s next for the Spurs and Timberwolves
The Spurs will now prepare for the Western Conference finals, where they’ll face either the Oklahoma City Thunder or Dallas Mavericks. San Antonio’s balanced attack and strong defense make them a formidable opponent in the next round. Meanwhile, Minnesota will regroup after a tough loss, focusing on building for the future.

The series win solidifies San Antonio’s return to championship contention. With a deep roster and improving young talent, the Spurs are poised for a deep playoff run.
