Country music legend [Dolly Parton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dolly_Parton) delivered a health update to fans on May 4, sharing both encouraging news and new challenges. In a short Instagram reel posted across her social media platforms, the 78-year-old singer acknowledged ongoing kidney health issues along with weaker immunity and digestive systems. She also confirmed that medication side effects had led to the cancellation of her scheduled Las Vegas residency shows.

Parton opened the video with a lighthearted tone, saying she had “some good news, and a little bad news” about her health. While she did not specify the exact medical issues, she referenced past kidney problems that have required ongoing monitoring. The side effects from new medications have impacted her ability to perform, prompting the difficult decision to postpone her Vegas shows.

## Las Vegas residency postponed due to medication effects

The singer confirmed that her planned Las Vegas residency at The Venetian Resort, originally scheduled to begin in early June, would not proceed as planned. “The bad news is, I had to cancel my Las Vegas shows because the medicine I’m on makes me too sick to perform,” Parton said in the video. The shows were part of her long-running commitment to live performances, though she has not announced rescheduled dates.

Parton emphasized that her health team made the decision based on her current condition and the need to prioritize recovery. “I don’t want to risk it. I want to be back on that stage when I’m 100%,” she stated. Fans have expressed support across social media, with many hoping for her speedy return.

## Past kidney concerns and ongoing health challenges

Parton has previously spoken about kidney health issues, including a 2021 diagnosis of early-stage breast cancer that required chemotherapy. While she recovered fully from cancer, she has continued to manage other health concerns. In her latest update, she referenced both weakened immunity and digestive system challenges, which have contributed to her current situation.

The singer has built a career spanning over six decades, known not only for her music but also for her philanthropy and business ventures. Despite her health concerns, she remains active in charitable work, including her long-standing support for literacy through the Imagination Library program.

## What happens next for Dolly Parton

Parton did not specify a timeline for her recovery or return to performing. However, she assured fans in the video that she is focused on getting better and will return when she is fully healthy. “I’ll be back, honey,” she said with her signature warmth. Industry insiders expect she will resume her Las Vegas residency once her doctors confirm it is safe.

The postponement marks the second time in recent years that Parton has altered performance plans due to health reasons. In 2021, she postponed tour dates during cancer treatment. Her resilience and transparency have earned widespread admiration from fans and colleagues alike.

As one of the most beloved figures in country music, Parton’s health continues to be of interest to millions. Her latest update reflects both honesty about current limitations and determination to return to the stage she loves.
