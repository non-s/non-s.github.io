Writer-director Aleshea Harris’s Black Southern Gothic thriller ‘Is God Is’ is transitioning from the stage to the screen in a high-profile adaptation. The project, backed by Beyoncé’s production company, Parkwood Entertainment, follows the play’s acclaimed 2018 debut at the Soho Repertory Theatre in New York. Harris, who wrote the original play, will direct the film version, marking her feature filmmaking debut.

Harris’s play, described as a revenge saga set in the American South, blends dark humor with brutal violence. The story follows twin sisters on a mission to avenge their mother’s death, exploring themes of family, race and justice. The play’s raw intensity and Harris’s sharp dialogue earned critical praise and a cult following, leading to its 2022 West End transfer in London.

The film adaptation retains the play’s Southern Gothic roots while expanding its scope. Beyoncé’s involvement signals strong industry interest in the project, which has attracted a roster of high-profile actors. Sources confirm the cast will include rising stars alongside established names, though specific roles and actors remain unannounced. Production is expected to begin in late 2024, with filming locations spanning Texas and Louisiana to preserve the story’s authentic Southern setting.

Harris, a Los Angeles-based writer, has long been praised for her unflinching portrayal of Black Southern life. Her work challenges traditional narratives, offering a fresh perspective on the region’s history and culture. ‘Is God Is’ stands out for its unapologetic depiction of violence and retribution, themes rarely explored with such ferocity in mainstream entertainment. The play’s success on stage paved the way for its screen adaptation, proving Harris’s ability to craft gripping, dialogue-driven stories.

The project also highlights the growing demand for Black-led narratives in Hollywood. Harris’s partnership with Beyoncé’s Parkwood Entertainment underscores the industry’s shift toward centering Black voices in genre films. The adaptation arrives amid a surge of Southern Gothic stories hitting screens, from television to film, reflecting a broader cultural fascination with the region’s complex legacy.

For Harris, the move to film represents a natural evolution of her artistry. She has spent years refining ‘Is God Is,’ first as a play and now as a script for the screen. The adaptation allows her to reach a wider audience while maintaining the story’s raw, visceral tone. Industry observers expect the film to generate significant buzz ahead of its release, particularly given Beyoncé’s involvement and Harris’s reputation for bold storytelling.

With pre-production underway, the film’s release date remains unconfirmed, but early projections place it in theaters by late 2025. The adaptation of ‘Is God Is’ could redefine Harris’s career and set a new standard for Black Southern Gothic storytelling in cinema.

Com a estreia prevista para os próximos meses, "Is God Is" deve acirrar debates sobre os limites entre arte e provocação, enquanto abre caminho para novas produções que ousem transitar entre gêneros e estilos.
