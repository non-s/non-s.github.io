Healthgrades, a leading healthcare ratings organization, has announced the winners of its 2026 Outstanding Patient Experience Awards. The list includes 373 hospitals across 46 states, representing the top 15% of U.S. hospitals for patient experience.

The awards recognize facilities that deliver high-quality clinical care while maintaining strong patient satisfaction. Healthgrades evaluates hospitals based on patient surveys, care outcomes, and experience metrics to determine recipients. Hospitals must meet rigorous standards to qualify for the honor.

Recipients span 29 specialties, with some hospitals earning recognition in multiple categories. The 2026 cohort includes both large academic medical centers and community hospitals. Texas leads with the most honorees, followed by California and Florida. The full list is available on Healthgrades’ official website.

## How hospitals qualify for the Outstanding Patient Experience Awards

Healthgrades uses a three-year rolling analysis of patient experience data to determine winners. The evaluation includes metrics from the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services [CMS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centers_for_Medicare_%26_Medicaid_Services), as well as patient feedback on communication, cleanliness, and overall care.

Hospitals must score in the top 15% nationally across all evaluated categories. The process excludes hospitals with fewer than three years of consistent data. This ensures only consistently high-performing facilities receive recognition.

## Why patient experience matters in hospital rankings

Research shows patient experience directly correlates with better health outcomes. Hospitals with strong patient satisfaction scores often see lower readmission rates and higher adherence to treatment plans.

The awards highlight hospitals that balance clinical excellence with compassionate care. Patients and families increasingly rely on these rankings when choosing healthcare providers. The 2026 honorees set a benchmark for quality in the U.S. healthcare system.

## What’s next for the awarded hospitals

Winning hospitals receive marketing support from Healthgrades, including award badges and online promotion. Many use the recognition in patient outreach and community engagement efforts. Some facilities also leverage the award for recruitment and retention of top medical staff.

The 2026 list will be published in Healthgrades’ annual report and on its website. Hospitals not selected this year can use the evaluation criteria to improve future performance. The organization will open the 2027 awards cycle later this year.
