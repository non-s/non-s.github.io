Italian Prime Minister [Giorgia Meloni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giorgia_Meloni) and President [Sergio Mattarella](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sergio_Mattarella) spent Sunday visiting victims of a violent attack that unfolded the day before in the northern Italian city of [Modena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modena). Around 4:30 p.m. local time on Saturday, a 31-year-old man identified as Salim El Khoudri plowed his car through crowds at high speed on the city’s main shopping street, Via Emilia, before stabbing a pedestrian and fleeing. Passers-by quickly intervened, tackling the driver and holding him until police arrived to take him into custody.

## Eight hospitalized, two with amputated legs
According to [ANSA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ANSA), a state news agency, eight people were taken to local hospitals in serious condition, including two women—aged 55 and 69—who had their legs amputated after suffering severe injuries. As of Sunday, one of the women remained in critical condition, while authorities continued to monitor the conditions of the others. The city prosecutor’s office described the attack as ‘indiscriminate, random, and deliberate,’ noting no clear target beyond the crowd itself.

## Leaders visit hospitals without public statements
Meloni and Mattarella spent Sunday touring two of Modena’s hospitals where the injured were being treated, but neither leader issued statements afterward. The prime minister had already called the attack ‘extremely serious’ on Saturday, publicly thanking residents who ‘courageously intervened’ to stop the attacker. Italian interior minister Matteo Piantedosi later told reporters the suspect’s motive appeared linked to ‘a situation of psychiatric distress,’ though an official investigation is still underway.

## Modena’s community rallies around victims
The city of Modena, known for its historic center and as home to luxury carmaker [Ferrari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferrari), was left shaken by the violence. Local residents and business owners quickly organized a fundraiser to support the victims, with donations pouring in from across Italy. Social media flooded with messages of shock and solidarity, as the country grapples with the latest in a series of violent incidents.

## Psychiatric distress cited, but investigation continues
While Italian authorities have not ruled out other motives, the interior minister’s early assessment points toward the suspect’s mental state as a possible factor. Police are reviewing surveillance footage, witness statements, and the suspect’s background to build a full picture of what led to the attack. Prosecutors have charged El Khoudri with multiple counts of attempted murder and assault, with a hearing scheduled for Monday to determine if he will remain in custody.

## Broader implications for Italy’s security debate
The attack in Modena comes amid growing discussions in Italy about public safety, mental health care, and the country’s response to isolated acts of violence. Prime Minister Meloni has previously emphasized law-and-order policies, but this incident—like others before it—raises questions about how to balance prevention with individual rights. As the investigation continues, the focus remains on supporting the victims and ensuring justice for those injured in an attack that has left the entire country stunned.
