Israel resumed air strikes on southern Lebanon on Saturday, hours after the United States announced a 45-day extension of a fragile truce between Israel and Hezbollah. The ceasefire, which began April 17, had not halted Israeli military operations in Lebanese territory, despite initial hopes of de-escalation. The renewed strikes followed high-level talks in the U.S., where diplomats pushed for a longer-term halt to hostilities.

The extension comes as diplomats warn that the truce remains fragile. Lebanese officials reported multiple strikes targeting Hezbollah positions near the border, with civilian areas also affected. Israel has not commented publicly on the specific targets or casualties. The strikes mark the first major military action since the temporary ceasefire was first implemented weeks ago, raising concerns about renewed instability in the region.

## US mediation key to fragile truce extension
Washington played a central role in brokering the extended ceasefire, with Secretary of State [Antony Blinken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antony_Blinken) engaging directly with both sides. The U.S. State Department confirmed the 45-day extension in a statement, emphasizing the need for sustained dialogue. However, the failure of the initial April 17 truce to fully halt hostilities underscores the persistent challenges in achieving lasting peace. Analysts note that Hezbollah’s military capabilities and Israel’s security concerns remain deeply entrenched obstacles.

Southern Lebanon, particularly areas near the border, has borne the brunt of the conflict. Local reports indicate that infrastructure damage and displacement have continued despite the ceasefire. The renewed strikes have raised fears of a broader escalation, with both sides accusing the other of violating the terms of the truce. The international community has called for restraint, but the situation remains volatile as sporadic violence persists.

## Broader implications for regional stability
The extended truce, while a temporary reprieve, does little to address the underlying tensions fueling the conflict. Israel has reiterated its right to defend against Hezbollah’s attacks, while the militant group has framed its actions as resistance against Israeli occupation. The U.S. has urged both parties to use the extended ceasefire to engage in substantive negotiations, but skepticism remains high. Regional analysts warn that without a comprehensive peace plan, the cycle of violence is likely to continue.

The humanitarian toll of the conflict has been severe, with thousands displaced and critical infrastructure damaged. Aid organizations are warning of a worsening crisis if the violence resumes. The United Nations has called for urgent funding to support affected communities, but access remains restricted due to ongoing hostilities. The extended truce offers a brief window for relief, but the risk of further escalation looms large.

## 🇧🇷 Resumo em Português

Israel reabriu fogo contra o Líbano nesta semana, renovando os ataques aéreos no sul do país apenas horas depois de Israel e o Hezbollah terem aceitado estender uma trégua mediada pelos EUA por mais 45 dias, reacendendo temores de uma escalada regional. Os bombardeios, que deixaram ao menos quatro mortos e vários feridos, marcam um novo capítulo na tensão que já dura meses nas fronteiras entre os dois países, com repercussões diretas para o Brasil.

O acordo de cessar-fogo, inicialmente negociado para conter a escalada após meses de trocas de fogos, vinha sendo monitorado de perto pela comunidade internacional, especialmente por países com grandes diásporas libanesa e israelense, como o Brasil. A renovação por 45 dias era vista como um alívio temporário, mas a retomada dos ataques expõe a fragilidade do cessar-fogo e a dificuldade de se estabelecer uma paz duradoura na região. Para o Brasil, que tem laços históricos com o Líbano e abriga uma das maiores comunidades libaneses do mundo, a instabilidade representa riscos não só humanitários, mas também econômicos e diplomáticos, especialmente considerando a dependência de remessas de dinheiro de imigrantes e a influência do Hezbollah nas comunidades muçulmanas brasileiras.

A pergunta que fica agora é até quando a trégua será capaz de resistir diante da escalada de violência, e se novas negociações de paz serão capazes de garantir um cessar-fogo permanente.
