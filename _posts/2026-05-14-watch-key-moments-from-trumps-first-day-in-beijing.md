Chinese officials greeted U.S. President Donald Trump with a lavish welcome ceremony at Beijing’s [Great Hall of the People](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Hall_of_the_People) Thursday, marking the start of a two-day summit focused on stabilizing relations between the world’s two largest economies.

The meeting between Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping comes at a time of heightened tensions over trade imbalances, military posturing in the South China Sea and disputes involving North Korea and Iran. Both leaders have signaled willingness to address these issues during high-stakes negotiations that could reshape global economic and diplomatic dynamics.

## Trump’s itinerary includes state banquet and policy talks

Trump’s first day in Beijing included a private dinner with Xi, a traditional state banquet and a series of policy discussions on trade, security and regional stability. Chinese state media highlighted the event as a critical opportunity to reset relations that have deteriorated under Trump’s administration, particularly over tariffs and intellectual property disputes.

White House officials confirmed that Trump raised concerns about China’s trade practices, including forced technology transfers and market access restrictions for U.S. companies. Xi, meanwhile, emphasized the importance of mutual respect and cooperation, according to Chinese state broadcaster [CCTV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CCTV).

## Trade dispute looms over summit

The summit follows months of escalating trade tensions, with the U.S. imposing tariffs on $250 billion worth of Chinese goods and China retaliating with its own levies. Analysts say the meeting could pave the way for temporary truce or renewed negotiations, though significant concessions are unlikely in the short term.

Tensions over Taiwan also remain a flashpoint, with China warning against any U.S. moves that could imply recognition of the self-governing island as an independent nation. Trump’s administration has approved new arms sales to Taiwan, drawing sharp rebukes from Beijing. The conflict involving Iran adds another layer of complexity, as both the U.S. and China seek to protect their strategic interests in the Middle East.

## Broader implications for global stability

The outcome of the Trump-Xi summit could influence global trade flows, supply chains and geopolitical alliances. A breakthrough in negotiations might ease pressure on multinational corporations operating in both markets, while failure to reach common ground could further fragment international commerce.

Chinese state media framed the visit as a diplomatic victory for Xi, showcasing China’s growing influence on the world stage. Trump, meanwhile, framed the talks as an opportunity to correct imbalances and secure better terms for American workers and businesses.

Talks are expected to continue Friday, with both sides planning to announce a joint statement or outline of agreements by the end of the summit. Observers will scrutinize the language used in any final documents for signs of progress or lingering disputes.
