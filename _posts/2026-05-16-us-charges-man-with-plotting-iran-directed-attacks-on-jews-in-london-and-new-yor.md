Mohammad Baqer Saad Dawood al-Saadi, an Iraqi national, was arraigned in a New York federal court Monday on six charges including conspiracy to commit acts of terrorism and providing material support to a foreign terrorist organization. Prosecutors allege al-Saadi acted on behalf of Iran’s Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) to target Jewish community centers, synagogues, and charities in London and New York in a series of coordinated plots spanning nearly two decades.

Federal authorities say the charges stem from an FBI investigation that uncovered al-Saadi’s alleged involvement in nearly 20 terror plots and attempted attacks across the United States and Europe. The most recent targets included a London synagogue vandalized last month and a New York Jewish charity whose offices were surveilled by al-Saadi’s associates, according to court filings. Investigators allege he coordinated with IRGC operatives to gather intelligence and procure weapons for the attacks.

## FBI affidavit details alleged Iran-directed operations
The 45-page affidavit unsealed Monday outlines a pattern of activity dating back to 2006, when al-Saadi allegedly began receiving training and funding from the IRGC’s [Quds Force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quds_Force), a branch known for extraterritorial operations. Court documents describe coded communications between al-Saadi and IRGC handlers, including instructions to target Jewish institutions during religious holidays. The affidavit also references intercepted messages in which al-Saadi discussed recruiting operatives in Europe and the US.

US officials say al-Saadi entered the country illegally in 2015 and obtained a fraudulent green card using forged documents. His alleged activities intensified in 2022 when he began traveling frequently between London, New York, and Beirut, where IRGC operatives reportedly provided him with operational support. The FBI’s Joint Terrorism Task Force arrested al-Saadi in New York last week after monitoring his movements for several months.

## Jewish communities in London and New York on heightened alert
London’s Metropolitan Police confirmed they are coordinating with US authorities following the unsealing of the indictment. A spokesperson for [Community Security Trust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community_Security_Trust), a UK charity protecting Jewish sites, said synagogues and schools have increased security patrols in response to the alleged plots. In New York, the NYPD’s Counterterrorism Bureau has deployed additional officers to Jewish institutions as a precaution.

The case comes amid rising tensions between the US and Iran, with State Department officials calling the alleged plots part of a broader pattern of aggression. A senior US official stated that al-Saadi’s arrest demonstrates the ongoing threat posed by Iran-backed militant groups, including the IRGC, which has been designated a terrorist organization by the US since 2019. The official added that investigations into al-Saadi’s network are ongoing.

Al-Saadi, who faces up to life in prison if convicted, is being held without bail. His next court appearance is scheduled for this Friday. Prosecutors have not yet disclosed whether they will seek extradition for additional charges in the UK or other European countries where attacks were allegedly planned. The case raises questions about the IRGC’s ability to operate covertly in Western nations and the effectiveness of counterterrorism measures in preventing such plots.
