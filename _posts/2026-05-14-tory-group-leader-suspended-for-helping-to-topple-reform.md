The leader of a Conservative Party group on a local council was suspended Wednesday after his councillors helped block a proposed coalition with Reform UK. [Nigel Farage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigel_Farage), leader of Reform UK, said the move proved the Conservatives were ‘terrified’ of competition. The suspension follows a vote in which councillors rejected the alliance despite party guidance against it.

## Party warns members against coalitions with Reform UK
The Conservative Party confirmed it had made its stance clear to councillors ahead of the vote. A spokesperson said the party had communicated that any coalition with Reform UK would not be permitted. The warning came as local Conservative associations faced pressure to consider alliances with Reform UK in several areas. Party officials emphasized that such coalitions would not align with national strategy.

The suspended leader, whose name has not been released by the party, led a group of Conservative councillors who voted against the coalition. The decision to suspend him comes amid growing tensions within the party over how to respond to Reform UK’s rising support. Some local Conservatives have publicly questioned the party’s refusal to engage with Reform UK despite its electoral gains.

## Reform UK responds to suspension
Nigel Farage criticized the Conservative Party’s action, calling it a sign of weakness. ‘This shows the Conservatives are scared of facing us,’ Farage said in a statement. His party has gained ground in recent local elections, siphoning votes from the Conservatives in several regions. Farage has repeatedly urged Conservative voters to switch to Reform UK, framing it as the only viable alternative to Labour.

The suspension highlights divisions within the Conservative Party over strategy ahead of the next general election. Some members argue the party should adopt harderline policies to win back voters, while others insist on maintaining traditional Conservative principles. The party’s leadership has so far resisted any formal cooperation with Reform UK, despite pressure from its grassroots.

The Conservative Party has not disclosed the length of the suspension or whether the councillor will face further disciplinary action. The decision to suspend him was made at the national level, according to party officials. Local Conservative associations have been instructed to follow the party line strictly moving forward.

What happens next remains unclear. The suspended councillor may appeal the decision or seek reinstatement through party channels. Meanwhile, Reform UK continues to push for more cooperation with local Conservatives, framing the suspension as proof of the party’s inability to adapt. The broader implications for Conservative strategy ahead of the next election are still unfolding.
