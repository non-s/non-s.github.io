Elina Svitolina [dominated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elina_Svitolina) Coco Gauff [6-2, 6-3](https://www.skysports.com/tennis) Saturday to secure her 20th career WTA singles title and third Rome crown, capping a week where she also defeated world No. 2 Elena Rybakina [of Kazakhstan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elena_Rybakina) and world No. 3 Iga Świątek [of Poland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iga%C5%9Bwi%C4%85tek). The 31-year-old Ukrainian, returning to the tour as a mother after giving birth to daughter Skai in October 2022, now turns her focus to the French Open starting May 24 in Paris, where she aims to win her first Grand Slam singles title.

Svitolina, who had already beaten Gauff at the Australian Open and Dubai Tennis Championships earlier this year, wasted no time establishing control. She broke Gauff’s serve twice to open the match before the American rallied to win a tight second set on a tiebreaker. The decisive third set saw Svitolina save two set points at 4-5 and break to force a deciding tiebreaker, which she won 7-5. A crucial double fault from Gauff at 5-5 sealed Svitolina’s victory.

## Svitolina’s emotional journey through Rome

Svitolina described the fortnight in Rome as grueling, with matches often lasting late into the night. She said losing the second set was painful but emphasized mental resilience. “Coco is a great fighter,” Svitolina told Sky Sports Tennis. “I tried to stay mentally tough and refocus after each set. The late matches were exhausting, but I’m proud of how I fought through.”

The victory marks Svitolina’s first title since returning from maternity leave in 2023. Her road to the final included wins over top seeds, showcasing her experience and shot-making under pressure. Gauff, the reigning French Open champion, fought hard but struggled with uncharacteristic errors in key moments.

## What’s next for Svitolina and Gauff

Svitolina now prepares for the French Open, where she has historically performed well on clay. Her aggressive baseline game and net play could translate into success on the red dirt. Gauff, meanwhile, will regroup after a disappointing loss in her title defense. The 19-year-old American remains a top contender for Grand Slams but must address her consistency in finals.
