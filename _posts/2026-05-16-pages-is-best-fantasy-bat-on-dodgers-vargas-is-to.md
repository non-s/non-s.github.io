Eric Karabell of ESPN declared James Pages the superior fantasy baseball bat over Julio Vargas for the Los Angeles Dodgers in 2024. Pages has emerged as the team’s most consistent power threat, posting 12 home runs in 68 games this season compared to Vargas’ seven in 72 games. Vargas, however, remains the Dodgers’ primary leadoff hitter, offering speed with 10 stolen bases but fewer RBI opportunities due to lower run production. Fantasy managers drafting now must decide whether to prioritize Pages’ power potential or Vargas’ dual-threat capabilities.

The Dodgers’ lineup has seen minimal changes since spring training, but injuries to key players like Mookie Betts [Mookie Betts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mookie_Betts) and Freddie Freeman [Freddie Freeman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freddie_Freeman) have created roster gaps. Pages, a 26-year-old outfielder, has capitalized on increased playing time, batting .278 with a .512 slugging percentage in his last 30 games. Vargas, a 28-year-old utility infielder, has struggled with consistency, hitting just .245 over the same span. The disparity in recent production highlights the dilemma facing fantasy owners in roto leagues.

## Vargas’ Speed vs. Pages’ Power
Vargas’ value lies in his speed and positional flexibility, able to play second, third, and shortstop. Fantasy managers in categories leagues may still favor him for his stolen base upside. However, in head-to-head leagues where power categories like home runs and RBI carry more weight, Pages presents a stronger case. Vargas’ 10 steals rank him among the Dodgers’ top three, but his 28 RBI trail six other teammates, including Freeman and Will Smith [Will Smith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Will_Smith_(baseball)).

Pages’ recent surge aligns with the Dodgers’ strategy to rest aging veterans. The team’s bullpen has also thrived, posting a 2.98 ERA in June, the second-best in MLB. This stability has allowed Pages to retain his role in the lineup, even as Betts and Freeman return from injury. Fantasy managers should monitor how the Dodgers’ lineup shifts in the coming weeks, as a healthy Betts would likely push Pages to a lower spot in the order.

## Fantasy Draft Strategy Shifts
For early-season drafts, Pages’ current production makes him the safer pick in power-focused leagues. His .321 on-base percentage ranks higher than Vargas’ .305, giving him a slight edge in runs scored. However, Vargas’ ability to play multiple positions adds versatility, a factor that may sway managers in deeper leagues. The Dodgers’ upcoming homestand against the Rockies and Marlins could further clarify their offensive plans.

The broader implication is how these trends reflect the Dodgers’ roster construction. General manager Brandon Gomes [Brandon Gomes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brandon_Gomes) has emphasized youth development, but injuries have forced early-season adjustments. Pages and Vargas represent two paths: one built on power and consistency, the other on speed and adaptability. Fantasy managers must weigh these factors against their team’s needs and league settings.
