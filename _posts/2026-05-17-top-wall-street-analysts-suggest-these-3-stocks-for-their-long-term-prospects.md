After a bruising selloff in tech stocks earlier this year, earnings season brought a rare bright spot: analysts now see real momentum behind companies tied to AI infrastructure. The surge isn’t just hype. Orders for AI chips are up sharply, data centers are expanding, and cloud providers are locking in long-term contracts. Three stocks keep coming up in conversations with analysts at firms like [Morgan Stanley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morgan_Stanley), [Goldman Sachs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goldman_Sachs), and [J.P. Morgan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JPMorgan_Chase). They’re betting these companies will ride the wave of AI spending for years to come.

## Nvidia still dominates, but these 3 stocks stand out
Nvidia’s dominance in AI chips is no secret. But analysts say three other stocks offer compelling alternatives for investors who want exposure to AI’s growing infrastructure without banking everything on one company. The picks span chipmakers, data center specialists, and cloud infrastructure providers—each with unique strengths in the AI supply chain. The common thread? All three are seeing accelerating revenue growth and have raised their outlooks for the year ahead.

First up is [Advanced Micro Devices (AMD)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_Micro_Devices). The chipmaker’s AI revenue nearly tripled last quarter, driven by strong demand for its Instinct MI300 series accelerators. Analysts at [Wedbush](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wedbush_Securities) now call AMD’s AI business a ‘core growth engine’ and expect it to keep gaining share from Nvidia in data centers. The stock’s up 22% this year, but analysts say it’s still undervalued compared to its growth potential. ‘AMD’s making real inroads in AI,’ said one analyst. ‘The numbers don’t lie.’

Next is [Super Micro Computer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supermicro). This lesser-known player builds the servers and racks that house AI chips, and it’s riding a wave of demand from cloud providers and enterprises. Super Micro’s revenue jumped 120% in its last quarter, and the company now expects annual sales to grow 50% in 2024. Analysts at [Piper Sandler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piper_Sandler) call it ‘the unsung hero of AI infrastructure.’ The stock’s up 55% this year, but they argue it still has room to run as data center buildouts accelerate.

Then there’s [Arista Networks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arista_Networks), a networking gear maker that supplies the high-speed switches powering AI data centers. Arista’s revenue grew 46% last quarter, and it’s winning contracts with hyperscalers like [Amazon Web Services](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon_Web_Services). Analysts at [Barclays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barclays) say Arista’s market share in AI networking is expanding faster than expected. ‘They’re the backbone of these massive AI clusters,’ said one analyst. ‘Without them, the whole system slows down.’

## Why these stocks? It’s all about the infrastructure
The common thread here isn’t just AI—it’s the infrastructure that makes AI possible. AI models don’t run on magic. They need powerful chips, custom servers, and high-speed networks to function. That’s where these three stocks come in. AMD provides the brains, Super Micro builds the body, and Arista supplies the nervous system. Together, they cover the three critical layers of AI infrastructure.

Analysts point to a few key trends driving demand. First, hyperscalers like [Microsoft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft) and [Google](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google) are racing to expand their AI data centers. Second, enterprises are starting to deploy AI tools in-house, creating new demand for on-premise infrastructure. Third, the U.S. and Europe are pouring billions into semiconductor and data center investments to reduce reliance on Asian suppliers. ‘We’re in the early innings of a multi-year infrastructure buildout,’ said an analyst at [UBS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UBS). ‘These stocks are positioned to benefit regardless of short-term market noise.’

## Risks and what to watch
Of course, no investment comes without risks. AMD faces stiff competition from Nvidia and Intel in AI chips. Super Micro’s rapid growth has drawn scrutiny over its supply chain and customer concentration. Arista’s high-speed switches are critical, but the company’s margins are under pressure as competitors enter the market. Analysts say investors should watch a few things: quarterly earnings from these companies, updates on AI chip orders, and any shifts in hyperscaler spending patterns.

Another risk is valuation. All three stocks have run up significantly this year, and some analysts warn they’re not cheap. ‘You’re paying up for growth,’ said one analyst. ‘But if the AI infrastructure story holds, it could be worth it.’ They recommend dollar-cost averaging into these positions rather than betting the farm all at once.

For now, the trend is clear: AI isn’t just a buzzword anymore. It’s a real, growing market with real, growing infrastructure needs. These three stocks give investors a way to play that trend without overconcentrating in Nvidia. Whether the broader market keeps climbing or hits another rough patch, analysts say these companies are built to last. The question isn’t if AI will keep growing—it’s how fast, and who will capture the most value along the way.
