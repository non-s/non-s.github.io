New York Mets right-handed reliever Clay Holmes [Clay Holmes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clay_Holmes) suffered a fractured right fibula Monday night when he was struck by a 111.1 mph line drive off the bat of New York Yankees designated hitter Giancarlo Stanton [Giancarlo Stanton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giancarlo_Stanton) during the fourth inning of the Subway Series opener at Yankee Stadium. The pitch was ruled a hit-by-pitch, and Holmes immediately crumpled to the ground, clutching his lower right leg before being helped off the field.

## Yankees score early, Mets bullpen faces setback
Stanton’s line drive marked the third hardest-hit ball in MLB history, according to Statcast data, and the hardest ever recorded off a pitcher. Yankees starter Gerrit Cole [Gerrit Cole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gerrit_Cole) set the tone early, striking out the side in the first inning. By the fourth, the Yankees had already built a 3–0 lead on a two-run homer by Juan Soto [Juan Soto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juan_Soto) and an RBI groundout by Aaron Hicks. Holmes, who had been dominant in 2023 with a 2.54 ERA out of the bullpen, was brought in to stop the bleeding but instead became the latest casualty in a season already marred by injuries.

The Mets, already without several key relievers, now lose Holmes for an unknown period. Initial reports suggested the injury could sideline him for at least six weeks, pending imaging results. Manager Carlos Mendoza [Carlos Mendoza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carlos_Mendoza) confirmed after the game that Holmes would undergo further evaluation, including X-rays and an MRI, to determine the full extent of the damage.

## Subway Series loss compounds Mets’ bullpen woes
New York dropped the first game of the interleague series 6–2, extending a season-long struggle for consistency. The loss was particularly costly given how thin the Mets’ bullpen has been. Holmes had been one of the team’s most reliable arms, appearing in 47 games last year and posting a 2.54 ERA with 68 strikeouts in 56.2 innings. His absence adds pressure to a relief corps already dealing with injuries to Edwin Díaz—still recovering from a torn ACL—and closer Jeurys Familia, who has struggled with command.

## Stanton adds insult to injury with Yankees’ big inning
The Yankees capitalized on Cole’s strong start, building a 3–0 lead by the fourth. Stanton’s blast off Holmes extended the damage, though the Mets fought back with two runs in the top of the fifth on a sacrifice fly and a groundout. But New York’s late push fell short, with the Yankees adding three insurance runs in the sixth and seventh innings to seal the win.

The Mets now face a challenging week with back-to-back games against the Yankees and a tough road trip to Philadelphia. Mendoza said the team would address Holmes’ injury and bullpen depth after the series. “We’ll get the tests and go from there,” he told reporters. “For now, we’ve got to move on and focus on the next game.”

With Díaz still on the mend and Familia’s status uncertain, the Mets may need to rely on lesser-known relievers or recall minor league pitchers to fill the void. Holmes’ injury also raises questions about the team’s medical staff protocols and whether additional protective measures could be considered for pitchers in high-velocity situations.

The Mets and Yankees will resume their three-game series Tuesday night at Citi Field, with Cole and Kodai Senga [Kodai Senga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kodai_Senga) set to face off in a marquee pitching matchup. The series looms larger now that the Mets have lost a key bullpen arm to injury.
