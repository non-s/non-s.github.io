## Introduction to the Rescue Mission: A military rescue crew in [Florida](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florida) has spoken of the “pretty miraculous” survival of all 11 people it saved from a plane crash in the [Atlantic Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean), and its own scramble to safety with five minutes of fuel left. The rescue crew, part of the [920th Rescue Wing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/920th_Rescue_Wing), based at [Patrick Space Force Base](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patrick_Space_Force_Base), had to act quickly to reach the passengers and crew in choppy seas. ## Details of the Plane Crash: The passengers and crew were on a small [Beechcraft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beechcraft) twin-propeller aircraft that ditched into the water about 80 miles east of [Melbourne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melbourne,_Florida), on [Florida's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florida) east coast. The cause of the crash is still unknown, but the rescue crew's swift action ensured that all 11 people on board survived. ## Aftermath and Reaction: The successful rescue mission has been hailed as a miracle, with the crew's bravery and quick thinking being praised. The incident is a testament to the skill and dedication of the rescue crew, who risked their own lives to save others. The [US Air Force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Air_Force) has commended the crew for their actions, and an investigation into the cause of the crash is underway.
