Russian and Ukrainian negotiators finalized a 72-hour ceasefire deal that begins at midnight local time, according to statements from both governments. The truce covers intense fighting in eastern Ukraine's Donetsk and Luhansk regions, where artillery exchanges and drone strikes have killed at least 14 civilians in the past week alone. Turkish [Recep Tayyip Erdoğan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recep_Tayyip_Erdo%C4%9Fan) hosted the talks in Istanbul, with delegations from Russia, Ukraine, Turkey, and the UN attending. A joint statement called the talks "constructive but challenging," noting disagreements over prisoner exchanges and troop withdrawals remain unresolved.

## EU energy ministers take emergency action

Energy ministers from the 27 EU countries will convene an emergency meeting in Brussels on Monday to address soaring natural gas prices that have doubled since March. The spike comes after Russia cut pipeline flows through Ukraine by 40% last week, citing "technical issues"—a claim Kyiv calls a political move. Germany's economy minister warned that without intervention, industrial plants could start shutting down by September. France and Poland are pushing for a $60 price cap on Russian gas imports, but Hungary and Austria oppose it, fearing supply disruptions.

The gas crisis is already hitting households. In Spain, electricity bills rose 22% last month as utilities passed on higher fuel costs. Italian Prime Minister [Giorgia Meloni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giorgia_Meloni) announced a €2 billion subsidy to help families cope with summer AC costs. Meanwhile, Poland announced it will restart two coal mines to reduce reliance on Russian gas, despite EU climate goals.

## Queen Elizabeth II's 100th birthday celebrations kick off

Buckingham Palace confirmed that [Queen Elizabeth II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_II)'s 100th birthday will be marked with a four-day festival in London starting May 20. The events include a military parade featuring 2,000 soldiers, a flyover by the Red Arrows, and a concert at Windsor Castle. The queen, who turned 100 on April 21, will attend a private service at St. George’s Chapel on May 22. Public events include a street party in Windsor with 5,000 invited guests and a nationwide singalong of her favorite songs, including "God Save the King."

## UK economy shows mixed signals

The UK's Office for National Statistics reported that inflation hit 9.1% in April, the highest since 1982, driven by rising food and energy prices. But retail sales unexpectedly grew 0.3% in April, defying economist predictions of a 0.4% drop. Analysts blame the discrepancy on consumers cutting back on non-essentials while splurging on staples. The Bank of England is expected to raise interest rates again next month, which could further squeeze mortgage holders already facing 5% rates.

## France bans short-haul domestic flights

France's transport minister announced that flights between Paris and cities less than 2.5 hours by train will be banned starting September 1. The move aims to cut CO2 emissions by 2% annually. Airlines including [Air France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_France) will receive compensation, but environmental groups say the ban doesn't go far enough. Trains on the Paris-Lyon route now take 2 hours, while flights take 1 hour 10 minutes. The government estimates the ban will save 120,000 tons of CO2 per year.

The policy has divided opinion. Business travelers argue it will hurt productivity, while climate activists call it a "long-overdue" step. A recent poll shows 63% of French voters support the ban, but 42% say they’ll still take flights to save time.

## What’s next

The Russia-Ukraine ceasefire expires at midnight, and both sides say they’ll use the pause to regroup. EU energy ministers will vote on the gas price cap this week, but divisions among member states could stall the plan. Meanwhile, Queen Elizabeth II's birthday celebrations will dominate British headlines, with millions expected to line the streets for the military parade.
