Celtic [FC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celtic_F.C.) ended an eight-month title chase on Friday, defeating Hearts [Hearts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heart_of_Midlothian_F.C.) 2-0 at [Celtic Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celtic_Park) to secure the Scottish Premiership. The win, sealed with goals from [Kyogo Furuhashi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kyogo_Furuhashi) and [Anthony Ralston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthony_Ralston), marked the club's 54th league title and first in 12 months. The 32-game pursuit spanned 2,880 on-field minutes and 48 hours of relentless pressure from rival fans and media alike.

Brendan Rodgers' [team](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celtic_F.C.) dominated possession and created 18 shots, including nine on target, but Hearts defended resolutely before collapsing in the 72nd minute. Furuhashi broke the deadlock with a clinical finish after a counterattack, and Ralston doubled the lead eight minutes later. The visitors struggled to respond, mustering just four attempts all game. Celtic’s defensive frailties earlier in the season were nowhere to be seen as they held firm under intense scrutiny.

## Hearts' collapse ends title hopes
The defeat ends Hearts' slim title hopes, leaving them in second place as Rangers finished third. Celtic’s triumph comes a year after they last lifted silverware, ending a frustrating run that included early exits in domestic cups. Rodgers acknowledged the weight of expectation but emphasized the team’s resilience. "This group has shown incredible character," he said. "The last eight months have been tough, but the players never wavered."

Celtic’s victory ensures they retain the trophy they first won in 2012, a decade-long streak that underscores their dominance in Scottish football. The title race was the most competitive in years, with points separating the top three teams until the final stretch. Hearts, managed by [Steven Naismith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steven_Naismith), pushed Celtic harder than any rival this season but ultimately fell short.

## Next season’s challenges loom
With the league secured, Celtic now turns its focus to Europe and next season’s preparations. The club must address squad depth after injuries and fatigue played key roles in their title fight. Rodgers hinted at reinforcements, stating: "We’ll look to strengthen in key areas over the summer." Hearts, meanwhile, will aim to bridge the gap for a sustained challenge after their best campaign in recent years.

The Scottish Premiership’s unpredictable season ends with Celtic as champions, but the battle for European spots and relegation places will dominate the offseason. Both clubs now face the challenge of sustaining momentum while navigating financial constraints in a post-pandemic football landscape.
