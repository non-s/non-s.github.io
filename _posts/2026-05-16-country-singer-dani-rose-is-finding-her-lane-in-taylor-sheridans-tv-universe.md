Country songwriter Dani Rose is stepping into the spotlight in Taylor Sheridan’s [*Yellowstone* universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yellowstone_(TV_series)) with a new song she co-wrote for the *1923* spin-off. The track, featured on the show’s Dutton Ranch scenes, marks Rose’s first major placement in a high-profile TV series, tying her work directly to one of the most-watched dramas on cable. Industry insiders say the move could accelerate her transition from Nashville stages to broader entertainment audiences.


Rose, a rising name in country music circles, has spent years writing for artists and releasing her own material. Her recent Landman feature in Rolling Stone highlighted her growing influence, but the *1923* placement signals a new level of exposure. The song, titled *Ranch Hand Blues*, plays during a pivotal ranch scene in the show’s 1923-era setting, blending traditional country sounds with modern storytelling. Sheridan’s production team reportedly sought out Rose after hearing her independent releases.


The Nashville-based songwriter’s rise comes as Taylor Sheridan’s [*Yellowstone* franchise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yellowstone_(TV_series)) expands beyond its Montana ranch roots. *1923*, starring Harrison Ford and Helen Mirren, explores the family’s origins, and Rose’s inclusion reflects the show’s blend of history and drama. Music supervisors for the series have increasingly turned to emerging artists to score key moments, giving Rose’s work a platform that many in the industry watch closely.


Rose’s Landman feature last month described her as a ‘bridge between Nashville’s honky-tonks and Hollywood’s soundstages.’ Fellow songwriters and producers describe her as a rare talent who can write for both the stage and the screen. Her management confirmed the *1923* placement but declined to share details about future projects, only noting that Rose is ‘exploring opportunities’ in both music and TV.


Industry analysts say the crossover appeal could position Rose for a bigger role in film and TV soundtracks. Country music’s influence in entertainment has grown in recent years, with artists like Kacey Musgraves and Chris Stapleton earning spots on major projects. Rose’s connection to Sheridan’s universe could open doors for similar placements, particularly as streaming platforms seek fresh sounds for period dramas.


Rose, who grew up in Texas and moved to Nashville to pursue songwriting, has built a following through local shows and independent releases. Her self-released 2022 album *Dust and Diamonds* received praise from critics for its blend of traditional and contemporary country. The *1923* placement arrives as Rose prepares for her first headlining tour next spring, with dates announced for Dallas, Austin, and Nashville.

Em um movimento estratégico que une música e televisão, Rose não só teve sua canção incluída na trama do universo Dutton Ranch como também garantiu um papel de destaque como *landman* — um personagem-chave no drama rural da série — marcando sua estreia como atriz em um projeto de grande repercussão.

A participação de Dani Rose ganha ainda mais relevância quando se considera o impacto cultural e comercial de *Yellowstone*, que já ultrapassou fronteiras norte-americanas e conquistou audiência global, inclusive no Brasil. Para um público brasileiro que consome cada vez mais produções em língua inglesa dubladas ou legendadas, a presença de uma artista brasileira no elenco e na trilha sonora da série reforça a diversidade e a capacidade de artistas nacionais de brilhar no exterior. Além disso, o sucesso de *Yellowstone* — que já gerou spin-offs como *1883* e *1923* — abre portas para que mais talentos de países de língua portuguesa sejam descobertos em projetos internacionais de grande orçamento.

Se a carreira de Dani Rose seguir nesse ritmo, é apenas questão de tempo até que mais brasileiros sejam chamados para participar do universo de Taylor Sheridan, provando que o talento nacional não tem fronteiras.
