> 📰 **Continuing coverage:** [West Ham's relegation fears deepen with defeat at Newcastle](/sports/2026/05/17/west-hams-relegation-fears-deepen-with-defeat-at-newcastle/)

West Ham United's relegation worries grew on Sunday after they shipped a heavy 3-1 defeat to Newcastle United at St James' Park. William Osula, who'd been a substitute just twice all season, came off the bench to score twice and seal a statement win for Eddie Howe's side. The 22-year-old striker, born in Denmark to Nigerian parents, now has five goals in his last six appearances, including two in this game alone.

The match started brightly for West Ham, who dominated the early exchanges but couldn't convert their chances. Alisson's save from Jarrod Bowen in the 12th minute was West Ham's best moment before Newcastle struck first. Bruno Guimarães played a quick one-two with Joelinton before smashing home past Alphonse Areola in the 25th minute. It was a goal that set the tone for Newcastle's performance.

West Ham nearly equalized before halftime when Mohammed Kudus headed against the crossbar from a Bowen free-kick. But Newcastle doubled their lead just before the break when Anthony Gordon turned in a cross from Matt Targett in the 43rd minute. The Magpies looked completely in control, with 18 shots by halftime—twice as many as West Ham managed.

## Osula's introduction changes everything
The second half saw West Ham push forward desperately, but Newcastle's defense held firm. Nuno Espirito Santo brought on Michail Antonio and Danny Ings, but the Hammers couldn't find a way through a stubborn Newcastle backline. Then, in the 67th minute, Osula came on for Gordon and turned the game in an instant. His first goal arrived in the 71st minute when he pounced on a loose ball after Areola's save from Sean Longstaff and slotted home. His second, in the 82nd minute, was a composed finish after a quick counterattack involving Bruno Guimarães and Alexander Isak.

The win leaves West Ham in 14th place, just three points above the relegation zone with six games left. Their next match is against Fulham, who sit one place above them. For Newcastle, the victory moves them up to 8th place, though they're still 11 points off the top four. Eddie Howe's side have now won four of their last five games, with only a draw at Wolves spoiling an otherwise impressive run.

The result means West Ham must win their remaining fixtures to avoid dropping into the Championship next season. Their poor away form—just two wins on the road all season—has been a major issue. Meanwhile, Newcastle have rediscovered their form at St James' Park, losing just once in their last 15 home games. 

What happens next? West Ham face Fulham at Craven Cottage on Saturday, while Newcastle host Aston Villa on the same day. With the bottom three teams looking increasingly vulnerable, every point will count in the final weeks of the season.
