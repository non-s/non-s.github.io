Israel bombed a kitchen in Gaza on Wednesday, providing meals to displaced families, and killed three Palestinians while injuring others. The strike took place in an area where families have been sheltering after fleeing their homes. The [Palestinian Health Ministry in Gaza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Health_(State_of_Palestine)) reported that at least 871 people have been killed in Israeli strikes since a ceasefire was declared last October. The ministry did not specify the identities of those killed in the kitchen strike, but said they were civilians, including two women and a child. Local reports described the kitchen as one of the few still operating in northern Gaza, where most aid infrastructure has been destroyed over the past months of fighting. Witnesses said the attack happened midday while people were preparing or receiving food, turning a place meant for survival into a scene of death. The [Israel Defense Forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel_Defense_Forces) has not publicly commented on the strike, but Israel has repeatedly stated it targets only military sites and claims most civilian casualties result from Hamas using civilians as human shields. The ceasefire, brokered by regional mediators, was supposed to halt fighting and allow aid into Gaza. Instead, aid groups say the territory’s infrastructure is in ruins, with hospitals, schools, and food distribution points frequently hit. The kitchen was run by a local NGO that relied on donations to feed hundreds of displaced families daily. One volunteer, who survived the strike, said the kitchen had become a lifeline for people who had lost everything. ‘We had just started serving lunch,’ he told reporters. ‘There was no military presence here, no fighters. Just civilians trying to eat.’ The strike adds to growing international concern over the humanitarian crisis in Gaza, where most of the population has been displaced and faces severe shortages of food, water, and medical supplies. The [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations) has repeatedly called for protection of civilians and aid workers, but strikes on aid facilities have continued. The Palestinian Authority has condemned the attack, calling it a deliberate targeting of civilians. Israel has not responded to these accusations. The ceasefire’s future is uncertain, with sporadic clashes reported along the border and no clear progress toward a lasting peace deal. Aid groups warn that without a full halt to fighting and guaranteed safe access, the humanitarian situation will only worsen. The kitchen’s destruction leaves hundreds of families without their only source of hot meals, forcing them to rely on limited rations or scavenged food. The strike also highlights the broader collapse of Gaza’s food system, where less than 10% of the pre-war food infrastructure remains operational. For now, displaced families are left to improvise, cooking whatever they can over open fires or sharing dwindling supplies. The international community has pledged billions in aid, but getting it into Gaza remains a challenge due to destroyed roads, fuel shortages, and ongoing hostilities. The next few weeks will be critical in determining whether the ceasefire holds or if fighting resumes, further deepening the crisis.
