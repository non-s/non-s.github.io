Two Israeli settlers drove a vehicle directly into a herd of sheep belonging to a Palestinian shepherd in the northern occupied West Bank on Thursday, killing or severely injuring several animals. Security camera footage obtained by Palestinian activists shows the settlers' car speeding toward the livestock before making contact, leaving two sheep trapped beneath the wheels. The settlers then reversed and drove away while the animals struggled to free themselves. The incident occurred near the village of Burqa, northwest of Nablus, a Palestinian Authority-controlled area under partial Israeli military administration.

Local Palestinian farmers told Al Jazeera that the sheep belonged to a shepherd who was grazing his animals near the road when the attack happened. "We heard the sound of tires screeching and then saw the car hit the herd," said one resident who asked to remain anonymous. "The sheep were screaming, and the car just drove off without stopping." The Palestinian Health Ministry reported that at least four sheep died in the incident, while two others suffered serious injuries requiring veterinary care.

## Israeli settlers and the cycle of violence in the West Bank

This isn’t the first time Israeli settlers have targeted Palestinian livestock in the West Bank. Last month, settlers slashed the tires of 20 cars and set fire to farmland in the same area, destroying hundreds of olive trees. The Israeli human rights group [B'Tselem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/B%27Tselem) has documented dozens of similar incidents in recent years, where settlers use vehicles, arson, or physical violence against Palestinian farmers and their property. According to the United Nations, more than 900 such attacks occurred in 2023 alone, up from around 700 in 2022.

The Israeli military [Israel Defense Forces (IDF)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel_Defense_Forces) rarely intervenes in these cases, and when it does, prosecutions are rare. A 2022 report by [Human Rights Watch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Rights_Watch) found that Israeli authorities closed 93% of investigations into settler violence without filing charges. Settlers often claim they mistook livestock for a security threat, but activists say these attacks are deliberate attempts to intimidate Palestinians and force them off their land.

## Palestinian shepherds face constant harassment

For Palestinian shepherds in the West Bank, these attacks are part of daily life. Many rely on livestock as their primary source of income, and losing animals can mean financial ruin. "We used to graze here every day without fear," said 58-year-old shepherd Abu Ahmed, whose flock was targeted in Thursday’s incident. "Now, we have to move our sheep to higher ground because settlers drive up and down these roads just to terrorize us." Israeli settlers have expanded into more areas of the West Bank since the October 7 attacks in southern Israel, with the government approving thousands of new housing units for settlers in recent months.

The Israeli government has repeatedly stated it opposes violence by settlers, but critics say its policies enable it. Earlier this year, Israel’s far-right Finance Minister [Bezalel Smotrich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bezalel_Smotrich) called for the annexation of the West Bank, calling the territory "an inseparable part of the land of Israel." The United States and European Union have condemned the expansion of settlements, which are considered illegal under international law. In response to Thursday’s attack, the Palestinian Authority called for international intervention, stating that Israel’s failure to protect Palestinian civilians in the West Bank emboldens settlers to escalate their violence.

## What happens next?

Palestinian activists have filed a complaint with the Israeli military, but similar complaints in the past have led to little action. The shepherd whose animals were killed has also contacted local human rights organizations, which are preparing to file another report. Meanwhile, settler groups have denied responsibility, with one local leader calling the incident an "accident" and blaming poor road conditions. The Israeli police have said they’re investigating, but no arrests have been made. For the shepherds of Burqa, the attack is just the latest reminder that their way of life in the West Bank is under constant threat.
