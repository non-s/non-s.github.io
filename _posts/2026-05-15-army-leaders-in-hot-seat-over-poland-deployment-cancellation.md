Army leadership faced sharp questioning Friday as lawmakers demanded answers over the Pentagon’s sudden decision to scrap a major deployment to Eastern Europe. Acting Army Chief of Staff [Gen. Christopher LaNeve](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christopher_G._LaNeve) told a budget hearing that Defense Secretary [Pete Hegseth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pete_Hegseth) personally ordered the halt to the 9-month rotation of the 2nd Armored Brigade Combat Team, 1st Cavalry Division to Poland. The unit had already begun preparations, including casing its colors on May 1, dispatching an advance team and shipping equipment overseas, only to be pulled back at the last minute.

Army Secretary Dan Driscoll confirmed he and LaNeve were consulted but refused to disclose when Hegseth made the decision. Lawmakers pressed for specifics on the rationale behind the cancellation, questioning whether it reflected shifting priorities in European security or logistical missteps. The abrupt change left soldiers and families in limbo, with some personnel already discussing the reversal publicly amid confusion over next steps.

## Pentagon cites shifting global priorities
The Defense Department has not issued a formal statement explaining the cancellation, but sources familiar with the matter suggest the decision may align with a broader review of U.S. troop deployments worldwide. The 2nd Armored Brigade Combat Team was set to replace another unit in Poland as part of a routine rotation to reassure NATO allies amid Russia’s ongoing war in Ukraine. The cancellation raises questions about whether the Pentagon is reallocating forces to other global hotspots or reassessing its European strategy.

Gen. LaNeve acknowledged the Army’s frustration but emphasized that soldiers remain ready to deploy if ordered, even as the abrupt change disrupts training schedules and family plans. The brigade’s equipment is already en route to Europe, complicating logistics if the decision is reversed. Lawmakers on Capitol Hill criticized the lack of transparency, demanding written explanations from the Pentagon by next week.

## Congress demands answers on deployment reversal
Republican and Democratic lawmakers alike pressed for clarity during Friday’s hearing, with some suggesting the cancellation could undermine U.S. commitments to NATO. The 2nd Armored Brigade Combat Team is a critical component of the Army’s armored forces, trained for rapid deployment in high-intensity conflicts. Its sudden withdrawal from Poland’s deployment pipeline leaves a gap in U.S. military presence in Eastern Europe at a time of heightened tensions.

The Pentagon’s decision also conflicts with recent statements from U.S. officials reaffirming support for NATO allies. Poland has been a key staging ground for U.S. and allied forces since Russia’s 2022 invasion of Ukraine, hosting rotational brigades and advanced military infrastructure. The cancellation could signal a broader shift in how the Pentagon manages its global force posture, though defense analysts warn such abrupt changes risk eroding allied confidence.

Moving forward, the Army must finalize whether the 4,000 soldiers will be reassigned to other duties or remain on standby. The uncertainty adds to growing concerns among military families and lawmakers about the Pentagon’s decision-making process. If the deployment is permanently canceled, the Army will need to explain how it plans to maintain deterrence in Eastern Europe without this critical unit.
