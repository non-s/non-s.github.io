## Introduction to the Crisis: Cuba has been experiencing severe power outages, with large parts of the eastern region plunged into darkness in the latest round of nationwide electricity shutdowns. The country's communist government has stated that fuel reserves have run out, leading to rare protests in neighborhoods around the capital, Havana. Energy Minister Vicente de la O Levy announced that Cuba has 'absolutely no fuel, oil, and absolutely no diesel', with the only available options being gas from domestic wells and domestic crude oil, whose production is increasing but not enough to meet the demand. ## Causes and Effects: The situation is very tense, with the impact of the US blockade causing significant harm to the country's economy and energy sector. Cuba is struggling to secure fuel imports, and the electrical system has become increasingly fragile, relying on limited domestic fuel production and solar energy to keep parts of the grid operating. This has resulted in widespread disruptions to daily life, including the functioning of hospitals, schools, and other essential services. The government has been trying to find alternative solutions, but so far, the crisis persists, and the people of Cuba are bearing the brunt of the shortage. ## Protests and Government Response: Protests have erupted in western areas near Havana, with demonstrators demanding an end to the blackouts and a solution to the energy crisis. The government has acknowledged the difficulties faced by the people and has promised to find a solution, but so far, no concrete measures have been announced. The Cuban President, Miguel Diaz-Canel, has been under pressure to address the crisis, and the international community is watching the situation closely, with some countries offering support and assistance to help Cuba overcome the crisis.
