Five years after the fourth and final season of the hit French series [Call My Agent!](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Call_My_Agent!) won an International Emmy Award, its beloved cast is reuniting for a feature film. The movie follows agent-turned-filmmaker Andréa Martel (Camille Cottin) as she heads to the south of France and gets tangled in a movie shoot spiraling out of control. Production started this summer in Cannes, where the team filmed scenes with a surprise guest star: George Clooney. ## The cast returns for a movie twist on the original series The original series, known in French as *Dix pour cent*, became a global hit for its sharp look at Paris’s high-pressure talent agency world. Its fourth season wrapped in 2018, ending with the agency’s future uncertain. Now, Andrea Martel takes center stage again, but this time she’s stepping behind the camera. The movie explores what happens when she’s pulled into a production nightmare on location. ## George Clooney’s cameo adds star power to the Cannes shoot Production began in late June along the French Riviera, with key scenes shot in and around Cannes during the film festival. Clooney, who’s filming *The Ticket* in France this year, dropped by for a cameo, according to sources close to the project. His brief role wasn’t part of the original plan, but his presence added buzz to the set. The cameo fits the show’s tradition of mixing real stars with its fictional universe. ## Andrea’s comeback mirrors the show’s own reinvention Andrea Martel’s return isn’t just a nostalgia play. The movie reimagines her as a director juggling egos, budgets, and on-set disasters—mirroring the chaos she once managed as an agent. The script leans into the same mix of sharp dialogue, industry satire, and heart that made the series a cult favorite. ## What’s next for the film and the team The movie is still in early production, with more scenes planned for Paris and the south of France. The original cast—including [Lydia Bosch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lydia_Bosch), [Nicolas Maury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicolas_Maury), and [Liliane Rovère](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liliane_Rov%C3%A8re)—is expected to reprise their roles. Netflix hasn’t announced a release date, but filming should wrap by early fall. The film could drop in late 2024 or early 2025, giving fans another hit from France’s sharpest workplace comedy.
