Aaron Rai made history Sunday, becoming the first Englishman to win the US PGA in 107 years. His final-round 6-under 65 at [Aronimink Golf Club](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aronimink_Golf_Club) in Pennsylvania sealed a two-stroke victory over defending champion Collin Morikawa. The win marks Rai’s first major title after nine years on the PGA Tour and countless near-misses in big events. It also ends England’s long drought in the championship, last won by [Ted Ray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ted_Ray_(golfer)) in 1920 when the event was still called the Professional Golfers’ Association of America Championship.

Rai started the day two behind Morikawa but surged with birdies on the first three holes. He never looked back, finishing with eight birdies and no bogeys. His 65 was the lowest final-round score by a champion since Jason Dufner’s 63 in 2013. The win catapults Rai to 24th in the world rankings and secures his spot in next year’s Masters and Open Championship.

## England’s long wait ends

The last English winner before Rai was [Ted Ray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ted_Ray_(golfer)), who claimed the title in 1920 at the age of 50. Since then, England has produced major winners but none in the US PGA. Rai joins a short list of English golfers to win a men’s major, including [Nick Faldo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_Faldo) and [Tony Jacklin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tony_Jacklin). His victory also ends a 24-year streak without an English winner in the PGA, the longest among major championships.

Rai’s path to glory wasn’t straightforward. He missed the cut in three of his last five majors before this week. But he arrived at Aronimink in form, finishing tied for 12th at the PGA Championship last year. His caddy for the week, [Paul Foulis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Foulis), played a key role, helping Rai dial in his irons on the tight, tree-lined course.

## Morikawa falls short in defense

Collin Morikawa, the world No. 7, entered the final round with a one-stroke lead but couldn’t hold off Rai’s charge. Morikawa’s final-round 71 included a costly bogey on the 18th hole, sealing his fate. It’s the second straight year Morikawa finished runner-up in a major, following his loss at the Masters in April. The loss drops him to No. 8 in the world rankings.

Rai’s win comes just months after he tied for fifth at the Players Championship, his best finish on the PGA Tour. His next stop is the FedEx Cup playoffs, where he’ll aim to build on this momentum. The victory also qualifies him for the 2025 Ryder Cup, adding to England’s already strong team.

## What’s next for Rai and the PGA?

Rai’s win means he’ll wear the PGA Championship’s iconic Wanamaker Trophy for a year and receive a five-year exemption on the PGA Tour. More importantly, it’s a career-defining moment that could open doors to sponsorships and bigger opportunities. For the PGA, the win helps boost England’s presence in the event after years of dominance from American and European players.

The 2025 US PGA will be held at [Quail Hollow Club](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quail_Hollow_Club) in North Carolina, a course known for its challenging final holes. If Rai defends his title, he’ll join an exclusive club of repeat winners like [Walter Hagen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Hagen) and [Jack Nicklaus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_Nicklaus). Until then, he’ll enjoy the moment and prepare for the Ryder Cup in September.
