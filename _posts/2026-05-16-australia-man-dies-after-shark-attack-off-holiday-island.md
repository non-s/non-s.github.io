A 38-year-old man died Saturday after a shark attacked him in waters off Rottnest Island, a popular holiday destination near [Perth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perth), [Western Australia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Australia). This is the second fatal shark attack in Australia this year, following a rise in incidents that scientists attribute to shifting migratory patterns and warming ocean temperatures.

## Shark attack occurred off Horseshoe Reef

The incident happened around 10:00 a.m. local time (0200 GMT) at Horseshoe Reef, a known surf and swimming area on the island’s northwest coast. Paramedics from [St John Ambulance Service](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St_John_Ambulance_Australia) treated the victim on the shore but were unable to revive him after the attack. [Surf Life Saving WA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surf_Life_Saving_Western_Australia), the state’s beach safety organization, reported a 5-meter great white shark was spotted 80 meters offshore near the time of the attack.

## Rising shark attacks raise safety concerns

The death marks the second fatal shark bite in Australia in 2026, following a January attack in [Sydney Harbour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sydney_Harbour) that killed a 12-year-old boy. That incident was one of four shark attacks in two days, prompting authorities to temporarily close multiple Sydney beaches. Scientists suggest the increase in encounters may be linked to changes in shark migratory patterns due to warming ocean temperatures and more crowded coastal waters.

Western Australia has implemented various shark mitigation measures over the past decade, including drone surveillance, shark-spotting networks, and targeted culls in certain zones. Despite these efforts, fatal attacks continue to occur, raising public debate over beach safety and ecological balance.

## Family and officials respond to tragedy

Authorities have notified the victim’s family and are investigating the incident with support from marine experts. Local officials urged beachgoers to follow posted warnings and avoid swimming in areas flagged for shark activity. The [Department of Primary Industries and Regional Development](https://www.dpi.wa.gov.au/) is reviewing data on recent shark sightings and will update safety recommendations as needed.

Rottnest Island, known for its turquoise waters and quokkas, attracts over 500,000 visitors annually. While shark attacks remain rare, this incident underscores ongoing risks in coastal recreation areas. Beach safety agencies continue to emphasize vigilance and adherence to local advisories.

What happens next? Authorities plan to review the attack’s circumstances and reassess current shark monitoring and response protocols. Further safety updates may be issued if patterns in shark behavior change.
