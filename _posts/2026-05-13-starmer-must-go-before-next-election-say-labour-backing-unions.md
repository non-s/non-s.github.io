Labour leader Sir Keir Starmer [Keir Starmer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keir_Starmer) is under mounting pressure to resign after several Labour-backing unions demanded he step down before the next general election. The calls from unions, including Unite the Union and the GMB, highlight growing dissatisfaction within the party over Starmer’s leadership and direction. The pressure comes as Labour MPs and cabinet members reportedly express concerns over internal splits and declining poll numbers.

Starmer has insisted he will press ahead with governing while addressing criticism internally. In response to the union calls, a Labour spokesperson said Starmer remains focused on delivering for working people and winning the next election. However, the calls for his resignation underscore deepening cracks within the party as it prepares for a potential electoral challenge. Recent polling has shown Labour’s lead over the Conservatives narrowing, adding to the urgency of internal debates.

## Labour’s internal divisions widen amid union pressure

The demand for Starmer’s resignation follows months of internal debate over policy direction and party strategy. Unite the Union, one of Labour’s largest backers, publicly urged Starmer to step aside, citing concerns over his handling of key issues such as workers’ rights and economic policy. The GMB union echoed similar sentiments, calling for fresh leadership to revive the party’s fortunes ahead of the next vote. These calls reflect broader unease among Labour’s traditional voter base, which has grown frustrated with the party’s shift toward centrist policies.

Starmer’s cabinet has also seen tensions, with some senior members privately expressing doubts about his leadership. While no public resignations have occurred, reports suggest discontent is simmering behind closed doors. The pressure on Starmer comes at a critical time as the Conservatives, led by Prime Minister Rishi Sunak [Rishi Sunak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rishi_Sunak), seek to regain ground in the polls. Labour’s internal strife risks overshadowing its efforts to present a united front against the government.

## Starmer defiant amid calls for change

Despite the union pressure, Starmer has remained defiant, insisting his focus remains on delivering Labour’s policy agenda. In a recent interview, he reaffirmed his commitment to leading the party into the next election, arguing that stability is needed to challenge the Conservatives effectively. However, the growing chorus of dissent suggests that his leadership is increasingly under scrutiny. Shadow ministers have been instructed to avoid public comments on the leadership debate, but behind-the-scenes discussions are likely intensifying.

Labour’s NEC (National Executive Committee) is expected to discuss the union calls at its next meeting, though no formal vote on Starmer’s leadership is currently scheduled. The party’s grassroots members have also weighed in, with some local branches openly criticising Starmer’s approach. The situation remains fluid, with the potential to escalate if more unions or MPs join the calls for change. For now, Starmer appears determined to weather the storm, but the pressure is unlikely to subside without significant concessions or shifts in policy.

Labour’s future now hinges on whether Starmer can regain the confidence of his party and its traditional supporters. The next few months will be critical as the party prepares for local elections in May, which could serve as a barometer for its electoral prospects. If Labour’s polling numbers continue to decline, the calls for Starmer’s resignation may grow louder, forcing the party to confront its internal divisions head-on. Until then, Starmer’s leadership remains under the microscope, with the next election looming large over the debate.
