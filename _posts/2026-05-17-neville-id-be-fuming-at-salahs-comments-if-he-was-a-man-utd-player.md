Liverpool striker Mohamed Salah didn’t hold back on Saturday, directly attacking manager [Arne Slot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arne_Slot) and the club’s current style of play after a damaging 4-2 loss to Aston Villa. The defeat dropped the Reds to fifth in the Premier League and left their Champions League spot for next season hanging by a thread. Salah, who is leaving Liverpool this summer, called for a return to the high-tempo, aggressive attacking football that defined Jürgen Klopp’s era at the club. It’s the latest in a string of public criticisms from Salah about Slot’s management this season, including a December interview where he admitted his relationship with the Dutch coach had broken down.

Gary Neville, the former Manchester United captain and Sky Sports pundit, said he wasn’t surprised by Salah’s comments but called them unhelpful. "He’s pulled the pin out of a grenade right in the middle of the room. And he’s walking out of the room," Neville said. "Mo’s not happy over there. It’s not great. If he was a Manchester United player, I’d be fuming. But what you can never do with this kind of player, with this kind of stature and personality, is keep them quiet. If they’ve got something to say, they’re going to say it. And they’re going to say it at the point where you don’t want to hear it."

## Salah’s frustration runs deep

Salah’s latest outburst wasn’t just about the loss to Aston Villa. He’s been openly critical of Slot’s tactics and approach since December, when he told a German outlet that their relationship had collapsed. The timing of his comments, with Liverpool’s season hanging in the balance, makes them especially damaging. Neville believes Slot won’t welcome the criticism, but the Dutchman’s focus now is simply getting through the rest of the season. "Arne Slot will not welcome it whatsoever," Neville said. "But he will just want to get to the end of the season, get out of there, get everyone out of there that’s not going to be there next season, and try and build."

The frustration among Liverpool’s squad appears to run deeper than just Salah. Reports suggest other players have privately questioned Slot’s methods, though none have spoken out publicly. The club’s attacking style under Slot has lacked the intensity and creativity that defined Klopp’s teams, and fans have grown restless as results have stagnated. With key players like Salah and likely others departing this summer, the club faces a major rebuild.

## Slot’s summer rebuild hangs in the balance

Slot has made it clear he wants a busy summer transfer window to reshape the squad. The Dutchman’s future at Liverpool isn’t guaranteed, but he’s pushing for the chance to shape the next phase of the club’s project. Neville believes Slot’s real opportunity to turn things around will come next season, once the summer exodus of disgruntled players is complete. "Slot will get the chance to turn results around next season," Neville said, hinting that the club’s leadership is already planning for a major overhaul.

The question now is whether Slot can survive the final stretch of this season. The loss to Aston Villa exposed Liverpool’s defensive frailties and lack of cutting edge in attack. With three of their next five matches against top-six sides, the pressure on Slot to deliver results is immense. If Liverpool miss out on the Champions League for the second time in three years, the calls for his dismissal could grow louder.

Liverpool’s next match is at home to Brighton on Wednesday, a must-win game if they’re to keep their top-four hopes alive. The result will tell us whether Slot can steady the ship in these final weeks or if the club’s leadership will start looking for a change in direction. Salah’s comments have only added to the noise surrounding the club’s future, but the real test will be on the pitch.
