Cloud customers using AI tools on Google Cloud and AWS are getting sticker shock from bills that run into the tens of thousands of dollars—and they didn’t see it coming. This week, The Register published two separate reports detailing how organizations were billed unexpectedly for AI services they thought were covered under their regular plans or included in free tiers. In one case, a startup saw a $12,000 charge from Google Cloud after running a batch of text analysis jobs. The bill arrived with no prior notice or warning. The company scrambled to contact support, but Google’s response didn’t come fast enough to stop the charge from hitting their credit card.

Over at AWS, a small business owner told The Register they were billed $8,500 for using an AI image recognition service they thought was free under a promotional offer. The service had been running in the background for weeks, processing thousands of images from their website. When they checked their dashboard, they found the charges had stacked up day by day, with no email alerts or pop-up warnings. Both companies have since updated their billing dashboards to show clearer usage breakdowns, but the damage was already done for these users.

## Why the bills are so high and why customers are caught off guard
The surprise charges stem from how AI services are priced on these platforms. Unlike regular cloud storage or compute, AI services often charge per API call, per token processed, or per image analyzed. A single batch job can trigger thousands of micro-transactions that add up fast. Many customers set up automated AI tools without realizing they’d hit usage limits or trigger billed tiers. Even worse, some AI services are enabled by default in new projects, quietly running in the background until the bill arrives.

The problem is worse for teams that don’t monitor their cloud spend closely. One developer told The Register they only noticed the charges when their finance team flagged the sudden $10,000 spike. By then, reversing the charges proved difficult. Google Cloud initially offered a partial refund but kept most of the charge, citing their terms of service. AWS eventually waived the bill after the customer escalated the issue publicly on social media, but not before the incident caused real financial strain.

## What’s being done—and what you can do now
Google and AWS have both updated their billing pages to show real-time AI usage and cost estimates. Google added a new "AI Services" section in its billing dashboard that breaks down costs by API, while AWS introduced a "Cost Explorer" tool that flags unusual AI spending spikes. But users say the changes came too late for those already burned by surprise bills.

If you’re using AI tools on either platform, experts recommend checking your billing dashboard daily for the first week after enabling a new AI service. Set up custom alerts for any AI-related charges over $50. Turn off any AI services you’re not actively using, even if they seem free. And read the fine print in the AI service’s pricing page—some include hidden fees for data storage or model training.

The bigger picture here is that AI services are moving faster than the billing systems can keep up. Platforms are rolling out new AI tools every month, and customers are adopting them without fully understanding the cost structure. Until the billing systems catch up, expect more surprise charges. The lesson? Don’t trust the defaults. Turn off what you don’t need, set spending alerts, and check your bill like it’s your credit card statement.
