Oscar winner Cate Blanchett [](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cate_Blanchett) dropped a bombshell at the Cannes Film Festival on Sunday when she revealed she’s already working on a new film with director Brady Corbet. The Australian actress, known for her roles in *Tár* and *Blue Jasmine*, let slip during a masterclass that she’s set to star in Corbet’s next project alongside Selena Gomez and Michael Fassbender. The trio’s collaboration was confirmed by *Variety*, which reported the film is an epic described internally as “X-Rated,” though details beyond that remain scarce.

Corbet, who previously directed *The Brutalist* and *The Childhood of a Leader*, has built a reputation for making bold, visually striking films that often explore dark themes. His 2018 film *Vox Lux*, starring Natalie Portman, tackled fame, violence, and pop culture in a way that divided critics but left a strong impression. Fans of Corbet’s work will likely expect something equally ambitious—and potentially controversial—given the “X-Rated” label, which usually signals explicit content or mature themes. The film’s title hasn’t been announced, and no release date has been set.

Blanchett’s announcement came as a surprise even to some in the industry. The actress, who’s currently promoting her latest film *The New Boy* at Cannes, casually mentioned the project during a Q&A session. “I’m working with Brady Corbet on something,” she said, drawing gasps and murmurs from the audience before she added, “It’s going to be fun.” The news quickly spread through film circles, with *Variety* confirming the casting shortly after. Gomez, who’s balancing her acting career with her music and beauty brand Rare Beauty, hasn’t commented publicly on the project yet.

Michael Fassbender [](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Fassbender), no stranger to intense roles, is the third confirmed lead. The British actor, known for his work in *Inglourious Basterds*, *12 Years a Slave*, and the *X-Men* franchise, has a knack for disappearing into complex characters. His involvement suggests the film will lean into heavy themes, possibly blending drama with elements of thriller or even horror. Fassbender’s last major role was in *The Killer*, a 2023 film directed by David Fincher, which also explored dark psychological territory.

The “X-Rated” label is intriguing because it’s rarely used in film marketing. In Hollywood, “X-rated” typically refers to films that were either rated NC-17 by the MPAA or contained explicit content that kept them out of mainstream theaters. Quentin Tarantino’s *The Hateful Eight* famously used 70mm roadshow screenings to avoid the NC-17 stamp, while *Showgirls* became infamous for its NC-17 rating. If Corbet’s film truly embraces the label, it could signal a return to the provocative, boundary-pushing cinema that defined the 1970s and ‘80s.

Production details are still under wraps. Blanchett’s comment suggests filming is already underway or about to begin, but no set visits, trailers, or official behind-the-scenes footage has surfaced. The Cannes announcement is likely the first of many drops—studios often tease major casting news during the festival to generate buzz. Given the pedigree of the cast and director, this project could become one of the most anticipated films of the next year or two, especially if it lives up to the “X-Rated” hype.

What happens next? Corbet’s team will likely keep the project’s details quiet while production continues. If the film follows Corbet’s usual style, we can expect a slow-burn, visually rich story with sharp dialogue and a cast that digs deep into their characters. Fans of Blanchett’s dramatic range and Fassbender’s intensity should mark their calendars—this one’s going to be intense.
