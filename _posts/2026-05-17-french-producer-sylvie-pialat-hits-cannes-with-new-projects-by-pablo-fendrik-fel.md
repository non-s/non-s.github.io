Cannes 2024 kicks off with one of the most eclectic lineups on the Croisette, all backed by veteran producer [Sylvie Pialat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sylvie_Pialat). The Cesar-winning French producer is rolling out three projects this year, each pushing different boundaries. The most anticipated is a geopolitical drama about Augusto Pinochet, featuring [Sebastian Stan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sebastian_Stan) and [Ana de Armas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ana_de_Armas). The film dives into Chile’s darkest era, with Stan playing a CIA operative tangled in the regime’s machinations and de Armas as a conflicted local journalist trying to expose the truth.


## Alpine revenge thriller joins Pinochet drama on Pialat’s slate

Pialat isn’t stopping with history. She’s also backing an alpine revenge thriller from Argentine director [Pablo Fendrik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pablo_Fendrik). The film follows a mountain guide who returns from war to find his family slaughtered, tracking down the killers in the snowy wilderness. Fendrik, known for gritty crime films like *The Aura*, shifts to high-stakes survival drama with this project. Cannes insiders say it’s already drawing buzz for its brutal tension and stunning visuals.


## France’s first animated feature in competition lands with Pialat

Breaking new ground, Pialat is also producing *The Island of the Lost*, the first animated feature from French director [Céline Devaux](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%C3%A9line_Devaux). The film reimagines a post-apocalyptic world through the eyes of a child searching for a mythical island. Devaux’s short *Make It Soul* won a Cesar in 2021, and this feature marks her boldest step yet. Pialat calls it “a gamble on fresh storytelling,” betting big on an untested format in Cannes’ main competition.


Pialat’s approach this year reflects her reputation for taking risks. While other producers chase safe bets, she’s mixing political drama, survival thriller, and experimental animation. The Pinochet film alone could spark Oscar conversations, given Stan and de Armas’ star power. But the alpine thriller and Devaux’s animation add layers—one brutal, one poetic—that make her slate impossible to ignore.


## Cannes 2024’s producer spotlight shines on Pialat’s eclectic taste

Pialat’s career spans decades, with credits like *Polisse* and *The Class*. She’s known for nurturing bold voices, and this year’s lineup proves it. The Pinochet project, co-produced with [Wild Bunch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wild_Bunch_(company)), has already secured U.S. distribution talks. The alpine thriller is in final negotiations for international sales, while Devaux’s animation is generating early festival interest.


What happens next? The films premiere in the next two weeks, and Pialat’s gamble will either pay off with awards or leave critics scratching their heads. Either way, Cannes 2024 just got a lot more interesting.

## 🇧🇷 Resumo em Português

Após uma década dominando o cinema europeu com produções impactantes, a lendária produtora francesa Sylvie Pialat desembarcou em Cannes com um pacote de projetos que promete agitar não só o festival, mas também o imaginário do cinema latino-americano. Em um movimento estratégico para expandir sua influência global, Pialat fechou acordos para três obras ambiciosas: um drama sobre a ditadura de Pinochet estrelado por Sebastian Stan e Ana de Armas, um thriller de vingança nos Alpes e uma co-produção chinesa dirigida por Hu Wei, sinalizando um interesse crescente em narrativas transnacionais.

Com a escolha de projetos que transitam entre gêneros e continentes, Pialat demonstra uma aposta em histórias capazes de dialogar com audiências diversas, um movimento que pode render frutos significativos para o cinema brasileiro. A presença de Felipe Gálvez, diretor chileno radicado na Argentina, em sua lista reforça a tendência de colaborações latino-americanas em produções de alto calibre, abrindo portas para financiamentos e coproduções internacionais — algo cada vez mais necessário em um setor audiovisual cada vez mais competitivo. Para o Brasil, que recentemente ampliou seus incentivos à coprodução internacional, a movimentação reforça a importância de estar atento a esses fluxos.

A próxima etapa deve ser a definição de parcerias locais para viabilizar as filmagens no país, caso algum dos projetos inclua locações ou equipes brasileiras.
