Jonathan Gjoshe, a 28-year-old former footballer, has spoken for the first time about the knife attack that left him with seven stab wounds on a train in Cambridgeshire last November. The brutal incident left eleven passengers seriously injured and terrified commuters across the UK. Gjoshe, who now plays semi-professionally in [Cambridgeshire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambridgeshire), said he’s sharing his story to warn others about the dangers of unchecked aggression.

## A night of chaos on the train

Gjoshe was traveling home from a match when the attack unfolded on a packed evening service between [Cambridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambridge) and [Peterborough](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peterborough). He told BBC Sport he was sitting quietly when a group of young men started arguing nearby. Within minutes, the argument escalated into violence. "I didn’t even see the knife coming," Gjoshe said. "One second I was talking to a friend, the next I felt this sharp pain in my side."

The attack lasted just seconds but left lasting scars. Gjoshe was rushed to [Addenbrooke’s Hospital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Addenbrooke%27s_Hospital) in Cambridge with multiple stab wounds to his torso and arm. Doctors told him he was lucky to survive. "I lost a lot of blood," he said. "They had to operate right away. It’s still hard to believe how close it was."

## The aftermath and recovery

Gjoshe spent weeks in hospital recovering from both his physical injuries and the psychological trauma. He’s undergone multiple surgeries and still has visible scars. "The pain was bad, but the mental side was worse," he admitted. "I used to be out all the time, now I’m more cautious. I don’t take things for granted anymore."

His recovery hasn’t been easy. He’s had to relearn basic movements and rebuild his strength. Football, his passion, has been a big part of his healing. "Getting back on the pitch helped," he said. "But it’s not the same. I’ll never forget what happened that night."

## Police investigation and suspect arrested

Police launched a manhunt after the attack, describing the scene as "horrifying." They arrested a 17-year-old boy from [Peterborough](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peterborough) in January, charging him with seven counts of attempted murder and grievous bodily harm. The case is still ongoing, and police have asked anyone with information to come forward.

Gjoshe wants the public to know the attack wasn’t random. "This wasn’t just some accident," he said. "It was deliberate. Those guys had knives. They meant to hurt people."

## A warning for others

Gjoshe’s story is a stark reminder of how quickly violence can erupt in public spaces. He’s urging people to stay alert and avoid confrontation. "If you see something, say something," he advised. "It’s not worth risking your life over a stupid argument."

The attack has also sparked conversations about knife crime in the UK, especially among young people. Campaigners say more needs to be done to prevent such incidents and support victims. Gjoshe agrees. "We need to talk about this more," he said. "People need to understand the real cost of carrying a knife."

For now, Gjoshe is focused on his recovery and getting back to normal life. But he won’t forget what happened. "I want people to know my story," he said. "Not for sympathy, but to make sure it doesn’t happen to anyone else."
