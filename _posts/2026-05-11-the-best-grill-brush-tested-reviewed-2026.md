Bon Appétit put 15 grill brushes to the test, pitting them against 24 pounds of sticky BBQ chicken to determine which tools clean grates most effectively. The experiment aimed to cut through marketing claims and identify brushes that actually remove baked-on gunk without damaging grates or posing safety risks. After months of grilling, scrubbing, and evaluating, five brushes stood out for their performance, durability, and safety.

The testing process involved caked-on chicken fat, charred residues, and frequent reapplication to mimic real-world grilling conditions. Brushes were evaluated on three criteria: effectiveness at removing debris, durability over multiple uses, and safety—including bristle retention and handle grip. Each brush faced the same 24 pounds of BBQ chicken, ensuring a fair comparison across gas and charcoal grills.

## How We Tested: The Grill Brush Showdown

The team at Bon Appétit set up a controlled grilling environment to test each brush under identical conditions. We started with clean grates, applied a thick layer of BBQ sauce mixed with chicken fat, then let it bake under high heat. The goal was to create a worst-case scenario for grill cleaning, forcing brushes to work hard to remove stubborn residue.

After each scrub, we inspected the grates for leftover debris and rated the brush on a 10-point scale for cleanliness. We also checked for bristle shedding, as loose wires can contaminate food and pose a safety hazard. Durability was tested by repeating the process over multiple grilling sessions, with brushes that showed wear or damage disqualified from consideration.

## Top 5 Grill Brushes for 2026

After rigorous testing, five grill brushes emerged as the best performers. The **Weber 3-Section Grill Brush** took the top spot for its stainless steel bristles and sturdy scraper, which easily tackled the thickest layers of baked-on gunk. Its angled handle provided excellent leverage, making it a favorite for both gas and charcoal grills.

The **Grillaholics Original Grill Brush** earned high marks for its heavy-duty construction and replaceable head, which extends its lifespan significantly. Testers appreciated its heat-resistant handle and the included scrapper for tough spots. The **OXO Good Grips Grill Brush** impressed with its comfortable grip and flexible bristles, which conformed to curved grates without missing spots.

For those seeking a budget-friendly option, the **Char-Broil Grill Cleaning Brush** delivered solid performance at a lower price point. Its 18-inch handle kept hands safely away from heat, and the stainless steel bristles held up well under repeated use. Rounding out the top five, the **Kona Safe/Gr8 Grill Brush** stood out for its unique design, featuring a looped bristle pattern that prevented debris from scattering.

## Brushes That Failed the Test

Not all brushes survived the trial. Several models were disqualified for shedding bristles, which can contaminate food and pose a choking hazard. Others failed to remove baked-on residue, leaving grates partially covered in grease and sauce. Safety concerns led to the exclusion of brushes with weak or brittle handles, as they posed risks of breaking mid-use.

The testing also revealed that cheaper brushes often sacrificed durability for affordability. Many low-cost options showed signs of wear after just a few uses, with bristles fraying or handles loosening. These brushes may work for light cleaning but struggled with the heavy-duty task of removing BBQ chicken residue.

## What’s Next for Grill Brush Buyers?

If you're in the market for a new grill brush, the top five models from this test offer reliable options for both casual and serious grillers. Weber, Grillaholics, and OXO are widely available at retailers and online stores, while Char-Broil and Kona Safe/Gr8 offer budget-friendly choices without sacrificing performance.

For those who prefer alternatives, consider grill stones or scrapers made of pumice or wood. These tools can be effective for light cleaning but may not replace a high-quality brush for heavy-duty grates. Always prioritize safety by inspecting brushes before each use and replacing them if bristles show signs of wear.

The right grill brush can make post-cook cleanup faster and more efficient, leaving grates ready for the next barbecue session. Choose wisely based on your grilling habits and grate type to ensure the best results.
