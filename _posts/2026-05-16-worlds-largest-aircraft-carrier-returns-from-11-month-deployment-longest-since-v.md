NORFOLK, Va. — The USS Gerald R. Ford, the world’s largest aircraft carrier, returned to Virginia on Saturday after an 11-month deployment, the longest since the Vietnam War. The [USS Gerald R. Ford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Gerald_R._Ford) and two destroyers docked at Naval Station Norfolk, where about 5,000 sailors waited to reunite with their families for the first time since June of last year.

The deployment involved combat operations across multiple continents, including support for U.S. military actions against Iran and participation in the capture of then-Venezuelan President [Nicolás Maduro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicol%C3%A1s_Maduro) in 2024. Defense Secretary [Pete Hegseth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pete_Hegseth) was present to welcome the returning ships, which included the destroyer USS Bainbridge.

## Fire aboard carrier forced repairs in Crete

During the deployment, the carrier’s crew faced a noncombat-related fire that damaged living quarters and disrupted operations. The incident left hundreds of sailors without sleeping quarters and required lengthy repairs on the Greek island of [Crete](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crete). The Navy has not disclosed the fire’s cause or estimated repair costs.

The USS Gerald R. Ford, commissioned in 2017, is the lead ship of its class and the largest aircraft carrier in the world. It features advanced technology, including electromagnetic aircraft launch systems and a new nuclear reactor. The carrier’s deployment was extended due to global tensions and operational demands.

## Mission included Iran conflict and Maduro raid

According to defense officials, the carrier’s mission included providing air support during U.S. operations against Iran and assisting in the 2024 raid that resulted in the capture of Maduro. The operation in Venezuela was part of U.S. efforts to counter perceived threats from the Maduro regime. The carrier’s presence also served as a deterrent in the Middle East amid escalating tensions.

The return of the USS Gerald R. Ford marks a significant milestone for the U.S. Navy, demonstrating its ability to sustain long deployments despite challenges. The carrier’s crew overcame logistical hurdles, including the fire in Crete, to complete their mission. The Navy has not announced the carrier’s next deployment timeline.

Experts say the extended deployment highlights the increasing demands on the U.S. military amid rising global conflicts. The USS Gerald R. Ford’s return also underscores the importance of aircraft carriers as mobile platforms for power projection. The vessel’s advanced capabilities ensure its role in future operations remains critical.
