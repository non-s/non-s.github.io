Celtic manager Martin O'Neill [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin_O%27Neill] said the club has given him renewed purpose after their dramatic Scottish Premiership title triumph. The 74-year-old, who took charge in 2019 following Brendan Rodgers' departure, admitted the club's success had restored his outlook on life. However, he stopped short of confirming his future beyond the current season during a post-match interview in Glasgow on Saturday.

Speaking after Celtic's 1-0 win over Hearts that sealed the title, O'Neill reflected on the emotional significance of the achievement. "Celtic have given me reasons to live," he said. "This club has been everything to me in recent years." The victory marked the club's third consecutive Premiership title under his leadership, extending their dominance in Scottish football. Despite the personal significance, O'Neill emphasized that his contract status remains unresolved.

## O'Neill's Celtic legacy takes shape

Since replacing Rodgers, O'Neill has rebuilt Celtic into a consistent force in Scottish football. His tenure has included three league titles, two Scottish Cups and a League Cup. The latest championship came despite a turbulent season marked by injuries and squad overhauls. Celtic's ability to grind out results in tight matches has become a hallmark of O'Neill's approach.

The manager's future now hangs in the balance as Celtic prepare for European competition. With the club's hierarchy yet to publicly address his contract situation, speculation has intensified over whether he will continue. O'Neill, known for his tactical acumen and man-management skills, has become synonymous with Celtic's recent success.

## Title win masks deeper club challenges

While the Premiership triumph offers a moment of celebration, underlying issues at Celtic remain unresolved. The club's transfer strategy has drawn criticism, particularly during a summer window that saw key departures without adequate replacements. O'Neill's ability to navigate these challenges has been central to Celtic's resilience.

The 74-year-old has previously spoken about the emotional toll of management, particularly after his departure from Nottingham Forest in 2019. His comments about Celtic giving him "reasons to live" suggest a deep personal connection to the club that extends beyond professional duty. This bond may influence his decision-making process regarding his future.

## What happens next for Celtic and O'Neill?

Celtic's next challenge comes in the Europa League, where they will look to prove their competitiveness against Europe's elite. The club's hierarchy faces a decision on O'Neill's contract, with the season's conclusion providing a natural juncture for discussions. Whether he continues or departs, his impact on Celtic's recent success appears set to endure.

For now, O'Neill's focus remains on the present, with the title win serving as a validation of his leadership. The coming weeks will determine whether he remains at the helm or steps away, leaving behind a legacy that has reshaped Celtic in his image.
