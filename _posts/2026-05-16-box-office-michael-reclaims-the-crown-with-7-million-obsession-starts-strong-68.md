Lionsgate’s Michael Jackson biopic ‘Michael’ reclaimed the domestic box office lead Friday with $7 million in earnings, projecting a $25 million to $28 million weekend total according to industry estimates. The biopic, which faced a 27% drop from last weekend’s take, now faces competition from the thriller ‘Obsession,’ which debuted at $6.8 million to challenge its top spot. Without any major new releases hitting theaters Friday, ‘Michael’ maintained its momentum despite the decline, signaling strong audience interest in the Jackson tribute film.

## ‘Michael’ maintains lead with modest weekend drop
The $7 million Friday haul for ‘Michael’ places it on track for a $25 million to $28 million weekend total, a notable decrease from the $38 million it earned in its opening weekend. Analysts attribute the drop to the absence of major new competition and the film’s established fanbase driving repeat viewings. The biopic, directed by Antoine Fuqua, has drawn praise for its performance by actor Jaafar Jackson, who portrays the pop icon. The film remains in theaters for a second consecutive weekend after its June 18 release date.

‘Obsession,’ a psychological thriller starring Jude Law and Rinko Kikuchi, entered the market with a strong $6.8 million Friday debut. The film, distributed by Sony Pictures, opened to mixed early reviews but benefited from Law’s star power and a marketing push emphasizing suspense and intrigue. Industry watchers expect ‘Obsession’ to generate a $15 million to $18 million weekend total, setting up a potential showdown with ‘Michael’ in the coming weeks.

## Box office dynamics shift with limited new releases
The weekend’s box office dynamics were shaped by the absence of major new releases, leaving established films to battle for audience attention. ‘Michael’ and ‘Obsession’ dominated the top two spots, followed by holdovers like ‘Inside Out 2’ and ‘A Quiet Place: Day One,’ which continued to draw families and genre fans. Theater counts for ‘Michael’ remained robust at 3,700 locations, while ‘Obsession’ expanded to 3,200 screens after a limited pre-release run.

Industry analysts noted that the weekend’s performance reflected a broader trend of audiences gravitating toward proven franchises and biopics, particularly those tied to cultural icons. ‘Michael’ joins a growing list of music biopics that have resonated with audiences this year, including ‘Bob Marley: One Love’ and ‘Back to Black,’ both of which performed strongly at the box office. The film’s success underscores the enduring appeal of Jackson’s legacy more than two decades after his death.

The box office performance of ‘Michael’ and ‘Obsession’ highlights the challenges and opportunities facing mid-budget films in a market dominated by blockbusters and franchises. With ‘Michael’ projected to surpass $50 million domestically by the end of its theatrical run, Lionsgate may extend its marketing push to capitalize on word-of-mouth buzz. Meanwhile, ‘Obsession’ could face stiff competition from an upcoming horror title debuting next weekend, setting the stage for a crowded summer box office.
