The 34th edition of London’s Raindance Film Festival just dropped its full lineup, and it’s packed with surprises. Connor Storrie headlines with April X, a feature he co-wrote and stars in. The festival also screens a Gorillaz documentary, a short film executive-produced by [Jane Fonda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jane_Fonda), and Pinocchio Unstrung with Robert Englund. But the biggest closing draw? The Eddie Cochran documentary Don’t Forget Me, hitting screens right after the festival ends.

Raindance has built a reputation for spotting fresh talent and bold stories, and this year’s slate leans into that tradition. April X follows a young man navigating grief after his brother’s death, a role that marks Storrie’s first major lead in a feature. The Gorillaz film, meanwhile, dives into the band’s creative process and cultural impact, offering fans a rare behind-the-scenes look at the animated quartet’s evolution. Jane Fonda’s involvement in the EP project adds star power, though details remain scarce about the short film’s plot or cast.

## Gorillaz and Jane Fonda join the Raindance mix
The Gorillaz documentary isn’t just another band retrospective. It’s a deep dive into how [Damon Albarn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damon_Albarn) and [Jamie Hewlett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_Hewlett) turned virtual personas into a real-world phenomenon, blending animation, music, and live-action interviews. Jane Fonda’s executive-produced short film, rumored to tackle environmental themes, could draw crowds curious to see how her activism translates to cinema. Neither project has released a trailer yet, but both are already generating buzz ahead of their premieres.

Pinocchio Unstrung, starring Robert Englund, reimagines the classic tale with a darker twist. The veteran actor, best known for his Freddy Krueger role, plays a puppet maker whose creations come to life in unsettling ways. Early reviews describe it as a visually striking but unsettling take on the story, perfect for fans of horror-tinged fairy tales. The film’s inclusion adds a genre-defying edge to Raindance’s usual mix of dramas and comedies.

## Eddie Cochran’s legacy closes the festival
The Eddie Cochran documentary Don’t Forget Me serves as the festival’s closing film, and it’s a fitting tribute. Cochran, a rock ‘n’ roll pioneer who died at 21 in a 1960 car crash, never got to see his cultural impact. The doc features rare archival footage and interviews with surviving bandmates, offering a raw look at his life and untimely death. Festival organizers chose it for its emotional punch and historical weight, marking the 64th anniversary of his passing.

Raindance 2024 runs from September 25 to October 6 at venues across London, including Vue West End and the Regent Street Cinema. Tickets go on sale August 15, with early-bird discounts available for the first weekend. Expect sold-out screenings for the Gorillaz film and the Eddie Cochran doc, but the festival’s strength lies in its hidden gems—like April X and Pinocchio Unstrung—where lesser-known films often steal the spotlight.

The festival’s lineup also reflects a broader trend in indie film: blending music, activism, and personal stories. Jane Fonda’s short film aligns with her long-standing environmental advocacy, while the Gorillaz doc taps into the band’s role as cultural commentators. Even Connor Storrie’s April X fits this mold, using grief as a lens to explore modern masculinity and loss. If Raindance’s past years are any indication, these films will spark conversations long after the festival wraps.
