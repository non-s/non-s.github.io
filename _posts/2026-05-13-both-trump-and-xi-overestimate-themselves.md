## Introduction to Overconfidence: Elites in both China and the US are often tempted to believe in their own myths, leading to an overestimation of their abilities and power. This phenomenon can have significant consequences for global politics and relationships between nations. The tendency to buy into one's own hype can lead to poor decision-making and a lack of understanding of the complexities of international relations. As a result, leaders may overestimate their own strengths and underestimate the challenges they face, ultimately leading to miscalculations and potential conflicts. ## The Role of Nationalism: Nationalism plays a significant role in the overconfidence of leaders in both the US and China. The emphasis on national pride and the need to assert dominance on the world stage can lead to a distorted view of reality, where leaders prioritize their own interests over the needs and concerns of other nations. This approach can lead to a breakdown in international cooperation and an increase in tensions between nations. Furthermore, the rise of nationalism can also lead to a decrease in critical thinking and a lack of nuance in foreign policy decision-making. ## Global Implications: The overconfidence of leaders in the US and China can have far-reaching implications for global politics and international relations. The potential for miscalculations and conflicts is high, and the consequences of such events could be severe. It is essential for leaders to take a more nuanced and realistic approach to international relations, recognizing the complexities and challenges of the global landscape. By doing so, they can work towards building stronger, more cooperative relationships with other nations and avoiding the pitfalls of overconfidence and nationalism.
