Brentford’s [Bryan Mbeumo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bryan_Mbeumo) nearly snatched all three points in stoppage time against Nottingham Forest, but his late chance ended up hitting the post after Morgan Gibbs-White’s equaliser was chalked off for offside. The controversial decision in the 95th minute meant the match finished 1-1 at the [Gtech Community Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gtech_Community_Stadium), leaving both sides still inching toward Premier League safety with six games left to play.

## The controversial finish that changed everything

The drama started when Forest goalkeeper [Wayne Hennessey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wayne_Hennessey) spilled a clearance straight to [Gibbs-White](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morgan_Gibbs-White), who poked the ball into the net. The assistant referee flagged for offside almost immediately, but the VAR review took over a minute to confirm the call. Replays showed Gibbs-White was marginally ahead of the ball when it was played, though the tight margin made it a tough call for fans in the stadium and at home.

Mbeumo then had the clearest chance of the match in the 96th minute. [Ivan Toney](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivan_Toney) threaded a through ball to the forward, who controlled it neatly before rounding Hennessey. His shot cannoned off the post, leaving Brentford players and fans stunned. The rebound fell to [Vitaly Janelt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitaly_Janelt), whose header was brilliantly saved by Hennessey to preserve the point.

## Forest survive another scare as both teams stay in the fight

Forest had started brightly, with [Taiwo Awoniyi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwo_Awoniyi) forcing an early save from [Mark Flekken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mark_Flekken). But it was Brentford who took the lead in the 28th minute when [Neal Maupay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neal_Maupay) capitalized on a defensive error to slot home. Forest responded well, with [Anthony Elanga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthony_Elanga) testing Flekken with a curling effort just before half-time.

The second half saw chances at both ends. [Murillo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Murillo) headed just wide for Forest, while Toney’s flick-on forced Hennessey into another save. But the real talking point was the Gibbs-White goal and Mbeumo’s near-miss, which summed up a match full of missed opportunities for both sides.

## Where this leaves the relegation scrap

With [Burnley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burnley_F.C.) and [Luton Town](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luton_Town_F.C.) still in the bottom three on inferior goal differences, this draw was huge for both Brentford and Forest. Forest move to 33 points, just two clear of the drop zone, while Brentford sit on 34 points—safe for now but still needing points to pull clear.

The next round of fixtures could be decisive. Forest host [Arsenal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arsenal_F.C.) next, while Brentford travel to [Manchester United](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C.). Both sides will be hoping to avoid the kind of late drama that defined this game.
