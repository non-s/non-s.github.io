Grace Caroline Currey, best known for her roles in Shazam! and Fall, will headline the upcoming crime thriller Dead Miles. The film is set to launch in the Cannes market by VMI Worldwide next month. Production is scheduled to begin in British Columbia this summer under director Tom Levesque, who also wrote the screenplay. Minds Eye Entertainment is producing the project, which is positioned as an elevated crime thriller—a genre known for stylish, character-driven stories blending grit and tension.

Dead Miles marks a shift for Currey, who’s built her career in superhero films [Captain Marvel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Captain_Marvel_(film)) and family-friendly blockbusters like The Christmas Chronicles 2. Her move to a darker, crime-focused role shows her range and could draw attention from both genre fans and critics looking for fresh talent. The film’s Cannes launch is timed to maximize visibility among international buyers ahead of its shoot, which will take place in Vancouver and surrounding areas.

Tom Levesque, who wrote and directs Dead Miles, previously helmed Shut Eye, a crime drama series for Hulu. His background in serialized storytelling suggests Dead Miles may carry a layered narrative with sharp dialogue and moral ambiguity. The script, still under wraps, is expected to explore themes of justice and survival in a modern, urban setting. Locations in British Columbia will double for multiple cities, adding visual depth to the story without the logistical hassle of shooting across multiple countries.

VMI Worldwide, the sales agent handling Dead Miles, specializes in placing genre films with distributors worldwide. They’ve positioned the project as a mid-budget thriller with high commercial potential, betting on Currey’s star power and Levesque’s track record to attract buyers. The company’s team will pitch Dead Miles to streamers, theatrical distributors, and indie labels during Cannes, where deals often close quickly in the packed market.

Filming is scheduled to run from July through September, covering 60 shooting days. Crews are already assembling in Vancouver, where tax incentives and experienced crews make British Columbia a top choice for U.S.-based productions. The region’s mix of urban backdrops, forests, and industrial zones gives Levesque flexibility to shoot chase sequences, night scenes, and intimate dramatic moments without leaving the area.

Dead Miles joins a wave of crime thrillers gaining traction in the post-pandemic market. Unlike splashy superhero films, these stories rely on tight scripts and strong performances to stand out. Currey’s involvement could help the film compete with titles like The Mother and Land of Bad, which also blend action with emotional depth. If the shoot goes smoothly and the script delivers, the film may secure a major festival premiere by early 2025.

For Currey, Dead Miles is a chance to build a portfolio beyond family entertainment. Levesque’s direction and the film’s crime genre positioning signal a clear creative pivot. VMI’s Cannes push aims to pair the project with a distributor that values strong storytelling over flashy spectacle. The next few months will determine whether Dead Miles becomes a breakout hit or another solid genre entry.
