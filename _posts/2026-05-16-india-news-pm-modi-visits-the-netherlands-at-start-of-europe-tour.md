Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi [started his five-nation European tour on Saturday](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Narendra_Modi) with a visit to the Netherlands, where he seeks to strengthen trade agreements and reduce India’s reliance on China-centered supply chains. The Netherlands serves as India’s largest export destination in Europe, making it a key partner in Modi’s diplomatic push.

During his visit, Modi addressed the Indian diaspora in The Hague, highlighting their contributions to Dutch society and economy. “Whenever I’ve had a conversation with the leadership of the Netherlands, they always praised the Indian diaspora,” he said. Over 90,000 non-resident Indians live in the Netherlands, with thousands more studying in Dutch universities, according to Indian government data.

## Modi focuses on trade diversification amid global supply chain shifts

Modi’s trip comes as India seeks to diversify its trade partnerships beyond China, which has dominated global supply chains for years. European nations, including the Netherlands, are viewed as critical partners in this effort. Indian officials have emphasized the need to secure alternative trade routes and reduce vulnerabilities exposed during recent geopolitical tensions and pandemic disruptions.

Separate protests disrupted the day as Indian farmers clashed with police near Chandigarh. Authorities deployed tear gas and water cannons to disperse demonstrators marching against government policies they say harm their livelihoods. The clashes underscore ongoing domestic challenges as Modi pursues international economic goals.

## Netherlands highlights as key partner in India’s trade strategy

The Netherlands has long been a top trading partner for India in Europe, with bilateral trade exceeding $10 billion annually. Dutch Prime Minister [Mark Rutte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mark_Rutte) and Modi are expected to discuss ways to expand economic cooperation, including in agriculture, technology, and renewable energy sectors. Dutch investments in India have grown significantly, particularly in infrastructure and smart cities.

Modi’s visit also includes stops in France, Germany, Belgium, and Poland, where he will hold high-level talks with European leaders. Trade, climate cooperation, and defense partnerships are expected to be key discussion points. Analysts say the tour is part of India’s broader strategy to reposition itself as a global trade hub amid rising U.S.-China tensions.

Indian officials say the push for new trade agreements is not just economic but geopolitical, aiming to balance influence between Western blocs and Asian powers. The Netherlands, with its strategic ports and business-friendly policies, is seen as an ideal gateway for Indian goods into the European single market.

What happens next? Modi’s European itinerary includes business forums, bilateral meetings, and cultural engagements. On Sunday, he is scheduled to travel to France for talks with President [Emmanuel Macron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmanuel_Macron), followed by visits to Germany and Belgium. The outcome of these meetings could shape India’s trade and investment landscape for years.
