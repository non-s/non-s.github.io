Russian President [Vladimir Putin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir_Putin) will travel to Beijing May 19-20 for high-level talks with China’s President [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping), the Kremlin and China’s Foreign Ministry confirmed Friday. The visit comes less than two weeks after U.S. President [Donald Trump](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Trump) wrapped up a three-day state visit to Beijing, where he met with Xi and other top officials.

Putin’s trip underscores deepening ties between Russia and China as both nations seek to counterbalance U.S. influence in global trade and security. The two leaders are expected to discuss bilateral trade, energy cooperation, and regional security issues, including North Korea and Ukraine.

## Economic cooperation tops agenda

Trade has been a major focus of recent Russia-China relations. Bilateral trade reached $108 billion in 2023, up 26% from the previous year, according to Chinese customs data. Energy deals, infrastructure projects, and technology exchanges are likely to dominate discussions. Russia has increased oil and gas exports to China amid Western sanctions following its invasion of Ukraine.

## Geopolitical implications grow

The meeting follows Trump’s visit, during which he and Xi held talks on trade, technology restrictions, and global supply chains. Trump’s trip highlighted tensions over tariffs, semiconductor restrictions, and U.S. support for Taiwan. Putin’s visit signals Moscow’s intent to strengthen its partnership with Beijing amid ongoing isolation from Western nations.

## Security and regional stability discussed

Russia and China have deepened military cooperation in recent years, including joint naval exercises and defense technology sharing. North Korea’s missile tests and the war in Ukraine are expected to feature in talks. Both countries have opposed U.S.-led sanctions against North Korea and supported Russia’s war efforts in Ukraine.

Analysts say the timing of Putin’s visit is strategic, coming ahead of a G7 summit and amid escalating U.S.-China trade disputes. The talks could signal a coordinated stance from Moscow and Beijing ahead of global economic and security negotiations. Further agreements on energy, technology, and infrastructure may be announced during the visit.
