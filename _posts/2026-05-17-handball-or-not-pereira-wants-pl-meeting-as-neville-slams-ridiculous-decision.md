Nottingham Forest [manager Nuno Espirito Santo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuno_%C3%89spirito_Santo) isn’t holding back after a nightmare 3-2 loss at [Manchester United](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C.). His biggest issue isn’t the result. It’s the way referee Michael Salisbury overruled the video assistant referee (VAR) to award a goal that Forest believe was clearly scored with a handball from [Bryan Mbeumo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bryan_Mbeumo). The ball struck Mbeumo’s arm as he controlled it between his body and forearm before it fell to [Matheus Cunha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matheus_Cunha), who slotted in for United’s second goal. VAR sent Salisbury to the pitch-side monitor to review the incident. The Stockley Park officials judged it handball, but Salisbury disagreed, calling it accidental and letting the goal stand. Forest left Old Trafford livid. "For me, it was handball, very clear," Espirito Santo said after the game. "It is sad not to cancel the goal. For me it was the decision which decided the game." The frustration isn’t just about handballs. Espirito Santo wants the Premier League to clarify rules around grappling in the box too. Last weekend, Arsenal’s controversial 1-0 win at West Ham turned on a late penalty call that many felt was harsh. "These are the doubts we have at the moment in the Premier League about the end decision," he said. "With the handball, we don’t know when it is a handball or not. The blocks in the box when it is free-kicks, we don’t know when it’s a foul or not." He’s pushing for a league-wide meeting to clear up the confusion. "I think it’s important to have a meeting with everybody and try to understand the rules, the decisions because everybody, all the managers in this moment, they have doubts about some decisions." The handball controversy isn’t new. In 2021, the rules changed to say accidental handballs leading to a goal or clear chance should still be penalized if the player’s position "makes their body unnaturally bigger." But the wording leaves room for interpretation, and officials seem to be applying it inconsistently. Gary Neville, working as a pundit for Sky Sports, didn’t hold back either. "It’s ridiculous," he said during the broadcast. "The law says if it’s deliberate or you’ve made your body unnaturally bigger, it’s handball. Mbeumo’s arm was in a position that made his body bigger. The referee shouldn’t be overruling VAR on that." The debate highlights a bigger problem in the Premier League. Managers, players, and fans are tired of seeing games decided by unclear or inconsistently applied rules. Forest’s loss wasn’t just about one decision. It was about a pattern. Espirito Santo isn’t the only manager frustrated. Arsenal’s Mikel Arteta and others have complained publicly about officiating this season. The league has tried to tighten handball interpretations, but the rules still feel vague in key situations. Until the Premier League brings everyone together to define clear, repeatable standards, these controversies will keep happening. It’s not just about one bad call. It’s about fairness and consistency across the league.
