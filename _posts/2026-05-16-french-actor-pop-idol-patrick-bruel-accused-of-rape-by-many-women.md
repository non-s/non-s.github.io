French actor and singer [Patrick Bruel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patrick_Bruel) has denied allegations of rape made by TV presenter [Flavie Flament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flavie_Flament), who alleges the assault occurred when she was 16 years old. Flament, a well-known French media personality, filed a formal complaint in 2022, prompting legal scrutiny of Bruel’s past behavior. Bruel, a former pop idol turned actor, has faced mounting accusations over recent years, with multiple women coming forward with similar claims.

Flament’s lawyer confirmed the complaint was submitted to prosecutors in Paris, citing the statute of limitations as a complicating factor due to the passage of time since the alleged incident. French law allows rape prosecutions up to 20 years after the crime, but the clock starts only when the victim turns 18, meaning Flament’s window remains open until 2026. Prosecutors have not yet filed charges, and Bruel’s legal team has dismissed the allegations as baseless, calling them an attempt to damage his reputation.

The case follows a pattern of accusations against Bruel, who rose to fame in the 1980s as a pop singer before transitioning into acting. Over the past decade, several women have publicly accused him of sexual misconduct, including coercion and assault, though none have resulted in criminal charges. Flament’s complaint marks the first time a formal rape accusation has been lodged against him, escalating the scrutiny around his past conduct.

Bruel, 62, has maintained his innocence, stating through his representatives that the encounter with Flament was consensual. His legal team has pointed to inconsistencies in Flament’s timeline and questioned the credibility of her claims. However, Flament has insisted her memory of the incident remains clear, describing it as a traumatic violation of her trust and consent. The French public has reacted with a mix of shock and divided opinion, with some defending Bruel’s decades-long career and others demanding accountability.

## Investigation and legal process underway

French prosecutors in Paris are reviewing Flament’s complaint to determine if there is sufficient evidence to proceed with a criminal investigation. If prosecutors find grounds for charges, Bruel could face a trial, where the burden of proof would rest on the accuser. Legal experts note that cases involving historical allegations often hinge on corroborating evidence, such as witness testimonies or documented communications, which can be challenging to obtain after decades.

Flament’s decision to come forward publicly has reignited discussions in France about consent, power dynamics, and the challenges faced by survivors of sexual violence when reporting crimes from their youth. Her story has resonated with other women who have shared similar experiences in the entertainment industry, highlighting systemic issues in how such cases are handled.
