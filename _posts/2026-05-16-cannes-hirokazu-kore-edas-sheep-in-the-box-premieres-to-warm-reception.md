Hirokazu Kore-eda’s latest film, *Sheep in the Box*, debuted at the 77th Cannes Film Festival on Wednesday evening, eliciting praise for its imaginative take on a world reshaped by artificial intelligence. The Japanese director’s 17th feature examines human relationships amid AI’s complete integration into society.

## Kore-eda’s Vision of an AI-Driven Future

The film unfolds in a near-future setting where artificial intelligence governs nearly every aspect of daily life. Kore-eda, known for his emotionally resonant storytelling, presents a society where technology’s dominance raises questions about autonomy and connection. Early screenings drew strong audience reactions, with many highlighting the film’s blend of satire and humanity.

Kore-eda has built a reputation for films like *Shoplifters* and *Nobody Knows*, which prioritize character depth over technological spectacle. *Sheep in the Box* continues this tradition while tackling a timely theme. Critics noted the film’s satirical edge, particularly in its portrayal of AI’s role in family dynamics and societal decisions.

## Cannes Audience Responds to Bold Premise

Attendees at the premiere praised the film’s ambition and emotional weight. Some reviewers compared its tone to Charlie Chaplin’s *Modern Times*, albeit with a more contemporary critique of automation. The audience’s response suggests the film may resonate beyond Cannes, potentially drawing interest from international distributors.

Kore-eda worked with a cast of rising Japanese actors, including newcomers and established stars. The director’s collaboration with cinematographer [Yutaka Yamazaki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yutaka_Yamazaki) ensured a visually distinct aesthetic, balancing stark futuristic elements with intimate human moments.

## Broader Implications for AI in Cinema

The film’s Cannes debut occurs amid growing public debate about AI’s societal impact. Recent studies show increasing concerns over AI’s influence on jobs and relationships, themes *Sheep in the Box* explores through fiction. Kore-eda’s approach frames AI as both a tool and a force altering human behavior.

Industry analysts predict the film could spark discussions about technology’s role in storytelling. Its premiere follows last year’s Cannes winner, *Anora*, and arrives before the festival’s awards announcement on May 25. Whether *Sheep in the Box* will secure distribution deals remains to be seen, but early buzz suggests strong potential.

Enquanto o diretor japonês costuma explorar laços familiares e sociais em seus trabalhos anteriores, desta vez ele mergulha em um enredo distópico, onde máquinas e humanos coexistem sob tensas relações de poder.

O filme chega em um momento crucial para o debate sobre IA, especialmente no Brasil, onde a regulamentação da tecnologia ainda é um tema em discussão no Congresso e entre especialistas. A partir de agora, o longa deve circular por festivais internacionais e, em breve, pode ganhar distribuição no Brasil, onde cineastas e estudiosos da sétima arte aguardam ansiosos por sua chegada.
