Aston Villa [secured Champions League football](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024%E2%80%9325_UEFA_Champions_League) for next season with a dominant 4-2 victory over Liverpool at Villa Park on Friday night. Ollie Watkins struck twice as Unai Emery’s side leapfrogged their rivals into fourth place with 62 points from 37 games, while Liverpool remain on 59 points.

The win extended Villa’s unbeaten run to five matches and moved them four points clear of their visitors. Liverpool, meanwhile, slipped to a 12th defeat of the season and have managed just one point from their last nine games, leaving them vulnerable to being caught by both Bournemouth and Brighton in the race for a top-four finish.

Morgan Rogers opened the scoring for Villa in the 17th minute, but Liverpool responded quickly through [Virgil van Dijk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgil_van_Dijk), who equalised just three minutes into the second half. The Dutch defender then restored parity for the visitors in added time at the end of the first half, leaving the score 2-2 at the break.

Watkins restored Villa’s lead in the 52nd minute with a clinical finish, and John McGinn sealed the victory with a brilliant late strike in the 89th minute. The result leaves Liverpool’s top-four hopes hanging by a thread as they prepare to face Brighton next.

## Villa dominant in front of home crowd

Aston Villa boss Unai Emery praised his team’s resilience after the victory. “We had to get over the line,” McGinn told Sky Sports. “It allows us to be excited and enjoy Wednesday properly.” The win comes just days before Villa face [SC Freiburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SC_Freiburg) in the Europa League final, adding extra incentive for the club to finish the season strongly.

## Liverpool’s title defense falters

Liverpool’s defeat marks their worst run of form in years, with only one win in their last 10 matches across all competitions. Manager Jürgen Klopp faces growing scrutiny over his tactics and squad selection as the Reds struggle to maintain consistency. With just two games remaining, their focus now shifts to securing a top-four finish and avoiding a potential Europa League exit.

The result also shifts the narrative in the Premier League title race, with [Arsenal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arsenal_F.C.) and [Manchester City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C.) maintaining their pursuit of the top spot. Villa’s victory ensures they will return to the Champions League for the first time since 2010-11, a major achievement for the club under Emery’s leadership.

With the Europa League final looming, Villa will be buoyed by their momentum, while Liverpool must regroup quickly to avoid missing out on Europe’s elite competition next season.
