Film and TV producers just got a faster way to find the right insurance. Twickenham Film Studios flipped the switch on Twickenham Investment Services, a brand-new FCA-regulated introducer business designed to match producers with specialist brokers. The first partner on board is Miller Insurance Services, a long-time player in entertainment risk. The move was announced at Variety’s International Movie lunch in Cannes, where industry execs were already talking about rising production costs and tighter budgets.

## Why this matters for producers
Producers have always struggled to cut through the noise when shopping for production insurance. Between general liability, equipment coverage, and cast insurance, the hunt for the right policy can eat up weeks. Twickenham’s new service skips the guesswork by doing the legwork for them. Instead of cold-calling brokers or sorting through endless options, producers get a shortlist of vetted specialists tailored to their project’s needs. The referral-only model means they’re not getting pitched by every broker under the sun.

The timing isn’t random. Production insurance premiums have climbed 25% in the last two years, thanks to higher equipment values and more location shoots. Indie producers, in particular, feel the squeeze. A quick referral service like this could save them thousands in both time and premiums. Twickenham isn’t the first to try this—companies like [Entertainment Partners](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entertainment_Partners) have offered similar services for years—but the FCA regulation adds a layer of trust that’s hard to ignore.

## Miller’s role in the rollout
Miller Insurance Services has been writing entertainment policies since the 1980s. They’re the kind of broker that knows the difference between a drone operator’s coverage and a stunt coordinator’s policy. For Twickenham, teaming up with Miller gives the service instant credibility. A spokesperson for Miller confirmed the partnership covers referrals for film, TV, and commercial productions, with a focus on fast turnaround for quotes.

The two companies aren’t revealing exact numbers, but insiders say the service will start with a handful of UK-focused brokers before expanding. Twickenham’s own studio space—home to hits like *Top Gun: Maverick* and *The Crown*—gives the project extra weight. If producers trust Twickenham’s track record, they’re more likely to trust the insurance referrals that come with it.

## What’s next for producers
For now, producers can sign up through Twickenham’s website, but the service isn’t live everywhere yet. The plan is to expand across the UK first, then look at Europe. Twickenham hasn’t said if they’ll charge brokers or producers for the referrals, but the bet is that volume will make up the difference. The bigger question is whether other studios or insurers will follow suit. If demand grows, Twickenham could end up running a de facto marketplace.

The real test will be how quickly producers adopt it. Early chatter in Cannes suggests some are already asking for the link. If the service lives up to the hype, it could become as routine as booking a studio space or hiring a line producer. For now, though, it’s just one more tool in a crowded toolbox—but one that’s finally tailored to the chaos of making movies.
