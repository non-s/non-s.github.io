Harry Styles [Harry Styles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Styles) launched his highly anticipated “Together Together” tour Saturday at Amsterdam’s Johan Cruijff Arena, marking the start of a global run expected to hit 150-plus cities. The singer opened the concert with Are You Listening Yet?, the lead single from his forthcoming fourth solo album, which shares the same title. Earlier this month, Styles previewed tour material during a one-night-only concert in Manchester, streamed exclusively on [Netflix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Netflix). That performance served as a soft launch for the tour’s set list and stage production, which featured elaborate lighting and a rotating central platform.

## Styles Mixes Hits, Deep Cuts in Amsterdam Debut

Styles performed 21 songs during the Amsterdam show, blending fan favorites with deeper cuts from his three previous solo albums. The set list included Golden, As It Was and Kiwi, alongside tracks from Harry’s House such as Music for a Sushi Restaurant and Satellite. A stripped-down piano version of Matilda surprised the crowd, while Styles also performed the unreleased song Who We Are, a fan-favorite from soundchecks. The concert’s production matched the album’s eclectic energy, with rapid costume changes and a dynamic stage that split into multiple sections.

The Amsterdam crowd of 60,000-plus reacted with near-constant standing ovations, particularly during the encore, which featured a soaring rendition of Love of My Life. Styles interacted frequently with fans, pausing between songs to share stories about the album’s recording process. The concert’s run time exceeded two hours, reflecting the tour’s expanded set list compared to previous live performances.

## Tour Follows Netflix Special, Fourth Album Release

Styles’ “Together Together” tour follows the release of his fourth solo album of the same name, released in May. The album debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 and spawned three singles: As It Was, Late Night Talking and Music for a Sushi Restaurant. The Netflix special, titled Harry Styles: Love On Tour, captured a 2021 performance in Los Angeles and served as a precursor to the tour’s stage production.

The tour’s European leg runs through July, with North American dates beginning in August. Tickets sold out within hours of going on sale, with resale prices reaching several times face value. Promoters added extra dates in major markets like London, Paris and New York due to overwhelming demand.

## Production Highlights, Fan Reactions Dominate Social Media

Social media erupted with praise for the Amsterdam show’s production, particularly the use of LED screens and pyrotechnics. Fans shared videos of the rotating stage and costume changes, which included everything from sequined suits to casual streetwear. The tour’s official Instagram account posted behind-the-scenes footage, showing Styles rehearsing with a full band and dancers in Los Angeles.

Critics noted the tour’s emphasis on choreography, marking a shift from Styles’ previous more static performances. The show’s lighting director told reporters the production aimed to mirror the album’s blend of soft rock and disco influences. Merchandise sales at the venue exceeded expectations, with fans purchasing tour shirts, scarves and limited-edition vinyl pressings.

Next up for the tour is a June 10 performance in Berlin, followed by stops in Stockholm, Hamburg and Vienna. The North American leg begins August 20 in Chicago, with additional cities yet to be announced. Industry analysts expect the tour to gross over $300 million, based on current ticket sales and venue capacities.
