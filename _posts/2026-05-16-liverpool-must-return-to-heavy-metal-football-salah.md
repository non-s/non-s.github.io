Liverpool striker Mohamed Salah has demanded his team rediscover the fearsome attacking style that once made them one of Europe’s most feared outfits. The Egypt international said the club must return to “heavy metal football” after a trophyless 2023-24 season left fans frustrated and questions swirling over their future direction.

Salah, speaking to BBC Sport, emphasized that Liverpool’s identity under former manager [Jürgen Klopp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_Klopp) was built on relentless pressure, high pressing, and explosive attacking play. His comments come as the club faces scrutiny over its recent performances, including a shock FA Cup exit to [Manchester United](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C.) and a disappointing Premier League campaign that saw them finish fifth.

## Liverpool's identity crisis

The 32-year-old forward, who has been with Liverpool since 2017, suggested the team has lost sight of its core principles. “We need to go back to playing the football that made us so dangerous,” Salah said. “Fans loved us when we were attacking, when we were fearless. That’s what we must bring back.” His remarks echo growing concerns among supporters about the club’s tactical direction under current manager [Arne Slot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arne_Slot), who took charge in June 2024 after the departure of [Klopp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_Klopp).

Liverpool’s 2023-24 season was marked by inconsistency, with key injuries to players like [Virgil van Dijk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgil_van_Dijk) and [Trent Alexander-Arnold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trent_Alexander-Arnold) exacerbating their struggles. The team’s inability to break down low blocks and their vulnerability in transitions were repeatedly exposed, culminating in a Europa League quarter-final exit to [Atalanta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atalanta_Bergamasca_Calcio).

## Financial pressures and transfer activity

Financial constraints imposed by UEFA’s [Financial Fair Play](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UEFA_Financial_Fair_Play_Regulations) regulations have limited Liverpool’s ability to spend freely this summer. Reports suggest the club has prioritized squad depth over marquee signings, with defensive reinforcements and a new striker expected to arrive before the transfer window closes. Salah, however, believes the solution lies not just in recruitment but in a cultural reset within the team.

The forward’s comments also come amid speculation over his own future. At 32, Salah’s contract expires in 2025, and reports in Egypt suggest he may return to [Al Ahly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al_Ahly) when his Liverpool deal ends. For now, he insists his focus is solely on helping the club regain its competitive edge.

## What happens next for Liverpool

Liverpool’s next major test comes on August 17, when they face [Brighton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C.) in the Premier League opener. Slot has promised a more aggressive, high-tempo style, but delivering on that promise will require immediate improvement from key players. The club’s hierarchy, meanwhile, is expected to support Slot’s vision while addressing any gaps in the squad.

For Salah, the message is clear: Liverpool must rediscover the swagger that made them champions in 2020. “The supporters deserve better,” he said. “We owe it to them to play with passion, with intensity. That’s how we win trophies.”
