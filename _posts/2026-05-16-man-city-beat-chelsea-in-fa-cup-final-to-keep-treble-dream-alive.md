Manchester City [won their eighth FA Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FA_Cup) with a tense 1-0 victory over Chelsea at Wembley on Saturday, keeping their treble aspirations intact. The match remained deadlocked until the 88th minute, when Ghanaian winger Antoine Semenyo produced a moment of magic to secure City’s second piece of silverware this season. Semenyo’s audacious back-flick finish, curling the ball past Chelsea goalkeeper Robert Sánchez, ended a largely scrappy final and etched his name among the competition’s most iconic scorers.

The goal marked the climax of a cagey encounter, with both teams struggling to break through each other’s defenses. Manchester City, chasing a historic treble, dominated possession but found Chelsea’s backline stubborn. Erling Haaland’s late cross provided the decisive moment, though Chelsea nearly equalized in stoppage time when Conor Gallagher’s effort rattled the crossbar. City manager Pep Guardiola [now has 20 trophies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pep_Guardiola) in a decade at the club, including three FA Cups, and extended his unbeaten run in finals to four.

## Pep Guardiola remains undefeated in FA Cup finals with City
Pep Guardiola’s [Manchester City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C.) have now won three FA Cups under his leadership, including back-to-back titles in 2023 and 2024. The victory also ended City’s two-game losing streak in FA Cup finals, having fallen to Crystal Palace and Manchester United in their last two appearances. Guardiola, who has reshaped English football since his arrival in 2016, emphasized his team’s resilience after a season marked by high expectations and occasional stumbles in the Premier League.

City’s triumph follows their [League Cup win over Arsenal in March](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_EFL_Cup_Final), giving them two domestic trophies in 2024 alone. The club remains on course for a potential treble, with the Champions League final still to come. Chelsea, meanwhile, suffered their third consecutive final defeat, extending a poor run of form in showpiece matches.

Semenyo’s strike was his first for the club and capped a remarkable rise for the 26-year-old, who joined from Bournemouth in January. Born just miles from Chelsea’s Stamford Bridge, the Ghana international became only the sixth player to score an FA Cup final goal in the 21st century. His improvised finish echoed the creativity of past winners like Michael Owen and Steven Gerrard, securing his place in FA Cup folklore.

City will now turn their attention to securing the Premier League title and progressing in the Champions League. A win over Real Madrid in the final would complete a historic treble, a feat no English club has achieved. Guardiola’s side will face Crystal Palace in their next Premier League fixture as they chase an unprecedented third consecutive league crown. The FA Cup victory serves as a morale boost ahead of what promises to be a pivotal run-in in multiple competitions.

The triumph also highlights the growing influence of African talent in English football, with Semenyo’s goal the latest example of the continent’s impact on the game. His performance earned praise from pundits and fans alike, with many tipping him for a bright future at City.
