Northern Ireland’s [Rory McIlroy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rory_McIlroy) fought back into contention at the US PGA Championship on Friday, carding a 4-under 66 to climb to five under for the tournament. The four-time major winner sits two strokes behind overnight leaders [Will Zalatoris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Will_Zalatoris) and [Xander Schauffele](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xander_Schauffele), who each posted 6-under rounds at [Aronimink Golf Club](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aronimink_Golf_Club) in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania.

McIlroy, a former PGA champion, recovered from a disappointing opening round of 72 with a steady back nine, including three birdies in his last five holes. His round was built on strong iron play and clutch putting, particularly on the treacherous greens that have already exposed flaws in many top-ranked players. Only 12 golfers broke par through two rounds at a course that has long been considered a stern test of accuracy and course management.

## Aronimink exposes world’s best

The 72-hole layout at [Aronimink Golf Club](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aronimink_Golf_Club) has repeatedly challenged elite fields in major championships, including the 2010 AT&T National and 2018 BMW Championship. This week’s PGA event is no exception, with only a handful of players managing to navigate the tree-lined fairways and undulating greens without major mistakes. Many of the world’s top-ranked golfers struggled with the firm, fast conditions, turning what is typically a par-70 course into a more formidable par-72 challenge.

Among those who faltered were world number two [Scottie Scheffler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scottie_Scheffler), who limped to a 3-over 73 on Friday, and [Jon Rahm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jon_Rahm), who carded a 2-over 72. Both entered the weekend with realistic chances of winning but now trail by five strokes or more. Even defending champion [Justin Thomas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justin_Thomas), playing in his home state of Alabama, has failed to find rhythm, sitting at even par through two rounds.

## McIlroy’s mental resilience on display

McIlroy’s resurgence comes after a frustrating Thursday where he missed the cut at the FedEx Cup Playoffs just one week prior. The 34-year-old admitted after his round that his focus had sharpened after watching leaders struggle to break par. “It’s a tough golf course,” McIlroy said. “You have to be patient and take what it gives you. Today, I felt like I did that for the most part.”

His playing partner, [Patrick Cantlay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patrick_Cantlay), also fired a 4-under 66 to move into a tie for third at six under, while [Max Homa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Homa) sits at four under after a 67. The thinning field now features a mix of proven champions and rising stars, with several players still within striking distance heading into the weekend.

## Weekend stakes rise as weather looms

With Saturday’s third round set to begin early, players will face the added challenge of potential weather disruptions, including possible thunderstorms forecasted for the afternoon. The PGA Championship traditionally plays through weather when necessary, but course conditions may shift dramatically if rain arrives. Leaders will need to manage risk carefully, as one poor hole could swing the momentum entirely.

For McIlroy, a PGA Championship victory would cement his legacy as one of the game’s all-time greats, while a strong weekend showing could restore confidence ahead of the Ryder Cup later this year. The 2023 event at [Aronimink Golf Club](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aronimink_Golf_Club) promises to deliver more drama as the cutline tightens and the pressure intensifies.

Com a competição ainda em andamento, a torcida brasileira já pode se preparar para acompanhar os próximos lances decisivos, que prometem definir não apenas o campeão do PGA Championship, mas também o futuro do golfe no cenário global.
