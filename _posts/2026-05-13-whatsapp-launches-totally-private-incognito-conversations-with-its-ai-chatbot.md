Meta-owned WhatsApp has launched a new 'incognito' mode for its AI chatbot, enabling users to engage in private conversations that don’t appear in their main chat history. The feature, available globally on both iOS and Android, adds a layer of privacy for users interacting with WhatsApp’s AI assistant.

## How the 'incognito' mode works
The 'incognito' mode allows users to start fresh chats with WhatsApp’s AI that remain hidden from the inbox and message archive. These conversations are not stored in the usual chat list, reducing clutter for users who frequently interact with the AI assistant. Users can still access these chats through a dedicated 'Incognito' folder in the app.

WhatsApp confirmed the rollout in a blog post, stating the feature was designed to give users more control over their privacy while using AI tools. The company did not specify whether these chats are end-to-end encrypted like regular messages, but emphasized that the mode prevents them from appearing in the main chat interface.

## Privacy concerns and accountability
Cybersecurity experts have raised questions about the implications of hiding AI chatbot conversations. Privacy advocate and cybersecurity researcher [Bruce Schneier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruce_Schneier) noted that while the feature enhances user privacy, it could also reduce accountability if AI responses lead to issues such as misinformation or harmful advice.

"If AI chats are deleted by default, there’s no record of what was said," Schneier said. "That could be a problem if the AI provides incorrect or harmful information." WhatsApp has not addressed whether users can export or retain copies of 'incognito' chats for accountability purposes.

## User experience and adoption
Early reactions from beta testers suggest the feature is well-received among privacy-conscious users. Many have praised the ability to keep AI interactions separate from personal chats, especially for sensitive queries. However, some users have expressed confusion over how to locate or manage these hidden conversations.

WhatsApp’s AI chatbot, introduced in 2024, has become a core part of the app’s functionality, offering features like language translation, trip planning, and general assistance. The 'incognito' mode is the latest in a series of updates aimed at refining the AI experience while addressing user concerns about data privacy.

## Broader implications for AI on messaging platforms
The introduction of 'incognito' mode reflects a growing trend among messaging platforms to integrate AI while balancing privacy expectations. Competitors like [Telegram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telegram_(software)) and [Signal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Signal_(messaging_app)) have also introduced AI features, though neither currently offers a similar hidden chat option. Industry analysts suggest this move could pressure other platforms to prioritize user privacy in AI interactions.

WhatsApp’s update comes amid increasing scrutiny over how AI tools handle user data. Earlier this year, the company faced criticism for its data-sharing practices with third-party AI models. The 'incognito' mode may help alleviate some concerns by giving users more control over their AI interactions.
